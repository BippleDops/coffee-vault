## üìã Goal Statement

### Primary Objective

To systematically explore and deeply understand African coffee origins through comprehensive tasting, research, and documentation of coffees from Ethiopia, Kenya, Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania, and other significant African producing countries. This exploration will develop sensory expertise in African coffee profiles, understanding of how terroir and processing shape flavor, and appreciation for the incredible diversity and quality of African coffees.

Africa is the birthplace of coffee‚ÄîCoffea arabica originated in Ethiopian highlands and spread from there across the world. African coffees, particularly from East Africa, are among the most prized and distinctive in specialty coffee. Ethiopian natural process coffees with their explosive fruit flavors, Kenyan washed coffees with their bright acidity and complex fruit notes, Rwandan coffees with their tea-like bodies‚Äîthese represent some of coffee's most exciting and unique flavor profiles.

This goal encompasses systematic tasting across regions and processing methods, research into each origin's history and production methods, understanding how varieties and terroir create distinctive profiles, documentation of tasting experiences, and building a comprehensive mental map of African coffee that informs future purchasing and brewing decisions.

### Comprehensive Scope

**Geographic Coverage**:
- **Ethiopia**: Multiple regions (Yirgacheffe, Sidamo, Guji, Harrar, Limu), both washed and natural processing
- **Kenya**: Different regions and processing stations, understanding SL-28/SL-34 varieties
- **Rwanda**: Bourbon varieties, understanding specialty coffee renaissance
- **Burundi**: Similar profiles to Rwanda, emerging specialty market
- **Tanzania**: Peaberry and standard beans, relationship to Kenyan profiles
- **Malawi, Zambia, Uganda**: Emerging origins worth exploring
- **Optional**: Yemen (technically Arabian Peninsula but coffee origins), other emerging African origins

**Processing Method Exploration**:
- **Washed/Wet Process**: Understanding clean, bright profile
- **Natural/Dry Process**: Understanding fruit-forward, complex profiles
- **Honey Process**: Less common in Africa but exists
- **Experimental Processing**: Carbonic maceration, anaerobic fermentation (increasingly common)

**Variety Understanding**:
- Ethiopian heirloom varieties (complex mix)
- Kenyan SL-28, SL-34 (developed for quality and disease resistance)
- Bourbon in Rwanda/Burundi
- Understanding how varieties affect flavor

**Systematic Tasting**:
- Minimum 40-50 distinct African coffees over 8 months
- Side-by-side comparisons (same origin, different processing)
- Blind tastings to develop identification skills
- Multiple brew methods (espresso, pour over, cupping)
- Detailed documentation of each coffee

**Knowledge Development**:
- History of coffee in each region
- Current production statistics and trends
- Processing infrastructure and methods
- Export systems and quality standards
- Specialty coffee development in each origin
- Fair trade, direct trade, and ethical sourcing considerations

### Why This Matters

**Sensory Development**: African coffees push flavor boundaries‚Äîtasting them develops palate in ways other origins cannot. The intensity and clarity of African coffee profiles trains sensory discrimination.

**Coffee Appreciation**: Understanding African origins deepens appreciation for all coffee. Recognizing how terroir, variety, and processing create distinctive flavors enhances every coffee experience.

**Purchasing Decisions**: After systematic exploration, coffee purchasing becomes more informed. Know what to expect from "Ethiopia Yirgacheffe Natural" vs. "Kenya AA Nyeri," can identify good values, avoid disappointments.

**Origin Identification**: Blind tasting skill‚Äîcan identify Ethiopian vs. Kenyan vs. Rwandan by taste profile. This is impressive skill and demonstrates deep coffee knowledge.

**Supporting Producers**: Understanding African coffee contexts‚Äîchallenges, innovations, quality initiatives‚Äîenables more conscious consumer choices and support of producers doing exceptional work.

**Cultural Connection**: Coffee is deeply embedded in African cultures (especially Ethiopia). Understanding origins means understanding people, history, and cultural practices around coffee.

### Personal Motivation

I'm drawn to African coffees more than any other origin. When I taste exceptional Ethiopian natural or bright Kenyan AA, it's transcendent. But my knowledge is fragmented‚ÄîI've tasted some African coffees but not systematically, can't reliably identify origins blind, don't understand regional distinctions well.

This goal represents commitment to deepening my favorite coffee category. Rather than random purchases hoping for good coffee, I want systematic understanding. I want to taste Yirgacheffe vs. Sidamo side-by-side and understand differences. I want to know why Kenyan coffees taste how they do. I want the satisfaction of blind tasting a coffee and correctly identifying "This is Ethiopian natural process."

I'm also motivated by storytelling‚Äîevery African coffee has a story of producers, communities, challenges overcome, quality achieved. Learning these stories enriches the tasting experience and connects me to coffee's human dimension.

### Success Vision

When I achieve this goal, I will:

- Have tasted 50+ distinct African coffees across regions and processes
- Understand regional flavor profiles deeply (can describe what makes Ethiopian, Kenyan, Rwandan coffees distinctive)
- Identify major African origins blind with 70-80% accuracy
- Have comprehensive tasting notes and documentation
- Understand processing's impact on flavor through direct comparison
- Know which African origins and processes I prefer and why
- Make informed African coffee purchases going forward
- Share knowledge with others interested in African coffees
- Feel deep appreciation and connection to African coffee origins

### Phase 1: Ethiopian Exploration (Months 1-3)

**Focus**: Deep dive into Ethiopian coffees‚Äîorigin of coffee, most diverse, most complex

**Month 1: Ethiopian Naturals**
- Purchase 3-4 Ethiopian natural process coffees from different regions:
  - Yirgacheffe natural
  - Sidamo natural
  - Guji natural
  - Harrar natural (if available)
- Taste each individually (pour over, cupping)
- Side-by-side comparisons (all four Ethiopians tasted same day)
- Blind tasting practice (can I identify which is which after initial tasting?)
- Documentation: Detailed tasting notes, flavor profiles, comparisons

**Month 2: Ethiopian Washed**
- Purchase 3-4 Ethiopian washed coffees from same regions as Month 1 if possible:
  - Yirgacheffe washed
  - Sidamo washed
  - Guji washed
  - Other regional washed
- Taste each individually
- Side-by-side comparisons (all washed Ethiopians)
- Cross-comparison: Same region, natural vs. washed (e.g., Yirgacheffe natural vs. washed)
- Documentation: Notes on how processing changes flavor

**Month 3: Ethiopian Deep Dive and Research**
- Purchase 2-3 additional Ethiopian coffees (fill gaps, explore more regions)
- Research: History of Ethiopian coffee, varieties, processing infrastructure, export systems
- Read: James Hoffmann Ethiopia section, online resources, roaster origin stories
- Compile: Ethiopian coffee guide for myself (regions, characteristics, preferences)
- Reflection: What have I learned about Ethiopian coffee? What surprised me?

**Success Criteria Phase 1**:
- 10-12 Ethiopian coffees tasted and documented
- Understand washed vs. natural processing impact deeply
- Can identify major Ethiopian regions with reasonable accuracy
- Have comprehensive Ethiopian coffee knowledge
- Clear preferences within Ethiopian coffees

### Phase 2: Kenyan Exploration (Months 3-5)

**Focus**: Understanding Kenyan coffee‚Äîbrightness, complexity, SL varieties

**Month 3-4: Kenyan AA and Regional Diversity**
- Purchase 4-5 Kenyan coffees from different regions/factories:
  - Nyeri region
  - Kirinyaga region
  - Kiambu region
  - Different grades (AA, AB, PB if available)
- Taste each individually
- Side-by-side comparisons
- Note: Kenyan acidity characteristics, SL variety flavors, body
- Documentation: Detailed notes on what makes Kenyan coffee distinctive

**Month 4-5: Kenyan Processing and Grading**
- Purchase 2-3 more Kenyan coffees (explore different factories, grades)
- Research: Kenyan coffee auction system, SL varieties development, grading system
- Understand: What does "AA" mean? What is a factory? How does auction work?
- Cross-comparison: Kenya vs. Ethiopia (how do they differ?)
- Blind tasting: Can I identify Kenyan vs. Ethiopian?

**Month 5: Kenyan Consolidation**
- Purchase 1-2 exceptional Kenyan coffees for final exploration
- Compile: Kenyan coffee guide (regions, grading, characteristics, preferences)
- Practice: Blind identification Kenya vs. Ethiopia (aim for 80%+ accuracy)

**Success Criteria Phase 2**:
- 8-10 Kenyan coffees tasted and documented
- Understand Kenyan coffee system (auction, grading, processing)
- Can identify Kenyan coffee characteristics reliably
- Understand SL varieties and their flavor impact
- Clear sense of favorite Kenyan regions/profiles

### Phase 3: Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania Exploration (Months 5-7)

**Focus**: Expanding to other East African origins

**Month 5-6: Rwandan Coffee**

- Research: Rwandan specialty coffee history, post-genocide development, Bourbon varieties

**Month 6: Burundian Coffee**
- Purchase 2-3 Burundian coffees

- Understand: What makes Tanzania distinctive vs. Kenya?

- 8-10 coffees from Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania tasted and documented
