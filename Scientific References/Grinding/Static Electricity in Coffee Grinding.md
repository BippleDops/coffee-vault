## üìã Overview

Static electricity plagues coffee grinding - friction creates electrical charge causing grounds to clump, stick to grinder, and distribute unevenly in portafilter, creating consistency and extraction problems. Understanding static and elimination techniques (RDT - Ross Droplet Technique) dramatically improves grinding quality and espresso consistency.

**Why Static is Problematic**:
- **Clumping**: Fines stick together, form false "larger" particles
- **Retention**: Grounds stick to grinder (increased retention, stale grounds)
- **Uneven Distribution**: Clumps create uneven espresso puck
- **Channeling**: Clumps in portafilter cause channeling
- **Messy**: Grounds stick everywhere, jump out of container

**Causes**:
- **Friction**: Grinding creates friction = static charge
- **Low Humidity**: Dry air increases static (winter worse)
- **Fast Grinding**: Higher RPM = more friction = more static
- **Fines**: Small particles especially prone (high surface area)

---

## üíß RDT (Ross Droplet Technique)

**Solution**: Add tiny amount of water before grinding

**Method**:
1. **Spray**: 1-2 drops water on beans before grinding
2. **Mix**: Shake beans to distribute moisture
3. **Grind**: Immediately after spraying
4. **Result**: Zero static, no clumping, perfect flow

**Why It Works**:
- **Conductivity**: Water conducts electricity (dissipates charge)
- **Surface Moisture**: Prevents charge buildup
- **Minimal**: 1-2 drops insufficient to affect extraction

**Benefits**:
- **No Static**: Grounds flow freely
- **No Clumping**: Particles stay separated
- **Lower Retention**: Grounds don't stick to grinder
- **Even Distribution**: Better espresso puck preparation
- **Cleaner**: Less mess, grounds don't jump

**Concerns**:
- **Moisture**: Some worry about adding water to coffee
- **Reality**: 1-2 drops = 0.1g water in 18g coffee (0.5% moisture) - negligible
- **Evaporates**: Moisture evaporates during grinding/brewing
- **Proven**: Used by professionals globally, no negative effects

## üîß Other Anti-Static Methods

**Grinder Sprays**:
- Commercial anti-static sprays
- Spray inside grinder
- Temporary effect

- Increase room humidity
- Reduces static generally
- Helps but doesn't eliminate

**Grinder Design**:
- **Niche Zero**: Designed to minimize static
- **Some Grinders**: Better designs reduce static inherently

**Best Solution**: **RDT** (simplest, most effective, free)

## üìù Summary

Static electricity from grinding friction causes clumping, retention, and uneven distribution - eliminated through RDT (Ross Droplet Technique: 1-2 drops water on beans before grinding) which dissipates charge without affecting extraction. RDT is simplest, most effective anti-static solution used by professionals globally.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Static**: Grinding friction creates electrical charge
- **Problems**: Clumping, retention, uneven distribution, channeling
- **RDT**: 1-2 drops water before grinding (eliminates static)
- **Safe**: Negligible moisture, no extraction impact
- **Best Solution**: Simplest, most effective anti-static method
