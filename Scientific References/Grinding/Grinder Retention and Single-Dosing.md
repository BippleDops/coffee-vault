## üìã Overview

Grinder retention (coffee grounds trapped inside grinder) affects freshness, flavor, and consistency - with retained grounds exiting in subsequent grinds creating staleness and inconsistency. Single-dosing workflow (weighing beans before grinding, grinding exactly what you need) minimizes retention effects while ensuring freshness and precise dosing.

**What is Retention**:
- **Definition**: Coffee grounds that remain in grinder after grinding
- **Location**: Burrs, chute, exit channel, static cling
- **Amount**: 0.2g to 5g+ depending on grinder design
- **Problem**: Stale grounds from previous session exit during next grind
- **Impact**: Flavor degradation, inconsistent dosing

**Why Retention Matters**:
- **Staleness**: Retained grounds oxidize, become stale (hours to days)
- **Inconsistency**: Unknown amount exits each grind (dose variation)
- **Flavor**: Stale grounds mix with fresh (compromised cup)
- **Espresso**: Especially problematic (precision required)
- **Single-Origin**: Mixing different coffees (contamination)

---

## üî¨ Types of Retention

**Exchange Retention** (Most Common):
- **Definition**: Old grounds exit as new grounds push through
- **Amount**: Relatively consistent gram-for-gram exchange
- **Effect**: Stale grounds in current dose
- **Example**: Grind 18g, get 18g out (but 2g old + 16g fresh)

**Dead Retention**:
- **Definition**: Grounds trapped, don't exit even with purging
- **Location**: Deep in burrs, crevices, static cling
- **Amount**: Accumulates over time
- **Effect**: Eventual buildup, cleaning required

**Popcorning Retention**:
- **Definition**: Whole beans bouncing in hopper, not feeding
- **Single-Dosing Issue**: Light beans, Niche Zero, DF64 (common complaint)
- **Solution**: Bellows, RDT (Ross Droplet Technique)

---

## üí° Single-Dosing Workflow

**What is Single-Dosing**:
- **Method**: Weigh exact dose needed, grind all, use immediately
- **No Hopper**: Don't leave beans in hopper
- **Fresh**: Grind only what you're brewing
- **Precise**: Weigh in, expect same weight out (minus retention)
- **Clean**: No mixing old and new beans

**Advantages**:
- **Freshness**: Maximum freshness (grind immediately before brewing)
- **Flexibility**: Switch beans easily, no waste
- **Precision**: Exact dosing (weigh beans, not grounds)
- **No Staleness**: No hopper beans going stale
- **Cleaner**: Less buildup, easier maintenance

**Disadvantages**:
- **Slower**: Must weigh each dose
- **Workflow**: Extra step vs. hopper grinding
- **Popcorning**: Some grinders struggle with single beans

---

## üéØ Minimizing Retention

**RDT (Ross Droplet Technique)**:
- **Method**: Spray 1-2 drops water on beans before grinding
- **Effect**: Water reduces static, grounds flow better
- **Result**: Lower retention, less clumping
- **Espresso**: Dramatically improves distribution

**Grinder Choice**:
- **Low Retention**: Niche Zero, DF64, Fellow Ode (designed for single-dosing)
- **High Retention**: Large commercial grinders (3-5g+ retention)
- **Design**: Vertical grind path, minimal bends = lower retention

**Purging**:
- **Method**: Grind extra beans to flush old grounds
- **Amount**: 1-2g purge between different coffees
- **Effect**: Clears retention, but wastes coffee

**Bellows** (Air Blower):
- Some grinders include bellows (Niche Zero, aftermarket for DF64)
- Blows grounds out, reduces retention
- Effective for popcorning and retention

---

## üìù Summary

Grinder retention (trapped grounds) creates staleness and inconsistency - retained old grounds mix with fresh, compromising flavor. Single-dosing workflow (weigh exact dose, grind all, use immediately) combined with low-retention grinder design and RDT (water droplet spray) minimizes retention effects while ensuring maximum freshness and precision.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Retention**: Grounds trapped in grinder (0.2-5g+)
- **Problem**: Stale grounds mix with fresh
- **Single-Dosing**: Weigh dose, grind all, use immediately
- **RDT**: Water spray reduces static and retention
- **Grinder Choice**: Low-retention designs optimal (Niche, DF64, etc.)
- **Freshness**: Single-dosing maximizes freshness
