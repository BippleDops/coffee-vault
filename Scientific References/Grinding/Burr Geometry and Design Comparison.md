## üìã Overview

Burr geometry - the shape, size, and cutting pattern of grinder burrs - fundamentally determines particle size distribution, grind uniformity, and ultimately coffee flavor. Understanding flat vs. conical burr designs, tooth patterns, and burr materials enables informed grinder selection and performance optimization.

**Why Burr Geometry Matters**:
- **Particle Distribution**: Geometry determines fines/boulders ratio
- **Flavor Impact**: Distribution affects extraction balance
- **Grinder Performance**: Design determines grinder capabilities
- **Method Suitability**: Some geometries better for espresso, others for filter
- **Uniformity**: Better geometry = more uniform particles
- **Price Correlation**: Advanced geometry costs more but performs better

---

## üî¨ Flat vs. Conical Burrs

### Flat Burrs (Parallel Discs)

**Design**:
- **Shape**: Two parallel flat discs with cutting teeth
- **Rotation**: One stationary, one rotating
- **Coffee Path**: Falls between burrs, cut radially outward
- **Exit**: Particles exit at outer edge

**Characteristics**:
- **Distribution**: Narrower peak, fewer boulders, **more fines**
- **Cutting Action**: More aggressive, shear-cutting
- **Uniformity**: Generally more uniform (when aligned)
- **Heat**: Can generate more heat (friction)

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Clarity**: High clarity, articulate flavors
- **Acidity**: Brightness emphasized
- **Body**: Can be lighter (fines filtered out in paper)
- **Espresso**: Intense, clear, sometimes sharp

**Examples**:
- Baratza Vario, Forte
- Mazzer Super Jolly
- Mahlk√∂nig EK43 (large flat burrs)
- Eureka Atom
- Weber EG-1

**Best For**: Filter coffee (pour-over, Chemex), clarity-focused espresso

### Conical Burrs (Nested Cones)

**Design**:
- **Shape**: Outer ring burr, inner cone burr
- **Rotation**: Usually outer stationary, inner rotates
- **Coffee Path**: Falls from top, cut downward between cones
- **Exit**: Particles exit at bottom

**Characteristics**:
- **Distribution**: Wider peak, more boulders, **fewer fines**
- **Cutting Action**: Less aggressive, crushing-cutting hybrid
- **Uniformity**: Wider distribution (generally)
- **Heat**: Less heat generation (gentler action)

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Body**: Fuller body, heavier mouthfeel
- **Sweetness**: Sweetness emphasized
- **Acidity**: Less sharp, rounder
- **Espresso**: Sweet, full-bodied, less clarity

**Examples**:
- Baratza Encore, Virtuoso+, Sette
- Comandante (hand grinder)
- 1Zpresso, Kinu (hand grinders)
- Mazzer Kony
- Eureka Mignon (some models)

**Best For**: Espresso (sweet, full-bodied), French Press, all-purpose

## üîß Burr Size Impact

**Larger Burrs** (68-83mm+):
- **Advantages**: 
  - More cutting edges = gentler per-edge
  - More uniform particle distribution
  - Less heat generation per particle
  - Can grind faster
- **Examples**: Mahlk√∂nig EK43 (98mm), Weber EG-1 (83mm)
- **Cost**: Expensive

**Smaller Burrs** (38-58mm):
- **Characteristics**:
  - Fewer cutting edges = more aggressive per-edge
  - Wider distribution generally
  - More heat per particle
  - Slower grinding
- **Examples**: Baratza Encore (40mm), most entry grinders
- **Cost**: Affordable

**Correlation**: Larger burrs generally = better distribution = more expensive

## üéØ Burr Materials

**Steel** (Most Common):
- Durable, sharp, affordable
- Requires replacement after ~500-1,000 lbs coffee

**Ceramic**:
- Very hard, long-lasting
- Can shatter if foreign objects
- Heat-resistant

**Titanium-Coated**:
- Extremely durable
- Very expensive
- Professional/commercial

---

## üìù Summary

Burr geometry fundamentally determines grind quality - flat burrs produce narrower distributions with more fines (clarity-focused), while conical burrs create wider distributions with fewer fines (body-focused). Larger burrs generally produce more uniform particles regardless of geometry. Understanding these differences enables informed grinder selection matched to brewing priorities.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Flat**: Narrow distribution, more fines, clarity-focused
- **Conical**: Wider distribution, fewer fines, body-focused
- **Size**: Larger burrs = better uniformity
- **Material**: Steel standard, ceramic/titanium premium
- **Flavor**: Geometry affects extraction and cup profile
