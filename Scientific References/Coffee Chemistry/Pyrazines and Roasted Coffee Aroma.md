## ğŸ“‹ Overview

Pyrazines are nitrogen-containing aromatic compounds creating coffee's characteristic nutty, earthy, roasted aromas - formed during Maillard reactions and increasing with roast level. Understanding pyrazines explains dark roast's nutty-earthy character and roast aroma development.

**What Are Pyrazines**:
- **Chemical Class**: Nitrogen-containing heterocyclic compounds
- **Formation**: Maillard reactions (amino acids + sugars + heat)
- **Aroma**: Nutty, earthy, roasted, coffee-like
- **Roast Level**: Increase with roast darkness

**Major Pyrazines in Coffee**:
- **2,5-Dimethylpyrazine**: Nutty, roasted, earthy (most abundant)
- **2-Ethyl-3,5-dimethylpyrazine**: Earthy, roasted
- **2,3-Dimethylpyrazine**: Nutty, cocoa-like
- **Others**: 30+ pyrazines identified in coffee

## ğŸ”¥ Roast Level Effects

**Light Roast**:
- **Low Pyrazines**: Minimal formation (less Maillard)
- **Aroma**: Floral, fruity aromas dominant

**Medium Roast**:
- **Moderate**: Balanced pyrazines with other compounds
- **Aroma**: Nutty, chocolate, balanced

**Dark Roast**:
- **High Pyrazines**: Maximum formation
- **Aroma**: Nutty, earthy, roasted aromas dominant

Pyrazines (nitrogen-containing compounds) create roasted coffee's nutty, earthy aromas through Maillard reactions - increasing with roast level to create dark roast's characteristic nutty-earthy-roasted aroma profile.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Pyrazines**: Nutty, earthy, roasted aromas
- **Formation**: Maillard reactions during roasting
