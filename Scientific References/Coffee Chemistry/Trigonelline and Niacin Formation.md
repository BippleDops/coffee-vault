## ðŸ“‹ Overview

Trigonelline is coffee's secondary alkaloid (after caffeine) - degrading during roasting to form niacin (vitamin B3), bitter compounds, and aromatic volatiles. Understanding trigonelline transformation explains roast level's health benefits (niacin formation) and bitterness development.

**Trigonelline Basics**:
- **Content**: 0.6-1.2% of green Arabica (second to caffeine among alkaloids)
- **Degradation**: Breaks down during roasting (heat-sensitive)
- **Products**: Niacin (vitamin B3), pyridines (bitter), volatiles (aroma)
- **Roast Level**: More roasting = more degradation

**Green vs. Roasted**:
- **Green**: 0.6-1.2% trigonelline
- **Light Roast**: 0.4-0.7% remaining
- **Medium Roast**: 0.2-0.4% remaining
- **Dark Roast**: <0.2% remaining
