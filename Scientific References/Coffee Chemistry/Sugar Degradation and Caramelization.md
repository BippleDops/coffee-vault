Sugars in coffee undergo dramatic transformations during roasting - from simple sweetness in green beans to complex caramel flavors through caramelization, Maillard reactions, and thermal decomposition. Understanding sugar chemistry explains coffee's sweetness development, roast flavor creation, and why roast level dramatically affects perceived sweetness.

**Sugars in Green Coffee**:
- **Sucrose**: 6-9% (dominant sugar in Arabica)
- **Reducing Sugars**: Glucose, fructose (1-2%)
- **Total**: 7-11% sugars (dry weight)
- **Robusta**: Lower sucrose (3-7%)

**What Happens During Roasting**:
- **Degradation**: Sugars break down progressively
- **Caramelization**: Heat-induced sugar breakdown (>160Â°C/320Â°F)
- **Maillard**: Sugars react with amino acids (browning)

**Roast Level Sugar Content**:
- **Green**: 7-11% total sugars
- **Light Roast**: 4-6% remaining
- **Medium Roast**: 1-3% remaining  
- **Dark Roast**: <1% remaining
- **Trend**: More roasting = less sugar (degradation)
