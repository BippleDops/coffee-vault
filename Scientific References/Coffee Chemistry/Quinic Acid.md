## üìã Overview

**Quinic Acid** is a roasting byproduct that creates bitter-sour, astringent character in coffee. Unlike desirable [[Citric Acid]] or [[Malic Acid]], quinic contributes to harshness and is a primary cause of "old coffee" bitterness, especially in dark roasts.

**Key Difference**: Quinic is *formed* during roasting (increases), while other acids are *degraded* (decrease).

**Source**: Breakdown product of [[Chlorogenic Acids]] during roasting

**Stability**:
- Forms during roasting (opposite of citric/malic)
- Highest in dark roasts
- Low in green coffee
- Increases with age (oxidation)

## üëÖ Flavor Profile

**Primary Characteristics**:
- **Flavor**: Sour, bitter, astringent
- **Character**: Unpleasant harshness
- **Quality**: Less desirable
- **Association**: Over-roasted, stale coffee

**Sensory Impact**:
- Creates mouth-drying sensation
- Harsh, unpleasant tartness
- "Cardboard" stale flavor
- Dominates other flavors when excessive

**Comparison**:
- Opposite quality to [[Citric Acid]] (bright)
- Less pleasant than [[Malic Acid]] (fruity)
- Harsher than [[Phosphoric Acid]] (smooth)
- More astringent than [[Acetic Acid]] (sharp)

---

## üî• Roasting Impact

**Light Roast**:
- **Quinic Acid**: Minimal formation (breakdown not started)
- **Effect**: Clean, bright, non-harsh
- **Character**: Pleasant acidity only
- **Application**: Premium specialty coffee

**Medium Roast**:
- **Quinic Acid**: Moderate formation (some breakdown)
- **Effect**: Balanced, some bitterness emerging
- **Character**: Good balance if managed
- **Application**: Daily drinker, balanced

**Dark Roast**:
- **Quinic Acid**: High formation (extensive breakdown)
- **Effect**: Harsh, bitter-dominant
- **Character**: Quinic dominating cup
- **Application**: Espresso, tolerant of bitterness

## ‚òï Staling Role

**Oxidation Process**:
- Forms quinic acid lactones
- Creates cardboard/stale flavor
- Indicator of oxidation
- Marker of coffee age

**Degradation Path**:
Chlorogenic Acids ‚Üí Quinic Acid ‚Üí Quinic Lactones ‚Üí Staleness

**Practical Impact**:
- Old coffee = High quinic
- Stale coffee = Quinic-dominant flavor
- Fresh coffee = Low quinic

**Prevention**:
- Proper storage (airtight, cool, dark)
- Fresh roasting
- Appropriate roast level
- Quick consumption

**Learn More**: [[Coffee Storage]], [[Staling and Degradation]]

## üéØ Over-Extraction Connection

**Brewing Error**:
- Over-extraction pulls out quinic
- Creates harsh, astringent cup
- Mouth-drying sensation
- Unpleasant bitter-sour balance

**Correction**:
- Shorter brew time
- Coarser grind
- Lower temperature
- Better technique

**Recognition**:
- Distinguish quinic harshness from citric brightness
- Recognize over-extraction vs. roast defect
- Understand brewing vs. roasting impact

## üåç Origin & Processing

**Origin Differences**:
- All origins can develop quinic
- Roasting level determines amount
- Processing affects chlorogenic precursors
- Not inherent to origin

**Processing Impact**:
- Natural process: More variably
- Washed process: More consistently controlled
- Fermentation affects chlorogenic content
