## üìã Overview

Coffee plants require specific nutrients for healthy growth, quality cherry production, and long-term farm sustainability - with nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium (NPK) primary, plus essential micronutrients. Understanding coffee nutrition enables optimal fertilization, soil health management, and sustainable coffee farming.

**Essential Nutrients for Coffee**:

**Macronutrients** (Large Quantities):
1. **Nitrogen (N)**: Vegetative growth, leaves, photosynthesis
2. **Phosphorus (P)**: Root development, flowering, cherry maturation
3. **Potassium (K)**: Cherry quality, disease resistance, water regulation

**Secondary Nutrients** (Moderate Quantities):
4. **Calcium (Ca)**: Cell wall structure, disease resistance
5. **Magnesium (Mg)**: Chlorophyll, photosynthesis
6. **Sulfur (S)**: Protein synthesis, amino acids

**Micronutrients** (Trace Amounts):
7. **Iron (Fe)**, **Zinc (Zn)**, **Manganese (Mn)**, **Boron (B)**, **Copper (Cu)**, **Molybdenum (Mo)**

---

## üî¨ NPK - Primary Nutrients

**Nitrogen (N)**:
- **Function**: Leaf growth, vegetative development, photosynthesis
- **Deficiency**: Yellowing leaves, stunted growth, low yield
- **Excess**: Excessive vegetation, disease susceptibility, delayed maturity
- **Sources**: Organic compost, nitrogen fertilizers, legume cover crops
- **Application**: Split applications during growing season

**Phosphorus (P)**:
- **Function**: Root development, flowering, fruit set, maturation
- **Deficiency**: Poor root growth, delayed flowering, low yield
- **Excess**: Can bind other nutrients (iron, zinc)
- **Sources**: Rock phosphate, bone meal, phosphorus fertilizers
- **Volcanic Soils**: Often naturally high in phosphorus

**Potassium (K)**:
- **Function**: Cherry quality, disease resistance, water regulation, flavor
- **Deficiency**: Poor cherry development, disease susceptibility
- **Excess**: Can affect calcium/magnesium uptake
- **Sources**: Potassium sulfate, wood ash, potassium fertilizers
- **Quality**: Directly affects cup quality (flavor development)

## üåç Soil Health Management

**Organic Matter**:
- **Benefits**: Nutrient retention, water holding, soil structure
- **Sources**: Compost, mulch, cover crops, coffee pulp
- **Target**: 3-5% organic matter in soil

**pH Management**:
- **Optimal**: pH 5.5-6.5 for coffee
- **Acidic Soils**: Common in coffee regions, liming if too acidic
- **Alkaline Soils**: Sulfur application if too alkaline

**Cover Crops**:
- **Legumes**: Fix nitrogen, improve soil
- **Benefits**: Erosion prevention, organic matter, biodiversity
- **Common**: Beans, vetiver grass, other species

**Shade Trees**:
- **Benefits**: Organic matter (leaf litter), nitrogen (legume trees), microclimate
- **Species**: Legume trees ideal (Inga, Erythrina, etc.)
- **Agroforestry**: Integrated tree-coffee systems

---

## üå± Organic vs. Conventional

**Organic**:
- **Inputs**: Compost, organic fertilizers, cover crops, mulch
- **Benefits**: Soil health, environmental, no synthetic chemicals
- **Challenges**: Lower yields (sometimes), slower nutrient release
- **Certification**: 3-year transition, annual inspections

**Conventional**:
- **Inputs**: Synthetic fertilizers, pesticides
- **Benefits**: Higher yields (often), precise nutrition
- **Challenges**: Environmental impact, soil degradation (long-term)
- **Trend**: Shift toward sustainable/organic practices

---

## üìù Summary

Coffee nutrition requires balanced NPK (nitrogen for growth, phosphorus for roots/flowering, potassium for quality), plus secondary nutrients and micronutrients. Soil health management through organic matter, pH optimization, cover crops, and shade systems enables sustainable high-quality coffee production. Organic methods prioritize long-term soil health; conventional provides immediate nutrition with environmental trade-offs.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **NPK**: Primary nutrients (N growth, P roots/flowering, K quality)
- **Secondary**: Ca, Mg, S important for health
- **Micronutrients**: Fe, Zn, Mn, B, Cu, Mo (trace but essential)
- **Soil Health**: Organic matter, pH, cover crops crucial
- **Sustainability**: Long-term soil health ensures farm longevity
