# Coffee Varietals Encyclopedia

## Overview

Coffee varietals are distinct genetic varieties within the Coffea genus that produce different characteristics in the cup. Understanding varietals is crucial for appreciating coffee's diversity and terroir.

## Major Species

### Coffea Arabica

- **Origin**: Ethiopian highlands (native)
- **Characteristics**: 
  - Smooth, complex flavors
  - Lower caffeine content (1-1.5%)
  - Grown at high altitudes (600-2,400m)
  - More fragile, susceptible to disease
- **Global Production**: ~60% of world production

### Coffea Canephora (Robusta)

- **Origin**: Central and West Africa
- **Characteristics**:
  - Bold, earthy flavors
  - Higher caffeine content (2-2.7%)
  - Grown at lower altitudes
  - More disease-resistant
- **Global Production**: ~40% of world production
- **Primary Use**: Instant coffee, espresso blends

## Heirloom Varietals (Ethiopia)

### Gesha/Geisha

- **Origin**: Gori Gesha forest, Ethiopia (adapted to Panama)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Exceptional floral and jasmine notes
  - Light, tea-like body
  - Highly sought after
  - Expensive due to low yield
- **Growing Regions**: Panama, Colombia, Ethiopia

### Ethiopian Heirlooms

- **Native Varietals**: Thousands of wild cultivars
- **Characteristics**: 
  - Great genetic diversity
  - Complex flavor profiles
  - Often categorized simply as "Heirloom"
  - Includes subtypes like Yirgacheffe, Sidamo, Harrar

### Typica

- **Origin**: Ethiopia, spread globally
- **Characteristics**:
  - Sweet, clean cup
  - Low yield but high quality
  - Parent of many modern varietals
- **Growing Regions**: Central and South America

### Bourbon

- **Origin**: Île Bourbon (Réunion Island)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Sweet, balanced cup
  - Slightly higher yield than Typica
  - Mutations include: Red, Yellow, Pink Bourbon
- **Growing Regions**: Latin America, Rwanda, Burundi

## Modern Cultivars

### Caturra

- **Origin**: Natural mutation of Bourbon (Brazil)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Higher yield than parent
  - Compact plant size
  - Bright, clean cup
- **Growing Regions**: Colombia, Central America

### Mundo Novo

- **Origin**: Natural hybrid of Typica x Bourbon (Brazil)
- **Characteristics**:
  - High yield
  - Disease resistance
  - Balanced cup quality
- **Growing Regions**: Brazil, Colombia

### Catuai

- **Origin**: Hybrid of Mundo Novo x Caturra
- **Characteristics**:
  - High yield
  - Dwarf plant suitable for dense planting
  - Good cup quality with proper care
- **Growing Regions**: Central and South America

### SL-28 & SL-34

- **Origin**: Scott Laboratories, Kenya (1930s)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Exceptional cup quality
  - High acidity and fruit notes
  - Susceptible to Coffee Leaf Rust
- **Growing Regions**: Kenya, Tanzania

### Pacamara

- **Origin**: Hybrid of Pacas x Maragogipe (El Salvador)
- **Characteristics**:
  - Large, distinct bean size
  - Complex, unusual flavors
  - Lower yield
- **Growing Regions**: El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras

## Processing Method Impact

Different processing methods affect how varietal characteristics express:

- **Washed**: Emphasizes pure varietal traits
- **Natural**: Adds fruit notes, can mask varietal differences
- **Honey**: Balances varietal and processing notes

## Geographic Expression

Varietals perform differently based on altitude, climate, and soil conditions. The same varietal can taste dramatically different across origins.

## Practical Applications

**For Roasters**: Understand how varietals behave during roasting  

**For Baristas**: Educate customers on varietal stories  

**For Enthusiasts**: Track varietals in tasting notes to understand flavor diversity
