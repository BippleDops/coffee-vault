## üìã Overview

Drying is coffee processing's quality-critical phase - reducing moisture from 50-60% (after washing/depulping) to 10-12% (storage-stable) while preserving quality and preventing defects. Proper drying method selection and moisture management separate exceptional from defective coffee, affecting flavor, longevity, and market value.

**Why Drying Matters**:
- **Quality**: Poor drying creates defects (mold, ferment, off-flavors)
- **Preservation**: Proper moisture enables storage without degradation
- **Flavor**: Drying speed and method affect final flavor
- **Economics**: Defective drying destroys crop value
- **Method**: Patio, raised beds, mechanical - each affects quality differently

**Target Moisture**: 10-12% (storage-stable, prevents mold/degradation)

---

## ‚òÄÔ∏è Drying Methods

**Patio Drying** (Traditional):
- **Method**: Concrete/brick patios, sun-dried, raked regularly
- **Duration**: 7-21 days (weather-dependent)
- **Pros**: Low cost, traditional, works when weather cooperates
- **Cons**: Weather-dependent, slower, mold risk if rain
- **Quality**: Good when weather ideal, risky when rainy
- **Common**: Most traditional origins

**Raised African Beds** (Specialty Standard):
- **Method**: Mesh screens elevated 3-4 feet, air circulation
- **Duration**: 10-21 days (slower than patio, better quality)
- **Pros**: Best quality, airflow prevents mold, easier sorting
- **Cons**: Higher labor/infrastructure cost
- **Quality**: Excellent - specialty standard
- **Common**: East Africa, specialty farms globally

**Mechanical Drying** (Guardiolas):
- **Method**: Rotating drums, temperature-controlled, forced air
- **Duration**: 24-72 hours (very fast)
- **Pros**: Weather-independent, fast, consistent
- **Cons**: "Baker" defect risk if too hot, quality lower than beds
- **Quality**: Acceptable if done carefully, inferior to sun-drying
- **Common**: Rainy climates (Colombia wet season), large operations

**Parabolic Solar Dryers** (Modern):
- **Method**: Greenhouse-style, protects from rain, uses solar heat
- **Duration**: 10-18 days
- **Pros**: Weather-protected, better than mechanical, affordable
- **Quality**: Very good, between patios and raised beds
- **Common**: Increasing adoption (cost-effective quality)

## üíß Moisture Management

**Stages**:
- **Initial**: 50-60% moisture (wet parchment)
- **Fast Drying**: First 3-5 days, rapid moisture loss
- **Slow Drying**: Days 5-15, gradual final drying
- **Target**: 10-12% final moisture

**Monitoring**:
- **Moisture Meters**: Measure parchment moisture
- **Feel Test**: Experienced processors test by hand
- **Bite Test**: Traditional method (bite parchment)

**Critical**: Neither too fast (cracking) nor too slow (mold)

---

## üìù Summary

Coffee drying reduces moisture from 50-60% to 10-12% through sun (patios), raised beds (best quality), mechanical (fast, weather-proof), or parabolic solar (modern compromise). Proper moisture management prevents defects while preserving quality - raised African beds produce best results through airflow and slow drying; mechanical is fastest but quality-compromised; patios are traditional but weather-dependent.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Target**: 10-12% final moisture
- **Raised Beds**: Best quality (airflow, slow drying)
- **Mechanical**: Fast but quality-compromised
- **Patios**: Traditional, weather-dependent
- **Critical**: Moisture management prevents defects
