Temperature is fermentation's master variable - controlling microbial activity rate, flavor compound development, and fermentation duration. Proper temperature management separates clean, complex fermentation from defective, over-fermented disaster, making temperature control critical for processing quality.

**Why Temperature Matters in Fermentation**:
- **Fermentation Rate**: Temperature determines speed (hotter = faster)
- **Microbial Selection**: Different temps favor different microorganisms
- **Flavor Development**: Temperature affects which compounds form
- **Defect Risk**: Wrong temps create off-flavors, defects
- **Duration**: Cold highlands vs. warm lowlands = 12 vs. 48 hour fermentation
- **Quality Control**: Consistent temp = consistent quality
