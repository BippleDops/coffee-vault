Coffee pulp and cascara (dried coffee cherry skins) represent processing byproducts with emerging value - traditionally waste, now utilized as tea (cascara), compost, animal feed, or coffee flour, creating additional farmer income while reducing environmental impact.

**What is Cascara**:
- **Definition**: Dried coffee cherry skins/fruit (after depulping)
- **Spanish**: "Cascara" = husk/peel/skin
- **Tea**: Brewed as herbal tea (fruity, sweet, low-caffeine)
- **Byproduct**: Was waste, now value-added product
- **Emerging**: Growing specialty market (caf√©s, retail)

**Coffee Pulp**:
- **Fresh Pulp**: Wet fruit material after depulping
- **Compost**: Traditional use (fertilizer)
- **Animal Feed**: Dried pulp for livestock
- **Coffee Flour**: Ground dried pulp (gluten-free flour alternative)
