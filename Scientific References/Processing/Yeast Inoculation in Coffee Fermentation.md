## üìã Overview

Yeast inoculation is controlled fermentation's cutting edge - adding specific yeast strains (like wine-making) to coffee fermentation tanks for predictable, consistent, enhanced flavor development. This innovation transforms fermentation from spontaneous process to controlled science, enabling repeatable complex flavors.

**What is Yeast Inoculation**:
- **Definition**: Adding specific yeast strains to fermentation
- **Wine-Making Parallel**: Borrowed from wine industry (controlled fermentation)
- **Predictable**: Known yeast strains create consistent flavors
- **Enhanced**: Develops flavors impossible with spontaneous fermentation
- **Premium**: Ultra-premium specialty, experimental lots
- **Innovation**: Cutting-edge processing (2015-present)

**Why Inoculate**:
- **Consistency**: Predictable results vs. wild fermentation variation
- **Flavor Development**: Specific yeasts create specific flavor compounds
- **Quality Control**: Reduces defect risk
- **Complexity**: Enhanced complexity, layered flavors
- **Differentiation**: Unique profiles, premium positioning

---

## üî¨ Science of Yeast Fermentation

**Yeast Function**:
- **Sugar Consumption**: Yeast consumes sugars in mucilage
- **Byproducts**: Alcohols, esters, organic acids, CO‚ÇÇ
- **Flavor Compounds**: Esters (fruity), alcohols (wine-like), acids (complexity)

**Spontaneous Fermentation** (Traditional):
- **Wild Yeast**: Whatever yeast naturally present on cherries, in environment
- **Variable**: Each batch different (yeast population varies)
- **Unpredictable**: Results vary season-to-season, lot-to-lot
- **Traditional**: How coffee was always processed

**Inoculated Fermentation** (Modern Innovation):
- **Specific Strains**: Known yeast species/strains added
- **Controlled**: Dominates fermentation (outcompetes wild yeast)
- **Predictable**: Same yeast = same flavor outcomes
- **Enhanced**: Selected yeast strains optimize flavor development

---

## üç∑ Yeast Strains Used

**Saccharomyces cerevisiae** (Wine Yeast):
- Most common inoculation strain
- **Flavors**: Fruity esters, wine-like complexity, tropical fruit
- **Proven**: Wine industry success translates to coffee

**Pichia** (Specialty Coffee Yeast):
- **Flavors**: Fruity, floral, complex esters
- **Specialty**: Selected for coffee specifically

**Candida** (Some Use):
- Creates unique flavor profiles
- Less common than Saccharomyces

**Lactic Acid Bacteria** (Sometimes Combined):
- **Bacteria, Not Yeast**: But used similarly
- **Flavors**: Lactic, creamy, yogurt, dairy notes
