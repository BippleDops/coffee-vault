## üìã Overview

Honey processing (pulped natural, semi-washed) is the sweet spot between washed and natural processing - removing the skin but leaving varying amounts of sticky mucilage on the bean during drying, creating a spectrum of sweetness, body, and fruit character between the extremes of washed brightness and natural fruit intensity.

**What is Honey Processing**:
- **Definition**: Coffee processed with skin removed, mucilage (sticky fruit layer) left on during drying
- **"Honey"**: Name from sticky, honey-like mucilage (not actual honey)
- **Spectrum**: White ‚Üí Yellow ‚Üí Red ‚Üí Black honey (% of mucilage retained)
- **Origin**: Costa Rica popularized and named levels (1990s-2000s)
- **Innovation**: Central American specialty coffee innovation
- **Sweetness**: Sweeter than washed, cleaner than natural
- **Body**: Fuller than washed, lighter than natural
- **Complexity**: Balance between washed clarity and natural fruit

**Why Honey Processing Emerged**:
- **Water Conservation**: Uses less water than fully washed (environmental benefit)
- **Differentiation**: Create unique profiles to compete in specialty market
- **Quality**: Access sweetness and body without natural process risk
- **Costa Rica**: Water scarcity and environmental regulations drove innovation
- **Adoption**: Spread throughout Central America, South America, Asia

## üçØ Honey Processing Levels

### The Spectrum (% Mucilage Retained)

**White Honey** (10-25% mucilage):
- **Mucilage**: Minimal, most removed
- **Drying**: Fastest (10-14 days)
- **Labor**: Less intensive
- **Appearance**: Beans appear pale/white
- **Flavor**: Closest to washed - clean, bright, subtle sweetness
- **Body**: Light-medium
- **Use**: When seeking clarity with slight sweetness boost

**Yellow Honey** (25-50% mucilage):
- **Mucilage**: Moderate amount retained
- **Drying**: 12-16 days
- **Labor**: Moderate
- **Appearance**: Yellow-golden beans
- **Flavor**: Balanced - sweetness evident, still clean
- **Body**: Medium, smooth
- **Use**: Versatile, balanced profile

**Red Honey** (50-75% mucilage):
- **Mucilage**: Most retained (but not all)
- **Drying**: 15-20 days, labor-intensive
- **Labor**: High (constant raking to prevent mold)
- **Appearance**: Red-brown beans
- **Flavor**: Sweet, fruity, complex - berry notes emerge
- **Body**: Medium-full, syrupy
- **Use**: When seeking fruit sweetness with some clarity

**Black Honey** (75-100% mucilage):
- **Mucilage**: Almost all or all retained
- **Drying**: 18-25+ days, very labor-intensive
- **Labor**: Extreme (hourly raking, mold prevention critical)
- **Appearance**: Dark brown/black beans
- **Flavor**: Very sweet, fruit-forward, almost natural-like
- **Body**: Full, syrupy, viscous
- **Use**: Maximum sweetness, approaching natural character

**Gold Honey** (Rare, Regional Term):
- Some regions use "gold" for yellow-red intermediate

## üîÑ Honey Processing Step-by-Step

**General Process** (Varies by Level):

1. **Selective Harvest**: Ripe cherries only
2. **Depulping** (Critical Step):
   - **Skin Removed**: Mechanical pulper removes outer skin
   - **Mucilage Retained**: Adjustable pulper setting controls mucilage %
   - **White Honey**: Most mucilage removed (pulper set tight)
   - **Black Honey**: All mucilage kept (pulper set loose, minimal removal)
3. **Immediate Drying** (No Fermentation Tank):
   - Beans moved directly to drying (sticky with mucilage)
   - **Speed Critical**: Must start drying immediately (mold risk)
4. **Drying Management** (Most Labor-Intensive):
   - **Raised Beds**: Essential for honey (airflow prevents mold)
   - **Constant Raking**: Every 30-60 minutes initially (prevent sticking, mold)
   - **Shade Management**: Partial shade prevents over-drying
   - **Turning**: Beans turned frequently for even drying
   - **Monitoring**: Constant supervision required
   - **Duration**: 10-25 days (depending on mucilage %, weather)
5. **Target Moisture**: 10-12% (standard)
6. **Dry Milling**: Dried mucilage removed mechanically
7. **Result**: Beans with honey-process flavor profile

**Challenge**: Mold Prevention
- Mucilage is sugar-rich (microbe food)
- Humid conditions = mold growth in hours
- Requires labor, attention, skill
- Failed honey = ruined lot
- Why labor-intensive and expensive

## ‚òï Flavor Impact by Level

**White Honey**:
- Clean, bright (like washed)
- +10-20% sweetness vs. washed
- Subtle fruit hints
- Light-medium body
- High acidity (preserved)

**Yellow Honey**:
- Balanced sweetness and clarity
- +30-40% sweetness vs. washed
- Gentle fruit notes (apple, mild berry)
- Medium body, smooth
- Medium-high acidity

**Red Honey**:
- **Sweet**, fruity, complex
- +50-70% sweetness vs. washed
- Berry, stone fruit notes present
- Medium-full body, syrupy
- Medium acidity (fruit-balanced)

**Black Honey**:
- **Very sweet**, approaching natural
- +80-100% sweetness vs. washed
- Berry, tropical fruit dominant
- Full body, viscous
- Lower acidity, fruit-driven

**Progression**: White ‚Üí Yellow ‚Üí Red ‚Üí Black = increasing sweetness, body, fruit; decreasing acidity, clarity

## üî• Roasting Honey Process Coffee

**General Recommendations**:
- **White/Yellow**: Light to Medium roasts (preserve clarity)
- **Red**: Light-Medium to Medium (balance fruit and chocolate)
- **Black**: Medium roasts (develop sweetness, balance fruit)

**Honey Coffee Roasting Characteristics**:
- **Higher Sugar Content**: More caramelization potential
- **Exothermic**: Stronger exothermic reactions (monitor RoR)
- **Sweet Spot**: Medium roasts typically ideal for most honey levels
- **Development**: 60-120 seconds post-crack for balanced profile

## üí∞ Economics of Honey Processing

**Cost Factors**:
- **Labor**: 2-4x more labor than washed (constant raking)
- **Risk**: Mold/defect risk higher (lost lots = financial loss)
- **Infrastructure**: Raised beds essential (investment)
- **Time**: Longer drying time (occupies beds longer)
- **Expertise**: Requires skill and experience

**Premium Pricing**:
- **White/Yellow**: 10-20% premium over washed
- **Red**: 20-40% premium
- **Black**: 40-80% premium
- **Justification**: Labor, risk, unique flavors, market demand

**Farmer Perspective**:
- **Viable**: Premiums can justify extra labor
- **Differentiation**: Stand out from commodity coffee
- **Specialty Access**: Honey process opens specialty market doors
- **Risk**: Failure means lost income (no commodity fallback)

## üìù Summary

Honey processing creates a spectrum of sweetness, body, and fruit character between washed and natural extremes - removing skin but retaining varying amounts of mucilage to create unique flavor profiles. The White‚ÜíYellow‚ÜíRed‚ÜíBlack progression provides precise control over sweetness and body, enabling producers to target specific flavor profiles while conserving water and creating specialty market value.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Honey = Mucilage**: Sticky fruit layer left on during drying
- **Levels**: White (least mucilage) ‚Üí Black (most mucilage)
- **Sweetness Spectrum**: Increases with mucilage retention
- **Labor**: Very intensive, constant raking required
- **Premium**: Commands 10-80% premiums over washed
- **Innovation**: Central American specialty coffee success

Understanding honey processing reveals how producers create value through processing innovation, differentiate from commodity, and access specialty markets through flavor development.
