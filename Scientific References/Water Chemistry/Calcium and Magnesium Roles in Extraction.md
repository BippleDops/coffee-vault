**Category**: Water Chemistry  
**Difficulty**: Advanced  
**Purpose**: Understand how Ca and Mg affect extraction and flavor

## üìã Overview

Calcium and magnesium are brew water's primary extraction minerals - binding to coffee compounds to pull them into solution, with each mineral extracting different flavor categories. Understanding Ca vs. Mg roles enables precise water formulation for desired flavor profiles: calcium for brightness/clarity, magnesium for body/sweetness.

**Why Ca and Mg Matter**:
- **Primary Extractors**: Most important minerals for coffee extraction
- **Different Roles**: Calcium extracts acids/brightness, magnesium extracts body/sweetness
- **Hardness**: Together create General Hardness (GH)
- **Ratio**: Mg:Ca ratio affects flavor balance profoundly
- **Optimization**: Matching minerals to coffee type enables ideal extraction

---

## üî¨ Calcium (Ca¬≤‚Å∫) - Brightness Extractor

**Function**:
- **Extracts Acids**: Binds to acidic compounds, pulls into solution
- **Clarity**: Enhances flavor articulation, definition
- **Brightness**: Amplifies [[Perceived Acidity]]
- **Fruity Notes**: Helps extract fruity, bright flavors

**Effect on Cup**:
- **High Ca** (40-60 ppm): Bright, acidic, clear, articulate
- **Low Ca** (<20 ppm): Dull, muted, less acidity
- **Optimal**: 30-50 ppm for balanced brightness

**Best For**:
- **Bright Coffees**: Kenya, Ethiopia, light roasts
- **Acidity Lovers**: When seeking maximum brightness
- **Pour-Over**: Clarity-focused brewing

---

## üî¨ Magnesium (Mg¬≤‚Å∫) - Body Extractor

**Function**:
- **Extracts Body**: Binds to heavier compounds, pulls body/sweetness
- **Sweetness**: Enhances perceived sweetness
- **Fullness**: Creates fuller mouthfeel
- **Richness**: Adds depth, richness, complexity

**Effect on Cup**:
- **High Mg** (30-50 ppm): Full body, sweet, rich, round
- **Low Mg** (<15 ppm): Thin body, less sweetness
- **Optimal**: 20-30 ppm for balanced body

**Best For**:
- **Full-Bodied Coffees**: Brazil, Sumatra, dark roasts
- **Sweetness Seekers**: When emphasizing sweetness
- **Espresso**: Body and sweetness critical

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è Mg:Ca Ratio Matters

**Body-Focused** (2:1 Mg:Ca):
- **Example**: 30 ppm Mg, 15 ppm Ca
- **Result**: Full body, sweet, round, rich
- **Use**: Brazilian, Indonesian, dark roasts, espresso

**Balanced** (1:1):
- **Example**: 25 ppm Mg, 25 ppm Ca
- **Result**: Balanced extraction, versatile
- **Use**: Colombian, Guatemalan, medium roasts, all-purpose

**Brightness-Focused** (1:2 Mg:Ca):
- **Example**: 20 ppm Mg, 40 ppm Ca
- **Result**: Bright, acidic, clear, articulate
- **Use**: Kenyan, Ethiopian, light roasts, pour-over

---

## üéØ Practical Water Recipes

**For Bright Coffee** (Kenya, Ethiopia):
- Ca: 40 ppm
- Mg: 20 ppm
- Ratio: 1:2 (Ca-heavy)

**For Full-Bodied** (Brazil, Sumatra):
- Ca: 20 ppm
- Mg: 40 ppm
- Ratio: 2:1 (Mg-heavy)

**For Balanced** (Colombia, Guatemala):
- Ca: 30 ppm
- Mg: 30 ppm
- Ratio: 1:1 (balanced)

---

## üìù Summary

Calcium and magnesium serve distinct extraction roles - calcium extracts acids/brightness/clarity (optimal 30-50 ppm), magnesium extracts body/sweetness/richness (optimal 20-30 ppm). The Mg:Ca ratio determines flavor balance: Mg-heavy (2:1) for body, balanced (1:1) for versatility, Ca-heavy (1:2) for brightness. Matching mineral ratio to coffee type enables optimal extraction tailored to desired profile.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Calcium**: Brightness, acidity, clarity extractor
- **Magnesium**: Body, sweetness, richness extractor
- **Ratio Matters**: Mg:Ca determines flavor balance
- **Customization**: Match ratio to coffee type
- **Optimal GH**: 50-100 ppm total (Ca + Mg)
