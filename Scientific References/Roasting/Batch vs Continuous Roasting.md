# Batch vs Continuous Roasting Systems

## Overview

Coffee roasting uses two fundamental approaches: batch roasting (discrete batches) and continuous roasting (constant feed). Each has distinct advantages for different applications.

## Batch Roasting

### Process

**Discrete Batches**:
- Load beans → Roast → Drop → Cool
- One batch at a time
- Variable batch sizes
- Clean between batches

**Typical Cycle**:
- Load: 1-2 minutes
- Roast: 10-15 minutes
- Drop and cool: 3-5 minutes
- Total: ~20 minutes per batch

### Equipment Types

**Drum Roasters**:
- Rotating cylinder
- Gas or electric heat
- 1kg-500kg+ capacity
- Most common

**Fluid Bed**:
- Hot air suspension
- Fast roasting
- Smaller batches typically
- Home and small commercial

### Advantages

**Flexibility**:
- Different profiles per batch
- Multiple roast levels
- Various origins simultaneously
- Product diversity

**Quality Control**:
- Sample each batch
- Cupping verification
- Traceability
- Defect isolation

**Specialty Standard**:
- Precision profiling
- Artisan approach
- Customer expectations
- Quality focus

### Disadvantages

**Efficiency**:
- Downtime between batches
- Loading/unloading time
- Cooling time
- Labor intensive

**Throughput**:
- Limited by batch size and cycle time
- Difficult to scale massively
- Labor scaling required

## Continuous Roasting

### Process

**Constant Operation**:
- Beans continuously fed
- Roasting while feeding
- Continuous output
- Steady-state operation

**Operation**:
- Pre-heat system
- Begin feeding beans
- Roasted beans continuously exit
- Runs for hours/days

### Equipment Types

**Conveyor Systems**:
- Beans on moving belt/screw
- Through heated zones
- Timed residence
- Industrial scale

**Tangential Roasters**:
- Centrifugal force
- Continuous flow
- Very large capacity
- Factory scale

### Advantages

**Efficiency**:
- No downtime
- Continuous output
- Lower labor per kg
- Energy efficient at scale

**Volume**:
- Tons per hour possible
- Industrial production
- Mass market supply
- Lowest cost per kg

### Disadvantages

**Flexibility**:
- One profile at a time
- Changing settings affects output for time
- Not suitable for variety
- Limited specialty application

**Quality Variability**:
- Less batch control
- Individual bean variation
- Harder to cup-verify
- Consistency challenges

## Applications by Market

### Specialty Coffee

**Batch Roasting Dominant**:
- Profile control needed
- Multiple SKUs
- Quality focus
- Traceability
- Customer expectations

**Typical Batch Sizes**:
- 1-30kg: Micro roasters
- 30-100kg: Small roasters
- 100-300kg: Medium commercial
- 300kg+: Large but still batch

### Commercial/Commodity

**Mixed Systems**:
- Continuous for volume products
- Batch for premium lines
- Hybrid operations
- Scale advantages

**Continuous Applications**:
- Instant coffee
- Commercial blends
- Private label
- Mass market

## Hybrid Approaches

### Semi-Continuous

**Concept**:
- Batch-style roasting
- Minimal downtime
- Quick batch cycling
- Higher throughput

**Implementation**:
- Rapid cool and reload
- Pre-warmed green beans
- Optimized workflow
- Labor efficiency

### Multiple Batch Roasters

**Parallel Operation**:
- Several roasters simultaneously
- Different profiles each
- Higher total throughput
- Flexibility maintained

## Environmental Considerations

### Energy Efficiency

**Batch**:
- Heat-up and cool-down cycles
- Energy loss between batches
- Less efficient per kg at small scale

**Continuous**:
- Steady-state operation
- Heat recovery possible
- More efficient at scale
- Lower energy per kg

### Emissions

**Both Systems**:
- Smoke and VOC emissions
- Catalytic afterburners common
- Environmental regulations
- Air quality concerns

## Practical Applications

### For Startup Roasters

**Batch Advantages**:
- Lower initial investment
- Flexibility for growth
- Quality positioning
- Learn roasting

**Recommended**: 5-15kg batch roaster

### For Established Specialty

**Batch Scaling**:
- Larger batch roasters
- Multiple roasters
- Maintained quality
- Increased capacity

**Typical**: 30-120kg batches

### For Large Commercial

**Continuous Consideration**:
- Volume needs (tons/week)
- Cost per kg optimization
- Consistent products
- Industrial efficiency

## Future Developments

### Automation

**Batch Automation**:
- Profile automation
- Automated loading/unloading
- Data logging
- Consistency improvements

**Continuous Improvements**:
- Better quality control
- Optical sorting integration
- Real-time adjustments
- Narrowing quality gap

## Conclusion

Batch vs continuous:
- Different applications
- Batch dominates specialty
- Continuous for volume
- Both have roles
- Choose based on goals

Understanding helps:
- Equipment selection
- Scaling decisions
- Quality expectations
- Cost optimization
