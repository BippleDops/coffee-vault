Proper charge temp and heat recovery management separate consistent, quality roasts from unpredictable, defective results.

**Why Charge Temp Matters**:
- **Turning Point**: Affects how low beans cool drum (turning point)
- **Heat Recovery**: Determines how quickly roaster recovers heat
- **Drying Phase**: Influences drying duration and evenness
- **Total Time**: Affects total roast time significantly

**What is Turning Point**:
- **Definition**: Lowest bean temperature after charge (before rising)
- **Cause**: Green beans (room temp) cool hot drum
- **Duration**: 30 seconds to 3 minutes after charge
- **Depth**: How much beans cool drum

**Turning Point Depth**:
- **Shallow** (bean temp drops 10-30Â°F): High charge, dense beans
- **Moderate** (drops 30-60Â°F): Standard conditions

## ğŸ”§ Heat Recovery Management

**After Charge** (First 2 Minutes):
- **Temperature Drops**: Beans cool drum/air
- **Turning Point**: Reaches lowest temperature
- **Recovery Begins**: Temperature starts rising
- **Heat Application**: Critical to manage recovery rate

**Ideal Heat Recovery**:
- **Turning Point**: 60-90 seconds after charge
- **Recovery**: Smooth, steady temperature rise
- **Target**: Return to charge temp by 3-4 minutes
- **Rate of Rise**: Building gradually, controlled

**Poor Heat Recovery**:
- **Too Slow**: Baking, flat flavors, insufficient heat
- **Too Fast**: Scorching, uneven roast, excessive heat
