## ğŸ“‹ Overview

Bean temperature (BT) and environment temperature (ET) provide different information - BT measures actual bean temp (what matters for roast development) while ET measures air/drum temp (heat source). Understanding both enables better roast control.

**Two Measurements**:
- **Bean Temperature (BT)**: Probe in beans, measures actual bean temp
- **Environment Temp (ET)**: Measures air/drum temp, heat source

**Why Both Matter**:
- **BT**: What actually matters (bean development)
- **ET**: Heat available for transfer
- **Gap**: BT-ET gap indicates heat transfer efficiency

**Bean Probe**: Thermocouple in bean mass  
**Environment Probe**: Measures air/drum temp  
**Difference**: ET higher than BT (heat transferring to beans)
