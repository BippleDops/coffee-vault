## ğŸ“‹ Overview

Smoke production during roasting requires proper ventilation - with smoke increasing at first crack and dramatically at second crack, containing volatile organics requiring exhaust for air quality, safety, and preventing smoke redeposition on beans.

**Smoke Sources**:
- **Chaff Burning**: Chaff combustion creates smoke
- **Pyrolysis**: Thermal decomposition releases volatiles
- **Oil Volatilization**: Oils vaporizing at high temps
- **Carbon Formation**: Dark roasts produce more smoke

**Why Ventilation Matters**:
- **Air Quality**: Smoke contains irritants
- **Safety**: Proper exhaust prevents accumulation
- **Flavor**: Smoke redeposition creates acrid flavors
- **Fire Prevention**: Removes combustible volatiles

## ğŸ”¥ Smoke by Roast Phase

**Drying** (Minimal):
- Little smoke, mostly steam

**Browning** (Moderate):
- Smoke begins, chaff may burn

**First Crack** (Increasing):
- Smoke production increases
- Chaff released and burns

**Development** (Heavy):
- Significant smoke (pyrolysis)

**Second Crack** (Very Heavy):
- Maximum smoke (severe pyrolysis, oil volatilization)
- Requires excellent ventilation

**Proper System**:

- **Maintenance**: Regular cleaning (creosote buildup)
