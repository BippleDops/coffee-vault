## üìã Overview

Roast color measurement provides objective, standardized roast level classification - replacing subjective visual assessment with precise numerical values that enable consistency, communication, and quality control across the coffee industry.

**Agtron System**:
- **Definition**: Near-infrared spectrophotometry measuring roast color
- **Scale**: 0-100 (higher number = lighter roast)
- **Standard**: Industry standard for roast level classification
- **Objective**: Removes subjective visual assessment
- **Consistency**: Enables repeatable roast levels
- **Communication**: Universal language for roast degree

**Roast Level Classifications**:
- **Very Light**: Agtron 95-100 (rare, almost green)
- **Light (Cinnamon)**: Agtron 85-95
- **Light-Medium (City)**: Agtron 70-85  
- **Medium (Full City)**: Agtron 60-70
- **Medium-Dark (Vienna)**: Agtron 50-60
- **Dark (French)**: Agtron 40-50
- **Very Dark (Italian)**: Agtron 25-40

**Why Measurement Matters**:
- **QC**: Quality control (roast consistency batch-to-batch)
- **Communication**: Precise roast level specification
- **Purchasing**: Buyers specify Agtron ranges
- **Optimization**: Track roast development precisely
- **Standards**: SCA Cupping Protocol specifies Agtron 58-63 for sample roasting

---

## üî¨ How Agtron Works

**Near-Infrared Spectrophotometry**:
1. **Light Source**: Device shines near-infrared light on coffee sample
2. **Reflection**: Lighter roasts reflect more light, darker absorb more
3. **Measurement**: Sensor measures reflected light
4. **Calculation**: Reflectance converted to Agtron number
5. **Result**: Numerical roast color value (0-100 scale)

**Whole Bean vs. Ground**:
- **Whole Bean**: Measures surface color
- **Ground**: Measures interior color (more representative)
- **SCA Standard**: Ground coffee measurement preferred
- **Difference**: Ground coffee typically 5-10 Agtron points darker than whole bean

## üìä Agtron Ranges & Characteristics

**Light Roast (Agtron 70-85)**:
- **Appearance**: Light brown, dry surface, no oils
- **Flavor**: Origin character dominant, bright acidity, floral/fruit notes
- **Development**: Minimal post-crack (30-90 seconds)
- **Use**: Specialty light roasts, origin showcase, pour-over

**Medium Roast (Agtron 55-70)**:
- **Appearance**: Medium brown, dry surface
- **Flavor**: Balanced origin and roast character, sweetness developed
- **Development**: 90-150 seconds post-crack
- **Use**: Versatile, broad appeal, all brew methods

**Dark Roast (Agtron 35-55)**:
- **Appearance**: Dark brown to black, oily surface
- **Flavor**: Roast character dominant, low acidity, chocolate-smoke
- **Development**: 150+ seconds post-crack, approaching/into second crack
- **Use**: Espresso, French Press, traditional dark roast preferences

**Professional Targets**:
- **Sample Roasting** (SCA Cupping): Agtron 58-63 (standardized evaluation)
- **Espresso**: Agtron 45-65 (varies by style)
- **Filter**: Agtron 60-80 (light to medium)

## üéØ Practical Applications

**Quality Control**:
- Measure each roast batch
- Target: ¬±3-5 Agtron points for consistency
- Track drift over time, adjust roast profile

**Purchasing Specifications**:
- Specify Agtron range for green coffee purchases
- Example: "Roast to Agtron 60-65 for this Guatemalan"

**Roast Development**:
- Track Agtron during profile development
- Establish target ranges for each coffee
- Communicate roasts precisely to customers
