## üìã Overview

Coffee extraction is diffusion and dissolution - soluble compounds dissolving into water and diffusing from coffee particle interior to surrounding liquid through concentration gradients. Understanding these fundamental processes explains extraction kinetics, time requirements, and optimization strategies.

**Two Processes**:
1. **Dissolution**: Soluble compounds dissolve into water (solid ‚Üí liquid)
2. **Diffusion**: Dissolved compounds move from particle to bulk water (concentration gradient)

**Why Understanding Matters**:
- **Extraction Rate**: Governed by diffusion/dissolution kinetics
- **Time**: Required time for diffusion explains brew times
- **Temperature**: Affects dissolution and diffusion rates
- **Grind Size**: Surface area affects dissolution rate
- **Optimization**: Understanding enables better brewing

---

## üî¨ Dissolution

**Process**:
- **Contact**: Water contacts coffee particle surface
- **Dissolution**: Soluble compounds dissolve into water at surface
- **Rate**: Depends on temperature, turbulence, concentration

**Factors Affecting Dissolution Rate**:
- **Temperature**: Higher = faster dissolution
- **Agitation**: Turbulence accelerates dissolution
- **Surface Area**: More surface = more dissolution sites
- **Saturation**: Slows as water becomes saturated

---

## üåä Diffusion

**Process**:
- **Concentration Gradient**: High inside particle, low in bulk water
- **Movement**: Molecules move from high to low concentration
- **Slow**: Diffusion is slow process (explains brew times)

**Factors Affecting Diffusion Rate**:
- **Temperature**: Higher = faster diffusion
- **Concentration Gradient**: Larger difference = faster diffusion
- **Distance**: Shorter distance = faster (finer grind = faster)
- **Agitation**: Removes boundary layer, maintains gradient

---

## ‚è±Ô∏è Time Requirements

**Why Brewing Takes Time**:
- **Diffusion is Slow**: Molecules move slowly from particle interior
- **Distance**: Must diffuse from center of particle to surface
- **Grind Size**: Finer = shorter diffusion distance = faster extraction

**Espresso** (25-30 seconds):
- Very fine grind (short diffusion distance)
- High pressure (accelerates mass transfer)
- High temperature (fast dissolution/diffusion)

**Pour-Over** (2.5-3.5 minutes):
- Medium grind (moderate diffusion distance)
- Gravity flow (no pressure)
- Hot water (good dissolution/diffusion)

**French Press** (4 minutes):
- Coarse grind (long diffusion distance)
- Immersion (no flow)
- Time compensates for grind size

**Cold Brew** (12-24 hours):
- Coarse grind + cold water (both slow diffusion)
- Time compensates for temperature disadvantage

## üìù Summary

Coffee extraction occurs through dissolution (soluble compounds dissolving at particle surface) and diffusion (dissolved compounds moving from particle interior to bulk water via concentration gradient). Understanding these fundamental physics explains brew time requirements (diffusion is slow), grind size effects (finer = shorter diffusion distance = faster), and temperature importance (higher = faster dissolution and diffusion).

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Dissolution**: Compounds dissolve at particle surface
- **Diffusion**: Dissolved compounds move through concentration gradient
- **Slow**: Diffusion is slow (explains brew times)
- **Grind**: Finer = shorter distance = faster extraction
- **Temperature**: Higher = faster dissolution and diffusion
