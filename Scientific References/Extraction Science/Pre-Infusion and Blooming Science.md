## üìã Overview

Pre-infusion/blooming is the initial wetting phase in brewing - adding small amount of water to saturate grounds and release CO‚ÇÇ before main extraction, creating more even, complete, balanced extraction. Understanding bloom science explains why fresh coffee behaves differently and how to optimize this critical phase.

**What is Blooming**:
- **Definition**: Initial saturation with 2-3x coffee weight in water
- **Duration**: 30-45 seconds before main pour/extraction
- **Purpose**: Release CO‚ÇÇ, pre-wet grounds, even saturation
- **Visual**: Grounds bubble, expand, dome (active degassing)
- **Critical**: Especially important for fresh coffee (7-21 days off roast)

**Why Blooming Matters**:
- **CO‚ÇÇ Removal**: Degassing prevents channeling, uneven extraction
- **Even Saturation**: Pre-wetting enables uniform extraction
- **Better Extraction**: Improves extraction efficiency and balance
- **Espresso**: Pre-infusion crucial for even espresso extraction
- **Pour-Over**: Bloom essential for balanced pour-over

---

## üî¨ The CO‚ÇÇ Problem

**Fresh Coffee Contains CO‚ÇÇ**:
- **Roasting**: Produces massive CO‚ÇÇ (pyrolysis byproduct)
- **Trapped**: CO‚ÇÇ trapped in cellular structure
- **Degassing**: Slowly releases over days/weeks
- **Fresh**: 7-14 days post-roast = high CO‚ÇÇ
- **Stale**: 30+ days = minimal CO‚ÇÇ

**CO‚ÇÇ Interferes with Extraction**:
- **Repels Water**: CO‚ÇÇ bubbles prevent water penetration
- **Channeling**: Gas creates dry pockets, uneven wetting
- **Floating Grounds**: CO‚ÇÇ makes grounds float, resist saturation
- **Uneven**: Results in simultaneous under and over-extraction

**Blooming Solves This**:
- **Gentle Degassing**: Initial water triggers CO‚ÇÇ release
- **Time**: 30-45 seconds allows gas escape
- **Result**: Grounds fully wetted, no gas interference
- **Main Pour**: Water can extract evenly

---

## üéØ Optimal Bloom Technique

**Pour-Over Blooming**:
- **Amount**: 2-2.5x coffee weight (e.g., 20g coffee = 40-50g water)
- **Pour**: Gentle spiral, saturate all grounds evenly
- **Swirl** (Optional): Gentle swirl ensures complete saturation
- **Duration**: 30-45 seconds
- **Observation**: Should dome, bubble actively
- **Proceed**: Main pours after bloom complete

**Espresso Pre-Infusion**:
- **Low Pressure** (2-4 bars): 5-10 seconds before full 9 bar
- **Purpose**: Gentle saturation, CO‚ÇÇ release, even wetting
- **Result**: More even extraction, less channeling
- **Equipment**: Requires machine with pre-infusion capability

**AeroPress/Immersion**:
- **Initial Stir**: Breaks up CO‚ÇÇ, ensures saturation
- **Effect**: Similar to bloom (degassing + wetting)

---

## üìä Fresh vs. Stale Coffee Bloom

**Fresh Coffee** (7-14 days):
- **Bloom**: Very active, vigorous bubbling, dramatic doming
- **CO‚ÇÇ**: High levels trapped in beans
- **Essential**: Blooming critical for even extraction

**Peak Fresh** (14-21 days):
- **Bloom**: Moderate, healthy bubbling
- **CO‚ÇÇ**: Moderate levels
- **Recommended**: Bloom still beneficial

**Stale** (30+ days):
- **Bloom**: Minimal, flat, little bubbling
- **CO‚ÇÇ**: Mostly degassed
- **Less Critical**: Bloom less impactful (but still good practice)

**Observation**: Bloom vigor = freshness indicator

---

## üìù Summary

Pre-infusion/blooming (initial 30-45 second saturation with 2-3x water weight) is critical for even extraction - releasing CO‚ÇÇ from fresh coffee that otherwise repels water and creates channeling. Active blooming indicates fresh coffee; flat blooming suggests stale. Proper blooming improves extraction evenness, balance, and final cup quality across all brewing methods.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Bloom**: 2-3x water weight, 30-45 seconds
- **Purpose**: CO‚ÇÇ release, even saturation
- **Critical**: Essential for fresh coffee (7-21 days)
- **Indicator**: Bloom vigor shows freshness
- **All Methods**: Benefits pour-over, espresso, immersion
- **Result**: More even, balanced extraction
