## üìã Overview

Brew ratio (coffee weight to water weight) determines brew strength and affects extraction yield - with higher ratios (more water per coffee) creating weaker, brighter cups while lower ratios (less water per coffee) create stronger, heavier cups. Understanding ratio optimization enables precise strength control independent of extraction quality.

**What is Brew Ratio**:
- **Definition**: Grams coffee : grams water
- **ExpressionMenu 1:15 means 1g coffee to 15g water
- **Example**: 20g coffee + 300g water = 1:15 ratio
- **Determines**: Final brew strength (TDS)
- **Affects**: Extraction yield (somewhat)

**Common Ratios**:
- **Strong**: 1:12-1:14 (concentrated)
- **StandardMenu 1:15-1:16 (balanced)
- **Light**: 1:17-1:18 (milder, brighter)
- **EspressoMenu 1:2-1:3 (concentrated)

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è Ratio Effects

**Lower Ratio** (1:12-1:14, More Coffee):
- **Strength**: Stronger, more concentrated
- **TDS**: Higher (1.45-1.60%)
- **Body**: Fuller, heavier
- **Extraction**: Can be lower % (more coffee, same water)
- **Use**: When wanting strength, robustness

**Standard** (1:15-1:16):
- **Strength**: Balanced, versatile
- **TDS**: Ideal (1.30-1.45%)
- **Body**: Medium, balanced
- **Extraction**: Optimal (19-22%)
- **Use**: Default, most coffees

**Higher Ratio** (1:17-1:18, More Water):
- **Strength**: Lighter, more delicate
- **TDS**: Lower (1.15-1.30%)
- **Body**: Lighter, tea-like
- **Extraction**: Can be higher % (more water extracts more)
- **Use**: Bright coffees, delicate profiles

---

## üéØ Optimization

**Find Your Preference**:
1. **Start**: 1:16 (standard)
2. **Taste**: Assess strength and flavor
3. **Adjust**:
   - Too weak ‚Üí 1:15 or 1:14 (more coffee)
   - Too strong ‚Üí 1:17 or 1:18 (more water)
4. **Refine**: Find personal sweet spot

**Ratio vs. Grind**:
- **Ratio**: Controls strength (TDS)
- **Grind**: Controls extraction (yield %)
- **Independent**: Adjust separately for strength AND extraction optimization

---

## üìù Summary

Brew ratio (coffee:water weight) determines brew strength - lower ratios (1:12-1:14) create concentrated strong coffee, standard ratios (1:15-1:16) create balanced strength, higher ratios (1:17-1:18) create lighter delicate coffee. Ratio controls strength independently from extraction yield, enabling separate optimization of strength (ratio) and quality (grind for extraction).

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Ratio**: Coffee:water weight (1:15 typical)
- **Strength**: Lower ratio = stronger, higher = lighter
- **Standard**: 1:15-1:16 for balanced strength
- **Independent**: Ratio (strength) separate from grind (extraction)
- **Personalize**: Find ratio matching strength preference
