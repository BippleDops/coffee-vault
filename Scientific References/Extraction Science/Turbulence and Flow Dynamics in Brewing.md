## üìã Overview

Turbulence and flow patterns during brewing dramatically affect extraction uniformity and efficiency - with turbulent flow creating better mixing and faster extraction while laminar flow risks channeling and uneven extraction. Understanding fluid dynamics explains pourover technique importance, espresso channeling, and why agitation accelerates extraction.

**Flow Types**:
- **Laminar**: Smooth, layered flow (risks channeling)
- **Turbulent**: Chaotic mixing (better extraction, even saturation)

**Why Flow Matters**:
- **Extraction Rate**: Turbulence accelerates mass transfer
- **Evenness**: Turbulence prevents channeling
- **Channeling**: Laminar flow can create bypass (water avoids coffee)
- **Technique**: Pour technique creates turbulence or laminar conditions

---

## üî¨ Flow Dynamics

**Espresso** (High Turbulence):
- **Pressure**: Creates extreme turbulence
- **Result**: Fast, even extraction

**Pour-Over** (Variable):
- **Pouring**: Creates turbulence in bed
- **Technique**: Affects uniformity

**Immersion** (Minimal Flow):
- **Static**: No flow during steep (minimal turbulence)
- **Stirring**: Creates turbulence, improves extraction

---

## üìù Summary

Turbulence (chaotic mixing) creates faster, more even extraction than laminar flow through enhanced mass transfer and prevention of channeling. Espresso's pressure creates maximum turbulence; pour-over technique affects turbulence levels; immersion relies on optional stirring for turbulence.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Turbulence**: Chaotic mixing, better extraction
- **Laminar**: Smooth flow, channeling risk
- **Espresso**: Maximum turbulence (pressure)
- **Pour-Over**: Technique-dependent turbulence
- **Agitation**: Creates beneficial turbulence
