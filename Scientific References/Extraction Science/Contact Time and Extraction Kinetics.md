**Purpose**: Understand how time affects extraction rate and final yield

## üìã Overview

Contact time (brew time) is coffee extraction's temporal dimension - determining how long water and coffee interact, how much flavor dissolves, and which compounds extract into the cup. Understanding extraction kinetics (how extraction progresses over time) enables optimization of brew time for desired flavor balance, strength, and complexity.

**What is Contact Time**:
- **Definition**: Duration of water-coffee contact during brewing
- **Measurement**: Seconds or minutes from first water contact to separation
- **Variable**: Differs dramatically by brew method (20 seconds espresso to 24 hours cold brew)
- **Critical**: Determines extraction yield and flavor balance
- **Control**: One of brewing's primary controllable variables

**Why Contact Time Matters**:
- **Extraction Yield**: Longer time ‚Üí higher extraction (up to equilibrium)
- **Flavor Development**: Different compounds extract at different rates
- **Optimization**: Each coffee has ideal contact time
- **Method Design**: Brew methods defined partly by contact time
- **Troubleshooting**: Adjusting time fixes under/over-extraction

---

## üî¨ Extraction Kinetics Theory

**Extraction Over Time**:
- **Rapid Initial** (0-30 seconds): Fastest extraction rate, acids and caffeine dissolve quickly
- **Moderate** (30-120 seconds): Sweetness and body compounds extract
- **Slow** (120+ seconds): Heavier compounds, bitters extract slowly
- **Equilibrium** (Variable): Extraction rate slows to near-zero (saturation limit reached)

**Compound Extraction Order** (Generally):
1. **First** (0-60s): Acids, caffeine, fruity volatiles (fast-extracting)
2. **Second** (60-180s): Sugars, caramels, body compounds (medium)
3. **Last** (180+s): Bitters, heavy compounds, over-extraction territory

**Implications**:
- **Too Short**: Under-extraction (sour, weak) - only acids/caffeine extracted
- **Optimal**: Balanced (sweet) - acids + sugars + body extracted
- **Too Long**: Over-extraction (bitter) - bitters and heavy compounds dominate

---

## ‚è±Ô∏è Contact Time by Brew Method

**Espresso** (20-30 seconds):
- **Very Short**: High pressure compensates
- **Extraction**: 18-22% achievable in 25 seconds
- **Control**: Grind size primary time variable

**AeroPress** (1-3 minutes):
- **Short-Medium**: Immersion + light pressure
- **Versatile**: Wide time range acceptable (30s to 5 min possible)
- **Extraction**: 18-22% typical

**Pour-Over** (2.5-4 minutes):
- **Medium**: Continuous percolation
- **Typical**: 3:00-3:30 total time ideal for most
- **Extraction**: 19-22% typical

**French Press** (4-5 minutes):
- **Medium**: Immersion without pressure
- **Standard**: 4:00 most common
- **Extraction**: 18-21% typical

**Clever Dripper** (2-4 minutes):
- **Medium**: Immersion then percolation
- **Versatile**: 2:00-3:30 common range
- **Extraction**: 19-22%

**Cold Brew** (12-24 hours):
- **Very Long**: Compensates for cold water (slow extraction)
- **Extraction**: 18-23% achievable (despite cold)
- **Time Makes Up**: For temperature disadvantage

## üéØ Optimizing Contact Time

**General Principles**:

**If Under-Extracted** (Sour, Weak):
- **Solution**: Increase contact time
- **Methods**: 
  - Pour-over: Finer grind (slows flow, increases time)
  - Immersion: Steep longer
  - Espresso: Finer grind (extends shot time)

**If Over-Extracted** (Bitter, Harsh):
- **Solution**: Decrease contact time
- **Methods**:
  - Pour-over: Coarser grind (faster flow, less time)
  - Immersion: Shorter steep
  - Espresso: Coarser grind (faster shot)

**Sweet Spot Finding**:
1. **Start**: Standard time for method
2. **Taste**: Assess extraction
3. **Adjust**: Change grind to modify time
4. **Iterate**: Find ideal time for specific coffee

---

## üìä Extraction Rate Over Time

**Rapid Extraction Phase** (First 30-60 seconds):
- **50-70%** of total extraction happens
- **Compounds**: Acids, caffeine, light volatiles
- **Critical**: Initial contact must be even, complete

**Medium Extraction** (60-180 seconds):
- **25-35%** of total extraction
- **Compounds**: Sugars, body, caramels
- **Important**: Sweetness development

**Slow Extraction** (180+ seconds):
- **5-15%** of total extraction
- **Compounds**: Bitters, heavy compounds
- **Risk Zone**: Over-extraction territory if extended too long

**Equilibrium**:
- **Eventually**: Extraction rate approaches zero (saturation)
- **Time**: Varies (espresso 30s, immersion 5-10 min)
- **Beyond**: No additional extraction benefit, only over-extraction risk

## üí° Practical Applications

**Espresso**:
- **Target Time**: 25-30 seconds
- **Adjustment**: Grind size (finer = longer, coarser = shorter)
- **Guideline**: 20-35 second range acceptable

**Pour-Over**:
- **Target**: 2:45-3:30 total time
- **Fast (<2:30)**: Likely under-extracted, grind finer
- **Slow (>4:00)**: Likely over-extracted or clogged, grind coarser

**French Press**:
- **Standard**: 4:00 steep
- **Range**: 3:00-5:00 acceptable
- **Longer**: Doesn't improve much beyond 5:00 (equilibrium)

**AeroPress**:
- **Versatile**: 1:00-4:00 all work (depends on recipe)
- **Standard**: 1:30-2:00 inverted method
- **Experimentation**: Wide acceptable range

**Cold Brew**:
- **Standard**: 12-18 hours
- **Range**: 8-24 hours works
- **Longer**: Diminishing returns beyond 24 hours

## üìù Summary

Contact time (brew time) determines extraction yield and flavor balance through extraction kinetics - rapid initial extraction (acids, caffeine), medium-phase sweetness development, and slow late-phase bitters. Each brew method has optimal contact time ranges; adjusting time (via grind size or steep duration) is primary variable for fixing under or over-extraction.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Contact Time**: Water-coffee interaction duration
- **Extraction Kinetics**: Acids first ‚Üí sugars ‚Üí bitters
- **Rapid Initial**: 50-70% extraction in first minute
- **Optimal Times**: Varies by method (25s espresso, 4min French Press, 12-18hr cold brew)
- **Adjustment**: Grind size modifies contact time (finer = longer)
- **Sweet Spot**: Each coffee has ideal time for balanced extraction

Understanding contact time and extraction kinetics enables precise brew time optimization for consistent, delicious, balanced coffee across all brewing methods.
