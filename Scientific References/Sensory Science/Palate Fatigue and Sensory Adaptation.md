## üìã Overview

Palate fatigue (sensory adaptation) is the diminishing ability to perceive flavors after repeated tasting - a physiological phenomenon where receptors become less responsive after continuous stimulation. Understanding and managing palate fatigue is critical for accurate cupping, quality control, and maintaining sensory acuity throughout tasting sessions.

**What is Palate Fatigue**:
- **Definition**: Decreased sensory perception after repeated exposure
- **Mechanism**: Taste/smell receptors adapt, become less sensitive
- **Timeline**: Begins after 5-10 samples, significant after 15-20
- **Reversible**: Rest and palate cleansing restore sensitivity
- **Universal**: Everyone experiences it (physiological, not weakness)

**Why It Matters**:
- **QC**: Quality control requires consistent sensory ability
- **Cupping**: Multi-coffee cupping affected by fatigue
- **Decisions**: Purchasing decisions require reliable palate
- **Training**: Must manage fatigue during learning
- **Fairness**: Late-tasted coffees disadvantaged if fatigued

---

## üî¨ Physiological Mechanisms

**Sensory Adaptation**:
- **Receptor Desensitization**: Taste buds, olfactory receptors become less responsive
- **Neural Adaptation**: Brain filters redundant signals
- **Temporary**: Reversible with rest (15-30 minutes)

**Contributing Factors**:
- **Number of Samples**: More samples = more fatigue
- **Time**: Longer sessions = more fatigue
- **Intensity**: Strong flavors fatigue faster
- **Temperature**: Hot liquids more fatiguing
- **Astringency**: Drying compounds accelerate fatigue

---

## üéØ Managing Palate Fatigue

**Palate Cleansing**:
- **Water**: Room temperature water between samples
- **Crackers**: Plain crackers, bread (neutral, mild)
- **Wait**: 30 seconds minimum between samples
- **Avoid**: Strong cleaners (citrus, carbonation can overstimulate)

**Session Structure**:
- **Limit Samples**: 10-12 coffees maximum per session
- **Breaks**: 5-minute break every 6-8 samples
- **Order**: Randomize to prevent bias
- **Time Limits**: Complete within 45-60 minutes

**Professional Strategies**:
- **Multiple Sessions**: Split large evaluations across days
- **Panel Rotation**: Rotate panel members (fresh palates)
- **Calibration**: Standard reference samples to detect fatigue
- **Documentation**: Note when fatigue suspected

---

## üìù Summary

Palate fatigue (sensory adaptation) diminishes taste perception after 10-15+ samples through receptor desensitization - manageable through water/cracker cleansing, session limits (10-12 coffees), breaks (every 6-8 samples), and time limits (45-60 minutes). Recognition and management ensure consistent, accurate sensory evaluation throughout cupping sessions.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Fatigue**: Inevitable after 10-15+ samples
- **Mechanism**: Receptor desensitization, neural adaptation
- **Management**: Water, crackers, breaks, limits
- **Session Structure**: 10-12 coffees max, 45-60 minutes
- **Reversible**: Rest restores sensitivity
