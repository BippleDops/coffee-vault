# üëÉ SCA Cupping Protocol - Step-by-Step Guide

The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) cupping protocol is the industry standard method for evaluating coffee quality. This systematic approach enables consistent, objective assessment of coffee samples.

---

## üìã Overview

**Purpose**: Objectively evaluate coffee quality using standardized procedures
**Time Required**: 30-45 minutes per session
**Difficulty**: Beginner-friendly, improves with practice
**Applications**: Quality control, bean selection, competition judging, professional development

## üõ†Ô∏è Equipment Required

### Essential Equipment
- **Cupping bowls**: 5-6 oz capacity (150-200ml), ideally glass or ceramic
- **Cupping spoons**: Deep, round-bowled spoons (8-10ml capacity)
- **Kettle**: For precise water delivery
- **Scale**: Accurate to 0.1g
- **Grinder**: Burr grinder capable of medium-coarse grind
- **Timer**: For precise timing
- **Thermometer**: Digital, reading to 0.1¬∞F or 0.1¬∞C

### Recommended Equipment
- **Spittoon**: For expectorating coffee
- **Water rinse cups**: For rinsing spoons between samples
- **Cupping forms**: Printed SCA forms for scoring
- **Notebook**: For detailed observations
- **Aroma lid**: To capture fragrance (optional)

## ‚öôÔ∏è Setup Parameters

### Coffee Preparation
- **Coffee weight**: 8.25g per 150ml water
- **Grind size**: Medium-coarse (slightly coarser than drip)
- **Grind timing**: Within 15 minutes of cupping
- **Sample roast level**: Light to medium (Agtron 58-63 for whole bean)
- **Rest time**: Roasted 8-24 hours before cupping

### Water Specifications
- **Temperature**: 200¬∞F (93¬∞C) at contact with grounds
- **TDS**: 125-175 ppm
- **Alkalinity**: 40-70 ppm
- **pH**: 6.5-7.5
- **Odorless and clean**

### Environment
- **Room temperature**: Comfortable (68-72¬∞F / 20-22¬∞C)
- **Lighting**: Bright, preferably natural light
- **Quiet space**: Minimal distractions
- **Neutral odors**: No competing smells

## üìä The Cupping Process

### Step 1: Sample Preparation (0-5 minutes)

**Dry Evaluation**:
1. Measure 8.25g of whole bean coffee
2. Observe whole bean appearance: color, uniformity, defects
3. Grind coffee immediately before cupping
4. Pour grounds into cupping bowl
5. Set up 2-3 bowls per sample (for consistency checking)

**Fragrance Assessment**:
1. Immediately smell dry grounds in bowl
2. Gently shake bowl to release aromatics
3. Note intensity and character of dry fragrance
4. Assess complexity and quality
5. Score **Fragrance/Aroma** (1-10 scale)

*Descriptors*: Floral, fruity, nutty, chocolatey, spicy, herbal, etc.

### Step 2: Adding Water (5-10 minutes)

**Wet Evaluation**:
1. Heat water to exactly 200¬∞F (93¬∞C)
2. Start timer
3. Pour water directly onto grounds
4. Fill bowl to rim (150ml total water)
5. Immediately assess wet aroma
6. Do NOT disturb crust yet

**Aroma Assessment**:
1. Lean over bowl and inhale deeply
2. Note how aroma changes from dry to wet
3. Assess intensity, complexity, quality
4. Compare across samples
5. Update **Fragrance/Aroma** score if needed

*Tip*: Wet aroma often reveals more complexity than dry fragrance

### Step 3: Crust Break (4 minutes steep time)

**Breaking the Crust**:
1. At exactly 4:00 minutes, prepare to break crust
2. Position nose close to bowl surface
3. Use cupping spoon to gently push crust from far side toward you
4. Break crust in 3 deliberate strokes
5. Inhale deeply as aromatics release
6. This is peak aroma intensity!

**Skim the Surface**:
1. After breaking crust on all samples, return to first bowl
2. Use two spoons to skim foam and grounds from surface
3. Remove as much floating material as possible
4. Clean spoons between samples
5. Final aroma assessment

**Scoring**:
- Update **Fragrance/Aroma** with final score
- Note any defects observed during breaking

### Step 4: First Tasting (13-15 minutes after pour)

**Slurping Technique**:
1. Allow coffee to cool to ~160¬∞F (71¬∞C) - about 13-15 minutes
2. Dip spoon into liquid, collecting generous amount
3. Bring spoon to lips
4. Slurp forcefully to aspirate liquid across entire palate
5. "Spray" coffee across tongue and roof of mouth
6. Hold briefly, then expectorate into spittoon

**Why Slurp?**
- Aerates coffee, releasing volatile aromatics
- Distributes liquid across entire tongue
- Engages retronasal olfaction
- Cools coffee as it enters mouth

**Initial Assessment Categories**:

**Flavor** (1-10):
- Primary taste: What do you taste?
- Complexity: Simple or multi-layered?
- Quality: Pleasant, distinctive, clean?

**Aftertaste** (1-10):
- Length: How long do flavors persist?
- Quality: Pleasant or unpleasant lingering?
- Evolution: Do flavors develop or fade quickly?

**Acidity** (1-10):
- Intensity: How bright?
- Quality: Crisp, bright, or sour, sharp?
- Type: Citric, malic, phosphoric, acetic?

**Body** (1-10):
- Weight: Light, medium, full?
- Texture: Silky, creamy, syrupy, watery?
- Mouthfeel: Coating, tongue weight?

### Step 5: Second Tasting (20-22 minutes after pour)

**As Coffee Cools to ~140¬∞F (60¬∞C)**:

Flavors develop and change as coffee cools. Reassess:

1. **Taste all samples again at this temperature**
2. **Confirm or adjust scores** based on evolution
3. **Note consistency** across multiple bowls of same sample
4. **Assess Balance**: How well do components harmonize?

**Balance** (1-10):
- Harmony: Do acidity, sweetness, body complement?
- Integration: Are flavors unified or disjointed?
- Completeness: Well-rounded or one-dimensional?

**Sweetness** (1-10 or points):
- Intensity: How much perceived sweetness?
- Quality: Honey, caramel, sugar, fruit sweetness?
- Clean: Or is bitterness/sourness masking?

Note: SCA scoring system gives 2 points per cup for Clean Cup, Sweetness, and Uniformity (across multiple bowls)

### Step 6: Final Tasting (25-30 minutes after pour)

**At ~130¬∞F (54¬∞C) or Room Temperature**:

Final assessment as coffee reaches optimal tasting temperature:

1. **Make final assessments of all attributes**
2. **Confirm consistency across sample bowls**
3. **Finalize all scores**
4. **Note any defects** that become apparent
5. **Calculate final score**

**Additional Attributes**:

**Clean Cup** (2 points):
- Free from defects?
- No off-flavors or taints?
- Transparent flavors?

**Uniformity** (2 points):
- Are all cups of same sample consistent?
- Check all 3-5 bowls
- Penalty for inconsistency

**Overall** (1-10):
- Your holistic assessment
- Does this coffee excite you?
- Would you want to drink this?
- "The gestalt"

## üèÜ Scoring System

### SCA Point Scale

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 6.00-6.75 | **Good**: Acceptable specialty grade, but not particularly notable |
| 7.00-7.75 | **Very Good**: Clean, good characteristics, minor faults allowed |
| 8.00-8.75 | **Excellent**: High quality, distinct attributes, few if any faults |
| 9.00-9.75 | **Outstanding**: Exceptional coffee with striking characteristics |
| 10.00 | **Extraordinary**: Perfection (rarely awarded) |

### Attribute Categories and Weights

**Primary Attributes** (Score 6-10 each):
1. Fragrance/Aroma (combined score)
2. Flavor
3. Aftertaste
4. Acidity
5. Body
6. Balance
7. Overall

**Secondary Attributes** (2 points each):
8. Clean Cup (2 pts x 5 cups = 10 points possible)
9. Sweetness (2 pts x 5 cups = 10 points possible)
10. Uniformity (2 pts x 5 cups = 10 points possible)

**Defect Deductions**:
- **Taint**: -2 points per occurrence (minor defect)
- **Fault**: -4 points per occurrence (major defect)

### Calculation

**Total Score** = Sum of all attribute scores - Defect deductions

**Final Score** = Total Score + 36 points (baseline)

**Specialty Grade** = 80+ points

**Grade Classifications**:
- **90+**: Outstanding Specialty
- **85-89.99**: Excellent Specialty
- **80-84.99**: Very Good Specialty
- **< 80**: Below Specialty Grade

## üìù Scoring Tips

### Calibration Guidelines

**7.5 is "Average" Specialty**:
- Use 7.5 as your baseline for acceptable specialty coffee
- Score relative to this baseline
- Reserve 9+ for truly exceptional attributes

**Differentiate Quality from Intensity**:
- High acidity intensity doesn't mean high acidity quality
- Separate how much from how good

**Use Half Points**:
- 0.25 increments allowed
- Increases precision
- Helps differentiate similar samples

**Be Consistent**:
- Use same reference points across session
- Cup same samples weekly for calibration
- Practice with experienced cuppers

## üö´ Common Defects to Identify

### Flavor Defects

**Ferment/Sour**:
- Over-fermented during processing
- Vinegary, acetic acid notes
- Score: Major fault (-4)

**Musty/Moldy**:
- Damp storage conditions
- Earthy, mildew notes
- Score: Major fault (-4)

**Phenolic/Medicinal**:
- Processing issues or packaging
- Band-aid, chemical notes
- Score: Major fault (-4)

**Potato Defect**:
- Enzymatic defect in East African coffees
- Raw potato smell/taste
- Score: Major fault (-4 per cup)

**Faded/Past Crop**:
- Aged beyond prime
- Flat, papery, cardboard notes
- Score: Taint (-2)

**Woody/Straw**:
- Prolonged aging
- Green wood, hay, straw notes
- Score: Taint (-2)

## üéØ Cupping Etiquette and Best Practices

### Professional Behavior

**Do**:
‚úÖ Arrive prepared with clean palate (no coffee, food, or toothpaste 30 mins prior)
‚úÖ Avoid wearing perfume, cologne, or scented products
‚úÖ Slurp loudly and confidently (it's professional!)
‚úÖ Rinse spoon thoroughly between samples
‚úÖ Expectorate don't swallow (to avoid palate fatigue and caffeine overload)
‚úÖ Cup in silence during evaluation
‚úÖ Make notes as you go
‚úÖ Be open to discussion after scoring

**Don't**:
‚ùå Talk during active cupping
‚ùå Lean over someone else's cups
‚ùå Double-dip spoon without rinsing
‚ùå Share unsolicited opinions while others are scoring
‚ùå Rush the process
‚ùå Skip samples or steps

### Palate Management

**Before Cupping**:
- Eat light, neutral meal 1-2 hours prior
- Hydrate well
- Avoid strong flavors, spices, garlic
- No smoking or vaping
- Brush teeth but no toothpaste close to session

**During Cupping**:
- Drink water between samples
- Cleanse palate with water and plain crackers
- Take breaks every 8-10 samples
- Don't swallow - spit!
- Manage caffeine intake

**After Cupping**:
- Review and finalize scores
- Compare with other cuppers
- Discuss findings
- Record insights in notes

## üìà Developing Your Cupping Skills

### Practice Routine

**Week 1-2**: Basic Protocol
- Focus on technique: grinding, pouring, breaking, slurping
- Don't worry about scoring yet
- Get comfortable with process
- Cup same coffee multiple times for consistency

**Week 3-4**: Sensory Awareness
- Begin scoring one attribute at a time
- Start with easier attributes: Body, Clean Cup
- Use flavor wheel for vocabulary
- Cup 3-4 samples per session

**Week 5-8**: Full Scoring
- Score all attributes in one session
- Cup 4-6 samples per session
- Compare scores with experienced cuppers
- Note personal biases and tendencies

**Ongoing**: Calibration
- Regular calibration sessions with others
- Cup same samples weekly for consistency
- Attend public cupping at roasters
- Participate in cupping competitions

### Calibration Exercises

**Triangle Test**:
- Two cups of same coffee, one different
- Identify the odd cup
- Builds discrimination ability

**Ranking Exercise**:
- 4-5 samples of varying quality
- Rank them without scoring
- Compare rankings with others

**Attribute Focus**:
- Cup 3-4 samples focusing on ONE attribute only
- E.g., "Acidity Day" - score only acidity
- Deepens understanding of specific characteristics

**Blind Calibration**:
- Score samples without knowing identity
- Compare scores to known benchmarks
- Identify personal biases
