## üìã Overview

Sensory panel training transforms individuals into calibrated tasters - systematic training using reference standards, discrimination exercises, and calibration samples to create consistent, reliable sensory evaluation teams essential for quality control, product development, and research.

**Why Panel Training Matters**:
- **Consistency**: Trained panels provide repeatable results
- **Reliability**: Calibration ensures agreement among panelists
- **QC**: Quality control requires consistent sensory ability
- **Research**: Scientific studies need trained panels
- **Professional**: Q Grader, Cup of Excellence require training

**Training Components**:
1. **Discrimination**: Ability to detect differences
2. **Identification**: Naming specific flavors accurately
3. **Intensity**: Judging strength of attributes
4. **Calibration**: Agreement with standards and other panelists

## üéì Training Methods

**Reference Standards**:
- **Flavor Standards**: Le Nez du Caf√© (aroma kit), reference solutions
- **Intensity Standards**: Graduated concentrations (weak to strong)
- **Triangle Testing**: Discrimination practice
- **Threshold Testing**: Sensitivity measurement

**Calibration**:
- **Standard Samples**: All panelists taste same coffee
- **Discussion**: Compare notes, agree on descriptors
- **Feedback**: Identify outliers, recalibrate
- **Repeat**: Regular calibration sessions maintain consistency

**Practice Exercises**:
- **Daily Cupping**: Regular practice maintains skills
- **Blind Tasting**: Remove bias, focus on sensory
- **Descriptor Matching**: Learn flavor vocabulary
- **Intensity Scaling**: Practice rating strength
