## üìã Overview

The Coffee Taster's Flavor Wheel (developed by SCA and World Coffee Research) is coffee's universal language for describing flavors - a radial diagram organizing descriptors from general (center) to specific (outer edge), enabling precise, standardized communication about coffee sensory experiences.

**What the Flavor Wheel Is**:
- **Universal Standard**: SCA (Specialty Coffee Association) official tool
- **Radial Organization**: Center = broad categories, outer = specific descriptors
- **Hierarchical**: General ‚Üí Specific moving outward
- **Scientific**: Based on sensory science research
- **Comprehensive**: ~110 specific flavor descriptors
- **Updated**: Current version released 2016 (major revision)

**Why Use It**:
- **Precision**: Specific vocabulary vs. vague descriptions
- **Communication**: Industry-standard language
- **Training**: Develops palate and sensory awareness
- **Consistency**: Repeatable tasting assessments
- **Professional**: Required for Q Grader certification

---

## üéØ Structure of the Flavor Wheel

### Center to Edge Progression

**Center (Most General)**:
9 Primary Categories:
1. **Fruity** (berry, citrus, other fruit)
2. **Sour/Fermented** (sour, alcohol, fermented)
3. **Green/Vegetative** (olive oil, raw, green)
4. **Other** (papery, chemical)
5. **Roasted** (pipe tobacco, tobacco, burnt)
6. **Spices** (pungent, pepper, brown spice, etc.)
7. **Nutty/Cocoa** (nutty, cocoa)
8. **Sweet** (brown sugar, vanilla, etc.)
9. **Floral** (black tea, floral)

**Middle Ring (Specific Categories)**:
- Each primary divides into 2-4 subcategories
- Example: Fruity ‚Üí Berry, Dried Fruit, Other Fruit, Citrus Fruit

**Outer Ring (Most Specific)**:
- Individual descriptors
- Example: Berry ‚Üí Blackberry, Raspberry, Blueberry, Strawberry

---

## üìù How to Use the Wheel

### Step-by-Step

1. **Start Center**: Identify general category first
   - Example: Taste fruity character ‚Üí "Fruity"

2. **Move Outward**: Narrow to subcategory
   - Example: What kind of fruit? ‚Üí "Berry"

3. **Outer Edge**: Specific descriptor
   - Example: Which berry? ‚Üí "Blueberry"

4. **Result**: Precise descriptor ("Blueberry")

**Don't Reverse**: Don't start specific - too many options overwhelm
**Build Outward**: General ‚Üí Specific creates logical progression

## üí° Practical Application

### During Cupping/Tasting

**Aroma Phase**:
- Smell dry grounds ‚Üí Note aromas using wheel
- Smell wet grounds ‚Üí Additional aromas

**Taste Phase**:
- Slurp, note flavors ‚Üí Use wheel to identify
- As coffee cools ‚Üí Flavors evolve, note changes

**Documentation**:
- Record 3-8 descriptors per coffee
- Include general and specific
- Note intensity (subtle, moderate, strong)

## üìù Summary

The Coffee Flavor Wheel is the industry-standard tool for describing coffee flavors precisely and consistently. By moving from center (general) to edge (specific), it enables anyone to develop sensory vocabulary and communicate about coffee flavors effectively.

**Key Takeaways**:
- **Start center, move outward**: General ‚Üí specific progression
- **9 primary categories**: Fruity, sour/fermented, green, other, roasted, spices, nutty/cocoa, sweet, floral
- **~110 descriptors**: Comprehensive flavor vocabulary
- **Training tool**: Develops palate and vocabulary
- **Communication**: Universal language for coffee professionals
