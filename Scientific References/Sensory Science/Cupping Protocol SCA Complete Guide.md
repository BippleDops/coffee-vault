## üìã Overview

The SCA (Specialty Coffee Association) Cupping Protocol is coffee's global standard for sensory evaluation - a precise, standardized methodology enabling consistent quality assessment, communication, and comparison across coffees, roasters, and tasters worldwide.

**What is Cupping**:
- **Definition**: Standardized coffee tasting/evaluation method
- **Purpose**: Quality assessment, comparison, flavor profiling
- **Standard**: SCA protocol is global industry standard
- **Professional**: Required for Q Grader certification
- **Consistency**: Same method worldwide enables communication
- **Objective**: Minimize variables, maximize sensory focus

**Why Cupping Matters**:
- **Quality Control**: Roasters cup to ensure quality
- **Purchasing**: Buyers cup to evaluate before purchasing
- **Competition**: Cup of Excellence, Best of Panama use SCA protocol
- **Training**: Develops palate and sensory skills
- **Communication**: Universal language for coffee quality
- **Comparison**: Compare multiple coffees side-by-side systematically

**SCA Protocol Standardization**:
- **Coffee Amount**: 8.25g per cup
- **Water**: 150ml per cup (1:18.18 ratio)
- **Grind**: Medium-coarse (70-75% passing through US#20 sieve)
- **Water Temp**: 200¬∞F (93¬∞C) at pouring
- **Steep**: 4 minutes before evaluation
- **Cups**: 5 cups minimum per coffee (consistency check)

## üî¨ SCA Cupping Setup

### Required Equipment

**Essential**:
- **Cupping Bowls**: 6-8 oz capacity (150-200ml), ceramic or tempered glass
- **Cupping Spoons**: Deep bowl spoons (silver-plated traditional)
- **Grinder**: Burr grinder, consistent medium-coarse grind
- **Scale**: 0.1g precision
- **Kettle**: Temperature control (200¬∞F/93¬∞C)
- **Timer**: Precise timing critical
- **Cupping Forms**: SCA standardized forms for scoring
- **Spittoon**: Cup or vessel for expectorating

**Optional but Professional**:
- **Sample Roaster**: For green coffee evaluation (light roast samples)
- **Agtron**: Roast color measurement
- **Refractometer**: TDS measurement
- **Rinse Cups**: Water for rinsing spoons between samples

### Sample Preparation

**Roasting** (For Green Coffee Cupping):
- **Roast Level**: Light (City roast, Agtron 58-63)
- **Time**: 8-12 minutes (avoid underdevelopment or scorching)
- **Purpose**: Standardized roast reveals inherent bean quality
- **Rest**: 8-24 hours post-roast before cupping

**Grinding**:
- **Timing**: Grind immediately before cupping (within 15 minutes)
- **Size**: Medium-coarse (70-75% through #20 sieve = 850 microns)
- **Consistency**: Same grind for all samples (fairness)

## üìù SCA Cupping Protocol Step-by-Step

### Setup (10 minutes before)

1. **Prepare Samples**:
   - **5 Cups per Coffee**: Consistency and defect detection
   - **8.25g Coffee** per cup (precise weighing)
   - **Label**: Identify each sample clearly
   - **Grind**: Medium-coarse, immediately before use

2. **Arrange Cups**:
   - **Row Format**: Each coffee in row of 5 cups
   - **Multiple Coffees**: Multiple rows for comparison
   - **Cleanliness**: Ensure bowls clean, no residual flavors

3. **Heat Water**:
   - **200¬∞F (93¬∞C)** at pouring moment
   - Adequate volume for all cups

### Evaluation Stages

**Stage 1: Fragrance - Dry Grounds** (0:00):
1. **Smell Dry Grounds**: Bring cup to nose, inhale deeply
2. **Note Aromas**: Document fragrance descriptors
3. **Score**: Fragrance/Aroma score (combined later)
4. **Repeat**: Smell each of 5 cups

**Stage 2: Pour & Aroma - Wet Grounds** (0:00-4:00):
1. **Pour Water** (0:00):
   - **150ml water** per cup
   - Pour gently to saturate all grounds
   - Fill to rim
   - **Start Timer**

2. **Wet Aroma** (0:30-3:00):
   - **Smell Wet Grounds**: Bring cup to nose
   - Inhale aroma from wet grounds (intensified from dry)
   - Document wet aroma descriptors

3. **Wait** (Let Steep):
   - **4:00 total** steep time before breaking crust

**Stage 3: Breaking the Crust** (4:00):
1. **Position**: Cup at nose level
2. **Stir**: Push back floating grounds with spoon (3 stirs)
3. **Inhale**: Smell released aromatics as crust breaks
   - **Aroma Release**: Breaking crust releases intense aromatics
   - **Peak Aroma**: Strongest aroma moment in cupping
4. **Score**: Complete Fragrance/Aroma score (combined dry + wet + break)
5. **Repeat**: All 5 cups per coffee

**Stage 4: Cleaning** (4:00-5:00):
1. **Skim**: Remove floating grounds and foam from surface
2. **Cleanliness**: Clean cup surface ensures clear tasting

**Stage 5: Tasting** (8:00-45:00):
1. **First Tasting** (~8:00, when cooled to ~160-170¬∞F/71-77¬∞C):
   - **Slurp**: Aspirate coffee (loud slurping aerates, coats palate)
   - **Assess**: Flavor, aftertaste, acidity, body, balance
   - **Expectorate**: Spit into spittoon (avoid caffeine overload)
   - **All Cups**: Taste all 5 cups per coffee

2. **Second Tasting** (~12-15 min, ~140-150¬∞F/60-65¬∞C):
   - Coffee cooled, new flavors emerge
   - Reassess, note evolution

3. **Third Tasting** (~20-25 min, ~120-130¬∞F/49-54¬∞C):
   - Room temperature approaching
   - Final assessment
   - Sweetness often peak here

4. **Final Tasting** (~30-45 min, room temp):
   - **Cup of Excellence**: Evaluates to room temperature
   - Defects often appear when cool
   - Final scores determined

## üìä SCA Scoring Attributes (10-Point Scale)

**Primary Attributes** (6-10 points each):
1. **Fragrance/Aroma** (Dry + Wet + Break)
2. **Flavor** (Primary taste characteristics)
3. **Aftertaste** (Finish length and quality)
4. **Acidity** (Brightness intensity and quality)
5. **Body** (Mouthfeel and texture)
6. **Balance** (How components harmonize)
7. **Overall** (Holistic assessment)

**Secondary Attributes** (2 points each, 0-10 total):
8. **Clean Cup** (0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 - per cup defect-free)
9. **Sweetness** (0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 - per cup)
10. **Uniformity** (0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 - consistency across 5 cups)

**Defects** (Subtract from total):
- **Taint** (minor): -2 points each
- **Fault** (major): -4 points each

**Total Score**: Sum of all attributes (maximum 100 points)

**Grade Categories**:
- **90-100**: Outstanding (extremely rare)
- **85-89.99**: Excellent (competition-level)
- **80-84.99**: Very Good (specialty-grade)
- **<80**: Below specialty grade
