## üìã Overview

Retronasal olfaction (smelling through mouth while eating/drinking) creates most of what we call "flavor" - up to 80% of flavor perception comes from aroma reaching nose from mouth, not taste buds. Understanding retronasal olfaction explains why coffee tastes different than it smells and why holding your nose eliminates most flavor.

**The Flavor Truth**:
- **Taste**: Only 5 tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami) - tongue
- **Smell**: Thousands of aroma compounds - nose
- **Flavor**: Taste + Smell combined = perceived flavor
- **Retronasal**: Most flavor from smell (through mouth to nose)

**Why It Matters**:
- **80% of Flavor**: Comes from aroma, not taste
- **Slurping**: Professional slurping aerates, maximizes retronasal
- **Cold Blocking**: Cold/congestion ruins coffee (can't smell)
- **Hot vs. Cool**: Temperature affects volatile release (retronasal changes)

---

## üî¨ Orthonasal vs. Retronasal

**Orthonasal** (Smelling Through Nose):
- **Definition**: Normal smelling (nose to brain)
- **Coffee**: Smelling dry/wet grounds, brewed coffee aroma
- **Function**: Preview, anticipation, attraction

**Retronasal** (Smelling Through Mouth):
- **Definition**: Aroma from mouth up to nasal cavity (back of throat)
- **Coffee**: While drinking, aroma travels from mouth to nose
- **Function**: **Creates "flavor"** perception
- **Dominates**: 70-80% of flavor experience

**Why Both Matter**:
- Orthonasal sets expectations
- Retronasal delivers flavor
- Together create complete experience
