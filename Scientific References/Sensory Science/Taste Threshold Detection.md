Taste thresholds (minimum concentration detectable) vary dramatically between individuals - with trained tasters having lower thresholds (higher sensitivity) than untrained, explaining why some people detect subtle flavors others miss and why sensory training improves detection ability.

**What is Threshold**:
- **Definition**: Minimum concentration detectable by taste/smell
- **Individual Variation**: 10-100x variation between people
- **Training**: Lowers thresholds (improves sensitivity)
- **Age**: Generally decreases with age (higher thresholds)

**Why Thresholds Matter**:
- **Flavor Detection**: Determines what flavors perceived
- **Quality Control**: Low thresholds detect defects earlier
- **Training**: Measures sensory improvement
- **Professional**: Q Grader tests threshold detection

## ğŸ”¬ Threshold Types

**Detection Threshold**:
- **Definition**: Minimum to detect presence (something vs. nothing)
- **Lowest**: Can detect but not identify

**Recognition Threshold**:
- **Definition**: Minimum to identify what it is
- **Higher**: Must be stronger to name flavor

**Difference Threshold**:
- **Definition**: Minimum difference detectable between two samples
- **Application**: Quality control, consistency checking
