## ðŸ“‹ Overview

Triangle testing is sensory science's gold-standard discrimination methodology - presenting three samples (two identical, one different) to determine if taster can identify the odd one, enabling objective assessment of perceptual differences, sensory training, and quality control validation.

**What is Triangle Testing**:
- **Setup**: Three samples (A, A, B presented blind)
- **Task**: Identify which sample is different
- **Goal**: Determine if difference is perceptible
- **Statistical**: Results analyzed statistically for significance
- **Applications**: QC, sensory training, processing comparison, variety discrimination

**Why Use Triangle Testing**:
- **Objective**: Removes bias, forces pure sensory discrimination
- **Statistical**: Provides statistical significance (not subjective opinion)
- **Training**: Develops sensory acuity through practice
- **QC**: Validates roast consistency, quality maintenance
- **Research**: Determines if processing/variety changes are perceptible

**Standard in**:
- Q Grader certification (required skill)
- Sensory panel training
- Quality control programs
- Coffee research
- Competition preparation

## ðŸ”¬ Triangle Test Protocol

**Setup**:
1. **Prepare Samples**: 
   - Sample A (control): Two cups
   - Sample B (test): One cup
   - **Randomize**: A-A-B, A-B-A, or B-A-A order (random)

2. **Present Blind**:
   - Cups labeled 1, 2, 3 (no indication which is different)
   - Tester doesn't know order
   - **Randomization**: Different for each tester

3. **Task**:
   - Taste all three samples
   - Identify which is different (forced choice - must guess even if unsure)
   - **No "don't know"**: Must choose one

4. **Record**:
   - Correct or incorrect
   - Confidence level (optional)
   - Descriptors (optional)

**Statistical Analysis**:
- **Chance**: 33.3% correct by guessing
- **Significance**: Need >33.3% across multiple testers
- **Minimum Testers**: 20-30 for statistical power
- **Calculation**: Binomial probability determines if results significant
