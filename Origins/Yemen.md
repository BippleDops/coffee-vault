# Yemen

## Geographic Overview

**Location**: Arabian Peninsula, Middle East  
**Coordinates**: 15°N latitude  
**Capital**: Sana'a  
**Coffee Regions**: Bani Matar, Haraz, Mattari, Ismaili

## Historical Significance

### Coffee's Journey to the World

**Birthplace of Coffee Trade**:
- Port of Mokha (Al-Mukha): Historic coffee export hub
- 15th-17th centuries: Only source of coffee globally
- Controlled world coffee trade
- Original "Mocha" coffee origin

**Etymology**:
- "Mocha" from port city name
- Mokha/Mocha/Moka variations
- Not chocolate (that's a modern conflation)
- Historical trade term

### Decline and Current Status

**Historical Dominance → Decline**:
- 17th century: Monopoly broken (Dutch stole plants)
- Cultivation spread globally
- Yemen production declined
- Civil conflicts affected industry

**Modern Production**:
- Very small global share (<1%)
- Artisanal, traditional methods
- Premium specialty market
- Challenging to source

## Growing Regions

### Haraz Mountains

**Characteristics**:
- Western Yemen highlands
- Altitude: 1,800-2,400m
- Terraced hillsides
- Ancient cultivation methods

**Coffee Character**:
- Complex, wild
- Fruit-forward
- Chocolate undertones
- Unique profile

### Bani Matar

**Characteristics**:
- North of Sana'a
- High altitude
- Extremely dry climate
- Traditional processing

**Reputation**:
- Premium Yemeni coffees
- Distinct terroir
- Sought after by collectors
- Limited availability

### Mattari

**Region**:
- Bani Matar subregion
- Very high altitude
- Specific microclimate

**Coffee**:
- Often considered finest Yemeni
- Complex, chocolatey
- Wine-like acidity
- Berry notes

## Coffee Characteristics

### Flavor Profile

**Typical Notes**:
- Dark chocolate, cocoa
- Red wine, dried fruit
- Spices (cinnamon, cardamom)
- Wild berry
- Earthy complexity

**Body**:
- Medium to full
- Syrupy texture
- Rich mouthfeel
- Heavy, complex

**Acidity**:
- Wine-like
- Moderate to high
- Complex acids
- Fermented quality

**Unique Characteristics**:
- "Wild" flavor profile
- Slightly fermented character
- Unusual complexity
- Love-it-or-hate-it for some

### Processing Method

**Natural/Dry Processing** (Traditional):
- Only method used
- Entire cherry dried
- Dried on rooftops, terraces
- Extended drying (weeks)
- Creates distinctive profile

**Why Natural Only**:
- Extreme water scarcity
- Desert climate advantage
- Traditional knowledge
- Infrastructure limitations

## Varieties

**Heirloom Varieties**:
- Ancient, indigenous cultivars
- Not catalogued scientifically
- Genetic diversity
- Unique to Yemen

**Types**:
- "Mocha" types (not a single variety)
- Small, peaberry-heavy
- Dense beans
- Distinct morphology

## Growing Conditions

### Climate

**Desert Highland**:
- Very dry (200-400mm rainfall)
- High altitude mitigates heat
- Extreme temperature variations
- Minimal irrigation

**Challenges**:
- Water scarcity
- Qat competition (alternative crop)
- Terraced cultivation
- Labor intensive

### Soil

**Characteristics**:
- Arid, mineral-rich
- Terraced plots
- Ancient agricultural land
- Low organic matter

**Terracing**:
- Steep mountainsides
- Hand-built stone terraces
- Centuries-old systems
- Erosion prevention

## Roasting Yemeni Coffee

### Recommendations

**Roast Level**:
- Medium to medium-dark preferred
- Develops chocolate notes
- Preserves fruit complexity
- Don't go too light (underdeveloped)

**Development**:
- Allow full development
- Dense beans need time
- Bring out sweetness
- Balance wild character

**Characteristics**:
- Can be forgiving
- Natural processing tolerates development
- Complex at multiple roast levels

## Brewing Recommendations

### Best Methods

**Pour-Over**:
- V60 or Chemex
- Highlights complexity
- Controls extraction
- Clean cup

**French Press**:
- Enhances body
- Full flavor extraction
- Traditional feel
- Rich cup

**Espresso**:
- Unusual but excellent
- Complex shots
- Chocolate-forward
- Unique experience

**Turkish/Ibrik**:
- Traditional method
- Very appropriate
- Historical connection
- Authentic preparation

### Brewing Parameters

**Temperature**:
- 92-95°C
- Don't go too hot
- Preserve complexity

**Ratio**:
- 1:15 to 1:17
- Medium strength
- Adjust to taste
