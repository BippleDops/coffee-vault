# Rwanda

## Geographic Overview

**Location**: East Africa, Great Lakes region  
**Coordinates**: 1-2°S latitude  
**Capital**: Kigali  
**Coffee Regions**: Southern Province, Western Province, Northern Province

## Coffee History

### Post-Genocide Renaissance

**Historical Context**:
- Coffee introduced by German colonists (1900s)
- Belgian colonial period expanded production
- 1994 genocide devastated industry
- 2000s: Remarkable rebuilding

**Modern Era** (2000-present):
- Quality focus over quantity
- Specialty coffee emergence
- International investment
- Cup of Excellence success

**Transformation**:
- From commodity to specialty
- Washing stations investment
- Farmer cooperatives strengthened
- Global recognition achieved

## Growing Regions

### Southern Province

**Districts**: Huye, Nyamagabe, Nyaruguru

**Characteristics**:
- Highest elevations (1,700-2,100m)
- Volcanic soils (historic volcanoes)
- Consistent rainfall
- Coolest temperatures

**Coffee Profile**:
- Bright, complex acidity
- Tea-like body
- Floral notes
- Citrus brightness

### Western Province

**Districts**: Nyamasheke, Rubavu, Rusizi

**Characteristics**:
- Lake Kivu proximity
- Altitude: 1,500-2,000m
- Consistent microclimate
- Volcanic soils

**Coffee Profile**:
- Balanced acidity
- Medium body
- Fruit-forward
- Clean cup

### Northern Province

**Districts**: Gakenke, Rulindo

**Characteristics**:
- Volcanic soils (Virunga volcanoes)
- High altitude
- Cooler climate
- Unique microclimate

**Coffee Profile**:
- Complex, layered
- Bright acidity
- Floral characteristics
- Distinctive

## Varietals

### Red Bourbon

**Primary Variety**:
- ~95% of production
- Sweet, complex
- Typical Rwandan character
- High quality potential

**Characteristics**:
- Low yield but high quality
- Susceptible to disease
- Requires care
- Premium pricing justified

### Other Varieties (Small %)

- Catuai (limited)
- Caturra (very limited)
- Modern hybrids (minimal)
- Traditional focus maintained

## Growing Conditions

### Climate

**Temperature**:
- 18-22°C average
- High altitude moderates heat
- Cool nights
- Consistent year-round

**Rainfall**:
- 1,200-1,800mm annually
- Two rainy seasons
- Short dry season
- Mostly reliable

### Soil

**Volcanic Origin**:
- Extremely fertile
- Good drainage
- Rich minerals
- Ideal for coffee

**Management**:
- Erosion control (terracing)
- Organic matter addition
- Soil conservation
- Sustainable practices

## Harvest and Processing

### Harvest Season

**Main Harvest**:
- March-July (primary)
- Fly crop: October-December (smaller)
- Selective picking standard
- Multiple passes

**Quality Control**:
- Cherry sorting
- Flotation separation
- Careful processing
- Defect removal

### Processing Method

**Fully Washed Standard**:
1. Cherry sorting and flotation
2. Pulping
3. Fermentation (12-24 hours)
4. Washing
5. Grading (density channels)
6. Drying (raised beds, 14-21 days)
7. Dry milling
8. Sorting and grading

**Quality Emphasis**:
- Multiple sorting stages
- Water quality controlled
- Fermentation monitored
- Drying carefully managed

## Roasting Rwandan Coffee

### Recommendations

**Roast Level**:
- Light to medium preferred
- Preserve acidity and complexity
- Highlight terroir
- Don't overdevelop

**Development**:
- Allow full development
- Watch for sweetness
- Don't rush
- Bourbon benefits from time

**Target**:
- Bring out red fruit notes
- Preserve floral characteristics
- Balance acidity with sweetness
- Clean finish

## Brewing Recommendations

### Best Methods

**Pour-Over**:
- V60, Chemex, Kalita Wave
- Highlights brightness
- Clean cup
- Complexity shines

**Aeropress**:
- Versatile
- Emphasizes sweetness
- Clean brew
- Quick and easy

**Cupping**:
- Shows full character
- Professional standard
- Quality assessment
- Origin expression

### Parameters

**Temperature**:
- 92-95°C
- Higher for brightness
- Lower for sweetness

**Ratio**:
- 1:15 to 1:17
- Medium strength
- Adjust to preference

**Grind**:
- Medium-fine for pour-over
- Medium for immersion
- Avoid too fine (over-extraction)
