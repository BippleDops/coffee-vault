## üìã Overview

Jamaica Blue Mountain is coffee's luxury enigma - a tiny Caribbean origin producing exceptionally mild, balanced, smooth coffee that commands astronomical prices ($50-100+/lb roasted) primarily from Japanese market dominance and legendary reputation, though specialty enthusiasts debate whether quality justifies stratospheric pricing.

**What Makes Blue Mountain Famous**:
- **Legendary Status**: One of world's most expensive coffees
- **Japanese Market**: 80-90% of production exported to Japan (premium pricing)
- **Mild Profile**: Extremely balanced, smooth, mild, low-acid
- **Blue Mountain Region**: Specific geographic designation (protected)
- **Limited Production**: Tiny production (~1,500 metric tons annually)
- **Barrel Aging**: Traditional wooden barrel storage/shipping
- **Certification**: Strict government regulation and certification
- **Celebrity**: Name recognition beyond coffee world

**The Controversy**:
- **Price vs. Quality**: Does cup quality justify $50-100/lb pricing?
- **Specialty View**: Many specialty experts find it overpriced for quality delivered
- **Japanese Premium**: Japan pays massive premium (cultural preference for mildness)
- **Rarity Premium**: Limited supply drives pricing beyond intrinsic quality
- **Balanced Perspective**: Excellent coffee, but price reflects marketing/scarcity not just cup quality

## üó∫Ô∏è The Blue Mountain Region

**Geographic Designation** (Legally Protected):
- **Blue Mountain Peak Parish**: Parishes of Portland, St. Andrew, St. Thomas, St. Mary
- **Altitude Requirement**: 910-1,700 meters (3,000-5,500 feet)
- **Specific Area**: Blue Mountain Range only (other Jamaican coffee not "Blue Mountain")
- **Total Area**: ~2,500 hectares (~6,000 acres) eligible for Blue Mountain designation

**Terroir**:
- **Altitude**: 910-1,700 MASL (1,200-1,500 optimal)
- **Climate**: Cool, misty, high rainfall (2,500+ mm)
- **Soil**: Volcanic, well-draining, mineral-rich
- **Temperature**: 13-24¬∞C (55-75¬∞F)
- **Cloud Cover**: Frequent mist and clouds (slow maturation)
- **Rainfall**: Abundant year-round

**Why Terroir Matters**:
- Cool temperatures, mist, clouds slow cherry maturation
- Slow maturation = denser beans, complex flavors develop
- Volcanic soil provides minerals
- Altitude sufficient for quality

## üí∞ Market & Pricing

**The Price Reality**:
- **Green**: $30-60/lb (standard Blue Mountain)
- **Roasted**: $50-100+/lb ($40-80/12oz)
- **Japan Market**: Pays even higher premiums

**Why So Expensive**:
1. **Limited Production**: Only ~1,500 metric tons annually
2. **Japanese Demand**: Japan buys 80-90% at premium
3. **Rarity**: Scarcity drives pricing
4. **Brand**: "Blue Mountain" legendary name recognition
5. **Certification**: Government regulation, authentication costs
6. **Traditional**: Wooden barrel aging/shipping (expensive)
7. **Marketing**: Centuries of reputation building

**Is It Worth It?**:
- **For Experience**: Try once, interesting
- **For Balance**: If you prize mildness above all, possibly
- **For Value**: No - better coffees at 1/3 the price
- **For Specialty**: Most specialty professionals say overpriced
- **For Japanese Culture**: Cultural appreciation makes sense
- **For Status**: If you want luxury coffee brand recognition

**Specialty Perspective**:
- Good coffee, not great
- Price reflects rarity and marketing, not just quality
- Many specialty coffees exceed quality at lower prices
- Better value exists in Guatemala, Colombia, Ethiopia, Kenya
