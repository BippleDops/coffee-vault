# Costa Rica

## Geographic Overview

**Location**: Central America, between Nicaragua and Panama  
**Coordinates**: 10°N latitude  
**Capital**: San José  
**Coffee Regions**: Tarrazú, West Valley, Central Valley, Brunca, Tres Rios, Orosi, Turrialba, Guanacaste

## Coffee History

### Pioneering Quality

**Early Adoption**:
- Coffee introduced 1779
- First Central American country to industrialize coffee
- Established coffee institute (ICAFE) in 1933
- Quality focus from early days

**Legal Framework**:
- Only Arabica allowed by law (Robusta prohibited since 1988)
- Quality over quantity mandate
- Traceability requirements
- Environmental regulations

### Modern Leadership

**Innovations**:
- Honey processing pioneered
- Micro-mill revolution
- Direct trade models
- Sustainability leadership

### Micro-Mills Revolution

**2000s Development**:
- Small-scale wet mills
- Farm-level processing
- Quality control
- Innovation freedom

**Impact**:
- Honey processing developed
- Experimental methods
- Premium pricing
- Direct trade facilitation

### Honey Processing Birthplace

**Development**:
- Costa Rica pioneered modern honey
- Color classifications (White, Yellow, Red, Black)
- Scientific approach
- Quality standards established

**Categories**:
- **White Honey**: 80-90% mucilage removed
- **Yellow Honey**: 50-75% mucilage
- **Red Honey**: 25-50% mucilage
- **Black Honey**: 0-25% mucilage

**Global Influence**:
- Method spread worldwide
- Costa Rica remains standard
- Innovation continues
- Quality benchmark

## Growing Conditions

### Climate

**Temperature**:
- 17-23°C average (altitude-dependent)
- Consistent year-round
- Cool nights important
- Tropical highland

**Rainfall**:
- 2,000-3,000mm annually
- Distinct dry season (December-April)
- Wet season (May-November)
- Harvest during dry season

### Soil

**Volcanic Origin**:
- Extremely fertile
- Excellent drainage
- Rich minerals
- Perfect for specialty coffee

**Types**:
- Andosols (volcanic)
- High organic matter
- Good CEC
- Nutrient-rich

## Quality Standards

### Classification System

**SHB (Strictly Hard Bean)**:
- Grown above 1,200m
- Highest quality designation
- Dense beans
- Premium category

**GHB (Good Hard Bean)**:
- 1,000-1,200m
- Good quality
- Commercial specialty
- Solid quality

**MHB (Medium Hard Bean)**:
- 500-1,000m
- Lower quality
- Less common for export
- Domestic market

### ICAFE Standards

**Regulation**:
- Quality control by law
- Traceability required
- Defect limits
- Processing standards

## Roasting Costa Rican Coffee

### Approach

**Roast Level**:
- Light to medium optimal
- Preserve acidity
- Develop sweetness
- Don't overdevelop

**Development**:
- Full development important
- Avoid baking
- Highlight terroir
- Balance brightness with body

**Honey Processing Specific**:
- Medium roast showcases best
- Caramel development
- Preserve fruit notes
- Optimal complexity

## Brewing Recommendations

### Best Methods

**Pour-Over**:
- V60, Kalita Wave
- Highlights brightness
- Clean cup
- Origin clarity

**Chemex**:
- Excellent for Costa Rican
- Balanced extraction
- Clean finish
- Showcases quality

**Aeropress**:
- Versatile
- Quick and consistent
- Highlights sweetness

### Parameters

**Temperature**:
- 92-95°C
- Higher for brightness (washed)
- Lower for honey processed

**Ratio**:
- 1:15 to 1:17
- Medium strength standard
- Adjust to taste
