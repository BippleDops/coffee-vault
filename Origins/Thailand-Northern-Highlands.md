## üìã Overview

Thailand's northern highland coffee represents one of Southeast Asia's most exciting emerging specialty origins - high-altitude Arabica from hill tribe communities replacing opium cultivation with coffee, producing balanced, clean, surprisingly excellent coffees that are rapidly gaining international specialty recognition.

**Why Thai Coffee is Emerging**:
- **Opium Replacement**: Coffee as alternative development crop (Royal Project)
- **Hill Tribes**: Akha, Lisu, Lahu communities cultivating coffee
- **High Altitude**: 800-1,500 MASL in northern mountains
- **Innovation**: Early adopter of specialty processing (honey, natural, experimental)
- **Royal Support**: Thai Royal Family-backed development programs
- **Quality Focus**: Chose specialty over commodity from the start
- **Unique Terroir**: Southeast Asian mountain microclimate
- **Underrated**: Exceptional quality, minimal international awareness
- **Growing Recognition**: Competition success, specialty market interest increasing

**Historical Context**:
- **1970s-1980s**: Royal Project initiates coffee as opium replacement
- **Doi Chaang**: Famous hill tribe cooperative established
- **2000s**: Specialty processing innovation begins
- **2010s**: International specialty buyers discover Thai coffee
- **Present**: Small but growing specialty presence globally

**The Royal Project**:
- Initiated by King Bhumibol Adulyadej
- Replace opium cultivation with sustainable crops
- Coffee primary alternative crop
- Technical support, infrastructure, market access
- Social development, community transformation
- Success model for alternative development

## üå± Varieties

**Typica** (Traditional):
- Introduced first, still grown
- Good quality, low yield
- Hill tribe favorite (simple, reliable)

**Catimor** (Common):
- Productive, rust-resistant
- Moderate quality
- Workhorse variety

**SL-28** (Emerging Specialty):
- **Innovation**: Thai farms planting Kenyan variety
- Bright, complex when grown high
- Limited but growing

**Geisha** (Experimental):
- Some farms trialing Geisha
- Very limited production
- High-altitude experiments

## üîÑ Processing Innovation

**Washed** (Primary):
- Clean, balanced, reliable
- Hill tribe cooperatives have washing stations

**Honey Process** (Growing):
- **Innovation**: Thai producers early adopters in Asia
- Multiple honey levels (yellow, red, black)
- Excellent results, competition-winning

**Natural** (Increasing):
- Dry season suitable for natural processing
- Berry-forward, sweet profiles

**Experimental**:
- **Anaerobic fermentation**: Some progressive farms
- **Carbonic maceration**: Limited trials
- **Innovation-Focused**: Thai producers willing to experiment
