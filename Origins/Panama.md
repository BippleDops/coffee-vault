# Panama

## Geographic Overview

**Location**: Central America, connecting North and South America  
**Coordinates**: 8-9°N latitude  
**Capital**: Panama City  
**Coffee-Growing Regions**: Boquete, Volcan, Renacimiento

## Coffee Growing Regions

### Boquete

**Characteristics**:
- Most famous Panamanian region
- Altitude: 1,400-1,900m
- Home of Gesha cultivation
- Volcanic soils
- Pacific slope

**Climate**:
- Distinct dry season (December-April)
- Cool temperatures
- Cloud cover afternoons
- Optimal for slow ripening

**Reputation**:
- World's most expensive coffees
- Gesha variety homeland (cultivated)
- Best of Panama competition origin
- Ultra-premium specialty

### Volcan-Candela

**Characteristics**:
- Volcan Baru slopes
- Altitude: 1,200-1,800m
- Volcanic soils
- Diverse microclimates

**Production**:
- Mix of varieties
- Gesha and traditional varieties
- Growing specialty focus
- Smaller farms

### Renacimiento

**Characteristics**:
- Western highlands
- Altitude: 1,100-1,600m
- Less famous than Boquete
- Emerging quality region

## Coffee Characteristics

### Typical Flavor Profile

**Gesha from Panama**:
- Exceptional floral notes (jasmine, bergamot)
- Tea-like, delicate body
- Complex fruit (tropical, citrus)
- Bright, elegant acidity
- Clean, refined cup

**Traditional Varieties**:
- Balanced, clean
- Citrus, caramel
- Medium body
- Good acidity
- Consistent quality

### Processing Methods

**Washed** (Dominant):
- Standard for premium coffees
- Highlights varietal character
- Clean, bright cups
- Origin transparency

**Natural** (Increasing):
- Experimental lots
- Fruit-forward
- Premium pricing
- Unique offerings

**Honey** (Growing):
- Sweetness focus
- Caramel notes
- Market differentiation

## Gesha/Geisha Story

### Introduction to Panama

**Timeline**:
- 1960s: Gesha seeds brought from Ethiopia
- Planted in Panama (Don Pachi estate)
- Initially overlooked
- Low yield, unusual cup

### Discovery

**2004 Breakthrough**:
- Hacienda La Esmeralda
- Best of Panama competition
- Extraordinary cup scores
- Record prices

**Impact**:
- Global Gesha phenomenon
- Panama specialty coffee boom
- Gesha planted worldwide
- Ultra-premium market created

### Current Status

**Production**:
- Limited acreage
- Extremely high prices ($100+/lb common)
- Auction lots reach $1,000+/lb
- Speculation and investment

**Quality**:
- Not all Panamanian Gesha equal
- Terroir matters enormously
- Specific farms excel
- Altitude and microclimate critical

## Production Statistics

## Growing Conditions

### Climate

**Temperature**:
- High-altitude: 15-24°C
- Cool nights essential
- Dry season distinct
- Cloud forest influence

**Rainfall**:
- Annual: 2,000-3,500mm
- Wet season: May-November
- Dry season harvest
- Consistent patterns

### Soil

**Volcanic Origin**:
- Nutrient-rich
- Excellent drainage
- High fertility
- Ideal for specialty coffee

**Mineral Content**:
- Volcanic minerals
- Good CEC
- Supports complex flavors
- Terroir expression

## Practical Applications

### For Buyers

**What to Look For**:
- Specific farm identification
- Altitude information
- Processing detail
- Varietal specification (especially Gesha)
- Competition lots

**Pricing Expectations**:
- Gesha: Premium to ultra-premium
- Traditional varieties: Specialty pricing
- Competition lots: Auction prices
- Direct trade common

### For Roasters

**Roasting Panama Coffee**:
- Light roast preferred (preserve delicacy)
- Careful development
- Don't over-roast Gesha
- Highlight floral and fruit notes

**Marketing**:
- Gesha is a selling point
- Farm stories important
- Transparent sourcing
- Premium positioning

### For Consumers

**Trying Panama Coffee**:
- Gesha: Unique experience, expensive
- Worth trying once
- Appreciate terroir
- Light roast, pour-over recommended
