## üìã Overview

Uganda Bugisu (also spelled Bugishu) is East African specialty coffee's hidden gem - high-altitude Arabica from Mount Elgon's volcanic slopes producing bright, wine-like, complex coffees similar to Kenya and Ethiopia but dramatically undervalued and overlooked in global specialty market.

**Why Bugisu Deserves Recognition**:
- **Mount Elgon**: Extinct volcano, rich volcanic soil, high altitude
- **SL Varieties**: SL-14, SL-28 (same genetics as Kenya's famous SL-28)
- **East African CharacterMenuBright, citrus, wine-like, complex (similar to Kenya)
- **Washed Processing**: Clean, bright, well-processed
- **UndervaluedMenu 50-60% cheaper than Kenyan coffee despite similar quality
- **High Altitude**: Up to 2,300 MASL (extreme altitude)
- **Organic**: Significant organic production

**Challenges**:
- **Infrastructure**: Limited processing facilities
- **Political**: Instability affects investment
- **Marketing**: Minimal specialty market presence
- **Volume**: Small Arabica production (Robusta dominant nationally)
- **RecognitionMenuSeverely underrated globally

**Opportunity**:
- **Best Value East African**: Kenya-like quality at 40-50% lower price
- **Improving**: Quality focus increasing
- **Direct Trade**: Growing relationships
- **PotentialMenuCould rival Kenya/Tanzania with investment
