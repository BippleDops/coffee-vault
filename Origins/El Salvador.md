# El Salvador

## Overview

**Location**: Central America, smallest country in region
**Regions**: Apaneca-Ilamatepec, Alotepec-Metapán, El Bálsamo-Quetzaltepec, Cacahuatique, Chichontepec, Tecapa-Chinameca
**Known For**: Bourbon, Pacamara variety, innovation

## Coffee Characteristics

**Flavor Profile**:
- Balanced sweetness
- Chocolate, caramel
- Mild, pleasant acidity
- Medium to full body
- Clean, consistent

**Pacamara Specialty**:
- Large beans
- Complex, unusual flavors
- Fruit-forward
- Unique character
- Premium pricing

## Varietals

**Bourbon**: Primary variety
**Pacamara**: Salvadoran specialty
- Pacas × Maragogipe hybrid
- Created in El Salvador
- Large bean size
- Complex cup
- National pride

**Others**:
- Pacas (Bourbon mutation)
- Tekisic
- Some modern hybrids

## Growing Conditions

**Altitude**: 1,200-2,000m
**Volcanic Soils**: Extremely fertile
**Climate**: Tropical highland, distinct seasons

## Regional Coffees

### Apaneca-Ilamatepec

**Western highlands**:
- Volcanic soils (Santa Ana volcano)
- High altitude
- Complex, balanced
- Premium quality

### Alotepec-Metapán

**Northern region**:
- High mountains
- Bright, fruity
- Specialty focus

## Processing

**Washed** (Traditional): Standard
**Honey** (Increasing): Innovation
**Natural** (Growing): Experimental lots

## Civil War Impact and Recovery

**1980-1992**: Civil war devastated industry
**Recovery**: Slow but steady
**Modern**: Focus on quality and specialty
**Resilience**: Rebuilt specialty reputation

## Sustainability

**Shade-Grown**: Common practice
**Certifications**: Fair Trade, Organic, Rainforest Alliance
**Environmental**: Biodiversity emphasis

## Market Position

**Pricing**: $15-25/lb (specialty), Pacamara higher
**Reputation**: Quality improving
**Availability**: Limited but growing

## Practical Applications

**For Buyers**: Try Pacamara
**For Roasters**: Medium roast ideal
**For Consumers**: Balanced, approachable
