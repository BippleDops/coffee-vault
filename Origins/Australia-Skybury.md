## üìã Overview

Australian coffee is specialty coffee's expensive curiosity - boutique micro-production from Queensland's Atherton Tablelands delivering balanced, clean coffee at luxury pricing ($40-80/lb roasted) reflecting Australian labor costs, tourist appeal, and rarity rather than exceptional cup quality. Skybury Estate is most famous, representing Australian coffee ambition and agricultural innovation.

**Why Australian Coffee Exists**:
- **Skybury Estate**: Largest, most famous (established 1980s)
- **Boutique**: Multiple small specialty farms
- **Innovation**: Modern processing, quality focus from inception
- **Australian Labor**: Highest labor costs globally = expensive coffee
- **Tourism**: Atherton Tablelands tourist destination
- **Local Market**: Australian domestic consumption
- **Novelty**: Geographic extremity, Southern Hemisphere specialty

**Reality Check**:
- **Quality**: Good (comparable to $15-20/12oz coffees globally)
- **Price**: $40-80/12oz (tourism, labor, novelty premium)
- **Production**: Tiny (500-1,000 bags annually across all farms)
- **Value**: Debatable (supporting Australian agriculture vs. pure quality-price)
- **Availability**: Almost impossible to find outside Australia
