**Region**: East Africa  
**Global Rank**: ~17th-19th largest producer  
**Annual Production**: 800,000-1,000,000 bags = ~48,000-60,000 metric tons

## üìã Overview

Tanzania produces exceptional specialty coffee that rivals neighboring Kenya in complexity and brightness, yet remains relatively unknown and undervalued in the global market. With similar terroir (volcanic soil, high altitude, East African genetics), Tanzanian coffee delivers bright berry and citrus notes at significantly lower prices than Kenyan counterparts, making it one of specialty coffee's best-kept secrets.

**Why Tanzania Deserves Attention**:
- **Similar to Kenya**: Same volcanic terroir, altitude, processing methods
- **Lower Prices**: 30-50% cheaper than comparable Kenyan coffee
- **Quality**: 85-90+ cup scores achievable, competition-level
- **Bright Acidity**: Berry, citrus, wine-like (East African signature)
- **Clean Cup**: Washed processing excellence
- **Mount Kilimanjaro**: Famous coffee from Africa's highest mountain slopes
- **Peaberry**: Significant peaberry production (round beans, concentrated flavor)
- **Underrated**: Quality exceeds recognition and pricing

**Historical Background**:
- **1890s**: Coffee introduced by German colonists
- **1900s-1920s**: Expansion under German then British colonial rule
- **1920s**: Kent variety introduced from India (disease resistance)
- **Post-Independence** (1961): Government control, cooperatives dominate
- **1990s**: Liberalization, quality focus begins
- **2000s-Present**: Specialty emergence, still undervalued
- **Challenge**: Political instability, infrastructure limits growth

## üó∫Ô∏è Growing Regions

### Primary Regions

**Northern Tanzania** (60% of Arabica):

**Kilimanjaro/Moshi Region**:
- **Altitude**: 1,400-2,000 MASL
- **Geography**: Slopes of Mount Kilimanjaro
- **Soil**: Rich volcanic, fertile
- **Flavor**: Berry, citrus, wine-like, bright, complex
- **Recognition**: Most famous Tanzanian coffee
- **Farms**: Small estates and smallholders

**Arusha/Meru Region**:
- **Altitude**: 1,200-1,900 MASL
- **Geography**: Mount Meru volcanic slopes
- **Soil**: Volcanic, mineral-rich
- **Flavor**: Bright, fruity, floral, clean
- **Quality**: Excellent, comparable to Kilimanjaro
- **Production**: Mixed estates and cooperatives

**Southern Highlands** (30% of Arabica):

**Mbeya Region**:
- **Altitude**: 1,200-2,100 MASL
- **Geography**: Southern highlands plateau
- **Flavor**: Complex, balanced, sweet, citrus-berry
- **Quality**: Excellent, emerging specialty recognition
- **Growing**: Increasing quality focus

**Ruvuma Region**:
- **Altitude**: 1,200-1,800 MASL
- **Character**: Balanced, fruity, clean
- **Status**: Smaller production, good quality

**Western Tanzania** (10% of Arabica):
- **Bukoba** (Lake Victoria region)
- **Robusta dominant**, some Arabica
- Lower quality generally

## üå± Varieties

**Bourbon** (Primary Quality Variety):
- **N39**: Bourbon selection, dominant in Tanzania
- Excellent cup quality, sweet, complex
- Susceptible to diseases

**Kent** (Unique to Tanzania in East Africa):
- **Introduced 1920s** from India
- Selected for leaf rust resistance
- **Quality**: Good, slightly less complex than Bourbon
- Reliable, productive

**Typica**:
- Traditional, declining
- Excellent quality, low yield
- Heritage farms only

**Modern Hybrids**:
- **KP423**: Tanzania-bred hybrid
- Rust-resistant but lower quality
- Increasing adoption

## ‚òï Flavor Profile

**Typical Tanzanian (Washed)**:
- **Berry**: Blackcurrant, blueberry, raspberry (Kenyan-like)
- **Citrus**: Grapefruit, orange, lemon (bright)
- **Wine**: Red wine, grape, wine-like complexity
- **Floral**: Jasmine, delicate florals (less intense than Kenya)
- **Body**: Medium-full (Fuller than Kenya)
- **Acidity**: Very High (Slightly less than Kenya)
- **Sweetness**: High, balances acidity
- **Complexity**: Very high, layered

**Comparison to Kenya**:
- **Similar**: Berry, brightness, complexity
- **Fuller Body**: Slightly heavier mouthfeel
- **Sweeter**: More pronounced sweetness
- **Softer Acidity**: Bright but less electric than Kenya
- **More Balanced**: Less extreme, more approachable
- **Better Value**: 30-50% cheaper for comparable quality
