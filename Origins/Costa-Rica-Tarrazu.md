## ğŸ”„ Processing Methods

### Honey Process - TarrazÃº's Signature

**Development**:
- Costa Rica pioneered modern honey processing
- TarrazÃº micro-mills leaders in innovation
- 2000s-2010s: Rapid adoption

**Method**:
- Cherries pulped, mucilage retained (partially or fully)
- Dried on patios or raised beds
- Mucilage sugars ferment during drying
- Creates "honey" sweetness and complexity

**Honey Categories**:

**White Honey**:
- 80-90% mucilage removed
- Lightest processing
- Closest to washed
- 7-10 days drying

**Yellow Honey**:
- 50-75% mucilage removed
- Moderate sweetness enhancement
- 10-12 days drying
- Balanced profile

**Red Honey**:
- 25-50% mucilage removed
- Significant sweetness
- 12-15 days drying
- Fuller body, fruit notes

**Black Honey**:
- 0-25% mucilage removed
- Maximum sweetness and body
- 15-20 days drying
- Most labor-intensive
- Closest to natural but cleaner

**Results**:
- **Flavor**: Enhanced sweetness, fruit notes, complexity
- **Body**: Fuller than washed, lighter than natural
- **Acidity**: Rounded, fruit-driven
- **Sweetness**: Honey, caramel, fruit sweetness
- **Cupping Scores**: 84-90 points (well-executed)

**Management Critical**:
- Drying speed control
- Even drying (constant turning)
- Weather monitoring
- Mold prevention

### Traditional Washed Process

**Prevalence**: 30% (declining in favor of honey)

**Process**:
- Standard Central American washed
- Pulping â†’ fermentation (12-36 hours) â†’ washing â†’ drying
- Solar drying on patios or raised beds

**Results**:

### Natural Process

**Prevalence**: 20% (increasing)

**Process**:
