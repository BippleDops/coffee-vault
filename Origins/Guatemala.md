## üìç Geography & Climate

### Location

Guatemala is located in Central America, bordered by Mexico, Belize, Honduras, and El Salvador. The country features dramatic volcanic highlands, creating exceptional coffee terroir.

**Key Geographic Features**:
- 37 volcanoes (3 active)
- Mountain ranges throughout
- Pacific and Caribbean coasts
- Diverse microclimates in small area
- Volcanic soil across coffee regions

### Growing Conditions

**Altitude Range**: 1,300 - 2,000 meters above sea level (MASL)
- Premium coffees: 1,500-2,000 MASL
- Strictly Hard Bean (SHB): Highest grade, 1,400+ MASL
- Hard Bean (HB): 1,200-1,400 MASL

**Climate**:
- **Temperature**: 16-32¬∞C (60-90¬∞F) varying by altitude
- **Rainfall**: 1,000-3,000mm annually (region dependent)
- **Dry Season**: November-April (harvest period)
- **Rainy Season**: May-October
- **Microclimates**: Dramatic variation across regions

**Soil**: 
- Volcanic (rich in minerals)
- Metamorphic (in non-volcanic areas)
- Limestone-based in some regions
- Excellent drainage
- High organic content

## üó∫Ô∏è Eight Coffee Regions

### Antigua Strict Hard Bean (SHB)

**Altitude**: 1,500-1,700 MASL  
**Volcanoes**: Agua, Fuego, Acatenango (three surrounding volcanoes)  
**Flavor**: Chocolate, spice, smoke, balanced acidity, full body  
**Character**: Complex, elegant, refined

**Notes**: Most famous Guatemalan region. Colonial-era city (UNESCO World Heritage). Volcanic soil + consistent microclimate = distinctive profile. "Antigua" alone on bag is premium indicator.

**Unique**: Regular volcanic ash deposits enrich soil, subtle smoke notes in coffee

### Huehuetenango (way-way-TEN-ango)

**Altitude**: 1,500-2,000 MASL (highest in Guatemala)  
**Mountains**: Cuchumatanes range (non-volcanic)  
**Flavor**: Wine-like, fruit-forward, complex acidity, floral, citrus  
**Character**: Bright, clean, distinctive, complex

**Notes**: Far from volcanoes, different terroir. Dry, hot winds from Mexico protect from frost. Exceptional cup quality, competes with Antigua for prestige.

**Indigenous**: Primarily Mayan indigenous farmers, strong cultural heritage

### Atitl√°n

**Altitude**: 1,500-1,700 MASL  
**Location**: Shores of Lake Atitl√°n, volcanic region  
**Flavor**: Floral, citrus, chocolate, balanced  
**Character**: Medium-full body, bright but not overwhelming acidity

**Notes**: Stunning lake surrounded by volcanoes. Unique microclimate from lake effect. Small production, high quality. Tourist region (cafes showcase local coffee).

### Cob√°n (Rainforest Coffee)

**Altitude**: 1,300-1,500 MASL  
**Climate**: Rainforest, very high rainfall (year-round clouds)  
**Flavor**: Fruity, wine-like, unique character  
**Character**: Distinctive, can be inconsistent

**Notes**: Only Guatemalan region with year-round rain. Cloudy, humid. Different processing challenges (drying difficult). Unique flavor profile unlike other regions.

### Fraijanes

**Altitude**: 1,400-1,800 MASL  
**Volcanoes**: Pacaya (active), others nearby  
**Flavor**: Bright acidity, full body, citrus, chocolate  
**Character**: Bold, lively, distinctive smoke notes

**Notes**: Active volcano Pacaya deposits fresh ash frequently. Pumice-rich soil (excellent drainage). Small region, high quality.

### San Marcos

**Altitude**: 1,300-1,800 MASL  
**Climate**: Wettest region, earliest harvest  
**Flavor**: Floral, citrus, acidity, delicate  
**Character**: Bright, clean

**Notes**: Warmest, wettest region. First to harvest (December-March). Smallest production of eight regions.

### Nuevo Oriente

**Altitude**: 1,300-1,700 MASL  
**Terrain**: Metamorphic rock (non-volcanic)  
**Flavor**: Chocolate, balanced, moderate acidity  
**Character**: Reliable, consistent

**Notes**: Newest official region. Different soil type. Growing reputation. Good value.

### Acatenango Valley

**Altitude**: 1,500-2,000 MASL  
**Volcano**: Acatenango and Fuego  
**Flavor**: Complex, floral, citrus, full body, bright  
**Character**: Elegant, refined

**Notes**: Between two volcanoes. Challenging terrain (steep slopes). Exceptional quality potential.

## üå± Coffee Varietals

### Bourbon

**Most Common**: 60% of Guatemalan coffee  
**Origin**: Bourbon variety from Yemen/R√©union  
**Characteristics**: Sweet, balanced, complex, full body  
**Flavor**: Chocolate, caramel, fruit, balanced acidity  
**Yield**: Lower than modern varieties, but excellent quality

### Caturra

**Percentage**: ~25% of production  
**Origin**: Bourbon mutation (compact)  
**Characteristics**: Higher yield than Bourbon, good quality  
**Flavor**: Bright, clean, sweet

### Catuai

**Origin**: Caturra x Mundo Novo hybrid  
**Characteristics**: Compact, productive, wind-resistant  
**Flavor**: Good quality, clean, balanced

### Typica

**Status**: Less common, declining  
**Characteristics**: Traditional variety, excellent quality, low yield  
**Flavor**: Complex, sweet, delicate

### Gesha/Geisha

**Status**: Experimental, very rare in Guatemala  
**Farms**: Few high-end estates experimenting  
**Prices**: $50-200+/lb when available  
**Flavor**: Floral, jasmine, bergamot, tea-like (if true Gesha)

## üîÑ Processing Methods

### Washed (Fully Washed) - Dominant

**Process** (Guatemala's meticulous approach):
1. Selective picking (ripe cherries only)
2. Pulping within hours of harvest
3. Fermentation 12-48 hours (varies by altitude/temperature)
4. Washing in channels (removes all mucilage)
5. Drying on patios or raised beds (10-20 days)
6. **Key**: Multiple water washes ensure cleanliness

**Result**: Clean, bright, chocolate-forward with good acidity

**Why Dominant**: Tradition, infrastructure, market preference, showcases terroir

### Honey Processing (Growing)

**Status**: Increasing experimentation, especially premium farms  
**Process**: Pulped, some mucilage retained, dried  
**Result**: More body, fruit-forward, sweeter than fully washed  
**Market**: Specialty buyers seek for variety

### Natural (Rare)

**Status**: Uncommon due to humidity challenges  
**Process**: Dried whole (cherry with fruit)  
**Result**: Fruit-forward, wine-like, full body  
**Availability**: Limited, mostly micro-lots

## üåü Flavor Profile Summary

### Classic Guatemalan Profile

**Washed Guatemalan SHB**:
- **Aroma**: Chocolate, spice, floral, smoke (if Antigua)
- **Flavor**: Dark chocolate, cocoa, spice, citrus, apple, caramel
- **Body**: Full, round, syrupy
- **Acidity**: Bright but balanced (less than Kenyan, more than Brazilian)
- **Finish**: Long, sweet, chocolate lingering

**Regional Variations**:
- **Antigua**: Chocolate, smoke, spice, elegant
- **Huehuetenango**: Bright, fruity, wine-like, complex
- **Atitl√°n**: Floral, chocolate, balanced
- **Others**: Variations on chocolate-forward theme

### What Makes Guatemalan Distinctive

1. **Chocolate Focus**: More pronounced than most origins
2. **Spice Notes**: Cinnamon, nutmeg, clove common
3. **Full Body**: Heavier than most Central Americans
4. **Balance**: Acidity present but not overwhelming
5. **Versatility**: Works in almost any brewing method

**Compared to Neighbors**:
- **vs Costa Rica**: Guatemala has more body, less bright
- **vs Honduras**: Guatemala more refined, complex
- **vs El Salvador**: Similar but Guatemala more famous

## üí° Buying Tips

**What to Look For**:
- **Region**: Antigua, Huehuetenango most prestigious
- **Grade**: SHB (Strictly Hard Bean) = 1,400+ MASL
- **Varietal**: Bourbon preferred, Caturra/Catuai good
- **Processing**: Washed standard, honey interesting
- **Farm/Coop**: Look for specific producer names

**Roast Level**:
- Light-medium to medium (highlights origin)
- Medium showcases chocolate notes beautifully
- Dark roasts OK (chocolate profile survives) but loses complexity

**Brewing Methods**:
- **Drip/Pour-over**: Excellent, balanced cup
- **Espresso**: Outstanding - chocolate shines, not too acidic
- **French Press**: Very good - body comes through
- **Chemex**: Good but may be light on body
- **Moka Pot**: Great for chocolate-forward profile

**Price Points**:
- **Good Guatemalan**: $14-18/12oz
- **Premium (Antigua, Huehue)**: $18-22/12oz
- **Estate micro-lots**: $22-30/12oz
- **Competition/Gesha**: $30-100+/12oz

**Value**: Guatemalan offers excellent quality-to-price ratio
