## üìã Overview

Huila is Colombia's largest and most celebrated specialty coffee region - southern Andean department producing balanced, sweet, complex coffees that define modern Colombian specialty and win international competitions regularly. With diverse microclimates, altitude range from 1,200-2,100 MASL, and innovation in processing, Huila represents Colombian coffee at its finest.

**Why Huila is Colombia's Star Region**:
- **Largest Department**: #1 Colombian coffee producer by volume
- **Quality Leader**: Disproportionate specialty and competition representation
- **Altitude Diversity**: 1,200-2,100 MASL (quality range)
- **Cup of Excellence**: Huila lots dominate Colombian CoE (40-60% of winners)
- **Processing Innovation**: Early adopter honey, natural, anaerobic
- **Pink Bourbon**: Epicenter of Pink Bourbon cultivation
- **Gesha Trials**: Experimental Gesha plantings (high altitude suitable)
- **Balanced Profile**: Chocolate-caramel-fruit, broadly appealing
- **Sweetness**: Exceptional sweetness signature

**Geography & Terroir**:
- **Location**: Southern Colombia, Andean mountains
- **Magdalena River**: Runs through department, watershed
- **Volcanic**: Some volcanic influence, mineral-rich soils
- **Mountains**: Steep slopes, high altitudes
- **Microclimates**: Extreme diversity in small area
- **Two Harvests**: Main (October-January), mitaca (April-June)
