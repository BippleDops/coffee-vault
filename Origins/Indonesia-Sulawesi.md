## üìã Overview

Sulawesi (formerly Celebes) produces Indonesian coffee's most refined expression - wet-hulled and washed coffees from high-altitude Tor aja region delivering earthy-herbal character with chocolate-spice complexity. Less extreme than Sumatra but maintaining Indonesian full-bodied signature, Sulawesi coffee (especially Toraja) represents Indonesian specialty at its most balanced.

**What Makes Sulawesi Unique**:
- **Toraja Region**: Famous high-altitude coffee (Tana Toraja Highlands)
- **Higher Altitude**: 1,000-1,800 MASL (higher than most Sumatra)
- **Balanced Indonesian**: Less extreme earthiness than Sumatra
- **Chocolate-Spice**: Sweet chocolate, spice notes prominent
- **Cultural Heritage**: Toraja people's funeral ceremonies and coffee culture
- **Wet-Hulled + Washed**: Both processing methods (Sumatra mostly wet-hulled only)
- **Smoother**: More approachable than Sumatra for non-Indonesian coffee fans

**Historical Context**:
- **Dutch Colonial**: Coffee introduced 1600s-1700s
- **Toraja Isolation**: Remote highlands preserved traditional methods
- **"Celebes"**: Former island name under Dutch (coffee sold as "Celebes")
- **Independence**: Name changed to Sulawesi (1945+)
- **Specialty**: Toraja coffee gained specialty recognition 1970s-1980s
- **Present**: Small but valued specialty origin

## üîÑ Processing

**Wet-Hulled** (Giling Basah) - 60-70%:
- Similar to Sumatra
- Creates earthy-herbal character
- Full body, low acidity

**Washed** (30-40%, More than Sumatra):
- Higher altitude enables better washed processing
- Cleaner, brighter, more chocolate-forward
- Less extreme earthiness

**Result**: Sulawesi offers both profiles (choice vs. Sumatra's wet-hulled dominance)

## ‚òï Flavor Profile

**Wet-Hulled Sulawesi**:
- **Earthy**: Forest floor, earth, mushroom (milder than Sumatra)
- **Herbal**: Herbs, sage, eucalyptus
- **Chocolate**: Dark chocolate, cocoa, sweet chocolate
- **Spice**: Black pepper, clove, cardamom, cinnamon
- **Body**: Full to Very Full - Syrupy, coating
- **Acidity**: Very Low - Smooth, gentle
- **Sweetness**: Moderate - Earthy-chocolate sweetness
- **Complexity**: Moderate - Layered earthiness, spice

**Washed Sulawesi**:
- **Chocolate**: Milk chocolate, sweet chocolate, cocoa
- **Spice**: Sweet spice, cinnamon, clove
- **Nuts**: Roasted nuts, walnut
- **Caramel**: Brown sugar, toffee
- **Earth**: Subtle, background (much less than wet-hulled)
- **Body**: Medium-Full - Smooth, creamy
- **Acidity**: Low-Medium - Balanced, gentle
- **Sweetness**: High - Chocolate-caramel, clean
- **Complexity**: Moderate-High - Cleaner, more nuanced

**Comparison**:
- **Milder than Sumatra**: Less intense earthiness
- **More Balanced**: Between Sumatra extreme and Central American brightness
- **Chocolate-Forward**: Both processes emphasize chocolate
- **Approachable**: More accessible than Sumatra for many palates
