## üìã Overview

Cerrado Mineiro is Brazil's first and most famous designated coffee region - vast plateau in western Minas Gerais with Protected Designation of Origin status, producing clean, sweet, balanced coffees through mechanized farming and consistent processing that make it ideal for blending and reliable specialty. Cerrado represents Brazilian coffee's industrialized quality: scale meeting standards.

**Why Cerrado Mineiro Matters**:
- **First Brazilian PDO** (2005): Protected geographic indication, quality standards
- **Massive Production**: 4-6 million bags (20-25% of Brazil's coffee)
- **Mechanization**: Flat terrain enables mechanical harvesting (efficiency)
- **Consistency**: Predictable climate creates reliable quality year-over-year
- **Clean Sweet Profile**: Chocolate-nut-caramel, balanced, versatile
- **Blending**: Perfect for espresso blends (consistency + sweetness)
- **Specialty Grade**: Achieves 82-86 points despite mechanization

**Geographic Advantages**:
- **Flat Plateau**: Cerrado (savanna) plateau, mechanization-friendly terrain
- **Predictable Climate**: Distinct wet/dry seasons, reliable rainfall pattern
- **Perfect Harvest**: Dry season harvest (May-September), ideal for natural processing
- **Soil**: Red-yellow latosol, iron-rich, well-draining
- **Altitude**: 800-1,300 MASL (moderate, consistent)
