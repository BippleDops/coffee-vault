## üìã Overview

Mexico Chiapas Bourbon Organic Washed represents traditional Mexican coffee at its finest. Chiapas, Mexico's southernmost state bordering Guatemala, produces the country's highest-quality specialty coffee. Traditional Bourbon variety, organic certification, and washed processing create a classic, balanced, approachable cup.

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Chiapas origin**: Mexico's premier coffee region
- **Bourbon variety**: Traditional quality variety
- **Organic certification**: Mexico is major organic coffee producer
- **Washed processing**: Clean, balanced, traditional
- **Chocolate-nutty flavor**: Classic Central American profile
- **Medium body**: Smooth, balanced mouthfeel
- **Medium acidity**: Balanced, not overly bright
- **Altura grade**: High-altitude designation
- **Fair Trade common**: Cooperative production
- **Excellent value**: Quality at accessible pricing

Chiapas shares borders and terroir similarities with Guatemala's renowned coffee regions. The mountainous terrain, volcanic-influenced soils, shade-grown tradition, and indigenous coffee-growing culture create ideal conditions for high-quality coffee. Mexico's proximity to the US makes it an accessible specialty origin with strong organic and Fair Trade movements.

**Historical Significance**: Coffee came to Mexico in the late 1700s, with Chiapas becoming a major producer by the 1800s. Indigenous communities and small farmers have maintained traditional cultivation practices. The organic coffee movement found strong adoption in Chiapas in the 1990s-2000s, with Mexico becoming one of the world's largest organic coffee exporters.

**Misconception Alert**: Mexican coffee is often overlooked in favor of Central American neighbors (Guatemala, Honduras). However, Chiapas Altura coffee rivals Guatemalan and Honduran quality while offering excellent value. This is high-quality specialty coffee, not commodity Mexican coffee.

- Soil health focus

### Harvest

- **Season**: December-March
- **Selective picking**: 3-5 passes
- **Ripe only**: Quality focus
- **Cooperative collection**: Same-day to wet mill

### Washed Processing

**Traditional method**:
1. **Reception**: Flotation, visual sorting
2. **Depulping**: Mechanical, skin/pulp removal
3. **Fermentation**: 12-24 hours, dry method common
4. **Washing**: Running water channels
5. **Drying**: Sun-drying (patios or raised beds), 10-15 days
6. **Target**: 10-12% moisture

**Cooperative Processing**:
- Shared wet mills
- Quality standards
- Controlled fermentation
- Clean water (mountain streams)

**Grading**:
- **Altura**: "Height" grade, high-altitude (1,300+MASL)
- **Screen sorting**: Size uniformity
- **Defect removal**: Hand-sorting passes
- **Organic verification**: Documented, certified
