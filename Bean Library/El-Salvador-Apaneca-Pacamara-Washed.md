## üìã Overview

El Salvador Apaneca Pacamara Washed represents the pinnacle of Salvadoran coffee innovation. Pacamara, a hybrid variety created in El Salvador in the 1950s, has become the country's signature specialty coffee. Combined with Apaneca-Ilamatepec's volcanic terroir and washed processing, this creates one of the world's most unique and sought-after coffee profiles.

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Pacamara variety**: Pacas √ó Maragogype hybrid (Salvadoran creation)
- **Large bean size**: Screen 18-20+ (elephant bean genetics)
- **Floral complexity**: Jasmine, orange blossom, perfume-like aromatics
- **Butter body**: Unique creamy, buttery mouthfeel (Pacamara signature)
- **Complex acidity**: Bright, structured, wine-like
- **Tropical fruit**: Mango, papaya, passion fruit
- **Citrus brightness**: Orange, tangerine, bergamot
- **Apaneca terroir**: Santa Ana volcanic soils, high altitude
- **Washed processing**: Clarity and complexity
- **Competition winner**: Cup of Excellence regular top scorer

Apaneca-Ilamatepec, named for the Santa Ana Volcano (Ilamatepec in indigenous language), is El Salvador's premier coffee region. The volcanic slopes, high altitude (1,400-1,900 MASL), and ideal microclimate create perfect conditions for Pacamara. This region's commitment to quality and variety innovation has made Salvadoran Pacamara world-renowned.

**Historical Significance**: Pacamara was developed at El Salvador's coffee research institute (ISIC) in 1958, crossing Pacas (Salvadoran Bourbon mutation) with Maragogype (elephant bean). The goal: combine large bean novelty with improved productivity and quality. The result exceeded expectations‚ÄîPacamara became El Salvador's signature variety and one of specialty coffee's most prized offerings.

**Misconception Alert**: Large beans don't automatically mean better coffee. However, Pacamara's specific genetics create genuinely unique cup quality‚Äîthe buttery body and floral complexity are intrinsic to the variety, not just marketing. This is one case where the hybrid breeding created something exceptional.

## üå± Variety: Pacamara

### Development

**Creation**: El Salvador, 1958, ISIC (Salvadoran Coffee Research Institute)

**Parents**:
- **Pacas**: Bourbon dwarf mutation (El Salvador, 1949)
- **Maragogype**: Elephant bean (Typica mutation, Brazil, 1870s)

**Goal**: Combine:
- Pacas: Compact, productive, quality
- Maragogype: Large bean, unique character

**Result**: Pacamara
- Large bean (screen 18-20+)
- Improved productivity vs. Maragogype
- Exceptional cup quality (exceeded expectations)
- Unique buttery body and floral complexity
- Became Salvadoran specialty signature

### Characteristics

**Tree**:
- **Height**: 2-3 meters (more compact than Maragogype)
- **Cherries**: Very large
- **Beans**: Screen 18-20+ (very large)
- **Shape**: Elongated, elephant bean characteristic

**Yield**:
- **Lower than standard varieties**: Large beans = fewer per cherry
- **Better than Maragogype**: Improved productivity
- **Economics**: Requires premium pricing (specialty market)

**Disease**:
- **Rust susceptible**: Both parents susceptible
- **Management**: Shade, nutrition, monitoring

### Cup Quality

**Pacamara Signature Profile**:
- **Butter body**: Unique creamy, buttery mouthfeel (defining trait)
- **Floral complexity**: Jasmine, orange blossom, perfume aromatics
- **Tropical fruit**: Mango, papaya, exotic fruit
- **Complex acidity**: Bright, structured, layered
- **Clean cup**: Washed processing showcases clarity
- **Balance**: Complex equilibrium of multiple dimensions

**Why Pacamara Is Special**:
1. Genuinely unique cup profile (butter body found nowhere else)
2. Floral complexity exceeds parent varieties
3. Large bean creates market interest and visual appeal
4. Competition success validates quality (Cup of Excellence winner)
5. Salvadoran terroir + variety synergy

**Pacamara + Apaneca = Excellence**:
- Volcanic soil minerality enhances complexity
- High altitude develops acidity and density
- Washed processing creates clarity
- Cool temperatures slow maturation (flavor development)
- Result: World-class specialty coffee

## üîÑ Processing: Washed

### Harvest

- **Season**: December-March
- **Selective picking**: 3-5 passes, ripe only
- **Large cherries**: Easier to identify ripeness
- **Quality critical**: Washed process requires perfect input

### Washed Processing

**Traditional Central American washed method**:

1. **Reception**: Flotation sorting, defect removal
2. **Depulping**: Skin/pulp removed, parchment + mucilage remains
3. **Fermentation**: 12-24 hours, mucilage breakdown
4. **Washing**: Running water channels, rinse cycles
5. **Drying**: Sun-drying on patios or raised beds (10-15 days)
6. **Target**: 10-12% moisture

**Quality Control**:
- Estate-level processing (many Pacamara farms)
- Controlled fermentation timing
- Clean water (mountain springs)
- Careful drying (preserve quality)

**Hulling & Grading**:
- **Screen sorting**: 18-20+ (large Pacamara beans)
- **Density sorting**: High-density beans selected
- **Hand sorting**: Multiple passes, defect removal
- **SHB grade**: Strictly Hard Bean (1,200+ MASL)
