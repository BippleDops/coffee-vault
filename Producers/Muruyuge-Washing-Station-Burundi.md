   - Exceptional cup quality
   - Sweet, balanced, complex

   - Adapted to high altitude

   - Some farmers maintain
   - Similar profile to Bourbon
   - Slightly different plant characteristics

3. **Mibirizi** (5%)
   - Bourbon selection
   - Developed in Burundi
   - Quality and disease management balance

**Bourbon Dominance**:
Burundi's coffee built on Bourbon:
- Belgian colonizers introduced Bourbon (1930s)
- Forced cultivation (colonial policy)
- Bourbon thrived at Burundi's high altitudes
- Post-independence, farmers maintained Bourbon
- No reason to replace (quality + altitude = excellent)

**Lack of Variety Diversity**:
Unlike other origins with variety diversification:
- Burundi remains almost entirely Bourbon
- Advantage: Consistency, quality recognition
- Risk: Disease vulnerability (especially Coffee Wilt Disease)
- BUT: Quality excellence compensates, specialty market values

**Altitude-Variety Synergy**:
Bourbon at 1,850m average altitude:
- Slow cherry development (9-10 months)
- Enhanced sugar accumulation
- Complex acidity development
- Dense bean structure
- Sweet, balanced, complex profile
- Similar to Rwanda's Bourbon expression (neighboring countries, similar conditions)

## üî¨ Processing Methods (Fully Washed Excellence)

**Centralized Washing Station Processing**:

Muruyuge Washing Station processes all member cherry with meticulous quality control:

**Washed Process** (100% of production - Burundian standard)

**Cherry Reception & Sorting** (Day of Picking):

1. **Delivery**:
   - Farmers deliver cherry same day as picked
   - Washing station operates during harvest season
   - Cherry weighed upon arrival (payment calculation)

2. **Floatation Sorting**:
   - Cherry placed in water tanks
   - Floaters removed (underripe, defects)
   - Density sorting
   - Quality incentive (premium for ripe cherry)

3. **Hand-Sorting**:
   - Visual inspection on sorting tables
   - Remove any missed defects
   - Final quality check

**Processing** (Within 6 hours of delivery):

4. **Depulping**:
   - Mechanical disc pulpers
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Sorted by density in depulping

5. **Dry Fermentation** (12-18 hours):
   - Parchment coffee in fermentation tanks
   - Natural enzymatic mucilage breakdown
   - No water added (dry fermentation)
   - Cool highland altitude = longer fermentation time
   - Monitored by touch, smell, pH testing

6. **Washing**:
   - Clean mountain water
   - Multiple wash cycles
   - Washing channels (density grading, grade separation)
   - Agitation ensures complete mucilage removal
   - Final rinse until water runs clear

7. **Soaking** (12-24 hours - Quality Enhancement):
   - Clean water soak (optional but common in Burundi)
   - Additional quality development
   - Further flavor enhancement
   - Clean water changed/refreshed

**Drying** (10-15 days):

8. **Raised Bed Drying**:
   - African raised beds (mesh for airflow)
   - Thin, even layers (2-3 cm)
   - Turned every 2-3 hours (first week)
   - Hand-sorted during drying (continuous defect removal)
   - Covered during hottest hours (prevent over-drying, cracking)
   - Night covering (dew protection)
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

9. **Resting**:
   - 30-60 days in parchment
   - Flavor stabilization and development
   - Climate-controlled storage (humidity management)

**Dry Milling** (Pre-Export):

10. **Hulling**:
    - Parchment removal

11. **Grading**:
    - Screen sizing (AA, A, AB, etc.)
    - Density sorting (gravity tables)
    - Hand-sorting (final defect removal)
    - Export-ready green coffee

**Quality Control** (Exceptional):
- Cherry floatation and hand-sorting
- Fermentation monitoring (time, pH, smell, touch)
- Washing thoroughness verification
- Drying moisture checks (multiple daily, handheld meters)
- Hand-sorting during drying (continuous)
- Post-mill hand-sorting (final quality)
- Cupping evaluation (all export lots)
- Lot traceability (day, farmer group if tracked)

**Washing Station Infrastructure**:

- 8 fermentation tanks (concrete, various sizes)
- Washing channels with density grading
- 60+ raised African drying beds
- Covered drying areas (rain protection)
- Moisture meters and monitoring
- Parchment storage (climate-controlled)
