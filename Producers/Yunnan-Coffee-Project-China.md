## ‚òï Varieties (Evolution from Catimor Dominance)

**Dominant Variety**:

1. **Catimor** (80-85%):
   - Introduced 1980s-1990s (Nestl√© era)
   - High yield, rust-resistant, Yunnan-adapted
   - Fast production (poverty alleviation priority)
   - **Quality Issue**: Catimor reputation poor in specialty (70-80 points typical)
   - **Specialty Challenge**: Overcoming Catimor stigma, improving through processing

**Catimor Context**:
Catimor (Caturra x Timor Hybrid) combines:
- Productivity (Caturra)
- Disease resistance (Timor Hybrid, with Robusta genetics)
- Adaptation (Yunnan climate and altitude)

But:
- Cup quality generally lower than pure arabica varieties (Typica, Bourbon)
- Specialty market skepticism
- Yunnan associated with Catimor = quality perception challenge

**Specialty Movement Varieties** (15-20%, growing):

2. **Typica** (8-10%):
   - Heritage variety (original Yunnan coffee from French missionaries)
   - Rediscovered and replanted by specialty farmers
   - Excellent cup quality (85-88+ points achievable)
   - Lower yield but specialty premium
   - Yunnan specialty identity (moving beyond Catimor)

3. **Bourbon** (3-5%):
   - Introduced in specialty era
   - High quality potential
   - Lower yield, more delicate
   - Specialty lots and experimental farms

4. **Geisha** (1-2%):
   - Experimental plots (mostly recent years)
   - Geisha in Chinese terroir (exploration)
   - Ultra-premium potential
   - Limited production, high interest

5. **Other Experimental** (<1%):
   - SL28, SL34 (Kenyan varieties)
   - Pacamara
   - F1 Hybrids
   - Variety trials for quality + resilience

**Variety Strategy Tension**:

**Government/Commodity**:
- Catimor promotion (yield, poverty alleviation, volume)
- Productivity priority
- Instant coffee and commercial market

**Specialty Movement**:
- Typica, Bourbon, Geisha focus (quality, differentiation)
- Catimor processing improvement (demonstrating quality potential)
- Variety diversification
- Specialty market access

**Processing-Driven Quality** (Catimor Innovation):
Some specialty producers demonstrate Catimor quality potential through processing:
- Exceptional processing (washed, natural, experimental)
- Selective picking (ripe cherry only)
- Innovative fermentation
- Achieving 83-86+ points with Catimor (vs 75-80 typical)

This challenges variety determinism and showcases processing impact.

## üî¨ Processing (Innovation & Quality Revolution)

**Historical Processing** (1980s-2010s):
- Wet processing (basic washed)
- Poor quality control (variable fermentation, drying)
- Mixed cherry ripeness (stripping vs selective picking)
- Instant coffee destination (quality less critical)
- 75-80 cupping scores typical

**Specialty Era Processing** (2010s-Present):

**Infrastructure Investment**:
- Modern wet mills (quality-focused cooperatives, estates)
- African raised beds (drying improvement)
- Processing labs (quality control)
- Cupping facilities (quality assessment)
- Storage improvements

**Processing Methods**:

1. **Washed** (60-70%):
   - Improved quality control (fermentation timing, water cleanliness)
   - Selective picking (ripe cherry)
   - Density sorting (floatation)
   - Moisture monitoring (consistent drying)
   - Clean, balanced profiles

2. **Natural** (15-20%):
   - Whole cherry drying (introduced in specialty era)
   - Yunnan dry season suitable
   - Fruit-forward profiles
   - Differentiation from washed

3. **Honey** (5-10%):
   - Mucilage retention during drying
   - Processing innovation (learned from Central America)
   - Sweetness enhancement
   - Specialty lots

4. **Experimental** (5-10%, growing):
   - Anaerobic fermentation
   - Extended fermentation
   - Carbonic maceration
   - Yeast inoculation
   - Thermal shock processing
   - Innovation and differentiation focus

**Processing Innovation Drivers**:
- **Specialty Market**: Processing differentiation valued (price premiums)
- **Catimor Improvement**: Processing can elevate Catimor quality
- **Competition**: Standing out from commodity Yunnan
- **Learning**: International partnerships, origin visits, knowledge transfer
- **Experimentation**: Young generation farmers innovating

**Quality Control Improvements**:
- Selective handpicking (vs stripping)
- Cherry sorting (ripe only, defect removal)
- Floatation (density separation)
- Fermentation control (temperature, timing)
- Drying monitoring (moisture meters, consistent turning)
- Cupping and grading (quality assessment)

**Challenges**:
- Infrastructure investment costs (smallholder limitations)
- Knowledge gaps (training ongoing)
- Processing tradition weak (newer coffee country)
- Volume vs quality pressure (economic)

**Success Stories**:
Specialty Yunnan coffees achieving:
- 83-86 points (good specialty)
- 86-88+ points (excellent, competition-level)
- International recognition (Cup of Excellence participation, roaster acclaim)
- Price premiums (3-5x commodity Yunnan)
