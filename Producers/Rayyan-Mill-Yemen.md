## üî¨ Processing Methods (Traditional Natural, Unchanged for Centuries)

**Traditional Yemeni Natural Process**:

Yemen invented natural processing (or maintained it from Ethiopia):

**At Farm Level** (Individual Farmers):

1. **Harvest**:
   - Selective picking (ripe cherry only)
   - Small quantities (tiny farms, hand harvest)
   - Sometimes picked slightly underripe (drought, water stress)

2. **Initial Drying** (Farm Level):
   - Whole cherry drying begins immediately
   - Spread on rooftops (traditional)
   - Or courtyard patios
   - Or laid on tarps
   - Sun drying in arid climate (fast, intense)
   - Days to initial drying (not weeks like humid origins)

3. **Hulling** (Often Farm Level):
   - Dried cherry hulled at farm
   - Manual or small mechanical hullers
   - Green coffee in parchment or dried with skin
   - Varies by farmer/village

4. **Delivery to Rayyan Mill**:
   - Dried coffee delivered (cherry or hulled)
   - Payment based on quality assessment
   - Cupping and grading

**At Rayyan Mill** (Quality Control & Export Prep):

5. **Reception & Quality Assessment**:
   - Dried coffee received from farmers
   - Visual inspection (color, uniformity, defects)
   - Cupping evaluation (quality verification)
   - Payment to farmers (quality-based premiums)

6. **Additional Drying** (If needed):
   - Moisture verification (target: 10-11%)
   - Additional drying if moisture too high
   - Rooftop drying at mill

7. **Hulling** (If not already):
   - Parchment/skin removal
   - Mechanical hulling

8. **Sorting & Grading**:
   - Hand-sorting (defect removal)
   - Screen sizing
   - Density sorting
   - Visual quality grading
   - Peaberry separation (sometimes)

9. **Lot Separation**:
   - By village/region
   - By altitude
   - By quality score
   - Micro-lots (single farm/village)
   - Competition lots (best coffees)

10. **Storage & Export**:
    - Green coffee stored for export
    - Challenging logistics (war, ports closed)
    - Smuggling routes (through Djibouti, Oman)
    - Container shipping (when possible)
    - Extraordinary effort to export

**Traditional Rooftop Drying**:
Iconic Yemeni method:
- Coffee dried on building rooftops
- Intense sun, arid climate
- Fast drying (days, not weeks)
- Creates distinctive Yemeni profile
- Traditional, unchanged for centuries

**Natural Processing Characteristics**:
Yemen's natural creates unique profile:
- Fruit-forward (dried fruit, raisin, date)
- Winey, fermented complexity
- Chocolate and spice (cinnamon, cardamom)
- Full body, heavy, syrupy
- Complex, layered, exotic
- Unlike any other natural coffee

**Quality Challenges**:
Traditional methods + war = quality variation:
- Some farmers lack knowledge for optimal processing
- Drying conditions vary (rooftop quality, weather)
- Defects can be high (sorting crucial)
- Moisture levels inconsistent
- Rayyan Mill's quality control essential

**Rayyan Mill's Quality Innovation**:
While respecting tradition, Rayyan Mill adds:
- Quality assessment and payment incentives
- Cupping and feedback to farmers
- Moisture monitoring
- Defect sorting and removal
- Lot traceability
- Quality standards for export
