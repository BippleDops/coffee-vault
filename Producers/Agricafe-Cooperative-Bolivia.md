## üî¨ Processing Methods

**Member-Level Processing**:

Most processing occurs at member farms:

**1. Washed Process** (85% of production)

**Home Processing** (Most Common):

1. **Harvest & Sorting**:
   - Selective hand-picking (multiple passes)
   - Floatation in buckets (remove underripes)
   - Same-day processing (crucial for quality)

2. **Depulping**:
   - Small hand-crank or mechanical pulpers at home
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Parchment coffee

3. **Fermentation** (24-48 hours):
   - Parchment in buckets or small tanks
   - Cool ultra-high-altitude temperature = longer fermentation
   - Natural enzymatic breakdown
   - Monitored by touch and smell

4. **Washing**:
   - Mountain spring water
   - Multiple rinse cycles
   - Washing in buckets or small channels
   - Final rinse until clean

5. **Drying** (12-20 days):
   - Patios (concrete or brick where available)
   - Tarps (most common - simple, portable)
   - Parabolic dryers (some members have, cooperative-provided)
   - Thin layers, regular turning
   - Hand-sorting during drying
   - Weather challenges (cloud forest rain, humidity)
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

6. **Delivery to Cooperative**:
   - Dry parchment delivered
   - Quality assessment and payment
   - Moisture verification
   - Cupping sample taken

**Cooperative Processing Centers**:

Some members deliver cherry to cooperative centers:
- Centralized wet mills in accessible communities
- Quality control oversight
- Consistent processing
- Training and support
- Shared infrastructure

**2. Natural Process** (10% - Growing Experimental)

Limited natural processing:
- Very challenging in humid cloud forest climate
- Requires raised beds (airflow essential)
- Extended drying (20-30+ days)
- Mold risk management
- Premium pricing potential
- Specialty roaster interest

**3. Experimental** (5%)

Cooperative supports limited experimentation:
- Honey processing trials
- Extended fermentation
- Micro-lot quality optimization

**Cooperative-Level Processing**:

**Reception & Quality Control**:
- Parchment received from members
- Moisture verification (10-11% required)
- Physical defect screening
- Cupping sample evaluation
- Payment to members (quality-based)

**Storage**:
- Parchment storage (climate challenges)
- Traceability systems
- Inventory management

**Dry Milling** (Pre-Export):
- Hulling (parchment removal)
- Screen grading
- Density sorting (gravity tables)
- Hand-sorting (final quality check)
- Export-ready green coffee

**Quality Control** (Multi-Level):
- **Farm Level**: Member training in selective picking, processing
- **Village Level**: Coordinators monitor quality
- **Cooperative Level**: Cupping lab, quality verification
- **Traceability**: Member, village, altitude tracked

**Processing Challenges**:
Ultra-high altitude and cloud forest create difficulties:
- High humidity (extended drying times, mold risk)
- Frequent rain (drying interruption)
- Limited infrastructure (remote locations)
- Small scale (individual member processing varies)
- BUT: Altitude quality advantage compensates for challenges
