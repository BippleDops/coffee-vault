## üî¨ Processing Innovation

Santa In√™s is recognized as a natural process innovation leader in Sul de Minas.

**Standard Processing Methods**:

1. **Natural Process** (50% of production):
   - Traditional Brazilian method perfected
   - Whole cherry drying on patios and raised beds
   - 25-35 day drying period
   - Multiple daily turning for even drying
   - Moisture monitoring (target: 11%)
   - Creates chocolate, fruit, sweet profile

   **Natural Process Protocol**:
   - Harvest: Selective picking, only ripe cherries
   - Reception: Immediate sorting, defect removal
   - Floating: Water flotation removes underripe/defective
   - Drying: Thin layers on patios, 3-5cm depth
   - Turning: 6-8 times daily for first week
   - Resting: Resting periods in afternoon heat
   - Storage: Rested in parchment 30-60 days
   - Milling: Gentle dry-milling to preserve quality

2. **Pulped Natural** (35%):
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Brazilian specialty processing
   - Faster drying than natural (18-25 days)
   - Less fermentation risk
   - Balanced cup profile

   **Pulped Natural Protocol**:
   - Pulping: Mechanical pulpers, skin removal only
   - Mucilage: Full mucilage retained on parchment
   - Drying: Raised beds and patios
   - Monitoring: Careful moisture and fermentation tracking
   - Result: Clean sweetness, moderate body

3. **Washed Process** (10%):
   - Full fermentation and washing
   - Mucilage completely removed
   - Clean, bright profile
   - Used for specific market requests
   - Shorter drying (12-18 days)

**Experimental Processing** (5% - Innovation Focus):

4. **Extended Fermentation Natural**:
   - Controlled pre-drying fermentation (12-24 hours)
   - Cherry whole in fermentation tanks
   - Temperature monitoring
   - Enhanced fruit complexity
   - Experimental micro-lots

5. **Honey Process Variations**:
   - Partial mucilage removal (25%, 50%, 75%)
   - Different fermentation profiles
   - Yellow, red, black honey process trials
   - Market differentiation

6. **Anaerobic Fermentation**:
   - Sealed tank fermentation
   - Controlled oxygen levels
   - Extended fermentation (48-72 hours)
   - Unique flavor development
   - Limited experimental lots

7. **Carbonic Maceration**:
   - CO2 environment fermentation
   - Whole cherry in sealed tanks
   - Wine-making technique adapted to coffee
   - Fruit-forward, complex results
   - Competition lots

**Processing Infrastructure**:

- **Wet Mills**: Three processing stations across estate
- **Reception**: Cherry sorting and quality control
- **Pulping**: Multiple mechanical pulpers
- **Fermentation**: Tiled tanks with temperature control
- **Washing**: Clean water washing systems
- **Patios**: 20,000+ square meters concrete patios
- **Raised Beds**: 500+ African raised drying beds
- **Mechanical Dryers**: Backup for weather, final moisture adjustment
- **Storage**: Climate-controlled warehouses (temperature/humidity)
- **Laboratory**: Sample roasting, cupping, moisture testing

**Quality Control in Processing**:

- Immediate cherry sorting upon harvest
- Density separation (water flotation)
- Multiple quality checks during drying
- Moisture monitoring (multiple times daily)
- Sample roasting and cupping at key stages
- Defect removal at dry-milling
- Final cupping before release

**Processing Philosophy**:
"Natural processing is our heritage, innovation is our future." Santa In√™s respects traditional Brazilian natural processing while actively experimenting with new techniques to create unique flavor profiles and market differentiation.
