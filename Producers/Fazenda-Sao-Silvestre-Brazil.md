## üî¨ Processing Methods (Brazilian Innovation)

**1. Natural Process** (60% of production)

**Brazilian Natural** (Traditional Method):
- Whole cherry drying (no depulping)
- Spread on concrete patios (terreiros)
- Sun dried 25-30 days
- Turned regularly (mechanized or manual rakes)
- Moisture target: 11-12%
- Creates Brazil's signature chocolate/nutty profile

**Modern Improvements**:
- Mechanical cherry sorting before drying
- Covered drying (rainy season protection)
- Moisture monitoring
- Defect removal during drying
- Lot separation by ripeness/density

**Why Natural in Brazil**:
- Distinct dry season (May-Sept) ideal for drying
- Large drying areas (patios, terreiros)
- Traditional method (since 1800s)
- Creates characteristic Brazilian flavor
- Cost-effective at scale

**2. Pulped Natural** (30% of production)

**Cereja Descascado** (Pulped Natural):
- Mechanical depulping (skin removed)
- Mucilage retained on bean
- Dried with mucilage intact
- Bridges natural and washed
- Faster drying than natural (15-20 days)
- Popular Brazilian innovation

**Benefits**:
- Cleaner than natural
- More body than washed
- Faster drying
- Less risk than natural
- Unique Brazilian profile

**3. Washed Process** (10% of production)

**Lavado** (Washed):
- Depulping
- Fermentation (12-24 hours)
- Washing
- Drying without mucilage
- Creates cleaner profile
- Used for specific lots/buyers

**Processing Infrastructure**:
- Mechanical harvesters
- Depulpers (multiple units)
- Floatation tanks (cherry sorting)
- Concrete drying patios (2+ hectares)
- Covered drying areas
- Mechanical dryers (backup/finishing)
- Storage facilities (climate controlled)
- Quality lab and cupping room
