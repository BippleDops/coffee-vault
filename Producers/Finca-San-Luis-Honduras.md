Finca San Luis response:

## üî¨ Processing Methods (On-Farm Innovation)

Finca San Luis operates complete on-farm processing, enabling quality control and experimentation.

**1. Washed Process** (60% of production)

**Traditional Honduran Washed**:

1. **Selective Harvest**:
   - Hand-picking, multiple passes
   - Only ripe cherry
   - Same-day processing (within 6 hours)

2. **Floatation & Sorting**:
   - Cherry floated in water tanks
   - Remove underripes, floaters, defects
   - Visual sorting

3. **Depulping**:
   - Mechanical disc pulper
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Parchment coffee (caf√© pergamino)

4. **Fermentation** (12-24 hours):
   - Concrete fermentation tanks
   - Natural enzymatic mucilage breakdown
   - Highland altitude = cooler temps = longer fermentation
   - Monitored by touch, smell, pH

5. **Washing**:
   - Clean mountain water
   - Multiple wash cycles
   - Washing channels (density sorting)
   - Final rinse until completely clean

6. **Drying** (10-15 days):
   - Concrete patios (primary)
   - Parabolic dryers (backup, poor weather)
   - Raised African beds (premium lots)
   - Thin layers, regular turning
   - Hand-sorted during drying
   - Night covering (dew protection)
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

7. **Resting**:
   - 30-60 days in parchment
   - Flavor stabilization
   - Climate-controlled storage

**2. Honey Process** (25% - Growing Specialty)

Finca San Luis has embraced honey processing for specialty market:

**Yellow Honey** (~25% mucilage):
- Light mucilage retention
- Enhanced sweetness vs washed
- 12-15 days drying
- Fruit notes emerging
- Specialty roaster interest

**Red Honey** (~50% mucilage):
- Substantial mucilage retention
- Significant sweetness boost
- Stone fruit complexity
- Fuller body
- 15-18 days drying
- Premium pricing

**Black Honey** (~90% mucilage):
- Maximum mucilage retention (experimental lots)
- Intense sweetness, dried fruit
- 18-22 days drying
- Challenging but rewarding
- Competition-level lots

**3. Natural Process** (10% - Experimental)

Recent natural processing experiments:
- Whole cherry drying
- 18-25 days drying time
- Fruit-forward profile
- Challenging in humid conditions
- Limited lots for specialty roasters

**4. Experimental Processing** (5%)

**Anaerobic Fermentation**:
- Sealed tanks, oxygen-free environment
- 48-72 hours controlled fermentation
- Unique flavor development
- Competition lots

**Carbonic Maceration**:
- Inspired by wine production
- Whole cherry, CO2-rich fermentation
- Experimental micro-lots

**Extended Fermentation**:
- 36-48 hour fermentation (vs standard 12-24)
- Enhanced complexity
- Careful monitoring

**Processing Infrastructure**:
- 2 mechanical depulpers
- 4 fermentation tanks (various sizes)
- Washing channels with density sorting
- 2 concrete drying patios (500+ square meters total)
- 3 parabolic dryers (covered drying, weather protection)
- 20+ raised African beds (premium lots)
- Moisture monitoring equipment
- Parchment storage facilities
- Basic cupping/roasting equipment

**Quality Control**:
- Cherry floatation sorting
- Fermentation monitoring (time, pH, smell)
- Drying moisture checks (daily)
- Hand-sorting during drying (continuous)
- Lot separation (variety, process, quality)
- Cupping evaluation (quality verification)
- Traceability (lot number system)
