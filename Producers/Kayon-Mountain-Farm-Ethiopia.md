## üî¨ Processing Methods (Innovation Leader)

Kayon Mountain operates state-of-art washing station with multiple processing capabilities.

**1. Washed Process** (50% of production)

**Ethiopian Washed Implementation**:

1. **Cherry Reception & Sorting**:
   - Delivered same day as picked
   - Floatation tanks (remove underripes, floaters)
   - Visual sorting (defect removal)
   - Ripeness grading

2. **Pulping** (Within 6 hours):
   - Disc pulpers (skin removal)
   - Mucilage retained
   - Sorted by density in channels

3. **Fermentation** (36-48 hours):
   - Longer than typical (highland altitude = cooler temps)
   - Monitored for mucilage breakdown
   - pH testing
   - Multiple tanks for lot separation

4. **Washing**:
   - Mountain spring water
   - Multiple wash cycles
   - Washing channels (additional density sorting)
   - Final rinse until clean

5. **Drying** (10-15 days):
   - Raised African beds (mesh, airflow)
   - Thin layers (2-3cm)
   - Hand-sorted during drying (defect removal)
   - Covered during hottest hours (prevent over-drying)
   - Night covering (dew protection)
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

6. **Resting**:
   - 30-60 days in parchment
   - Flavor development and stabilization

**Ethiopian Washed Profile**:
- Tea-like clarity and refinement
- Floral (jasmine, bergamot, honeysuckle)
- Citrus (lemon, lime, bergamot)
- Stone fruit (peach, apricot)
- Light body, silky
- Sparkling acidity
- Clean, sweet, complex finish

**2. Natural Process** (35% of production)

**Ethiopian Natural Implementation**:

1. **Cherry Sorting**:
   - Only fully ripe cherry accepted
   - Floatation sorting
   - Hand-sorting on tables
   - Defect removal

2. **Drying** (18-25 days):
   - Raised African beds
   - Whole cherry, skin intact
   - Thin, even layers
   - Turned every 2-3 hours (first week)
   - Hand-sorted multiple times daily
   - Covered during rain, heavy dew
   - Slow, controlled drying
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

3. **Resting**:
   - 60-90 days in cherry (flavor development)
   - Climate-controlled storage

**Ethiopian Natural Profile**:
- Fruit-forward, explosive
- Berry (blueberry, strawberry, mixed berry)
- Stone fruit (nectarine, apricot)
- Tropical fruit (mango, passionfruit)
- Wine-like complexity
- Floral undertones
- Full body, syrupy
- Long, fruity finish

**3. Experimental Processing** (15% - Innovation)

**Anaerobic Fermentation**:
- Sealed fermentation tanks (oxygen-free)
- Whole cherry or depulped
- 48-96 hours controlled fermentation
- Temperature monitoring
- Creates unique flavor development
- Intense fruit, funky complexity

**Extended Fermentation**:
- Washed process with 72-96 hour fermentation
- Enhanced fruit and complexity
- Risk management (careful monitoring)
- Creates bridge between washed and natural

**Carbonic Maceration**:
- Inspired by wine production
- Whole cherry sealed fermentation
- CO2-rich environment
- Unique flavor profiles
- Experimental micro-lots

**Honey Process**:
- Rare in Ethiopia but experimental at Kayon
- Varying mucilage retention
- Creates unique profiles different from typical Ethiopian

**Processing Infrastructure**:
- 4 disc pulpers
- 12 fermentation tanks (various sizes)
- 50+ raised drying beds (African beds, mesh)
- Covered drying areas (rain/dew protection)
- Climate-controlled storage
- Quality lab with cupping room
- Moisture meters and monitoring
- Sorting tables and equipment

**Quality Control**:
- Multiple sorting stages (cherry, parchment, green)
- Daily cupping of lots
- Moisture monitoring throughout process
- Defect counting and removal
- Lot traceability (variety, day, process)
- Sample roasting and evaluation

## üìù Producer Story

**Esmael Hassen's Journey**:

Esmael Hassen comes from multi-generational coffee farming family in Guji. Growing up, he watched his family grow exceptional coffee but receive modest prices through traditional cooperative system. Cherry was delivered to cooperative washing station, processed in bulk, sold through ECX with no traceability, and farmers received commodity pricing regardless of quality.

**The Decision** (2010-2011):
In early 2010s, Ethiopian government began allowing private washing stations - previously banned. Esmael saw opportunity:
- Coffee was exceptional but undervalued
- Processing quality was inconsistent
- No incentive for quality in traditional system
- Specialty market paying premiums for quality Ethiopian coffee
- Direct trade relationships emerging globally

**The Investment** (2012):
Esmael invested his family's savings and borrowed to build Kayon Mountain washing station:
- Modern equipment (pulpers, fermentation tanks, drying beds)
- Quality control systems (lab, cupping room)
- Processing knowledge (trained in washed and natural methods)
- Export license (navigate bureaucracy)
- International relationships (attend coffee conferences)

**Early Challenges**:
- **Financing**: High initial investment, limited access to credit
- **Logistics**: Export paperwork, regulations, shipping
- **Market Access**: Building roaster relationships from scratch
- **Quality Learning**: Perfecting processing through trial and error
- **Community Trust**: Convincing outgrowers to sell to new operation

**Breakthrough** (2014-2015):
Kayon Mountain coffee scored 88+ points at international cupping competitions. Specialty roasters took notice. Direct relationships formed. Premium prices justified investment. Quality reputation grew.

**Expansion** (2015-2020):
- Outgrower program expanded (50 farmers ‚Üí 300+)

**Current Success** (2020-Present):
