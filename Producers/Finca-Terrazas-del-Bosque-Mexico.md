## üî¨ Processing Methods (Traditional + Innovation)

**On-Farm Processing**:

Small wet mill enables processing control:

**1. Washed Process** (70% of production)

**Traditional Mexican Washed**:

1. **Selective Harvest**:
   - Hand-picking, multiple passes
   - Only fully ripe cherry
   - Family members inspect picking quality
   - Same-day processing (within 6 hours)

2. **Floatation & Sorting**:
   - Cherry floated in tanks
   - Remove underripes, floaters, defects
   - Visual hand-sorting

3. **Depulping**:
   - Small mechanical depulper
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Parchment coffee

4. **Fermentation** (18-30 hours):
   - Concrete fermentation tanks
   - Natural enzymatic breakdown
   - Cool highland/cloud forest temperature = longer fermentation
   - Monitored by touch, smell, pH testing

5. **Washing**:
   - Spring water (mountain source)
   - Multiple wash cycles
   - Washing channels
   - Final rinse until completely clean

6. **Drying** (12-18 days):
   - Raised African beds (mesh, airflow)
   - Some patio drying (concrete)
   - Thin layers, regular turning
   - Hand-sorted during drying
   - Covered at night (humidity, dew protection)
   - Careful moisture management (cloud forest humidity challenges)
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

7. **Resting**:
   - 30-60 days in parchment
   - Flavor development
   - Storage in dry area

**2. Natural Process** (20% - Growing Specialty)

Recent natural processing for specialty market:

1. **Cherry Selection**:
   - Only fully ripe, perfect cherry
   - More selective than washed
   - Floatation and hand-sorting

2. **Drying** (20-30 days):
   - Raised beds (essential in humid climate)
   - Whole cherry drying
   - Thin layers (single cherry layer)
   - Turned frequently (prevent mold in humidity)
   - Covered during rain (frequent in cloud forest)
   - Slow, careful drying
   - Challenging in humid climate but rewarding

3. **Resting**:
   - Extended rest (60-90 days in cherry)
   - Flavor development

**3. Honey Process** (10% - Experimental)

Limited honey processing experiments:
- Yellow honey (light mucilage)
- Red honey (substantial mucilage)
- Challenging in humid climate (mold risk)
- Careful monitoring required
- Specialty roaster interest
- Premium pricing potential

**Quality Control**:
- Cherry sorting (floatation, visual)
- Fermentation monitoring (timing, pH)
- Drying moisture checks (daily, handheld meter)
- Hand-sorting during drying (continuous defect removal)
- Lot separation (variety, ripeness, process)
- Cupping evaluation (quality verification)

**Processing Challenges**:
Cloud forest humidity creates processing difficulty:
- Longer drying times (18+ days vs 10-12 in dry climates)
- Mold risk (natural process especially challenging)
- Careful monitoring essential
- Infrastructure investment (covered drying, raised beds)
- Experience and knowledge critical
