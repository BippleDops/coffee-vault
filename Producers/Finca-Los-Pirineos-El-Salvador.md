   - Very large bean (elephant bean)

   - Complex, layered, competition-level

   - Multiple Cup of Excellence wins

2. **Bourbon** (30%)
   - Heritage variety
   - Excellent cup quality
   - Sweet, balanced, complex
   - Traditional Salvadoran variety
   - Premium lots

3. **Pacas** (20%)
   - Salvadoran Bourbon mutation (discovered El Salvador 1949)
   - Compact plant, higher productivity
   - Excellent cup quality
   - Unique to El Salvador region
   - Reliable specialty production

4. **Caturra** (15%)
   - Bourbon mutation
   - Productive, reliable
   - Good cup quality
   - Standard specialty lots

**Pacamara - Salvadoran Innovation**:

Created 1958 at El Salvador's ISIC (Salvadoran Coffee Research Institute):
- **Cross**: Pacas (Bourbon mutation) x Maragogipe (Typica mutation, large bean)
- **Result**: Very large bean with exceptional quality
- **Characteristics**: Complex, layered, floral, fruit, chocolate, wine-like
- **Challenge**: Lower yield, larger plant, more selective picking
- **Reward**: Premium pricing, competition success, specialty fame

Pacamara put El Salvador on specialty map. It's El Salvador's gift to coffee world.

**Varietal Strategy**:
- **Pacamara Focus**: Estate specializes, invests in, showcases Pacamara
- **Altitude Matching**: Pacamara at highest elevations (1,700-1,800m)
- **Selective Picking**: Pacamara requires more careful, selective harvest
- **Processing Variation**: Pacamara processed multiple ways (washed, honey, natural)
- **Competition Lots**: Best Pacamara for Cup of Excellence

## üî¨ Processing Methods (Innovation Leadership)

Los Pirineos operates comprehensive on-estate processing, enabling experimentation.

**1. Washed Process** (50% of production)

**Traditional Salvadoran Washed**:

1. **Selective Harvest**:
   - Pacamara requires extra selectivity (size and ripeness variation)
   - Multiple passes, only fully ripe
   - Same-day processing

2. **Floatation & Sorting**:
   - Density sorting in water tanks
   - Visual hand-sorting
   - Defect removal

3. **Depulping**:
   - Mechanical disc pulpers
   - Adjusted for Pacamara (larger cherry)
   - Mucilage retained

4. **Fermentation** (12-24 hours):
   - Concrete fermentation tanks
   - Cool highland altitude = longer fermentation
   - pH monitoring
   - Touch, smell, timing

5. **Washing**:
   - Volcanic spring water
   - Multiple wash cycles
   - Washing channels (density sorting)
   - Clean rinse

6. **Drying** (10-15 days):
   - Raised African beds (premium lots)
   - Concrete patios (standard lots)
   - Thin layers, regular turning
   - Hand-sorted during drying
   - Night covering (dew protection)
   - Target: 10-11% moisture

**2. Honey Process** (25% - Specialty Growing)

Multiple honey levels:

**Yellow Honey** (~25% mucilage):
- Light sweetness enhancement
- Fruit notes emerging
- 12-15 days drying
- Balanced profile

**Red Honey** (~50% mucilage):
- Substantial sweetness boost
- Stone fruit complexity
- Fuller body
- 15-18 days drying
- Premium pricing

**Black Honey** (~90% mucilage):
- Maximum mucilage retention
- Intense sweetness, dried fruit
- Full body, syrupy
- 18-22 days drying
- Competition lots

**3. Natural Process** (15% - Experimental)

El Salvador embraced natural processing post-2000s:

1. **Cherry Selection**:
   - Ultra-selective (only perfect cherry)
   - Pacamara naturals especially prized

2. **Drying** (18-25 days):
   - Raised beds (essential for air circulation)
   - Whole cherry drying
   - Thin layers
   - Frequent turning (multiple times daily)
   - Hand-sorting continuously
   - Covered nights and rain
   - Slow, careful drying

3. **Resting**:
   - Extended rest (60-90 days in cherry)
   - Flavor development

**4. Experimental Processing** (10%)

**Anaerobic Fermentation**:
- Sealed tanks, oxygen-free environment
- 48-96 hours controlled fermentation
- Temperature monitoring
- Unique flavor development
- Competition and experimental lots

**Extended Fermentation**:
- 36-72 hour fermentation (vs standard 12-24)
- Enhanced complexity
- Careful monitoring (risk management)

**Carbonic Maceration**:
- Inspired by wine production
- Whole cherry, CO2-rich environment
- 72+ hours
- Unique, experimental profiles

**Lactic Fermentation**:
- Specific bacterial culture inoculation
- Controlled fermentation environment
- Creamy, lactic complexity

**Processing Infrastructure**:
- 2 mechanical depulpers (standard + Pacamara-adjusted)
- 8 fermentation tanks (various sizes, some sealed for anaerobic)
- 2 large concrete drying patios (600+ square meters total)
- 40+ raised African beds (premium lots)
- Parabolic dryers (weather backup)
- Temperature/humidity monitoring
- Moisture meters (multiple units)
- Cupping lab and roasting equipment
- Climate-controlled storage

**Quality Control** (Rigorous):
- Cherry sorting (floatation, density, visual)
- Fermentation monitoring (time, pH, temperature, smell)
- Drying moisture checks (multiple daily)
- Hand-sorting during drying (continuous)
- Lot separation (variety, process, quality tier)
- Cupping evaluation (all lots)
- Competition lot preparation (special protocols)
