**Indonesian Coffee Context**:
Indonesia is world's 4th largest coffee producer (after Brazil, Vietnam, Colombia). Unlike those origins, Indonesia produces distinctive profile through unique "wet-hulled" processing (Giling Basah). Sumatra, especially Gayo Highlands, produces Indonesia's premier specialty coffee - earthy, herbal, full-bodied, complex.

**Gayo Highlands Significance**:
The Gayo Highlands of Aceh province represent Indonesia's specialty heartland:
- Only Arabica growing region in Sumatra (most is Robusta)
- High altitude (1,200-1,600m)
- Distinctive wet-hulled processing
- Protected Geographic Indication (IGP)
- Fair Trade and Organic leadership
- Post-conflict agricultural renewal

**Cooperative Formation** (2005):
Permata Gayo formed after 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami devastated Aceh. The disaster ended 30-year separatist conflict (Aceh Independence Movement), bringing peace but economic devastation. Coffee farmers organized cooperative to:
- Rebuild coffee economy
- Access Fair Trade and Organic markets
- Eliminate exploitative middlemen
- Create sustainable livelihoods
- Support post-conflict community healing

**Cooperative Structure**:
- **General Assembly**: All members, democratic voting, major decisions
- **Board of Directors**: Elected representatives (9 members)
- **Manager**: Professional cooperative management
- **Technical Team**: Agronomists, quality control, processing supervisors
- **Warehouse & Dry Mill**: Cooperative-owned facilities
- **Village Coordinators**: 35 coordinators across member villages

**Philosophy**:
"Kopi untuk Perdamaian" (Coffee for Peace). Coffee cultivation as path to economic security, community healing, and sustainable livelihoods after decades of conflict. Cooperative empowerment ensures fair pricing, market access, and community development investment.

## ‚òï Varieties Cultivated (By Members)

**Primary Varieties**:

1. **Timor Hybrid** (50% of collective)
   - Arabica-Robusta hybrid (Timor IHCAFE90)
   - Developed in Timor, adapted to Indonesia
   - Disease resistant (especially coffee leaf rust)
   - Good cup quality despite hybrid genetics
   - Productive and reliable
   - Adapted to Gayo conditions

2. **Ateng** (30%)
   - Local name for Catimor varieties
   - Compact plant, high productivity
   - Rust resistant
   - Decent cup quality
   - Quick maturation
   - Popular among smallholders

3. **Catimor Selections** (15%)
   - Various Catimor cultivars
   - Bred for disease resistance and productivity
   - Adequate cup quality
   - Consistent production
   - Reliable income source

4. **Typica Heritage** (5%)
   - Traditional variety (increasingly rare)
   - Some older farms maintain
   - Lower productivity
   - Exceptional cup quality when found
   - Heritage preservation

**Varietal Reality**:
Unlike origins with heritage varieties (Ethiopia, Panama, Yemen), Indonesia's varieties are primarily hybrids and Catimors - chosen for disease resistance and productivity over cup quality. However, Sumatra's unique wet-hulled processing and terroir create distinctive profile regardless of variety.

**Rust Resistance Focus**:
Coffee leaf rust (Hemileia vastatrix) is major threat in humid Indonesia. Farmers prioritize rust-resistant varieties (Timor, Ateng, Catimor) for survival and economic viability. The trade-off (yield/disease resistance vs cup complexity) is necessary in this environment.

**Variety Management**:
- Cooperative provides seedlings (improved varieties)
- Education on varietal characteristics
- Replanting programs (old trees ‚Üí rust-resistant varieties)
- Lot separation by variety when possible
- Quality feedback to guide variety selection

## üî¨ Processing Methods (Wet-Hulled / Giling Basah)

**Wet-Hulled Processing** (Giling Basah) - 90% of production

Wet-hulled processing is unique to Indonesia and defines Sumatran coffee's distinctive character.

**Why Wet-Hulled?**:
- **Climate**: High humidity makes traditional drying difficult
- **Economics**: Faster process (farmers receive payment sooner)
- **Tradition**: Centuries-old Indonesian method
- **Infrastructure**: Limited drying space on small farms
- **Market**: Creates distinctive Sumatran profile

**Wet-Hulled Process Steps**:

**At Farm Level** (Member Farmers):

1. **Harvest & Floatation**:
   - Selective hand-picking (ripe cherry)
   - Floatation in buckets (remove underripes)
   - Same-day processing

2. **Depulping**:
   - Small hand-crank or mechanical pulpers at home
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Parchment coffee (kopi gabah)

3. **Brief Fermentation** (6-12 hours):
   - Short fermentation overnight
   - Mucilage partially broken down
   - Washed in morning

4. **Partial Drying** (1-2 days):
   - Parchment dried on tarps/patios
   - Target: 40-50% moisture (still very wet)
   - NOT fully dried (key difference from washed process)

5. **Delivery to Cooperative**:
   - Wet parchment sold to cooperative
   - Farmers receive payment (crucial cashflow)
   - Moisture content: 40-50%

**At Cooperative Level**:

6. **Wet Hulling**:
   - Parchment removed while coffee still wet (40-50% moisture)
   - Exposes green bean to air while wet
   - Creates distinctive blue-green color
   - Bean swells and softens

7. **Final Drying** (5-7 days):
   - Green coffee dried on large patios
   - Target: 12-13% moisture
   - Hand-sorted during drying
   - Covered at night (humidity protection)

8. **Dry Milling & Sorting**:
   - Screen sizing
   - Density sorting
   - Defect removal
   - Hand-sorting (final quality check)
   - Grading (Grade 1, Grade 2, etc.)

**Wet-Hulled Process Effects**:
- **Body**: Creates Sumatra's signature full, heavy body
- **Earthy/Herbal**: Distinctive earthy, herbal, cedary notes
- **Low Acidity**: Muted acidity, smooth
- **Savory/Complex**: Umami, tobacco, earth, spice
- **Color**: Blue-green bean appearance
- **Consistency**: More variable than washed (nature of process)

**Quality Challenges & Management**:
Wet-hulling creates quality risks:
- Mold potential (wet conditions)
- Inconsistent drying
- Defects more likely
- Variable quality

Cooperative's quality control:
- Moisture monitoring
- Defect removal (multiple stages)
- Lot separation
- Storage management
- Cupping and grading

**Alternative Processing** (10% - Recent Innovation):

**Washed Process**:
- Some members experiment with full washed
- Longer drying (10-14 days to 10-11% moisture)
- Hulled when dry (like typical washed coffee)
- Creates cleaner, brighter profile (different from typical Sumatra)
- Premium pricing for specialty roasters

**Natural Process**:
- Limited experimental lots
- Whole cherry drying
- Challenging in humid climate
- Rare but increasing

## üìù Producer Story

**Historical Context - Aceh Conflict**:

Aceh province experienced 30-year armed conflict (1976-2005) between Indonesian government and GAM (Free Aceh Movement) seeking independence. The conflict caused:
- 15,000+ deaths
- Economic devastation
- Infrastructure destruction
- Community trauma
- Agricultural disruption

**Tsunami & Peace** (2004-2005):

December 26, 2004: Indian Ocean tsunami hit Aceh with devastating force:
- 170,000+ deaths in Aceh alone
- Complete coastal destruction
- International aid influx
- Peace negotiations accelerated

August 15, 2005: Helsinki Peace Accord signed, ending 30-year conflict.

**Coffee Farming During Conflict**:
Gayo Highlands (inland, mountainous) were conflict zone but coffee continued:
- Farmers persevered despite danger
- Limited market access (checkpoints, violence)

Sumatra's wet-hulled profile is coffee world's most polarizing:
- **Characteristics**: Earthy, herbal, savory, full-bodied, low acidity
- **Lovers**: Find it complex, unique, perfect for espresso
- **Haters**: Find it "dirty" or "off" compared to clean washed coffees

If you appreciate earthy, herbal, savory complexity, Sumatra is unmatched. If you prefer bright, clean, fruity profiles, Sumatra may not appeal.

**Sumatra's Perfect Uses**:
- **Espresso**: Full body, low acidity, chocolate notes excel
- **Blending**: Provides body and earthiness to balance bright coffees
- **French Press**: Heavy body shines
- **Cold Brew**: Smoothness and low acidity ideal
- **Milk Drinks**: Body and chocolate work with milk
