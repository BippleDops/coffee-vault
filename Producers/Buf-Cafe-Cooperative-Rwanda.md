## üî¨ Processing Methods (Fully Washed Excellence)

**Fully Washed Process** (100% of production)

Rwanda produces exclusively fully washed coffee - government decision post-genocide to compete on quality.

**Why Fully Washed**:
- Clean, bright profile showcases coffee quality
- Specialty market preference
- Quality differentiation from neighbors
- Processing control at washing stations
- Traceability and lot separation
- Premium pricing

**Rwandan Washed Process Implementation**:

**At Farm Level** (Member Farmers):
1. **Selective Harvesting**:
   - Only fully ripe red cherry picked
   - Multiple passes during season (5-7 passes)
   - Training in ripeness selection
   - Quality premiums incentivize care
   - Same-day delivery to washing station (crucial)

**At Washing Station** (Cooperative Processing):

2. **Cherry Reception & Payment**:
   - Delivered same day as picked
   - Weighed and paid (immediately or weekly)
   - Floatation sorting (remove underripes, defects)
   - Quality assessment (ripeness premium paid)

3. **Depulping** (Within 6 hours):
   - Mechanical disc pulpers
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Sorted by density in depulping

4. **Dry Fermentation** (12-18 hours):
   - Parchment coffee in fermentation tanks
   - Natural enzymatic mucilage breakdown
   - No water added (dry fermentation)
   - Monitored by touch, smell, pH testing
   - Highland altitude = cooler temps = longer fermentation

5. **Washing** (Multiple stages):
   - Clean mountain water
   - Washing channels with density sorting
   - Multiple rinse cycles until completely clean
   - Final rinse inspected for clarity

6. **Soaking** (12-24 hours):
   - Clean water soak (optional but common in Rwanda)
   - Further flavor development
   - Additional quality enhancement
   - Clean water changed regularly

7. **Drying** (14-21 days):
   - Raised African beds (mesh for airflow)
   - Thin, even layers (1-2 cm)
   - Turned every 2-3 hours (first week)
   - Hand-sorted during drying (defect removal)
   - Covered during hottest hours (prevent over-drying, cracking)
   - Night covering (dew and rain protection)
   - Target moisture: 10-11%
   - Slow, controlled drying (never rushed)

8. **Resting**:
   - 30-60 days in parchment
   - Flavor stabilization and development
   - Climate-controlled storage

9. **Dry Milling** (Pre-Export):
   - Hulling (parchment removal)
   - Screen grading
   - Density sorting (using Oliver gravity tables)
   - Hand-sorting (final defect removal)
   - Grading: Screen 15+, A/AA grades

**Quality Control** (Exceptional):
- Reception sorting (floatation, visual)
- Fermentation monitoring (timing, pH, smell, touch)
- Drying moisture checks (multiple daily)
- Hand-sorting during drying (continuous defect removal)
- Pre-mill sorting
- Post-mill sorting
- Cupping of every lot (quality verification)
- Lot traceability (station, date, member group)

**Processing Infrastructure** (Each Washing Station):
- Mechanical depulpers (disc pulpers)
- Fermentation tanks (concrete, multiple sizes)
- Washing channels (density sorting, grading)
- 30-50 raised African drying beds
- Covered drying areas (rain protection)
- Moisture meters and monitoring
- Cupping lab and roasting equipment
- Storage facilities (climate control)
- Quality control systems

**Double Fermentation** (Premium Lots):
Some exceptional lots receive extended processing:
- First fermentation: 12-18 hours
- Partial wash
- Second fermentation: 12-18 hours
- Final washing
- Creates enhanced complexity, sweetness, clarity
