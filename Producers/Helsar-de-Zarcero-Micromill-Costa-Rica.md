## üî¨ Processing Innovation (Micro-Mill Strength)

**Processing Methods Available**:

**1. Washed Process** (Traditional Costa Rican):
- Mechanical depulping
- 12-24 hour fermentation
- Multiple washing cycles
- Patio or raised bed drying
- Creates classic Costa Rican clean profile

**2. Honey Process Series** (Helsar Specialty):

**White Honey** (~10% mucilage):
- Minimal mucilage retained
- Close to washed
- Slight sweetness increase

**Yellow Honey** (~25% mucilage):
- Light mucilage layer
- Enhanced sweetness
- Fruit notes emerging
- Balanced acidity

**Red Honey** (~50% mucilage):
- Substantial mucilage retention
- Significant sweetness boost
- Stone fruit complexity
- Fuller body

**Black Honey** (~90-100% mucilage):
- Maximum mucilage retention
- Intense sweetness
- Dried fruit, raisin notes
- Full body, syrupy
- Longest drying (3-4 weeks)
- Most challenging processing

**3. Natural Process** (Experimental):
- Whole cherry drying
- Extended drying period
- Fruit-forward profile
- Limited production

**4. Anaerobic Fermentation**:
- Sealed tank fermentation
- Controlled atmosphere
- 48-96 hours
- Unique flavor development
- Premium micro-lots

**Processing Infrastructure**:
- Mechanical depulpers
- Fermentation tanks (multiple sizes)
- Drying patios (controlled environment)
- Raised African beds
- Greenhouse dryers
- Moisture monitoring equipment
- Cupping lab
- Quality control systems

**Honey Process Mastery**:
Costa Rica pioneered honey processing, and Helsar is recognized leader. The controlled mucilage retention creates spectrum of profiles while maintaining Costa Rican cleanliness.

## üìù Producer Story

Helsar de Zarcero was established in 2000 as part of Costa Rica's micro-mill revolution. Owned by Volcaf√© Specialty (ECOM), it operates as estate farm and processing innovation center in Zarcero's highlands (1,700-1,900m).

**The Micro-Mill Revolution**:
Before 2000, Costa Rican coffee system was centralized - farmers sold cherry to large cooperatives or mills. The micro-mill movement democratized processing:
- Farmers/estates control processing
- Experimentation with methods
- Direct relationships with buyers
- Value capture from processing
- Quality differentiation

**Honey Process Innovation**:
Costa Rica pioneered honey processing, and Helsar perfected it. By controlling mucilage retention (0% to 100%), they create spectrum of profiles:
- White/Yellow: Clean with sweetness boost
- Red: Balanced sweetness and fruit
- Black: Intense sweetness, dried fruit

Each requires different drying protocols, moisture management, and quality control. Helsar's mastery made them honey process reference.

**Processing Lab**:
Helsar functions as processing laboratory:
- Testing fermentation protocols
- Anaerobic experiments
- Drying method optimization
- Varietal-process matching
- Quality benchmarking

Results inform not just Helsar but broader Costa Rican coffee industry.

**Quality Focus**:
All processing innovation serves cup quality. Helsar cupping lab evaluates every lot, ensuring processing enhances rather than masks coffee quality. This quality-first approach maintains Costa Rica's clean-cup reputation while expanding flavor diversity.

**Current Operations**:
Helsar balances:
- Traditional washed (Costa Rican identity)
- Honey series (innovation and differentiation)
- Experimental processing (future development)
- Sustainability (environmental and social)
- Education (sharing knowledge)
