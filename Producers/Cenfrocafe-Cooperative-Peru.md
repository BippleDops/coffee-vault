## üî¨ Processing Methods

**Washed Process** (85% of production)

**Member-Level Processing**:

Most members process at home using traditional Peruvian washed method:

1. **Harvest & Sorting**:
   - Selective hand-picking (ripe cherry only)
   - Floatation in buckets (remove underripes, floaters)
   - Visual sorting (defect removal)

2. **Depulping** (Same day):
   - Small hand-crank or mechanical pulpers
   - Skin removed, mucilage retained
   - Parchment coffee (caf√© pergamino)

3. **Fermentation** (18-36 hours):
   - Parchment in buckets or small tanks
   - Natural enzymatic mucilage breakdown
   - Cool highland temperature = longer fermentation
   - Monitored by touch and smell

4. **Washing**:
   - Clean mountain/spring water
   - Multiple rinse cycles
   - Washing in buckets or channels
   - Final rinse until clean

5. **Drying** (7-14 days):
   - Patio drying (concrete slabs or tarps)
   - Some use parabolic dryers (plastic greenhouses)
   - Thin layers, regular turning
   - Hand-sorting during drying
   - Target moisture: 10-11%

6. **Delivery**:
   - Dry parchment delivered to cooperative
   - Quality assessment and payment
   - Moisture checked
   - Cupping sample taken

**Cooperative-Level Processing**:

7. **Reception & Quality Control**:
   - Parchment received from members
   - Moisture verification (10-11% required)
   - Physical defect screening
   - Cupping sample evaluation
   - Lot separation (quality, variety, community)

8. **Storage**:
   - Climate-controlled parchment storage
   - Inventory management
   - Traceability systems

9. **Dry Milling** (Pre-Export):
   - Hulling (parchment removal)
   - Screen grading (size sorting)
   - Density sorting (gravity tables)
   - Electronic sorting (color/defect removal)
   - Hand-sorting (final quality check)
   - Export-ready green coffee

**Alternative Processing** (15% - Growing):

**Natural Process**:
- Some members experiment with natural processing
- Whole cherry drying on patios/beds
- 18-25 days drying time
- Creates fruity, sweet profile
- Specialty roaster interest
- Premium pricing potential

**Honey Process**:
- Limited experimental lots
- Mucilage retained during drying
- Yellow/red honey variations
- Processing innovation
- Specialty market demand

**Cooperative Support**:
- Training in alternative processing
- Processing equipment support
- Quality feedback and guidance
- Market connections for experimental lots
- Incentivizing innovation

**Quality Control** (Multi-Level):

- **Village Level**: Local coordinators monitor quality
