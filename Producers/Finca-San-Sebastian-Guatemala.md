## üî¨ Processing Methods

San Sebasti√°n specializes in traditional washed processing with recent honey and natural process experimentation.

**Primary Processing - Washed** (85% of production):

**Washed Process Protocol**:
1. **Harvest**: Selective picking, ripe cherries only
2. **Reception**: Immediate sorting, defect removal
3. **Flotation**: Water flotation removes floaters (underripe, defects)
4. **Pulping**: Mechanical pulper, skin removal (within 6 hours of harvest)
5. **Fermentation**: Tiled tanks, 18-24 hours (temperature-dependent)
6. **Washing**: Clean water washing, multiple rinses
7. **Drying**: Patio drying 10-14 days, turned regularly
8. **Resting**: Parchment rest 30-45 days
9. **Milling**: Dry-milling before export

**Fermentation Precision**:
- Temperature monitoring (fermentation time adjusted)
- Cool nights = longer fermentation (24+ hours)
- Warm days = shorter fermentation (16-18 hours)
- Touch and smell testing (traditional knowledge)
- Goal: Complete mucilage breakdown, no over-fermentation

**Drying Protocol**:
- Patio drying primary method
- Thin layers (2-3cm depth)
- Turned 6-8 times daily
- Covered during hottest hours (prevents cracking)
- Night coverage (dew protection)
- Parabolic dryers backup (rain protection)
- Target moisture: 10-11%
- Slow drying (10-14 days) for quality

**Water Management**:
- Spring water used (clean, mineral-rich)
- Water recycling system
- Pulp composting (not water discharge)
- Wastewater treatment
- Zero contamination goal

**Secondary Processing - Honey & Natural** (15% experimental):

**Honey Process** (10%):
- Recent innovation (started 2018)
- Partial mucilage removal (yellow to red honey)
- Raised bed drying primarily
- Market differentiation
- Learning and experimentation

**Natural Process** (5%):
- Very limited (experimental lots only)
- Highest altitude Bourbon only
- Perfect weather required (dry season)
- Raised bed drying exclusively
- Slow drying (25-30 days)
- Competition lots

**Processing Infrastructure**:

- **Wet Mill**: Traditional gravity-fed system
- **Pulper**: Mechanical disk pulper
- **Fermentation**: 6 tiled tanks (500-1000 kg capacity each)
- **Washing**: Three-stage washing channels
- **Patios**: 1,500 sq meters concrete
- **Parabolic Dryers**: Weather backup
- **Raised Beds**: 50+ beds for honey/natural
- **Storage**: Climate-controlled warehouse
- **Laboratory**: Sample roaster, cupping, moisture testing

**Quality Control**:
- Immediate cherry sorting
- Flotation density separation
- Fermentation monitoring
- Drying moisture checks (multiple daily)
- Sample roasting at key stages
- Cupping before release
- Lot separation and traceability

**Processing Philosophy**:
"Washed processing is our heritage and strength. We perfect this tradition while carefully exploring honey and natural to offer diversity."
