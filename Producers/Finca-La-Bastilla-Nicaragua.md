## üî¨ Processing Methods (Innovation Laboratory)

La Bastilla operates as processing laboratory - constantly experimenting, sharing results.

**1. Washed Process** (40% of production)

**Traditional Nicaraguan Washed**:

Standard washed with quality focus:
1. Selective harvest ‚Üí 2. Floatation sorting ‚Üí 3. Depulping ‚Üí 4. Fermentation (12-24 hours) ‚Üí 5. Washing ‚Üí 6. Drying ‚Üí 7. Resting

**2. Honey Process Series** (30% - Specialty Focus)

Multiple honey levels:

**White Honey** (~10% mucilage):
- Minimal mucilage retention
- Slight sweetness enhancement

**Yellow Honey** (~25% mucilage):
- Light mucilage layer
- Enhanced sweetness
- Fruit notes emerging

**Red Honey** (~50% mucilage):
- Substantial mucilage
- Significant sweetness boost
- Fuller body

**Black Honey** (~90% mucilage):
- Maximum mucilage retention
- Intense sweetness
- Dried fruit character
- Syrupy body

**3. Natural Process** (20% - Growing)

Nicaragua's dry season ideal:
- Whole cherry drying
- 18-25 days
- Raised beds and patios
- Fruit-forward profile
- Competition lots

**4. Experimental Processing** (10% - Innovation Laboratory)

**Anaerobic Fermentation**:
- Sealed tanks, oxygen-free
- 48-120 hours controlled fermentation
- Temperature monitoring
- Unique flavor development
- Competition and experimental lots

**Carbonic Maceration**:
- Whole cherry, CO2-rich environment
- Wine production inspiration
- 72-96 hours
- Unique, complex profiles

**Yeast Inoculation**:
- Specific yeast strain inoculation
- Controlled fermentation environment
- Flavor profile manipulation
- Experimental lots

**Extended Fermentation**:
- 36-72 hour fermentation (vs standard 12-24)
- Enhanced complexity
- Careful monitoring

**Koji Fermentation**:
- Japanese koji mold inoculation
- Unique enzyme activity
- Experimental profiles
- Cutting-edge experimentation

**Double Fermentation**:
- First fermentation, partial wash, second fermentation
- Enhanced sweetness and complexity

**Variety-Specific Processing**:
- Pacamara: Specific protocols optimized
- Java: Processing to enhance unique character
- Maracaturra: Specialized handling
- Each variety matched to optimal processing

**Processing Infrastructure**:
- 2 mechanical depulpers
- 12+ fermentation tanks (various sizes, including sealed anaerobic)
- Temperature control systems (experimental fermentation)
- Drying infrastructure:
  - Concrete patios
  - 50+ raised African beds
  - Parabolic dryers
  - Mechanical dryers (backup)
- Moisture monitoring equipment
- Cupping lab and roasting (extensive - daily cupping)

**Early Generations** (1908-1979):

- Estate expanded
- Traditional cultivation
- Export to European markets
- Family prospered

**Sandinista Revolution** (1979):

Sandinista Revolution overthrew Somoza dictatorship:
- Large estates nationalized (including La Bastilla)
- Mierisch family lost estate
- Government control of coffee sector
- Family persevered elsewhere

**Contra War** (1980s):

Civil war devastated Nicaragua:
- Coffee production disrupted
- Infrastructure damaged
- Economic crisis

**Estate Return** (1990s):

Sandinistas voted out (1990):
- Estate privatization began
- Mierisch family regained estate (early 1990s)
- Rebuilding after war and government management

**Hurricane Mitch** (1998):

Category 5 hurricane devastated Nicaragua:
- Massive infrastructure destruction
