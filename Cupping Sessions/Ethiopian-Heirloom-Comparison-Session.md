## ðŸ“‹ Session Overview

This cupping session focused exclusively on Ethiopian natural-processed heirloom varieties from five distinct regions. The objective was to evaluate and compare the floral profiles characteristic of Ethiopian coffees while understanding how terroir, altitude, and processing station practices influence the expression of heirloom genetics.

**Session Goals**:
- Compare floral profiles across Ethiopian regions
- Evaluate natural process execution quality
- Identify terroir markers in heirloom varieties
- Assess genetic diversity expression in cup
- Understand altitude impact on complexity
- Evaluate processing station quality standards

**Context**: Ethiopia represents the birthplace of coffee with incredible genetic diversity. Heirloom varieties are often unclassified landraces that have evolved over centuries in specific microclimates. This session aimed to capture that diversity through systematic evaluation.

Harrar uses traditional processing methods distinct from other Ethiopian regions. Coffee is dried on the ground (not raised beds) using mats or cleared earth. Harrar coffees are known for wild, wine-like character with blueberry and dry fruit notes. The region has distinct heirloom genetics not found elsewhere in Ethiopia.

**Historical Context**:
Harrar is one of the world's oldest coffee trading regions - coffee from Harrar has been exported since the 1600s. Traditional processing methods have remained largely unchanged for centuries. Harrar's wild, funky character was historically prized in Yemen and Turkey for traditional coffee preparation.

- 8.0-8.75: Excellent
- 9.0-9.75: Outstanding
- 10.0: Extraordinary (rare)

**Categories Evaluated** (6.0-10.0 scale, 0.25 increments):
1. **Fragrance/Aroma** - Dry and wet aromatics
2. **Flavor** - Primary flavor characteristics and complexity
3. **Aftertaste** - Length and quality of finish
4. **Acidity** - Intensity, quality, and character
5. **Body** - Tactile mouthfeel and weight
6. **Balance** - How components work together
7. **Sweetness** - Scored 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, or 10 (2 points per clean cup)
8. **Clean Cup** - Scored 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, or 10 (2 points per clean cup)
9. **Uniformity** - Scored 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, or 10 (2 points per uniform cup)

**Defects**:
- Counted and severity assessed
- Taint (minor): -2 points per occurrence
- Fault (major): -4 points per occurrence

**Final Score Calculation**:
- Sum of all category scores
- Minus defects
- Total out of 100 points

### Processing Method Impact Study

**Central Question**: How does processing execution quality impact cup profile in Ethiopian naturals?

**Methodology Comparison**:

**Premium Modern Processing** (Chelelektu, Hambela):
- Cherry selection: 3+ sorting passes, float test, Brix measurement
- Drying: African raised beds, 18-28 days

- Night covering: Prevents moisture reabsorption
- Environment: Controlled humidity, optimal airflow
- Result: Clean, complex, fruit-forward without defects

**Standard Modern Processing** (Bombe, Limu):
- Cherry selection: 2 sorting passes, float test
- Drying: African raised beds, 15-24 days
- Turning frequency: Every 45-60 minutes
- Night covering: Standard practice
- Environment: Good airflow, variable humidity
- Result: Clean, balanced, minor uniformity variation

**Traditional Processing** (Harrar):
- Cherry selection: Single pass, visual only
- Drying: Ground/mat drying, 14-21 days
- Turning frequency: Periodic (not constant)
- Night covering: Not standard
- Environment: Variable conditions
- Result: Wild character, defects present, polarizing

**Findings**:
1. Processing execution matters more than we historically acknowledged
2. Modern raised-bed processing can produce "washed-clean" naturals
3. Traditional processing adds terroir-masking funk vs flavor
4. Investment in infrastructure (beds, covering, sorting) directly correlates to cup quality
