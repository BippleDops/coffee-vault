## üîÑ Maintenance & Care

### Routine Maintenance

**After Each Brew**:
- Remove filter: Dispose coffee grounds (compost-friendly)
- Rinse filter: Cold water (remove residue)
- Wash brew container: Soap and water (remove oils)
- Wash carafe: Soap and water (clean concentrate storage)
- Dry components: Air dry (prevent mold)
- Time required: 10-15 minutes

**Filter Care**:
- Rinse after each use: Cold water (extend lifespan)
- Occasional soap wash: Gentle soap, rinse thoroughly (every 3-4 uses)
- Dry between uses: Air dry (prevent mold, odor)
- Replace after: 10-12 uses (or when clogged/worn)
- Disposal: Compost or trash (natural materials)

**Deep Cleaning** (Monthly):
- Brew container: Soak in vinegar-water (remove coffee oils)
- Carafe: Dishwasher (glass version) or hand-wash (plastic)
- Rubber stopper: Soap wash (remove residue)
- Components: Dry thoroughly (prevent mold)
- Time required: 20-30 minutes

**Storage** (Between Batches):
- Dry storage: Store components dry (prevent mold)
- Carafe: Refrigerate concentrate (airtight, 2-3 weeks)
- Brew container: Store empty, dry (closet or pantry)

**Common Issues & Solutions**:
- **Slow draining**: Filter clogged (replace filter), grind too fine (use coarser grind)
- **Weak concentrate**: Insufficient brew time (extend to 18-24 hours), too little coffee (increase ratio)
- **Bitter concentrate**: Over-extraction (reduce brew time to 16-18 hours), grind too fine
- **Sediment in concentrate**: Filter worn (replace), didn't rinse properly

## üéì Skill Level & Learning Curve

**Recommended Skill Level**: Beginner

**Beginner Suitability**:
- Excellent first cold brew system
- Simple operation (add coffee, add water, wait)
- No technique required (passive brewing)
- Forgiving (hard to mess up)
- Success rate: 95%+ first attempt

**Operation Steps**:
1. Rinse felt filter (cold water)
2. Place filter in brew container bottom
3. Dampen filter (helps seal)
4. Add coarse ground coffee (12-16oz, ~350-450g)
5. Add room temperature water (slowly, saturate grounds)
6. Fill to ~1 inch from top (~48oz water)
7. Cover (plastic wrap or lid if available)
8. Let brew 12-24 hours (room temp or refrigerate)
9. Remove rubber stopper (gravity drains through filter)
10. Wait 15-30 minutes (complete draining)
11. Discard grounds and filter
12. Store concentrate in carafe (refrigerate)
13. Dilute to taste (1:1 or 1:2 concentrate:water/milk)

**Coffee-to-Water Ratio**:
- Standard: 1:4 (12oz coffee to 48oz water)
- Stronger: 1:3 (16oz coffee to 48oz water)
- Weaker: 1:5 (10oz coffee to 50oz water)
- Experimentation: Adjust to preference

**Dilution Ratios**:
- 1:1 (concentrate:water): Standard strength cold brew
- 1:2 (concentrate:water): Lighter, more servings
- Over ice: Use less dilution (ice melts, further dilutes)
- With milk: 1:1 or 1:1.5 (concentrate:milk)

**Skills Developed**:
- Coffee-to-water ratios (concentrate brewing)
- Dilution adjustment (customization)
- Grind size awareness (coarse grind importance)
- Batch planning (brew ahead discipline)

**Learning Resources**:
- Filtron manual (included, simple instructions)
- YouTube: "Filtron cold brew tutorial"
- Coffee forums: Cold brew ratio discussions

**Common Beginner Mistakes**:
- Grind too fine (slow draining, sediment, bitter)
- Insufficient brew time (<12 hours, weak concentrate)
- Over-extraction (>24 hours, bitter)
- Not rinsing filter (previous batch residue affects flavor)
- Forgetting to dilute (drinking concentrate straight - very strong)

**Mastery Timeline**:
- Week 1: First successful batch
- Week 2-4: Ratio experimentation, find preferred strength
- Month 2-3: Dilution mastery, consistent results
- Month 4+: Second nature, batch planning routine
