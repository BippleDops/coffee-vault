## üîÑ Maintenance & Care

### Routine Maintenance

**Daily Cleaning** (after each use):
- Rinse portafilter and baskets (hot water)
- Wipe group head gasket (damp cloth)
- Wipe steam wand (immediately after steaming)
- Purge steam wand (before and after steaming)
- Empty drip tray (if indicator shows full)
- Wipe exterior (microfiber cloth)
- Time required: 2-3 minutes

**Weekly Cleaning** (regular use):
- Deep clean portafilter and baskets (Cafetto soak)
- Soak panarello frother (remove scale/milk)
- Clean drip tray thoroughly (wash with soap)
- Wipe water tank (prevent algae growth)
- Clean steam wand tip (descale if needed)
- Backflush: Not applicable (no blind basket capability)
- Time required: 10-15 minutes

**Monthly Maintenance**:
- Replace water in tank (fresh water prevents buildup)
- Check gasket condition (wear, cracks, compression)
- Inspect steam wand o-rings (milk residue)
- Clean water tank thoroughly (remove and wash)
- Wipe internal drip tray area (hidden buildup)
- Check for leaks around group head
- Test auto-shutoff function
- Time required: 15-20 minutes

**Descaling Schedule**:
- Soft water: Every 3-4 months
- Medium water: Every 2-3 months
- Hard water: Every 1-2 months
- DeLonghi recommends: Every 300 shots or 3 months
- Descaling solution: DeLonghi EcoDecalk or citric acid
- Process time: 30-45 minutes
- Critical for: Temperature stability, longevity, pump health

**Descaling Procedure**:
1. Fill tank with descaling solution + water mix
2. Run solution through group head (500ml)
3. Run solution through steam wand (200ml)
4. Let solution sit 15-20 minutes (loosens scale)
5. Run remaining solution through both outputs
6. Rinse tank thoroughly (3 times)
7. Fill with fresh water, run 2 full tanks through system
8. Test shot for chemical taste (rinse more if needed)
9. Total process: 35-50 minutes

**Gasket Replacement**:
- Frequency: Every 12-18 months (heavy use), 24 months (light use)
- Signs needed: Coffee leaking around portafilter, loose lock
- Part number: 51mm silicone gasket (DeLonghi 5313217751 or generic)
- Cost: $5-$8
- Difficulty: Easy (screwdriver, 5 minutes)
- Procedure: Remove screw plate, pop out old gasket, insert new, reassemble

**Common Replacement Parts**:
- Group head gasket: $5-$8, yearly
- Shower screen: $8-$12, every 2-3 years
- Steam wand o-rings: $3-$5, every 1-2 years
- Water tank: $25-$35, if cracked (rare)
- Drip tray parts: $15-$25, if broken
- Portafilter basket: $10-$15, if damaged
- Panarello frother: $8-$12, if lost/damaged

**Deep Cleaning Products**:
- Cafetto Espresso Clean: $12-$15 (backflush powder - not applicable)
- Cafetto MFC Green: $10-$12 (milk frother cleaner - recommended)
- DeLonghi EcoDecalk: $12-$15 (descaler, 2-pack)
- Citric acid: $8-$10 (budget descaler alternative)
- Cleaning brushes: $10-$15 (group head brush, steam wand brush)
- Microfiber cloths: $8-$12 (set of 3-5)

**Water Quality Management**:
- Filtered water recommended (Brita or similar)
- Avoid: Distilled water (no minerals for proper extraction)
- Avoid: Very hard water (rapid scale buildup)
- Ideal: 50-150 ppm TDS (total dissolved solids)
- DeLonghi water filter: SER3017 ($12-$15, lasts 2 months)
- Filter benefits: Reduced descaling frequency, better taste
- Third Wave Water: Compatible (brewing optimization)

**Storage & Non-Use**:
- Long-term storage (1+ months): Empty water tank completely
- Descale before storage (prevent scale hardening)
- Store in dry location (prevent moisture/mold)
- Run cleaning cycle after storage (before first use)
- Winter RV storage: Ensure all water evacuated (freeze protection)

**Troubleshooting Maintenance Issues**:
- Slow flow / no flow: Descale immediately (clogged thermoblock)
- Leaking portafilter: Replace gasket
- Weak steam: Descale, check steam wand for clogs
- No power: Check outlet, thermal cutoff may have tripped (let cool)
- Pump running but no water: Refill tank, prime pump (run empty shot)
- Spitting group head: Descale needed
- Sour/off taste: Deep clean, rinse thoroughly, check water freshness

**Professional Servicing**:
- DeLonghi service centers: Limited (especially North America)
- Authorized repair: $80-$150 typical
- Worthiness: Questionable (machine cost $280-$350)
- DIY repair: Possible for adventurous users (YouTube tutorials)
- Common failures: Thermoblock failure (5-7 years), pump failure (3-5 years)
- Repair vs replace: Usually replace (economics favor new machine)

**Lifespan Expectations**:
- Light use (1 shot/day): 7-10 years potential
- Medium use (2-3 shots/day): 5-7 years typical
- Heavy use (5+ shots/day): 3-5 years typical
- Critical factor: Descaling adherence (proper maintenance extends life significantly)
- Failure modes: Thermoblock failure, pump failure, electronics
- Maintenance impact: Proper care can double lifespan

### Popular Modifications

**Non-Pressurized Basket Upgrade** (Essential):
- Cost: $25-$40
- Difficulty: Easy (direct replacement)
- Impact: Transformative (true espresso extraction)

**OPV (Over Pressure Valve) Modification**:
- Cost: $0-$5 (adjustment only) or $15-$25 (replacement spring)
- Difficulty: Moderate (requires disassembly)
- Impact: Significant (reduces pressure from 15 bar to 9-10 bar)
- Benefit: Better extraction, less channeling, improved flavor
- Process: Adjust existing OPV screw or replace spring
- Requires: Basic tools (screwdrivers), YouTube tutorial
- Risk: Warranty void, potential damage if done incorrectly
- Recommended for: Advanced users comfortable with DIY
- Testing: Pressure gauge portafilter ($40-$60) to verify results

**Panarello Removal** (Free):
- Cost: $0 (remove existing sleeve)
- Difficulty: Trivial (unscrews or pulls off)
- Impact: Moderate to Significant (manual steaming capability)
- Benefit: Microfoam possible, latte art achievable (with skill)
- Drawback: Requires manual steaming technique learning
- Process: Unscrew panarello sleeve, expose single-hole tip
- Recommended for: All users after mastering basic operation (1-2 months)
- Requires: Milk steaming pitcher ($15-$30), thermometer ($8-$15)

**Better Tamper Upgrade**:
- Cost: $20-$60
- Difficulty: Easy (direct replacement)
- Impact: Moderate (more consistent tamping)
- Options: 51mm flat tamper (Motta, Rattleware, Concept Art)
- Stock tamper: Plastic, lightweight, poor ergonomics
- Upgrade benefits: Even distribution, comfortable grip, better results
- Recommended: $25-$35 range (Motta or similar)
- Premium options: Pullman BigStep 51mm ($85) - overkill for Dedica

**Precision Basket** (IMS/VST):
- Cost: $25-$40
- Difficulty: Easy (drop-in replacement)
- Impact: Significant (more even extraction)
- Options: IMS Competition basket (most common for 51mm)
- Benefits: Precision holes, even flow, reduced channeling
- Compatible: Requires non-pressurized technique
- Recommended timing: Same as non-pressurized upgrade
- Pairs with: Quality grinder, proper distribution tool

**Distribution Tool / WDT**:
- Cost: $8-$40
- Difficulty: Easy (add to workflow)
- Impact: Moderate (reduces channeling)
- Options: BPlus WDT tool ($8-$15), 3D printed options, DIY acupuncture needles
- Benefit: Breaks up clumps, evens coffee bed, better extraction
- Technique: Stir grounds in basket before tamping
- Recommended for: Non-pressurized users (essential), pressurized users (unnecessary)

- Cost: $35-$60
