# Third Wave Water Classic Profile

## Overview

Third Wave Water represents a paradigm shift in approaching coffee brewing water, transforming it from an overlooked variable into a precisely controlled parameter. The Classic Profile mineral capsules, introduced in 2016, provide specialty coffee enthusiasts with a simple solution to a complex problem: creating optimal brewing water with controlled mineral content, total dissolved solids (TDS), and buffering capacity regardless of source water quality.

Water constitutes 98-99% of brewed coffee, yet home brewers historically paid minimal attention to water chemistry beyond basic filtration. Third Wave Water emerged from recognition that water mineral content profoundly impacts extraction efficiency, flavor balance, and consistency. The Classic Profile targets the SCA (Specialty Coffee Association) water quality standards, delivering 75-150 ppm TDS with balanced hardness and alkalinity that optimizes extraction across brewing methods while preventing scale buildup in equipment.

The product concept is elegantly simple: single-serving capsules containing precisely measured mineral blends that dissolve into distilled or reverse osmosis water, creating ideal brewing water. This approach bypasses the complexity of water testing, mineral measurement, and chemistry knowledge that previously made water optimization accessible only to advanced enthusiasts or professionals. At $15 for 12 capsules (making 12 gallons of water), the cost per gallon (~$1.25) positions Third Wave Water as a specialty consumable justified by extraction improvement and equipment protection.

The Classic Profile specifically balances magnesium sulfate (extraction catalyst), calcium citrate (flavor enhancement and mild buffering), and sodium bicarbonate (pH buffering) to create water chemistry that extracts coffee efficiently while maintaining flavor clarity. This formulation works universally across brewing methods from espresso to immersion brewing, making it a versatile solution for enthusiasts seeking water optimization without method-specific customization.

Third Wave Water's impact extends beyond individual consumers—specialty cafes, competition baristas, and coffee professionals use the product for consistency and quality control. Its presence in multiple World Barista Championship routines and widespread adoption in specialty cafes validates the approach and underscores water chemistry's importance in coffee quality.

### Product Development and Design Philosophy

Third Wave Water's development centered on balancing scientific rigor with practical accessibility. The mineral profile needed to satisfy chemistry requirements while remaining simple enough for non-technical users to implement successfully.

**Mineral Selection and Ratios:**

The Classic Profile combines three primary minerals:

**Magnesium Sulfate (Epsom Salt):** Provides magnesium ions critical for coffee extraction. Magnesium enhances extraction of desirable compounds including caffeine, acids, and flavor precursors. Without adequate magnesium, extraction remains incomplete even with proper brewing technique. The sulfate component contributes subtle flavor enhancement and doesn't significantly buffer pH.

**Calcium Citrate:** Supplies calcium ions that contribute to extraction and mouthfeel while providing mild buffering capacity. Calcium helps extract certain flavor compounds and creates perceived body in brewed coffee. The citrate form provides better solubility than calcium carbonate while contributing less alkalinity than carbonate forms.

**Sodium Bicarbonate (Baking Soda):** Provides buffering capacity to prevent excessive acidity in brewed coffee. Coffee naturally produces acids during extraction; without buffering, coffee can taste thin, sour, and unbalanced. Sodium bicarbonate raises buffering capacity (measured as alkalinity) to optimal levels for flavor balance.

The ratio of these minerals was optimized through extensive testing to create water chemistry meeting SCA standards while delivering excellent extraction across brewing methods and coffee types. The formulation avoids excessive mineralization that would cause scale buildup or flavor distortion while ensuring sufficient mineral content for effective extraction.

**Target Water Chemistry:**

The Classic Profile aims for:
- **TDS (Total Dissolved Solids):** 75-150 ppm (ideal range for coffee extraction)
- **Hardness:** 50-75 ppm (sufficient for extraction without excessive scale)
- **Alkalinity:** 40-70 ppm (balanced buffering without flavor suppression)
- **pH:** 6.5-7.5 (neutral to slightly alkaline, prevents corrosion)

These parameters align with SCA recommendations and research from coffee science organizations. The range allows some variation based on source water starting point while ensuring results remain within optimal extraction window.

**Capsule Format Design:**

The single-serving stick capsule format provides several advantages:

**Precision:** Each capsule contains exactly the mineral quantity needed for one gallon of water, eliminating measurement errors and ensuring consistency.

**Convenience:** Users simply add capsule to distilled water and shake—no weighing, measuring, or calculation required.

**Storage Stability:** Sealed capsules prevent moisture absorption and mineral degradation, maintaining potency for 24+ months.

**Portability:** Individual capsules can be taken traveling for consistent water anywhere, important for competition baristas and traveling enthusiasts.

**Prevention of Segregation:** Mineral blends in bulk form can separate with handling and storage. Single-serving capsules ensure each dose contains the correct ratio.

### How It Works

**Basic Usage Process:**

1. Purchase distilled water or produce reverse osmosis water (starting point must be very low TDS, ideally <10 ppm)
2. Pour water into clean container (gallon jug, large bottle, etc.)
3. Add one Third Wave Water Classic capsule to gallon of water
4. Shake or stir to dissolve minerals completely (typically 2-5 minutes)
5. Wait 5-10 minutes for complete dissolution and equilibration
6. Use mineralized water for brewing coffee through any method
7. Store remaining water in sealed container for future brewing

**Science Behind the Approach:**

Coffee extraction involves water dissolving soluble compounds from ground coffee. The efficiency and selectivity of this extraction depend significantly on water chemistry:

**Mineral Content and Extraction:** Pure water (distilled or RO) actually extracts coffee poorly because it lacks the ionic strength to efficiently dissolve many flavor compounds. Minerals in water, particularly magnesium and calcium, act as extraction catalysts by providing ionic environment that promotes compound solubility. Studies show that water with optimal mineral content extracts 15-25% more desirable compounds than pure water under identical brewing conditions.

**Buffering and Flavor Balance:** Coffee brewing produces acidic compounds that lower pH. Water with appropriate buffering capacity (alkalinity) neutralizes excess acidity, creating balanced flavor. Too little buffering results in sour, sharp coffee; too much buffering suppresses desirable brightness and creates flat, dull flavor. The Classic Profile's alkalinity targets the sweet spot for most coffees and roast levels.

**Equipment Protection:** While optimized for flavor, the mineral content also considers equipment longevity. Excessively hard water causes scale deposits in boilers, pipes, and heat exchangers, reducing efficiency and requiring maintenance. The Classic Profile's controlled hardness provides extraction benefits while maintaining scale risk at manageable levels. Periodic descaling remains necessary but frequency decreases compared to very hard source water.

### Source Water Requirements

**Optimal Starting Water:**
- Distilled Water: Ideal (TDS <5 ppm typically)
- Reverse Osmosis (RO): Excellent (TDS <10 ppm)
- Deionized Water: Suitable (TDS <5 ppm)
- Filtered Tap: NOT recommended (existing minerals interfere)
- Spring Water: NOT recommended (variable mineral content)

**Why Starting Water Matters:**

Third Wave Water formulation assumes near-zero starting TDS. Using water with existing mineral content creates unpredictable results:
- Source water TDS adds to capsule minerals
- Resulting TDS may exceed optimal range
- Mineral ratios become unbalanced (source water minerals not in optimal proportions)
- Consistency impossible (tap water chemistry varies seasonally and regionally)

## Research & Documentation

### Water Chemistry Fundamentals

**TDS (Total Dissolved Solids):**
- Measurement of all dissolved minerals
- Optimal range for coffee: 75-250 ppm (SCA), TWW targets 75-150 ppm
- Too low: Weak extraction, flat flavor
- Too high: Over-extraction, bitterness, equipment scale

**Hardness:**
- Calcium and magnesium content
- Affects extraction efficiency and mouthfeel
- Optimal: 50-175 ppm, TWW provides 50-75 ppm
- Too high: Scale buildup, over-extraction
- Too low: Poor extraction, thin body

**Alkalinity:**
- Buffering capacity (typically bicarbonate/carbonate)
- Prevents excessive acidity in brewed coffee
- Optimal: 40-75 ppm, TWW delivers 40-70 ppm
- Too high: Flat, dull coffee (suppresses brightness)
- Too low: Sour, sharp, unbalanced coffee

**pH:**
- Acidity/alkalinity measure
- Optimal: 6.5-7.5 for brewing water
- Affects extraction and equipment corrosion
- TWW achieves neutral to slightly alkaline pH

### Technical Notes

**Using Third Wave Water Effectively:**

**Dissolution Process:**
1. Start with distilled or RO water (TDS <10 ppm critical)
2. Pour water into clean container (gallon jug ideal)
3. Add entire capsule contents
4. Shake vigorously for 1-2 minutes
5. Let rest 5-10 minutes for complete dissolution
6. Shake again before using
7. Water ready for brewing

**Storage:**
- Store mineralized water in sealed container
- Use within 1-2 weeks for best results
- Longer storage acceptable but may develop slight off-taste
- Refrigeration extends storage life

**Troubleshooting:**

**Cloudy Water After Dissolution:**
- Normal initially, clears within minutes
- Calcium citrate may take longer to fully dissolve
- Shake periodically if cloudiness persists
- Doesn't affect performance if residual cloudiness exists

**Results Don't Match Expectations:**
- Verify starting with distilled/RO water (most common error)
- Test source water TDS to confirm <10 ppm
- Check coffee freshness and quality (water can't fix bad coffee)
- Evaluate brewing technique (water optimizes good technique)

**Too Much Mineralization:**
- Don't use multiple capsules per gallon
- Don't add to already-mineralized water
- Don't use with filtered tap water
