### Usage Techniques

**Basic V60 Technique**:
1. Rinse filter with hot water (preheat and remove paper taste)
2. Add coffee grounds (medium-fine grind)
3. Create well in center (optional, helps bloom)
4. Start timer, pour bloom water (2x coffee weight)
5. Wait 30-45 seconds (bloom)
6. Pour remaining water in stages or continuously
7. Target total time: 2:30-3:30 (varies by recipe)
8. Enjoy clean, complex cup

**Common Mistakes**:
1. Grind too coarse (under-extraction, sour, weak)
2. Grind too fine (over-extraction, bitter, astringent)
3. Water too hot (>96Â°C can scald)
4. Water too cool (<88Â°C under-extracts)
5. Uneven pouring (channeling, uneven extraction)
6. Too fast (under-extracted)
7. Too slow (over-extracted)
8. Not rinsing filter (papery taste)
9. Wrong coffee dose (weak or strong)
10. Old coffee (stale, flat flavor)

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Sour/under-extracted**: Grind finer, use hotter water, pour slower
- **Bitter/over-extracted**: Grind coarser, use cooler water, pour faster
- **Weak**: More coffee, grind finer, slower pour
- **Too strong**: Less coffee, grind coarser, faster pour
- **Astringent**: Lower water temp, grind coarser
- **Flat**: Fresher beans, check grind uniformity
