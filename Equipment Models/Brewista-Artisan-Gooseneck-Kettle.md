### Daily Pour-Over Workflow

**Morning V60 Routine**:
1. **Fill kettle**: Add 600ml fresh filtered water
2. **Place on stove**: Gas/electric/induction, medium-high heat
3. **Monitor thermometer**: Watch needle rise (glance every minute)
4. **Prepare coffee**: While heating, grind 15g coffee, set up V60
5. **Target temp**: When thermometer hits 93-95¬∞C, remove from heat
6. **Rinse filter**: Pour small amount through filter (warm V60, rinse paper)
7. **Add coffee**: Dump rinse water, add grounds
8. **Bloom**: Pour 30-40g water, wait 30-45 seconds
9. **Main pour**: Slow spiral to 250g total (monitor timer/scale)
10. **Clean kettle**: Rinse kettle, dry, store

**Temperature Targeting**:
- **Light roasts**: 94-96¬∞C (201-205¬∞F) - extract delicate acids
- **Medium roasts**: 92-94¬∞C (198-201¬∞F) - balanced
- **Dark roasts**: 88-92¬∞C (190-198¬∞F) - avoid over-extraction bitterness
- **Technique**: Remove from heat 1-2¬∞C before target (carryover heat)

### Maintenance Workflow

**Daily Care**:
- **After use**: Rinse with clean water
- **Dry**: Wipe exterior, dry interior (prevent mineral buildup)
- **Lid**: Remove lid, dry thermometer area (prevent fogging)
- **Storage**: Store with lid off (air circulation prevents moisture)

**Weekly Descaling** (hard water areas):
1. **Fill kettle**: 50% water, 50% white vinegar
2. **Heat**: Bring to gentle simmer (60-70¬∞C, not full boil)
3. **Soak**: Let sit 15-30 minutes (dissolve mineral deposits)
4. **Scrub**: Soft brush or cloth, scrub interior
5. **Rinse**: Multiple rinses with fresh water (remove vinegar smell)
6. **Boil**: One full boil with fresh water, dump (final rinse)

**Thermometer Care**:
- **Avoid submersion**: Don't submerge kettle underwater (dishwasher)
- **Gentle cleaning**: Wipe dial exterior with damp cloth
- **Fogging**: If fogged, let dry completely (usually clears)
- **Replacement**: If accuracy drifts >5¬∞C, replace thermometer ($15-20)

### Troubleshooting

**Thermometer reads incorrectly**:
- **Test**: Boil water, check if thermometer hits 100¬∞C (212¬∞F)
- **Variance**: ¬±3-5¬∞C acceptable, >5¬∞C = replace
- **Replacement**: Order replacement thermometer from Brewista
- **Workaround**: Use separate digital thermometer until replacement arrives

**Handle gets hot**:
- **Cause**: Extended boiling (>15 minutes)
- **Solution**: Use towel or glove, remove from heat promptly
- **Prevention**: Heat to target, remove immediately (don't leave on stove)

**Mineral deposits buildup**:
- **White residue**: Mineral deposits from hard water
- **Solution**: Vinegar descaling (weekly if hard water)
- **Prevention**: Use filtered water (less minerals)

**Spout drips after pouring**:
- **Cause**: Surface tension, normal for gooseneck
- **Solution**: Quick upward tilt at end of pour (stops drip)
- **Prevention**: Pour technique (practice smooth finish)

## üéì Tips & Techniques

### Optimizing Temperature Control

**Precision Targeting**:
- **Overshoot compensation**: Remove from heat 1-2¬∞C before target (carryover brings to target)
- **Example**: Target 93¬∞C, remove at 91-92¬∞C (coasts to 93¬∞C)
- **Induction advantage**: Reduce power at 85¬∞C (slower final 10¬∞C heating = more precision)

**Temperature Surfing** (Electric Stove):
- **Heat to 95-97¬∞C**: Slightly above target
- **Let cool**: Remove from heat, wait 2-3 minutes
- **Check thermometer**: When hits target (e.g., 93¬∞C), pour immediately
- **Result**: Precise temperature without constant monitoring

**Maintaining Temperature** (Induction):
- **Heat to target**: Bring to 93¬∞C
- **Reduce power**: Set induction to low (200-300W)
- **Hold temp**: Kettle maintains 90-95¬∞C for 10-15 minutes
- **Simulate hold function**: Like electric kettle hold temp (manual monitoring)

### Pour Control Mastery

**Beginner Pour-Over Technique**:
1. **Start low**: Spout close to grounds (5-10cm above)
2. **Slow pour**: Gentle tilt, 20-30 ml/second flow
3. **Spiral pattern**: Center outward spiral (even saturation)
4. **Maintain height**: Keep spout 5-10cm above (consistent flow)
5. **Practice**: Empty kettle practice (water into sink, develop muscle memory)

**Advanced Pouring**:
- **Variable flow**: Fast initial wetting, slow spiral, fast final pour
- **Height control**: Higher = faster flow, lower = slower, more control
- **Pulse pouring**: Pour in pulses (40g, wait 30s, repeat)
- **Spot targeting**: Hit dry spots, avoid over-saturated areas

**Troubleshooting Pour Issues**:
- **Splashing**: Pouring too high or too fast (lower spout, slower tilt)
- **Uneven extraction**: Rushed pour (slow down, be deliberate)
- **Channeling**: Uneven saturation (spiral from center, ensure all grounds wet)

### Battery Life & Energy Efficiency

**Gas Stove Efficiency**:
- **Medium-high heat**: Fastest without wasting gas (high flame wraps around, wastes energy)
- **Lid on**: Heats 20-30% faster (retain heat)
- **Right burner size**: Match burner to kettle base (140mm base = small/medium burner)

**Induction Efficiency**:
- **Best efficiency**: 85-90% energy transfer (vs. gas 40-50%)
- **Power setting**: High (1800W) to 85¬∞C, then medium (900W) to target
- **Energy use**: ~0.08 kWh to boil 1L (vs. gas ~0.15 kWh equivalent)
- **Cost**: $0.01 per boil (varies by region)

**Electric Coil**:
- **Slower**: 9-11 minutes (least efficient stovetop option)
- **Recommendation**: Use induction if available, or gas (electric coil slowest)
