### Optimal Milk Steaming Technique

**Milk Volume** (20 oz pitcher):
- Fill to 1/3 to 1/2 full (8-10 oz milk for 20 oz pitcher)
- Allows room for milk expansion (doubles in volume during steaming)
- Too little: Difficult to create vortex (milk spins out of pitcher)
- Too much: Overflows during frothing

**Temperature Control**:
- Target: 140-150Â°F (60-65Â°C) for optimal sweetness/texture
- Hand on pitcher: When too hot to touch comfortably (5-7 seconds), stop steaming
- Thermometer (beginners): 140-150Â°F (remove at 130-135Â°F, milk continues to heat)
- Over-temp: >160Â°F burns milk (proteins denature, taste scalded)

**Microfoam Texture**:
- Goal: Velvety microfoam (wet paint consistency)
- Aeration phase: 2-3 seconds (introduce air, creates foam)
- Texturing phase: 5-10 seconds (create vortex, incorporate air)
- Result: Glossy, pourable microfoam (no large bubbles)

**Latte Art Pouring**:
- Hold pitcher high: Thin stream (base layer, doesn't disturb crema)
- Lower pitcher: Thick stream (white foam surfaces, creates patterns)
- Spout control: Angle pitcher to control flow rate
- Wrist motion: Small wrist movements create rosetta, tulip details

### Common Mistakes

**Overfilling Pitcher**:
- **Issue**: Milk overflows during frothing (mess, wasted milk)
- **Solution**: Fill to 1/3 to 1/2 full only (allows expansion room)

**Over-Aerating Milk**:
- **Issue**: Dry, stiff foam (cappuccino-style, not latte art microfoam)
- **Solution**: Aerate 2-3 seconds only (brief "chirping" sound)

**Under-Texturing Milk**:
- **Issue**: Large bubbles (not microfoam), separate foam and milk
- **Solution**: Create vortex (swirling motion), incorporate air thoroughly

**Burning Milk**:
- **Issue**: Milk >160Â°F tastes scalded (proteins denature)
- **Solution**: Stop steaming at 140-150Â°F (hand on pitcher guideline)

**Pouring Too Fast**:
- **Issue**: Milk splashes into espresso (disrupts crema, no pattern)
- **Solution**: Start with high, thin stream (gentle, controlled pour)

### Maintenance

**After Each Use**:
- Rinse pitcher with hot water immediately (prevents milk residue drying)
- Wipe with cloth (remove external milk drips)
- Air dry or towel dry

**Daily** (home use) or **Hourly** (cafe use):
- Rinse with dish soap (remove milk oils)
- Rinse thoroughly (no soap residue)
- Dry with towel

**Weekly**:
- Dishwasher clean (top rack, high heat)
- Deep clean (Urnex Rinza soak for stubborn milk residue)
- Inspect spout (ensure no milk buildup narrowing spout)
