## üîç Technical Details & Deep Dive

### Alignment Precision

**What is Burr Alignment?**:
- **Concept**: Parallelism between burr faces (flat, even gap)
- **Importance**: Even extraction (all beans cut uniformly)
- **Poor alignment**: Channeling, uneven extraction, off flavors
- **EG-1 alignment**: ¬±5 microns (industry-leading)

**How EG-1 Achieves Alignment**:
1. **Precision machining**: CNC machined burr carriers (¬±0.01mm tolerance)
2. **No shims**: Design eliminates need for shimming (precision manufacturing)
3. **Factory calibration**: Each grinder measured, calibrated
4. **Calibration certificate**: Actual alignment data included (transparency)

**Comparison to Other Grinders**:
- **EG-1**: ¬±5 microns
- **Niche Zero**: ¬±50-100 microns (10-20x worse, still good for price)
- **DF64**: ¬±100-200 microns (user shimming can improve to ¬±20-50 microns)
- **Baratza Sette**: ¬±200-300 microns (adequate for home use)
- **Commercial grinders**: ¬±10-50 microns (EG-1 equals or exceeds)

**Impact on Coffee**:
- **Flavor clarity**: Better alignment = clearer flavors
- **Sweetness**: Even extraction = balanced sweetness
- **Consistency**: Shot-to-shot repeatability
- **Dial-in**: Easier, faster (predictable results)

### Single-Dose Optimization

**What is Single-Dosing?**:
- **Concept**: Weigh beans before grinding (no hopper storage)
- **Benefits**: Fresh beans every shot, no retention mixing, precise dosing
- **EG-1 design**: Optimized for single-dose (low retention, funnel hopper)

**How EG-1 Excels at Single-Dosing**:
1. **Ultra-low retention**: 0.05-0.2g (99%+ output)
2. **No hopper**: Funnel design (no stale beans in hopper)
3. **Straight grind path**: Hopper ‚Üí burrs ‚Üí cup (minimal retention points)
4. **Anti-static design**: Metal construction (grounds don't cling)
5. **Fast grinding**: 10-15 seconds per dose (quick workflow)

**Comparison to Hopper-Style Grinders**:
- **Hopper grinders**: 2-10g retention (old grounds mixing with new)

- **EG-1**: No purging needed (retention so low, negligible impact)

**Single-Dose Workflow Benefits**:

## üéì Learning & Education

### Getting Started with EG-1

**Unboxing & Setup** (first-time):
1. **Unbox**: Beautiful wooden crate (save for storage)
2. **Inspect**: Calibration certificate, included accessories
3. **Placement**: Sturdy counter (18kg, stable surface)
4. **Power**: Plug in (grounded outlet)
5. **Test grind**: Run 10-20g beans (season burrs, verify operation)
6. **Read manual**: Comprehensive guide (adjustment, maintenance)

**Initial Dial-In** (espresso):
1. **Start at 10-15**: Mid-espresso range (factory starting point)
2. **Grind 18g**: Dose into portafilter
3. **Pull shot**: Aim for 25-30 seconds, 36g yield
4. **Taste**: Adjust by ~1-2 on dial (stepless, small increments)
5. **Repeat**: 1-3 shots to perfection (low retention = fast feedback)
6. **Lock collar**: Prevent drift once dialed in

**Burr Break-In** (new grinder):
- **First 10 lbs**: Burrs seasoning (flavor improves over first weeks)
- **Full performance**: 20-30 lbs (burrs fully seated)
- **Don't judge early**: Give grinder 5-10 lbs before final assessment

### Advanced Techniques

**Micro-Dialing** (seasonal adjustments):
- **Ambient temp**: Colder = slightly coarser (beans grind finer in cold)
- **Bean age**: Older = slightly finer (degassing complete, less resistance)
- **Humidity**: Higher = slightly coarser (moisture affects grind)
- **EG-1 advantage**: Stepless adjustment (infinite precision for micro-changes)

**Burr Swapping** (seasonal or preference):
1. **Turn off, unplug**: Safety first
2. **Remove hopper funnel**: Access burr carrier
3. **Remove burr carrier screws**: Allen key, 3-6 screws
4. **Lift out burr carrier**: Careful (heavy, don't drop)
5. **Swap burr**: Remove old, install new (align tabs)
6. **Reassemble**: Reverse process, torque screws evenly
7. **Re-dial**: New burrs, new grind setting (start fresh)
8. **Time**: 15-20 minutes (easy, user-serviceable)

**RPM Optimization**:
- **Espresso** (SSP burrs): 150-200 RPM (balance speed + quality)
- **Filter coffee**: 200-250 RPM (faster acceptable, less grind-sensitive)
- **Light roasts**: Slower RPM (cooler, gentler, preserve delicate flavors)
- **Dark roasts**: Faster RPM (less heat-sensitive, harder beans)

### Maintenance Mastery

**Daily**:
- Brush out burrs (soft brush, 10 seconds)
- Wipe funnel (remove chaff, oils)
- Empty dosing cup (grounds don't sit)

**Weekly**:
- Vacuum burr chamber (remove accumulated chaff)
- Wipe exterior (clean, beautiful grinder)
- Check alignment marks (verify no drift)

**Monthly**:
- Deep burr cleaning (disassemble, brush thoroughly)
- Inspect burrs (wear, damage, corrosion)
- Clean adjustment collar threads (smooth operation)
- Check electrical connections (safety)

**Annual**:
- Full disassembly (deep clean every component)
- Re-calibrate if needed (verify alignment, rarely necessary)
- Inspect motor (brushless, minimal maintenance needed)
- Check burr wear (1,500+ lbs = consider replacement)

**Burr Cleaning Methods**:
- **Grindz tablets**: Weber-approved (monthly, seasonal)
- **Manual brushing**: Preferred (disassemble, brush, no chemical residue)
- **Avoid rice**: Not necessary (precision grinder, gentle cleaning only)
