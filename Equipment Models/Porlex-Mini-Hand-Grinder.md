## ‚öôÔ∏è Usage & Workflow

### Initial Setup

**Out of Box**:
1. **Unbox**: Remove grinder from packaging
2. **Disassemble**: Separate components (hopper, body, handle, collection chamber)
3. **Clean**: Rinse all components (remove manufacturing oils)
4. **Dry**: Thoroughly dry (especially burrs)
5. **Reassemble**: Press-fit assembly (hopper on body, chamber on bottom)
6. **Set grind**: Tighten to zero, back off to desired clicks
7. **Test grind**: Grind test batch, check grind size, adjust

**Finding Zero Point**:
1. **Remove handle**: Detach handle from shaft
2. **Tighten**: Screw adjustment collar clockwise (from bottom)
3. **Listen**: Continue until burrs touch (slight scraping, resistance)
4. **Feel**: Burrs should barely touch (don't over-tighten)
5. **Mark zero**: Note this position as "zero"
6. **Back off**: Unscrew by desired clicks (e.g., 8 clicks for Aeropress)

**Grind Setting Guide**:
- **Turkish/Espresso**: 1-3 clicks (ultra-fine, not recommended)
- **Espresso**: 3-5 clicks (fine, marginal for espresso)
- **Aeropress**: 6-8 clicks (medium-fine, optimal)
- **Pour-over**: 9-12 clicks (medium, acceptable)
- **French press**: 13-16 clicks (medium-coarse, good)
- **Cold brew**: 16-18 clicks (coarse, best consistency)

### Daily Grinding Workflow

**Standard Aeropress Grind**:
1. **Measure beans**: Weigh 15g beans on scale
2. **Load hopper**: Pour beans into hopper (don't exceed 18g)
3. **Attach handle**: Insert handle into shaft hole
4. **Stabilize**: Hold body firmly (lap, palm, or silicone band grip)
5. **Grind**: Crank handle steadily ~60 RPM (45-60 seconds)
6. **Retrieve**: Unscrew bottom collection chamber
7. **Dump**: Pour grounds directly into Aeropress
8. **Cleanup**: Tap out residual grounds, wipe burrs

**Travel Setup** (Aeropress integration):
1. **Store grinder**: Slide Porlex into Aeropress chamber
2. **Add filters**: Tuck filter papers around grinder
3. **Pack**: Place Aeropress cap on, secure in travel bag
4. **Brewing**: Remove grinder, grind beans, brew, clean, re-store

**Batch Grinding** (for larger brews):
1. **First batch**: Grind 18g (max safe capacity)
2. **Empty**: Dump into container
3. **Second batch**: Grind another 18g
4. **Repeat**: Continue until desired total dose
5. **Combine**: Mix all batches together (improves uniformity)
6. **Limitation**: Tedious for French press 40g+, not recommended

### Maintenance Workflow

**After Each Use**:
- **Tap out grounds**: Shake/tap grinder to remove residual
- **Wipe burrs**: Quick wipe with dry cloth (removes oils)
- **Reassemble**: Put back together for next use

**Weekly Deep Clean** (daily use):
1. **Disassemble completely**: Hopper, body, burrs, shaft, collection
2. **Rinse metal parts**: Warm water rinse (no soap)
3. **Wipe ceramic burrs**: Damp cloth only (avoid thermal shock)
4. **Dry thoroughly**: Air dry all components (2-3 hours)
5. **Reassemble**: Press-fit back together
6. **Re-zero**: Check grind setting, adjust if drifted

**Monthly Deep Clean**:
1. **Cafiza soak**: Soak metal parts in espresso cleaner (not burrs)
2. **Burr inspection**: Check for chips, cracks, wear
3. **Alignment check**: Ensure burrs align properly
4. **Lubrication**: None needed (dry ceramic burrs)

### Troubleshooting

**Hard to Crank**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine, overfilled, light roast beans
- **Solution**: Coarser setting, 15g max, dark roast easier
- **Immediate**: Take breaks, slower cranking

**Inconsistent Grind**:
- **Cause**: Budget burrs, misalignment, worn components
- **Solution**: Check alignment, clean burrs, accept limitations
- **Expectation**: Porlex always produces fines (design limitation)

**Setting Drifts During Grinding**:
- **Cause**: Original Mini lacks lock ring
- **Solution**: Upgrade to Mini II, or tighten carefully
- **Workaround**: Check setting before each grind

**Beans Jam in Hopper**:
- **Cause**: Overfilled (>20g), very light/hard beans
- **Solution**: Load 15g max, pre-crack very hard beans
- **Immediate**: Disassemble, remove jammed beans, reassemble
