## üîÑ Maintenance & Care

### Routine Maintenance

**Daily Maintenance** (after grinding):
- Bellows purge (push 3-5 times to expel retained grounds)
- Wipe catch cup (remove static-clung grounds)
- Brush grind chute (quick sweep of exit chute)
- Empty catch cup (if not immediately used)
- Power off (unplug if not using again soon)
- Time required: 30 seconds

**Weekly Cleaning** (regular use):
- Remove hopper and bellows assembly
- Brush accessible burr chamber areas
- Wipe exterior (microfiber cloth)
- Clean catch cup (wash with soap, dry thoroughly)
- Vacuum grind chute (handheld vacuum or brush)
- Check for bean slivers (remove any stuck beans)
- Time required: 3-5 minutes

**Monthly Deep Cleaning**:
- Remove top burr (unscrew, lift out)
- Brush both burrs thoroughly (stiff brush)
- Vacuum burr chamber (remove all accumulated grounds)
- Clean adjustment collar threads (prevent seizing)
- Inspect burrs for damage (chips, wear)
- Reassemble (ensure proper seating)
- Run 10g cheap beans (purge cleaning debris)
- Time required: 15-20 minutes

**Quarterly Maintenance**:
- Grindz cleaning tablets (optional, 10-15g)
- Full disassembly and deep clean (if desired)
- Check burr alignment (marker test if concerned)
- Inspect motor brushes (brushless = no maintenance)
- Test adjustment mechanism (smooth operation)
- Clean bellows interior (remove, wash, dry)
- Time required: 20-30 minutes

**Grindz Tablets** (optional):
- Frequency: Every 2-3 months or after oily beans
- Product: Urnex Grindz or similar (food-safe grinder cleaner)
- Dose: 10-15g tablets (grind through at medium setting)
- Follow-up: Grind 20-30g cheap beans (purge tablets)
- Benefit: Removes oils, reduces rancidity, freshens grinder
- Caution: Some users avoid (prefer manual cleaning only)

**Burr Replacement Timeline**:
- Lifespan: 1,500-2,000 kg (titanium coating extended)
- Home use: 5-10 years typical (500-1,000g/week)
- Heavy use: 3-5 years (2-3 kg/week)
- Signs needed: Grind slower, inconsistent, flavor decline
- Replacement cost: $150-$200 (Timemore OEM burrs)
- Upgrade option: SSP burrs ($200-$250, requires modification)

**Burr Cleaning Methods**:
- **Brush method** (preferred): Stiff brush, remove grounds, no water
- **Vacuum method**: Handheld vacuum or shop vac
- **Compressed air**: Blow out grounds (caution: messy, use outdoors)
- **Grindz tablets**: Occasional (not replacement for brushing)
- **Water washing**: NOT recommended (burrs rust, motor risk)
- **Ultrasonic cleaner**: Possible (remove burrs, clean separately, dry thoroughly)

**Static Reduction Techniques**:
- **RDT (Ross Droplet Technique)**: 1-3 drops water on beans (most effective)
- Spray bottle: Fine mist (avoid over-wetting)
- Humidity control: 40-60% room humidity ideal
- Anti-static spray: Coat catch cup interior (occasional)
- Grounding: Ensure grinder properly grounded (three-prong plug)

**Alignment Maintenance**:
- Initial alignment: Check at 50-100g (after break-in)
- Re-alignment: Rarely needed (stable over time)
- Marker test: Sharpie on burr teeth, observe wear pattern
- Shim adjustment: Add/remove shims for alignment (advanced)
- Professional alignment: $50-$80 service (if uncomfortable DIY)

**Calibration & Zero-Point**:
- Factory zero: Set at manufacturing (should be accurate)
- User calibration: Possible (adjustment tool included)
- Zero-point drift: Minimal (stable mechanism)
- Re-zeroing: Rarely needed (unless disassembled)
- Procedure: Adjust until burrs just touch (chirp), back off slightly, set to "0"

**Storage & Non-Use**:
- Short-term (days): Leave as-is (no special storage)
- Medium-term (weeks): Clean thoroughly, store in dry place
- Long-term (months): Clean, remove burrs, store separately, desiccant pack
- Climate: Avoid humidity extremes (rust risk)
- Dust cover: Optional (keeps clean when not in use)

**Common Issues & Solutions**:
- **Static/mess**: Use RDT (few drops water on beans)
- **Retention increasing**: Deep clean, bellows purge technique
- **Grind inconsistent**: Check burr alignment, clean thoroughly
- **Adjustment stiff**: Clean threads, light food-safe lubricant
- **Motor noise increase**: Check for bean jams, foreign objects
- **Burr chirping**: Zero-point too tight, back off adjustment slightly

**Cleaning Supplies Needed**:
- Stiff brush (included with grinder)
- Microfiber cloth ($3-$5)
- Handheld vacuum or shop vac ($30-$80, optional)
- Grindz tablets ($10-$12, optional)
- Spray bottle for RDT ($5-$10)
- Shim kit for alignment ($15-$25, optional)
- Food-safe lubricant ($8-$12, rare use)

**Lifespan Expectations**:
- Light use (250-500g/week): 10-15 years potential
- Medium use (500-1,000g/week): 7-10 years typical
- Heavy use (1,500-2,500g/week): 5-7 years
- Critical factors: Cleaning frequency, burr care, motor quality
- Motor: Brushless design = long lifespan (15+ years potential)
- Burrs: Replaceable (extended grinder life indefinitely)
- Failure modes: Motor failure rare, burr wear expected, adjustment mechanism robust
