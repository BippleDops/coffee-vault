# Kalita Wave 185 Dripper

## Overview

The Kalita Wave 185 represents Japanese precision design philosophy applied to manual pour-over coffee brewing, creating a dripper that balances consistency, forgiveness, and quality. Introduced around 2010, the Wave quickly became a favorite among specialty coffee professionals and home enthusiasts for its flat-bottom design and distinctive wave filters that promote even extraction and reduce channeling compared to traditional conical drippers.

The Wave's defining characteristic is its flat-bottom geometry with three small drainage holes, contrasting with the single large hole of conical drippers like the [[Hario-V60-Plastic-Dripper]]. This design creates a coffee bed with more uniform depth and controlled flow rate, making it inherently more forgiving of pour technique variations while still rewarding precision. The result is a brewing device that produces excellent coffee reliably, making it ideal for both learning pour-over fundamentals and achieving professional-level consistency.

At $25-40 depending on material choice (stainless steel, glass, or ceramic), the Kalita Wave 185 positions itself as accessible yet premium manual brewing equipment. The price includes exceptional build quality reflecting Japanese manufacturing standards, with precise dimensions and consistent performance unit to unit. The 185 designation indicates the filter size (suitable for 2-4 cups), with the smaller Wave 155 available for single-cup brewing.

The Wave's proprietary filter design features accordion-like pleats creating 20 ridges that reduce contact between filter and dripper walls. This minimizes heat loss through the walls and prevents filter clogging against surfaces, promoting even extraction as water flows uniformly through the coffee bed. The filters themselves became so integral to the Wave experience that the dripper and its filters are essentially inseparable—using standard cone filters defeats the Wave's design advantages.

Professional baristas and specialty cafes worldwide have adopted the Kalita Wave for its consistency and quality. Its presence in World Barista Championship routines and widespread use in high-end cafes validates its effectiveness. For home brewers, the Wave offers professional-quality results with gentler learning curve than more technique-dependent drippers.

### Product Development and Design Philosophy

The Kalita Wave design addresses specific limitations observed in conical drippers:

**Flat-Bottom Geometry:**

Traditional conical drippers create V-shaped coffee beds where water flows preferentially through the center, potentially under-extracting coffee near the filter walls. The flat bottom creates uniform bed depth, encouraging water to penetrate evenly across the entire surface rather than channeling through the center.

The three small drainage holes restrict flow rate moderately, creating slight water pooling on the coffee bed surface. This immersion aspect (coffee submerged briefly before draining) promotes more complete extraction compared to purely percolation-based drippers where water immediately drains.

**Wave Filter Design:**

The accordion-pleated filters create 20 vertical ridges running from top to bottom. These ridges serve multiple purposes:

**Reduced Wall Contact:** Filters only touch dripper walls at ridge points, minimizing contact area. This reduces heat loss through walls (particularly important with metal drippers) and prevents filter from clogging against surfaces, which can create uneven flow.

**Air Circulation:** The gaps between ridges allow air to circulate, facilitating smooth water flow through the bed without vacuum effects that can create uneven extraction.

**Structural Stability:** The pleats provide structural rigidity, preventing filter collapse or deformation during brewing that might create channeling.

**Even Extraction:** The geometry promotes uniform water saturation across the coffee bed, reducing likelihood of dry spots or preferential flow paths.

**Three-Hole Bottom:**

The three holes (each approximately 5mm diameter) are positioned in triangular arrangement. This configuration provides:

**Flow Rate Control:** Three small holes restrict total flow compared to single large hole, creating more controlled drawdown and longer contact time.

**Even Drainage:** Triangular positioning promotes symmetric drainage across the flat bottom, preventing water accumulation on one side.

**Clog Resistance:** Multiple holes reduce brewing disruption if one hole partially clogs with fines.

### How It Works

**Basic Brewing Process:**

1. **Preparation:** Place Wave filter in dripper, wet filter with hot water (preheats dripper, removes paper taste)
2. **Dosing:** Add ground coffee (typically 20-30g for Wave 185)
3. **Bloom:** Pour 2-3x coffee weight in water, let bloom 30-45 seconds (CO2 release)
4. **Main Pour:** Pour remaining water in controlled manner (various techniques)
5. **Drawdown:** Allow coffee to drain completely (total brew time 3-4 minutes typical)
6. **Serve:** Remove dripper, enjoy brewed coffee

**Why Flat-Bottom Works:**

The flat coffee bed creates more uniform extraction compared to V-shaped beds because:

**Even Saturation:** Water contacts all coffee simultaneously rather than flowing preferentially through center
**Consistent Contact Time:** Coffee at edges receives similar water contact duration as center
**Predictable Flow:** Flat geometry creates more laminar flow patterns, reducing turbulence and channeling
**Forgiving of Pour Technique:** Even with imperfect pour distribution, flat bed compensates

**Temperature Stability:**

The wave filter design minimizes heat loss by reducing contact between filter and walls. Heat primarily escapes through the coffee bed itself rather than through wall contact. This maintains brewing temperature better than drippers where filter presses against walls.

Metal drippers require thorough preheating due to high thermal conductivity. Glass and ceramic drippers retain heat better but benefit from preheating regardless.

## Brewing Guide

### Basic Technique

**Setup:**
1. Boil water, let rest to 90-96°C
2. Place Wave filter in dripper, wet with hot water
3. Discard rinse water
4. Add 25g coffee (medium-fine grind) to filter
5. Tare scale to zero

**Brew Process:**
1. **Bloom (0:00-0:45):** Pour 50g water (2× coffee weight), circular motion, saturate all coffee
2. **First Pour (0:45-1:30):** Pour to 200g total, slow circular pour
3. **Second Pour (1:30-2:15):** Pour to 350g total, maintain water level
4. **Third Pour (2:15-2:45):** Pour to 400g total (final)
5. **Drawdown (2:45-4:00):** Allow complete drainage

**Target Time:** 3:30-4:00 total

### Advanced Techniques

**Pour Patterns:**
- Center pour: Good agitation, extracts thoroughly
- Spiral from center out: Even saturation
- Pulse pouring: Multiple smaller pours for control

**Grind Adjustment:**
- Faster than 3:00: Grind finer
- Slower than 4:30: Grind coarser
- Sour: Grind finer or increase temperature
- Bitter: Grind coarser or decrease temperature

## Notes

### Research Notes

The Kalita Wave exemplifies Japanese design philosophy: subtle improvement through careful engineering rather than radical innovation. The flat-bottom geometry and wave filters address real extraction challenges in conical drippers while maintaining simplicity and elegance.

The Wave's greatest strength is consistency. Less technique-dependent than V60, it produces excellent coffee reliably—critical for cafes serving multiple cups daily and home brewers seeking repeatable results without mastering complex pour techniques.

The flavor profile differs noticeably from V60: fuller body, more balanced acidity, less bright but equally clean. This makes Wave excellent for coffees where body and balance matter more than extreme clarity and acidity.

Filter cost and availability represent practical considerations. At $0.10 per filter versus $0.03-0.05 for cone filters, the Wave costs more per cup. Filters also less universally available than standard cones.

The steel version offers best value: durable, affordable, professional appearance. Glass beautiful but fragile. Ceramic excellent heat retention but highest price and fragility risk.

### Technical Notes

**Grind Size Optimization:**
- Start medium-fine (slightly finer than V60)
- Adjust based on brew time and taste
- Consistency crucial—use quality burr grinder
- Fresh grinding dramatically improves results

**Water Temperature:**
- Light roasts: 93-96°C for full extraction
- Medium roasts: 91-94°C balanced
- Dark roasts: 88-91°C prevents bitterness
- Adjust for taste preference

**Troubleshooting:**
- Too fast (<3:00): Grind finer, pour slower
- Too slow (>4:30): Grind coarser, pour faster
- Sour: Higher temp, finer grind, better saturation
- Bitter: Lower temp, coarser grind, less agitation
