**Upper Filter**:
- **Position**: Attached to plunger, descends with press
- **Function**: Primary filtration, stops coffee grounds
- **Design**: Cylindrical cage with 360¬∞ micro-mesh
- **Mesh openings**: 70 microns (consistent across entire surface)
- **Flow rate**: High flow despite fine mesh (large surface area)

**Lower Filter**:
- **Position**: Fixed at bottom of plunger assembly
- **Function**: Secondary filtration, captures fines that pass first filter
- **Design**: Flat circular disc with micro-mesh
- **Trapping zone**: 10mm chamber between filters traps particles
- **Sediment catch**: Isolated sediment stays above lower filter

**Filtration Process**:
1. **Descending**: Plunger descends, upper filter pushes grounds down
2. **First pass**: Coffee flows through upper cylindrical filter (70 microns)
3. **Settling chamber**: 10mm gap allows fines to settle
4. **Second pass**: Coffee flows through lower flat filter (70 microns)
5. **Pour**: Only double-filtered coffee exits spout
6. **Sediment trap**: Grounds and fines trapped between filters

**Particle Size Comparison**:

- **Espro P3**: Allows only <70 micron particles ‚Üí clean
- **Paper filter (Chemex)**: ~20 microns ‚Üí cleanest

**Filter Maintenance**:

- **Clogging**: Minimal with regular rinsing

### Troubleshooting

**Difficult to Plunge**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine, over-filling
- **Solution**: Coarser grind, respect max fill line
- **Immediate fix**: Plunge slower, apply gradual pressure
- **Prevention**: Use medium-coarse grind (slightly coarser than pour-over)

**Weak/Watery Coffee**:
- **Cause**: Insufficient dose, grind too coarse, water too cool
- **Solution**: Increase dose to 60-70g/L, medium grind, 93-96¬∞C water
- **Ratio check**: Use 1:15 minimum (1:17 maximum)
- **Extraction check**: Steep 4:00-5:00 (not less)

**Bitter/Over-Extracted**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine, steep too long, water too hot
- **Solution**: Coarser grind, steep 3:30-4:00, use 92-94¬∞C water
- **Prevention**: Don't leave coffee in carafe after plunging

**Still Seeing Sediment**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine, damaged filter, improper assembly
- **Solution**: Check filter integrity, ensure proper assembly
- **Grind check**: Should be medium-coarse (breadcrumb size)
- **Filter check**: Inspect mesh for tears, gaps, damage
- **Expectation**: Some ultra-fines normal (far less than traditional press)

**Filters Clogging**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine, insufficient rinsing after use
- **Solution**: Coarser grind, backflush filters after each use
- **Deep clean**: Soak in Cafiza/Espresso Clean solution weekly
- **Replacement**: If clogged beyond repair, replace filter assembly

## üéì Tips & Techniques

### Grind Size Optimization

**Ideal Grind**:
- **Setting**: Medium-coarse (slightly finer than traditional French press)
- **Visual**: Breadcrumb to sea salt size
- **Baratza Encore**: 18-22 (vs. traditional press 26-30)
- **Hand grinder**: 20-25 clicks from zero (varies by grinder)
- **Comparison**: Coarser than pour-over, finer than traditional press

**Why Finer Than Traditional Press**:
- **Double filtration**: Can handle slightly finer without grit
- **Better extraction**: Increased surface area improves extraction
- **Flow rate**: Micro-mesh provides resistance, finer grind acceptable
- **Limit**: Don't go too fine (medium is limit, medium-fine clogs)

**Grind Consistency Impact**:
- **Consistent grind**: Even extraction, clean cup
- **Uneven grind**: Fines clog filter, boulders under-extract
- **Burr grinder**: Highly recommended (vs. blade)
- **Quality threshold**: Mid-range burr grinder sufficient (Encore-level)

### Water Temperature

**Optimal Range**:
- **Light roasts**: 95-96¬∞C (203-205¬∞F) - extract bright acids
- **Medium roasts**: 93-95¬∞C (199-203¬∞F) - balanced extraction
- **Dark roasts**: 91-93¬∞C (196-199¬∞F) - avoid bitter over-extraction
- **Default**: 94¬∞C (201¬∞F) - works for most coffees

**Temperature Stability**:
- **Glass carafe**: Loses 4-8¬∞C during 4-minute brew (preheat recommended)
- **Stainless carafe**: Loses 1-2¬∞C (minimal, preheating optional)
- **Impact**: 2-3¬∞C variance has minimal effect (forgiving method)

### Steep Time Variations

**Standard**: 4:00 minutes (optimal for most coffees)
**Short**: 3:00-3:30 (brighter, lighter body, less extraction)
**Long**: 4:30-5:00 (fuller, richer, risk of over-extraction)
**Extended**: 6:00+ (Hoffmann method, ultra-clean by settling)

**Coffee Type Adjustments**:
- **Light roasts**: 4:30-5:00 (need more time to extract)
- **Medium roasts**: 4:00 (standard)
- **Dark roasts**: 3:30-4:00 (extract faster, avoid bitterness)
- **Decaf**: 4:00-4:30 (often denser, needs more time)

### Maximizing Filtration

**Filter Maintenance**:
- **Daily rinse**: Backflush filters immediately after brewing
- **Weekly deep clean**: Soak in Cafiza or baking soda solution
- **Monthly inspection**: Check for damage, bent mesh, gaps
- **Proper assembly**: Ensure filters seated correctly (no gaps)

**Plunging Technique**:
- **Speed**: Slow, steady (15-20 seconds total)
- **Pressure**: Gentle, consistent (not forceful)
- **Seal**: Ensure even plunge (not tilted/angled)
- **Final**: Don't press hard at bottom (compacting grounds = harder cleanup)

**Pre-Settling** (maximum clarity):
- **After steep**: Wait 2-3 minutes before plunging
- **Gravity settling**: Grounds settle, reducing filter load
- **Gentle plunge**: Minimal disturbance, cleanest extraction
- **Trade-off**: Longer total time (7-8 minutes)
