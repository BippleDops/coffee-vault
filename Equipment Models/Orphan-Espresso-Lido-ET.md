## üîÑ Maintenance & Care

### Routine Maintenance

**Daily Maintenance** (After Grinding):
- Knock grinder: Tap gently to dislodge retained grounds
- Brush catch cup: Remove grounds with brush
- Wipe exterior: Microfiber cloth (oils, spills)
- Time required: 30 seconds

**Weekly Cleaning**:
- Disassemble: Remove catch cup, top burr (allen key)
- Brush burrs: Stiff brush (remove accumulated grounds)
- Vacuum grind chamber: Handheld vacuum or compressed air
- Wipe catch cup: Wash with soap, dry thoroughly
- Reassemble: Ensure proper alignment
- Time required: 10-15 minutes

**Monthly Deep Cleaning**:
- Full disassembly: Remove both burrs, adjustment mechanism
- Brush thoroughly: All components (burrs, chamber, threads)
- Compressed air: Blow out fine particles
- Inspect burrs: Check for wear, damage
- Light lubricant: Food-safe grease on threads (optional, rare)
- Reassemble: Re-calibrate zero-point if needed
- Time required: 20-30 minutes

**Grindz Tablets** (Optional):
- Frequency: Monthly or after oily beans
- Product: Urnex Grindz (10-15g)
- Grind through: Medium setting
- Follow-up: Grind 20-30g cheap beans (purge tablets)
- Benefit: Removes oils, freshens grinder

**Burr Cleaning**:
- Brush method: Stiff brush, remove grounds (weekly)
- Vacuum method: Handheld vacuum (effective)
- Compressed air: Blow out particles (outdoors, messy)
- Water: NEVER (hardened steel rusts, not stainless)
- Dry cloth: Wipe burrs if needed (dry only)

**Zero-Point Calibration**:
- When needed: After full disassembly, adjustment drift
- Method: Adjust until burrs chirp (just touching), back off 1/4 turn
- Mark zero: Note position on dial (reference point)
- Espresso setting: Typically 8-15 rotations from zero (bean-dependent)

**Storage** (Travel, Non-Use):
- Empty grinder: Remove all beans (oils go rancid)
- Clean thoroughly: Brush, vacuum, wipe
- Travel pouch: Protect from impacts (if traveling)
- Dry location: Room temperature (avoid moisture for burrs)
- Duration: Indefinite (aluminum/steel stable)

**Common Issues & Solutions**:
- **Difficult grinding**: Check zero-point (burrs may be too close), clean burrs (buildup adds resistance)
- **Inconsistent grind**: Check burr alignment (may need adjustment), clean thoroughly
- **Static/messy**: Use RDT (1-2 drops water on beans)
- **Handle wobble**: Tighten ball-bearing assembly (occasional)
- **Adjustment drift**: Re-calibrate zero-point, check threads for debris

**Cleaning Supplies**:
- Stiff brush: Included (replacement ~$8-$12)
- Microfiber cloth: $5-$10 (3-pack)
- Handheld vacuum: $30-$80 (optional, helpful)
- Compressed air: $5-$10 (can, outdoors use)
- Grindz tablets: $10-$12 (optional, occasional use)
- Allen key: Included (burr removal)

**Burr Replacement Timeline**:
- Lifespan: 1,000-1,500 kg (8-15 years home use, 18g/day)
- Replacement cost: $80-$120 (Orphan Espresso replacement burrs)
- DIY replacement: Yes (user-serviceable)
- Worthiness: Absolutely (extends grinder life indefinitely)
