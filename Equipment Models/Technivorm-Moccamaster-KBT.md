### Troubleshooting Guide

**Coffee Too Weak/Watery**:
- **Cause**: Insufficient coffee dose, grind too coarse
- **Solution**: Use 60g (10 tablespoons) per full pot, grind medium (like sand)

**Coffee Too Strong/Bitter**:
- **Cause**: Excessive coffee dose, grind too fine, over-extraction
- **Solution**: Reduce dose to 50-55g, coarsen grind, ensure brew time ~5 minutes

**Coffee Not Hot Enough**:
- **Cause**: Thermal carafe not preheated, old carafe (vacuum seal failed)
- **Solution**: Preheat carafe with hot water before brewing; replace carafe if >5 years old

**Brew Time Too Slow** (>8 minutes):
- **Cause**: Grind too fine (clogging), mineral buildup in spray arm
- **Solution**: Coarsen grind; descale machine; check spray arm holes for clogs

**Brew Time Too Fast** (<3 minutes):
- **Cause**: Grind too coarse, insufficient coffee dose
- **Solution**: Grind finer (medium, like sand); increase dose

**Water Overflowing Brew Basket**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine (filter clogged), brew basket valve stuck
- **Solution**: Coarsen grind; clean brew basket drip-stop valve; use fresh #4 filter

**Machine Not Heating**:
- **Cause**: Copper element failure, thermal fuse blown, power switch failure
- **Solution**: Contact Technivorm support for warranty service or part replacement

**Carafe Not Keeping Coffee Hot**:
- **Cause**: Vacuum seal failed in carafe
- **Solution**: Replace thermal carafe ($65); preheat carafe before brewing as workaround

**Spray Arm Dripping After Brew**:
- **Cause**: Normal (residual water in element), or clogged spray arm
- **Solution**: Normal drips expected; if excessive, descale and clean spray arm

**Unusual Noise During Brewing**:
- **Cause**: Mineral buildup in copper element (scaling)
- **Solution**: Descale machine; use filtered water to prevent future buildup

### Performance Optimization

**Optimal Coffee Recipe** (full pot):
- **Water**: 1,250 ml (40 oz, full reservoir)
- **Coffee**: 60-65g (10-11 tablespoons, medium grind)
- **Ratio**: 1:19 to 1:21 (coffee:water)
- **Grind**: Medium (like coarse sand, slightly finer than French press)
- **Filter**: Fresh #4 cone filter (Melitta or Technivorm)
- **Brew time**: 5-6 minutes total
- **Temperature**: 196-205Â°F (automatic, copper element)
- **Result**: 8-10 cups (4 oz each) balanced, flavorful coffee

**Half-Batch Brewing** (KBT-741 without half-batch switch):
- **Water**: 625 ml (20 oz, half reservoir)
- **Coffee**: 32-35g (5-6 tablespoons)
- **Ratio**: 1:18 to 1:20 (slightly stronger for smaller batch)
- **Grind**: Medium (same as full batch)
- **Note**: KBT-741 doesn't have half-batch switch; may slightly over-extract small batches
- **Recommendation**: Consider KBGT Select for frequent half-batches

**Water Quality**:
- **Ideal**: Filtered tap water (moderate mineral content)
- **TDS**: 75-150 ppm (total dissolved solids)
- **Avoid**: Distilled water (no minerals, flat taste), very hard water (scaling)
- **Treatment**: Third Wave Water packets for RO/distilled water

**Thermal Carafe Preheating**:
1. Fill carafe with hot tap water
2. Let sit 2-3 minutes while machine heats
3. Discard water immediately before brewing
4. **Result**: Coffee stays hot 30-60 minutes longer

**Coffee Freshness**:
- **Optimal**: 7-21 days post-roast
- **Grind**: Immediately before brewing (whole beans stay fresh longer)
- **Storage**: Airtight container, cool/dark location
- **Avoid**: Pre-ground coffee (stales quickly)

**Best Practices**:
- Use scale to weigh coffee (more accurate than volume)
- Grind fresh immediately before brewing
- Use filtered water (not distilled, not heavily mineralized)
- Preheat thermal carafe for maximum heat retention
- Descale regularly (prevents mineral buildup affecting temperature)
- Replace paper filter each brew (no reuse)
