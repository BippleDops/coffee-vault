## üî¨ Technical Notes

### Optimal Technique

**Recommended Workflow**:
1. **Grind**: 12g coffee, medium-fine (espresso grind, similar to pump machine)
2. **Boil water**: Bring 200¬∞F water in kettle (use thermometer for accuracy)
3. **Preheat chamber**: Pour hot water into Rok chamber, let sit 30 seconds, discard
4. **Preheat portafilter**: Run hot water through portafilter/basket
5. **Dose and tamp**: Add 12g coffee to basket, tamp firmly (30 lbs pressure)
6. **Fill chamber**: Pour 200¬∞F water into Rok chamber (fill to top)
7. **Lock portafilter**: Attach portafilter to chamber, ensure seal
8. **Pre-infusion**: Gently apply light pressure (arms ~25% down), hold 5-10 seconds
9. **Full extraction**: Apply full pressure (arms fully down), maintain pressure
10. **Extract**: 25-35 seconds total extraction (including pre-infusion)
11. **Target output**: 30-40ml (single shot)
12. **Cleanup**: Rinse chamber and basket immediately

**Pressure Control**:
- **Pre-infusion (0-10 seconds)**: Light pressure (~3-5 bar), wet grounds, bloom
- **Extraction (10-30 seconds)**: Full pressure (~9 bar), steady arm force
- **Finish**: Maintain pressure until desired output volume
- **Feedback**: Arm resistance indicates pressure (harder to push = more pressure)

**Temperature Management** (critical for Rok):
- **Preheat chamber**: Essential (aluminum cools water quickly)
- **Preheat portafilter**: Reduces additional temperature loss
- **Work quickly**: Pour water, attach portafilter, extract immediately (minimize cooling time)
- **Expected temperature**: 185-195¬∞F at extraction (10-15¬∞F loss from 200¬∞F boil)

**Grind Size**:
- **Espresso-fine**: Similar to pump machine grind
- **Adjustment**: If channeling, grind finer; if too slow, grind coarser
- **Fresh grind**: Critical (pre-ground coffee stales quickly)

### Common Mistakes

**Insufficient Preheating**:
- **Issue**: Aluminum chamber cools water to <185¬∞F (under-extraction)
- **Solution**: Always preheat chamber with hot water rinse (2-3 times if necessary)

**Inconsistent Pressure**:
- **Issue**: Variable arm force = variable pressure = inconsistent shots
- **Solution**: Practice consistent, steady arm pressure (9 bar = moderate resistance)

**Overfilling Chamber**:
- **Issue**: Water overflows during extraction (mess)
- **Solution**: Fill chamber to indicated line (not to brim)

**Too Much Dose**:
- **Issue**: Shallow basket (15mm depth) overflows if >14g dose
- **Solution**: Stick to 12g dose (optimal for 38mm basket depth)

**Skipping Preheating**:
- **Issue**: Cold chamber = sour, under-extracted shots
- **Solution**: Never skip preheating step (non-negotiable with aluminum chamber)

### Maintenance

**After Each Use**:
- Rinse brew chamber with hot water
- Remove and discard coffee puck
- Rinse basket and portafilter
- Wipe arms with damp cloth
- Dry all components (prevent water spots)

**Weekly** (if used daily):
- Deep clean basket with espresso cleaner (Cafiza)
- Scrub chamber interior with soft brush
- Inspect gasket for wear/cracking

**Monthly**:
- Remove and clean gasket (rinse, inspect for damage)
- Check screws/fittings (tighten if loose)
- Polish aluminum arms (restore shine)

**Annually**:
- Replace gasket ($8-12) if worn or leaking
- Inspect all seals (piston seal, group gasket)
- Deep clean all components

### Troubleshooting

**Sour Shots**:
- **Cause**: Under-extraction (temperature too low, grind too coarse, insufficient pressure)
- **Solution**: Preheat chamber thoroughly, grind finer, apply more arm pressure

**Bitter Shots**:
- **Cause**: Over-extraction (grind too fine, excessive pressure, too slow extraction)
- **Solution**: Grind coarser, reduce arm pressure, extract faster

**No Crema**:
- **Cause**: Stale coffee, insufficient pressure, grind too coarse
- **Solution**: Use fresh coffee (<2 weeks post-roast), increase arm pressure, grind finer

**Leaking During Extraction**:
- **Cause**: Worn gasket, insufficient portafilter lock
- **Solution**: Replace gasket ($8-12), ensure portafilter fully locked

**Channeling** (fast extraction, uneven flow):
- **Cause**: Uneven distribution, poor tamping, grind too coarse
- **Solution**: WDT distribution, level tamping, grind finer

**Difficult to Press Arms**:
- **Cause**: Grind too fine (excessive resistance)
- **Solution**: Grind coarser (should require moderate effort, not extreme force)
