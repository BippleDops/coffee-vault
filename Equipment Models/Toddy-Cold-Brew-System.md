### Practical Workflow

**Standard Brewing Process**:
1. **Prepare filter** (5 min):
   - Rinse felt filter thoroughly (cold water, remove any residue)
   - Place filter in bottom of brewing container (flat, centered)
   - Insert rubber stopper from underneath (seals hole)

2. **Add coffee and water** (5 min):
   - Measure 12 oz (340g) coarse ground coffee
   - Pour into brewing container on top of filter
   - Measure 56 oz (1.65L) cold water
   - Pour slowly over coffee grounds (saturate evenly)
   - Stir gently if needed (ensure all grounds wet)
   - Place lid on brewing container (optional, reduces evaporation)

3. **Steep** (12-24 hours passive):
   - Place brewing container in refrigerator (or cool room temperature)
   - Leave undisturbed for 12-24 hours (16-18 hours optimal)
   - No stirring, no intervention (passive extraction)

4. **Decant concentrate** (10-15 min):
   - Remove brewing container from fridge
   - Place on edge of counter (carafe below)
   - Remove rubber stopper from bottom (gravity drains concentrate)
   - Allow to drip fully (10-15 minutes, all liquid drains)
   - Concentrate collects in glass carafe below

5. **Cleanup** (5-10 min):
   - Discard spent coffee grounds (compost, trash)
   - Rinse felt filter immediately (cold water, squeeze gently)
   - Wash brewing container (soap and water)
   - Store filter and stopper (dry thoroughly, ready for next batch)

**Time Investment**:
- Setup: 5-10 minutes (day 1)
- Steeping: 12-24 hours (passive, no work)
- Decanting: 10-15 minutes (day 2)
- Cleanup: 5-10 minutes (day 2)
- **Total active time**: 20-35 minutes (over 2 days, minimal hands-on)

### 24-Hour Immersion Extraction

**Chemical Engineering Approach**:
- Developed by Todd Simpson (chemical engineer background)
- Optimized for low-acid extraction (cold water, long time, specific ratio)
- Scientific testing: Ratios, times, temperatures tested (not accidental discovery)
- **Result**: Intentional flavor profile (sweet, smooth, low-acid - patented process)

**Immersion Benefits**:
- Even extraction: All grounds contact water equally (vs pour-over variable)
- Forgiving method: Temperature, grind size less critical (easy consistency)
- Passive brewing: No stirring, no intervention (hands-off simplicity)
- Full extraction: Long time allows complete flavor development (thorough extraction)

**Time Impact**:
- 12 hours: Lighter, brighter (less extraction, more acidity)
- 16-18 hours: Balanced, optimal (recommended sweet spot)
- 20-24 hours: Stronger, richer (more extraction, risk of over-extraction)
- 24+ hours: Over-extracted (bitter, harsh notes emerge - avoid)
- **Recommendation**: 16-18 hours (Toddy official guidance, optimal balance)

### Low-Acid Coffee

**Acidity Reduction**:
- Cold water extraction: Chlorogenic acids less soluble (major acid compounds stay in grounds)
- Acid reduction: 60-70% less acid than hot-brewed coffee (significant reduction)
- pH level: 6.0-6.5 (vs 4.5-5.0 hot coffee - less acidic)
- **Benefit**: Gentler on stomach (acid-sensitive drinkers appreciate this)

**Health Considerations**:
- Acid reflux: Reduced trigger (lower acid content)
- Dental health: Less enamel erosion (lower acidity)
- Digestive: Easier on stomach (smooth, gentle)
- **Medical**: Not medical advice, but anecdotally reported benefits (consult doctor for health concerns)

**Flavor Impact**:
- Sweetness emphasized: Less acid = more perceived sweetness (flavor balance shift)
- Smoothness: Low acid = smooth mouthfeel (less bite, more cream)

### Cold Brew Quality Factors

**Coffee Selection**:
- **Roast level**: Medium to dark roasts ideal (sweet, chocolatey, smooth notes emphasized)
- Light roasts: Acceptable but less common (bright, tea-like, delicate - cold brew mutes some complexity)
- Blends: Excellent (balanced, consistent, forgiving)

**Grind Size Impact**:
- Coarse (recommended): 12-24 hour steep optimal (balanced extraction)
- Medium-coarse: Acceptable (slightly faster extraction, may over-extract at 24 hours)
- Medium: Too fine (over-extraction, bitterness, filter clogs)
- Fine: Unsuitable (clogs filter, over-extracts, bitter)
- **Rule**: Coarser than French press or equal (chunky, distinct particles)

**Water Quality**:
- Filtered water: Recommended (chlorine, off-flavors impact cold brew significantly)
- Spring water: Excellent (mineral content enhances flavor)
- Tap water: Acceptable if good quality (taste water first, if good for drinking, good for cold brew)
- Distilled: Not recommended (too pure, flat flavor)
- **Impact**: Water quality crucial (cold brew highlights water flavor, 16-hour contact time)

**Steep Time Optimization**:
- **12 hours**: Lighter-bodied, brighter (less extraction, more acidity, refreshing)
- **16-18 hours**: Balanced, optimal (Toddy recommendation, sweet spot)
- **20-24 hours**: Stronger, richer (more extraction, full-bodied, risk over-extraction)
- **24+ hours**: Over-extracted (bitter notes emerge, harsh, avoid)
- **Personal preference**: Experiment 12-24 hour range (find your sweet spot)

### Expert Tips & Tricks

**Pre-Wetting the Grounds**:
- Technique: Add 1 cup water first, wait 5 minutes (grounds bloom)
- Benefit: Ensures even saturation (CO2 release, better extraction)
- Then: Add remaining water (proceeds normally)
- **Impact**: Slightly improved extraction, more even flavor (optional refinement)

**Refrigerator vs Room Temperature**:
- **Refrigerator brewing**: Cleaner, brighter flavor (controlled temperature, prevents fermentation)
- **Room temperature**: Slightly faster extraction (warmer = more active, but risk off-flavors)
- **Hot climates**: Definitely refrigerate (prevent fermentation, bacterial growth)
- **Recommendation**: Refrigerate if possible (best quality, food safety)

**Concentrate Dilution Customization**:
- **Start 1:1**: Concentrate:water (standard starting point)
- Too strong: Dilute 1:2 or 1:3 (adjust to personal taste)
- Too weak: Use less water or straight concentrate (boost intensity)
- **With milk**: Use less water dilution (milk further dilutes, account for this)
- **Experimentation**: No wrong answer (personal preference varies, adjust to taste)

**Extending Filter Life**:
- Rinse immediately: After every use (prevents grounds drying, clogging)
- Gentle cleaning: Soft brush, cold water (avoid harsh scrubbing, damages felt)
- Air dry: Before storage (prevents mildew, extends life)
- **Reality**: 10-12 batches maximum (felt wears out, replace for best quality)
