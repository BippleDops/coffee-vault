**Technique**:
1. **Dose**: Grind coffee into portafilter (desired dose, e.g., 18g)
2. **Insert needles**: Place WDT needles into coffee bed (perpendicular to basket)
3. **Stir**: Gentle circular/zigzag motion, needles reaching bottom of basket
4. **Pattern**: Circular passes from center to edge, 5-10 seconds total
5. **Depth**: Needles must reach basket floor (ensure full-depth distribution)
6. **Tamp**: After WDT, tamp as normal (distribution complete, now compress)
7. **Extract**: Pull shot (observe even extraction across puck surface)

## üî¨ Technical Notes

### Technique Optimization

**Optimal WDT Technique** (community consensus):
1. **Dose**: Grind 18g (or desired dose) into portafilter
2. **Funnel**: Place dosing funnel on portafilter (contains mess)
3. **Insert**: Place WDT needles into coffee bed (perpendicular entry)
4. **Depth**: Needles reach basket floor (critical for full-depth distribution)
5. **Stir**: Gentle circular motion, center to edge, 3-4 passes
6. **Pattern**: Zigzag or circular (both effective; avoid excessive stirring)
7. **Duration**: 5-10 seconds total (diminishing returns after 10 seconds)
8. **Remove funnel**: Lift dosing funnel carefully (grounds now distributed)
9. **Tamp**: Tamp as normal (WDT distributes, tamp compresses)
10. **Extract**: Pull shot, observe even extraction

**Needle Depth**:
- **Full depth** (needles touch basket floor): Most effective (distributes entire puck)
- **Partial depth**: Less effective (top layer only)
- **Verdict**: Always use full depth (needles must reach bottom)

**Needle Count**:
- **4 needles**: Minimal coverage, faster stirring
- **8 needles** (most common): Optimal balance of coverage and clump avoidance
- **12 needles**: More coverage, but needles too close (can create new clumps)
- **Verdict**: 8 needles ideal

**Needle Diameter**:
- **0.3mm**: Very thin, minimal displacement, fragile
- **0.35mm** (most common): Optimal balance of strength and thinness
- **0.4mm**: Stronger, but more displacement (can compact grounds)
- **0.5mm+**: Too thick (displaces grounds excessively, creates new density variations)
- **Verdict**: 0.35mm ideal

**Stirring Pattern**:
- **Circular** (center to edge): Even coverage, intuitive
- **Zigzag** (side to side): Thorough mixing, slightly slower
- **Random**: Effective but inconsistent
- **Verdict**: Circular or zigzag both work; consistency matters more than pattern

**Stirring Duration**:
- **<5 seconds**: Insufficient (clumps remain)
- **5-10 seconds**: Optimal (clumps broken, no excessive disruption)
- **>15 seconds**: Diminishing returns (over-working grounds, potential density issues)
- **Verdict**: 8-10 seconds ideal

### Common Mistakes

**Too Aggressive Stirring**:
- **Issue**: Excessive force displaces grounds unevenly (creates new density variations)
- **Solution**: Gentle stirring (needles should glide through grounds)

**Insufficient Depth**:
- **Issue**: Needles only stir top layer (bottom clumps remain)
- **Solution**: Ensure needles reach basket floor (full-depth distribution)

**Over-Stirring**:
- **Issue**: >20 seconds stirring creates new problems (density variations, settling issues)
- **Solution**: 8-10 seconds sufficient (stop when clumps visually broken)

**No Dosing Funnel**:
- **Issue**: WDT flicks grounds out of basket (messy, dose inaccuracy)
- **Solution**: Always use dosing funnel during WDT

**Bent Needles**:
- **Issue**: Bent needles less effective (don't reach full depth evenly)
- **Solution**: Replace needles ($5-10), avoid excessive force during stirring

**Skipping WDT**:
- **Issue**: Convenience temptation (skip WDT to save 10 seconds)
- **Solution**: WDT should be non-negotiable (quality improvement worth 10 seconds)

### Maintenance

**Needle Cleaning**:
- **Frequency**: Every 1-2 weeks (coffee oils/fines accumulate)
- **Method**: Brush needles with dry brush or rinse with water (dry thoroughly)
- **Avoid**: Dishwasher (needles can bend), harsh detergents

**Needle Replacement**:
- **Frequency**: Every 1-3 years (needles bend over time)
- **Cost**: $5-10 per 100-pack acupuncture needles
- **Process**: Pull bent needles, insert new needles (friction-fit for cork/wood, threaded for metal)

**Base Cleaning**:
- **Cork/wood**: Wipe with damp cloth (avoid soaking)
- **Metal**: Rinse with water, dry thoroughly
- **Plastic/resin**: Dishwasher safe (top rack)

**Storage**:
- **Protective sleeve**: Prevents needle bending (many commercial WDTs include sleeve)
- **Upright storage**: Needles pointing up (prevents bending)
- **Avoid**: Drawer storage (needles bend easily)

### Scientific Basis

**Why WDT Works**:
- **Grinder clumping**: All grinders produce clumps (static, grind chamber geometry)
- **Density variation**: Clumps create high-density zones (water flows around, not through)
- **Channeling**: Water follows path of least resistance (low-density zones over-extract)
- **WDT solution**: Breaks clumps, homogenizes density, eliminates preferential flow paths

**Measurable Impact**:
- **Extraction yield**: +1-3% (refractometer measurements, 19% ‚Üí 21% typical)
- **Channeling reduction**: Visual reduction (bottomless portafilter observation)
- **Consistency**: Lower shot-to-shot TDS variance (more repeatable)
