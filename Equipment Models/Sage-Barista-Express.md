## ðŸ”¬ Technical Notes

### Common Modifications

**OPV (Over-Pressure Valve) Adjustment**:
- **Issue**: Factory OPV set at 12-14 bar (too high for optimal extraction)
- **Target**: 9 bar at group head during extraction
- **Difficulty**: Moderate (requires disassembly)
- **Cost**: $0-15 (spring replacement or adjustment)
- **Process**:
  1. Remove water tank and unscrew rear panel
  2. Locate OPV valve (brass component with spring)
  3. Option A: Loosen adjustment screw to reduce pressure
  4. Option B: Replace spring with softer spring (various sellers on eBay)
  5. Test with portafilter pressure gauge
  6. Adjust until 9 bar during extraction
- **Impact**: Significantly improves extraction quality, reduces bitterness
- **Warranty**: Voids warranty if detected
- **Prevalence**: Very common mod (~40% of enthusiast owners)

**Grinder Shim/Burr Alignment**:
- **Issue**: Integrated grinder won't grind fine enough for light roasts
- **Solution**: Add shims to lower burr carrier (reduces minimum grind size)
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Cost**: $5-10 (shims from hardware store)
- **Impact**: Allows finer grinding, better for light roasts
- **Risk**: Can damage burrs if shimmed too aggressively

**Temperature Surfing** (not modification, technique):
- **Issue**: No PID means temperature varies
- **Technique**:
  1. Wait for machine to fully heat (30-45 seconds)
  2. Run blank shot to cool group slightly
  3. Wait for heating light to turn off
  4. Immediately pull shot
- **Impact**: More consistent shot temperature
- **Difficulty**: Easy (requires timing practice)

**IMS Shower Screen Installation**:
- **Upgrade**: Replace stock shower screen with IMS precision screen
- **Cost**: $25
- **Benefit**: Improved water distribution, more even extraction
- **Difficulty**: Easy (single screw removal)
- **Impact**: Modest improvement in shot quality

**Grinder Chute Cleanup**:
- **Issue**: Coffee grounds build up in grinder chute (stale coffee in shots)
- **Solution**: Weekly vacuum or brush cleaning of chute
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Impact**: Fresher shots, reduced stale flavors

### Maintenance Requirements

**Daily**:
- Wipe group gasket and shower screen with damp cloth
- Purge steam wand before and after steaming
- Empty drip tray when full
- Wipe exterior surfaces

**Weekly**:
- Backflush with water (no detergent): 5 cycles
- Clean steam wand with milk frother cleaner (Rinza)
- Remove and rinse drip tray thoroughly
- Wipe bean hopper interior (remove oils)

**Bi-Weekly**:
- Backflush with detergent (Cafiza): 3-5 cycles
- Deep clean steam wand (soak tip in Rinza solution)
- Clean grinder chute with brush/vacuum

**Monthly**:
- Remove and clean shower screen and dispersion plate
- Inspect group gasket for wear
- Deep clean grinder (remove top burr, brush/vacuum)
- Replace water filter

**Quarterly** (every 3 months):
- Descale machine (hard water areas)
- Full grinder disassembly and cleaning
- Inspect all gaskets for wear/cracking
- Check OPV pressure (if modded)

**Annually**:
- Replace group gasket (shows wear/compression)
- Replace shower screen (shows pitting/wear)
- Full machine servicing (if out of warranty)
- Consider burr replacement (heavy use >500 lbs coffee)

**Burr Replacement** (every 3-5 years):
- Cost: $50-80 for replacement burr set
- Difficulty: Moderate (requires grinder disassembly)
- Lifespan: 500-800 lbs coffee (typical home use: 4-6 years)
- Signs: Inconsistent grind, more fines, slower grinding

### Troubleshooting Guide

**Low/No Pressure During Extraction**:
- **Cause**: Grind too coarse, dose too low, improper tamp
- **Solution**: Grind finer (lower number), increase dose to 18g, firm tamp
- **Cause**: OPV modded too low
- **Solution**: Re-adjust OPV spring to higher pressure

**Sour Shots**:
- **Cause**: Under-extraction (too fast, too cool, too coarse)
- **Solution**: Grind finer, increase dose, hotter temperature (temperature surf)

**Bitter Shots**:
- **Cause**: Over-extraction (too slow, too fine, OPV too high)
- **Solution**: Grind coarser, reduce dose, adjust OPV to 9 bar

**Channeling** (uneven extraction):
- **Cause**: Uneven distribution, poor tamping, old coffee
- **Solution**: WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique) with toothpick/needle tool, level tamp, fresh coffee (<2 weeks post-roast)

**Steam Wand Not Producing Steam**:
- **Cause**: Machine not switched to steam mode, insufficient heat-up
- **Solution**: Press steam button, wait for indicator light, purge briefly

**Grinder Not Grinding**:
- **Cause**: Bean hopper not seated correctly, beans too oily/clogging
- **Solution**: Re-seat hopper with twist-lock, clean chute, use less-oily beans

**Water Leaking from Group Head**:
- **Cause**: Worn group gasket, loose portafilter
- **Solution**: Replace group gasket ($8), ensure portafilter fully locked

**Machine Not Heating**:
- **Cause**: Thermostat failure, thermal fuse blown
- **Solution**: Service required (not DIY repair)

**Grinder Grind Size Inconsistent**:
- **Cause**: Burr carrier wear, burr alignment issue
- **Solution**: Replace burr carrier ($30-40), shim adjustment

### Performance Optimization

**Dialing In Process**:
1. **Start point**: Grind setting 8-10 (medium-fine), 18g dose
2. **Extract**: Aim for 36g output in 25-30 seconds (1:2 ratio)
3. **Taste**: Evaluate for sourness (under-extracted) or bitterness (over-extracted)
4. **Adjust grind**: Finer if sour/fast, coarser if bitter/slow
5. **Repeat**: Small adjustments (1 setting at a time)
6. **Note**: Light roasts need finer grind (6-8), dark roasts coarser (10-12)

**Optimal Parameters** (community consensus):
- **Dose**: 18-19g (double-shot basket)
- **Output**: 36-40g (1:2 to 1:2.2 ratio)
- **Time**: 25-35 seconds (from first drip)
- **Grind**: Setting 7-9 for medium roasts
- **Temperature**: Stock (cannot adjust, ~93Â°C/200Â°F)
- **Pre-infusion**: Automated ~7 seconds (cannot adjust)

**Best Practices**:
- Use fresh coffee (<3 weeks post-roast, ideally 7-14 days)
- Preheat machine 10-15 minutes before use
- Preheat portafilter and cup with blank shot
- Use filtered water (chlorine-free, moderate hardness)
- Weigh dose and output for consistency
- Clean machine regularly to prevent buildup
