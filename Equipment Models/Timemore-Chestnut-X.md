# Timemore Chestnut X

## Overview

The Timemore Chestnut X represents modern Chinese engineering excellence: premium S2C 660 stainless steel burrs, 42-click precision adjustment system, ultra-lightweight aluminum construction (500g/17.6oz), and pour-over-to-espresso versatility—all at mid-tier price of $140. Released in 2021 as Timemore's flagship hand grinder evolution, the Chestnut X challenged European hand grinder dominance (Comandante, Kinu) by delivering comparable grind quality, superior ergonomics, and better value through Chinese manufacturing efficiency and design innovation.

The Chestnut X's defining characteristics include 38mm S2C 660 steel burrs producing exceptional grind consistency across brew methods, 42-click adjustment (10 clicks per rotation, ultra-fine adjustment capability), lightweight aerospace aluminum body making travel effortless, magnetic catch cup preventing spills, and critically, dual-purpose capability—excellent for pour-over (primary strength) while capable of espresso with technique and patience. The 500g weight makes Chestnut X one of the lightest quality hand grinders available, enabling backpack travel without weight penalty.

For pour-over enthusiasts wanting portable quality, travelers requiring lightweight grinders, home baristas on moderate budgets, or espresso experimenters willing to hand-grind, the Chestnut X provides compelling package: grind quality rivaling $200-$300 European grinders, 1/3 the weight of Comandante, and price point ($140) accessible to committed enthusiasts without luxury investment.

## Design Philosophy & Development

### The Chestnut X Project

The original Timemore Chestnut (2016) established foundations: affordable hand grinder with decent burrs and simple adjustment. The C2 (2018) improved significantly: better burrs (S2C 660), refined adjustment mechanism, enhanced build quality. However, limitations remained: heavier than ideal (700g), adjustment not precise enough for espresso, ergonomics improvable.

The Chestnut X project (developed 2019-2021) represented complete redesign addressing every limitation:

**What Changed from C2:**
- **Weight reduction:** 700g → 500g (200g/29% lighter through aluminum optimization)
- **Adjustment precision:** 30 clicks → 42 clicks (40% finer adjustment)
- **Burr retention:** Redesigned burr chamber (lower retention, easier cleaning)
- **Ergonomics:** Longer crank handle, better grip ergonomics
- **Aesthetics:** Refined modern design language
- **Catch cup:** Magnetic attachment (secure, quick-release)
- **Assembly:** Improved tolerances, smoother operation

**What Stayed the Same:**
- S2C 660 steel burrs (proven performance)
- 38mm burr diameter (optimal size for hand grinding)
- Aluminum body construction (lightweight priority)
- Clockwise adjustment (finer grinding direction)

This evolution positioned Chestnut X as Timemore's answer to Comandante C40—comparable grind quality, better portability, lower price.

### S2C 660 Steel Burrs

The Chestnut X employs 38mm S2C 660 stainless steel conical burrs—Timemore's proprietary burr design:

**Burr Specifications:**
- **Diameter:** 38mm (smaller than Comandante's 40mm, larger than entry grinders' 28-33mm)
- **Material:** Stainless steel (not ceramic, not coated)
- **Designation:** S2C 660 (Timemore proprietary designation)
- **Geometry:** Conical burrs optimized for pour-over
- **Coating:** None (raw stainless steel)
- **Origin:** Manufactured in China to Timemore specifications

**Burr Performance:**
- **Grind consistency:** Excellent (tight particle distribution for hand grinder)
- **Fines production:** Low to moderate (typical conical burr behavior)
- **Uniformity:** Very good across pour-over range, good at espresso range
- **Clarity in cup:** Excellent for pour-over (distinct flavor notes)
- **Body:** Good (conical burrs produce balanced body)

**Comparison to Other Burrs:**
- **Comandante burrs:** Slightly better uniformity (marginal difference in cup)
- **S2C 660:** Very close to Comandante, better than entry grinders
- **Ceramic burrs (Hario):** S2C 660 significantly better consistency
- **Flat burrs (DF64):** Different profile (flat burrs produce more clarity, less body)

**Expected Lifespan:**
- **Burr longevity:** 1,000-2,000 lbs coffee (5-10+ years typical home use)
- **Replacement cost:** $40-$60 (burrs replaceable, not lifetime warranty)
- **Maintenance:** Periodic cleaning only (no sharpening, no adjustment)

### 42-Click Adjustment System

The Chestnut X features 42-click adjustment mechanism providing precise grind size control:

**Adjustment Specifications:**
- **Total clicks:** 42 clicks full range
- **Clicks per rotation:** 10 clicks (approximately)
- **Click spacing:** Precise detents (audible/tactile feedback)
- **Range:** Turkish coffee fine → French press coarse
- **Direction:** Clockwise = finer, counterclockwise = coarser
- **Zero point:** Burrs touching (chirping sound), back off 1-2 clicks for finest setting

**Grind Range by Clicks:**
- **Espresso:** 10-16 clicks from zero (tight range, precise adjustment critical)
- **Moka pot:** 14-18 clicks
- **Aeropress:** 16-22 clicks
- **Pour-over (V60):** 20-26 clicks
- **Chemex:** 24-28 clicks
- **French press:** 28-35 clicks
- **Cold brew:** 32-40 clicks

**Adjustment Precision:**
The 42-click system provides finer adjustment than entry grinders (20-30 clicks typical) but coarser than some espresso-focused grinders (90-200 clicks):

- **Entry grinders (30 clicks):** Large adjustment steps (difficult espresso dialing)
- **Chestnut X (42 clicks):** Moderate steps (workable for espresso, excellent for pour-over)
- **1Zpresso K-Max (90 clicks):** Fine steps (espresso-optimized)
- **Comandante C40 (30 clicks + Red Clix):** Similar to Chestnut X with modification

**Practical Impact:**
For pour-over brewing, 42 clicks provides excellent precision—1-2 click adjustments noticeably impact extraction. For espresso, adjustment steps larger than dedicated espresso grinders but workable with patience.

## Maintenance & Care

### Routine Maintenance

**After Every Grind:**
- Tap out loose grounds from catch cup
- Wipe exterior with dry cloth
- Store in dry location

**Weekly (Daily Use):**
- Knock out retained grounds (tap grinder)
- Wipe catch cup interior
- Check magnetic connection cleanliness

**Monthly:**
- Brush out burr chamber (remove catch cup, insert brush, rotate)
- Wipe adjustment mechanism
- Check for unusual sounds/resistance

**Every 3-6 Months:**
- Full disassembly and deep clean
- Remove burrs, brush thoroughly
- Wipe all internal surfaces
- Inspect for wear
- Reassemble and test

### Deep Cleaning Procedure

**Disassembly:**
1. Remove catch cup
2. Unscrew adjustment knob
3. Remove top cap/hopper
4. Remove handle
5. Separate burr carrier from body
6. Remove burrs from carrier

**Cleaning:**
1. Brush all burr surfaces (soft brush, no water)
2. Compressed air (optional, removes fine particles)
3. Wipe body interior with dry cloth
4. Clean adjustment threads (remove coffee oil buildup)
5. Wipe external surfaces

**Reassembly:**
1. Replace burrs in carrier (correct orientation)
2. Insert burr carrier into body
3. Replace handle
4. Replace top cap
5. Screw in adjustment knob (find zero point)
6. Back off 1-2 clicks from zero
7. Test grind (should be very fine)

**Frequency:**
- Light use (1 brew daily): Every 6 months
- Moderate use (2-3 brews daily): Every 3 months
- Heavy use (5+ brews daily): Every 1-2 months

### Burr Calibration & Adjustment

**Finding Zero Point:**
1. Adjust to finest setting (clockwise)
2. Continue adjusting until burrs touch
3. Listen for chirping/squealing sound
4. Back off 1-2 clicks (burrs separated slightly)
5. This is "zero" reference point

**Documenting Settings:**
- Record clicks from zero for each brew method
- Example: "V60 = 24 clicks from zero"
- Adjust ±1-2 clicks to fine-tune extraction

**Burr Alignment:**
- Factory-aligned (minimal wobble)
- No user adjustment needed typically
- If grinding inconsistent, check burr installation orientation

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Grinding Feels Difficult:**
- **Cause:** Grind too fine, burrs misaligned, coffee oil buildup
- **Solution:** Coarsen grind, clean burrs, verify burr installation

**Adjustment Clicks Feel Loose:**
- **Cause:** Thread wear (eventual with aluminum)
- **Solution:** Clean threads, apply light food-safe grease, eventual replacement (years of use)

**Grounds in Catch Cup Inconsistent:**
- **Cause:** Uneven bean feeding, popcorning
- **Solution:** Grind smaller batches (20-25g max), shake during grinding

**Catch Cup Falls Off:**
- **Cause:** Magnet weakening, coffee residue on magnetic surface
- **Solution:** Clean magnetic surfaces, verify magnet intact, replace if severely weakened

**Grinding Sounds Abnormal:**
- **Cause:** Foreign object (stone, unroasted bean), burr damage
- **Solution:** Stop immediately, disassemble, inspect burrs, remove foreign object
