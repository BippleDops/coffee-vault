## üî¨ Technical Notes

### Optimal Settings

**Recommended Grind Settings** (counted from finest):
- **French press**: 15-18 clicks from finest (coarse)
- **Drip coffee**: 10-13 clicks from finest (medium)
- **Pour-over** (V60): 8-10 clicks from finest (medium-fine)
- **AeroPress**: 6-8 clicks from finest (medium-fine)
- **Espresso**: 0-3 clicks from finest (fine, but still inadequate for espresso)

**Note**: Settings unmarked; must count clicks from fully-tightened position.

### Common Mistakes

**Grinding Too Fine for Espresso**:
- **Issue**: Grinder produces excessive fines (channeling, bitter shots)
- **Solution**: Accept JavaPresse not suitable for espresso; use for drip/pour-over instead

**Not Counting Adjustment Clicks**:
- **Issue**: Unmarked settings make it difficult to return to specific grind size
- **Solution**: Count clicks from zero position (fully tightened), note setting for each brew method

**Overfilling Hopper**:
- **Issue**: 35g max capacity; overfilling causes beans to jam
- **Solution**: Fill to 35g maximum (~2 heaping tablespoons beans)

**Grinding Oily Dark Roasts**:
- **Issue**: Ceramic burrs clog with oily beans (slow grinding, inconsistent)
- **Solution**: Use medium roasts; clean burrs frequently if using dark roasts

### Maintenance

**After Each Use**:
- Brush out residual grounds (included brush)
- Wipe catch container with dry cloth

**Weekly** (daily use):
- Disassemble and brush burrs
- Rinse catch container (hand wash, not dishwasher)

**Monthly**:
- Deep clean burrs with Grindz tablets or dry rice
- Check adjustment ring for cracks
- Tighten handle shaft if loose

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Grind too coarse even at finest setting**: Burrs worn (500-800 lbs coffee), replace burrs or grinder
- **Handle wobbles**: Shaft loose, tighten center screw
- **Adjustment shifts during grinding**: Hold adjustment ring steady while grinding
- **Catch container threads stripped**: Replace catch container ($5-8) or entire grinder

**The Reality**: The JavaPresse is a disposable grinder, not a long-term investment. The 38mm ceramic burrs produce inconsistent grinds with visible fines and boulders, causing channeling in pour-over and making espresso impossible despite marketing claims. Grinding 18g takes 2-3 minutes of moderate hand effort, becoming tedious for daily use. The plastic adjustment ring and catch container threads crack and strip within 1-3 years. The unmarked adjustment settings (count clicks from zero) make it difficult to return to specific grind sizes. The ceramic burrs dull faster than steel and clog when grinding oily dark roasts.
