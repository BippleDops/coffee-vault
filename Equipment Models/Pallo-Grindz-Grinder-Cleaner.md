## üî¨ Technical Notes

### Optimal Usage

**Recommended Cleaning Frequency**:
- **Light use** (<1 kg/month): Every 3-6 months
- **Moderate use** (1-3 kg/month): Every 1-3 months
- **Heavy use** (>3 kg/month): Monthly
- **Commercial use** (cafes): Weekly to monthly
- **Espresso grinders**: More frequent (oily beans, fine grinding)
- **Brew grinders**: Less frequent (coarser grinding, less oil)

**Step-by-Step Optimal Technique**:
1. **Remove beans**: Empty hopper completely (do not mix Grindz with coffee)
2. **Adjust grind**: Set to medium-coarse (avoid espresso-fine, may clog)
3. **Measure Grindz**: 35-40g Grindz (use included scoop or kitchen scale)
4. **Run grinder**: Turn on, run Grindz through burrs completely (listen for all tablets to pass)
5. **Inspect**: Open grinder (if accessible), verify Grindz passed through (tan powder visible)
6. **Vacuum**: Vacuum out Grindz dust from catch bin, grinder exterior
7. **Purge**: Grind 30-50g fresh coffee beans (discard this coffee‚Äîcontains Grindz residue)
8. **Inspect purge**: Check purge coffee for tan color (if tan visible, purge another 20-30g)
9. **Resume**: Grinder now clean, ready for normal coffee grinding

**Purge Coffee Amount**:
- **Minimum**: 30g coffee (most users sufficient)
- **Recommended**: 50g coffee (ensures complete Grindz removal)
- **Paranoid**: 100g coffee (complete peace of mind, zero residue)
- **Espresso grinders**: 50-100g purge (retention higher in doserless grinders)

**Signs Grinder Needs Cleaning**:
- Grind clumping increases (oils accumulating)
- Rancid smell from grinder (months-old coffee oils)
- Grind consistency degrades (visible boulders/fines increase)
- Shots channeling (espresso extraction uneven)
- Coffee tastes off (rancid oil flavors)

### Common Mistakes

**Using Grindz on Blade Grinder**:
- **Issue**: Tablets may damage blade mechanism (not designed for burr cleaning)
- **Solution**: Only use Grindz on burr grinders

**Insufficient Purge**:
- **Issue**: Grindz residue remains in grinder, contaminates next coffee
- **Solution**: Purge 50g coffee (discard), inspect for tan color

**Using on Espresso-Fine Setting**:
- **Issue**: Grindz may clog burrs at very fine settings
- **Solution**: Set grinder to medium-coarse before running Grindz

**Not Vacuuming Dust**:
- **Issue**: Grindz creates more dust than coffee (messy)
- **Solution**: Vacuum grinder exterior and catch bin after Grindz

**Expecting Full Clean**:
- **Issue**: Grindz is maintenance cleaning (not deep clean)
- **Solution**: Still disassemble grinder quarterly for full burr cleaning

### Troubleshooting

**Grindz Not Passing Through**:
- **Cause**: Grind setting too fine (tablets clogging burrs)
- **Solution**: Set grinder to medium-coarse, run again

**Tan Residue in Purge Coffee**:
- **Cause**: Insufficient purge (Grindz residue remains)
- **Solution**: Continue purging until coffee is normal color (no tan tint)

**Grinder Still Oily After Grindz**:
- **Cause**: Heavy buildup requires full disassembly (Grindz surface-level only)
- **Solution**: Disassemble grinder, manually brush burrs with Grindz or Full Circle

**Dust Everywhere**:
- **Cause**: Grindz creates more dust than coffee
- **Solution**: Vacuum grinder exterior immediately after use
