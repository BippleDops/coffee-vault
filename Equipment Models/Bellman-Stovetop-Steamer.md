**Routine Maintenance**:
- After each use: Rinse all components, dry thoroughly
- Weekly: Deep clean steam wand (milk residue removal critical)
- Monthly: Inspect gaskets, check safety valve operation
- Seasonally: Descale if using hard water
- Annually: Replace gaskets and seals

**Cleaning Procedure**:
1. Cool completely after use (NEVER open hot - dangerous)
2. Disassemble: Remove brew head, filter basket
3. Rinse boiler chamber with clean water
4. Clean steam wand: Purge steam, wipe exterior, soak tip if milk buildup
5. Wash removable parts with soap and water
6. Rinse thoroughly (no soap residue)
7. Dry all components completely (prevent corrosion)
8. Reassemble or store disassembled

**Steam Wand Deep Cleaning** (Critical):
- Daily: Purge steam after each milk steaming (blows out milk residue)
- Daily: Wipe steam wand with damp cloth immediately after steaming
- Weekly: Soak steam wand tip in hot water or coffee cleaner
- Monthly: Use steam wand cleaning product (dissolves milk buildup)
- Why critical: Milk residue in steam wand creates bacteria, affects performance

**Descaling** (Hard Water Areas):
- Frequency: Every 1-3 months (depends on water hardness)
- Method: Fill boiler with descaling solution or white vinegar diluted 50/50
- Heat: Bring to light boil, let sit 30 minutes
- Rinse: Empty, rinse thoroughly 3-4 times with clean water
- Run through: Brew water through system to flush completely

**Gasket Replacement**:
- Signs of wear: Coffee/steam leaking, hissing sounds, visible cracks
- Frequency: Every 1-2 years (depends on use frequency)
- Removal: Pry out old gasket carefully
- Installation: Press new gasket into groove evenly
- Cost: $5-15 for replacement gaskets
- Availability: Specialty coffee retailers, online (search "Bellman gasket")

**Safety Valve Maintenance**:
- Check regularly: Ensure valve moves freely
- Never block: NEVER obstruct safety valve (explosion risk)
- Clean: Remove any coffee or mineral buildup
- Test: Periodically check that valve releases pressure (carefully)
- Replace if damaged: Critical safety component

**Common Issues & Solutions**:
- **Weak steam pressure**: Descale boiler, check for leaks, ensure adequate heat
- **Leaking from gasket**: Replace gasket
- **Steam wand clogged**: Soak in cleaner, use wire to clear milk buildup
- **Metallic taste in coffee**: Descale, rinse thoroughly
- **Safety valve releasing prematurely**: Check for blockages, may need replacement
- **Won't build pressure**: Check all seals, ensure proper assembly

**Longevity Tips**:
- Always use filtered water (reduces descaling need)
- Purge steam wand after every milk steaming (prevents buildup)
- Never overfill water reservoir (fill to line only)
- Don't leave water sitting in boiler (empty after use)
- Replace gaskets proactively (before failure)

### Technique Resources

**How to Use Bellman for Milk Steaming**:

**Preparation**:
1. Fill water reservoir to line (500ml, do NOT overfill)
2. Ensure safety valve is clear and functional
3. Close lid securely

**Heating**:
1. Place on stovetop (medium heat)
2. Heat 10-15 minutes until steam pressure builds
3. Monitor pressure gauge if equipped (3-4 bar target)
4. When steam begins to escape from steam wand, pressure is ready

**Steaming Milk**:
1. Pour cold milk into pitcher (fill 1/3 to 1/2 full, allows expansion)
2. Insert steam wand tip just below milk surface
3. Open steam valve slowly (adjust flow with valve knob)
4. Create whirlpool motion (incorporate air, develop microfoam)
5. When milk reaches 60-65¬∞C (140-150¬∞F), close valve (use thermometer)
6. Tap pitcher on counter (removes large bubbles)
7. Swirl milk (polishes microfoam to velvety texture)

**Purging Steam Wand** (Critical):
1. Immediately after steaming, open valve briefly (purge milk residue)
2. Wipe steam wand with damp cloth
3. Prevents milk buildup (critical for hygiene and performance)

**Brewing Espresso with Bellman** (Optional):
1. After heating water/building steam, can brew espresso-style coffee
2. Fill filter basket with fine-ground coffee (14-18g)
3. Tamp lightly
4. Attach brew head
5. Place cup under spout
6. Open brew valve (pressurized water extracts coffee)
7. Result: Moka-style espresso (1-2 bar, not true 9-bar espresso)

**Safety Critical Steps**:
- NEVER overfill water reservoir (dangerous overpressure)
- ALWAYS ensure safety valve is clear (never block)
- NEVER open when hot (wait until cool, pressure released)
- Use low-medium heat (high heat can damage seals, cause overpressure)
- Monitor pressure gauge (don't exceed safe limits)

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**:
1. Overfilling water (causes overpressure, dangerous)
2. Blocking safety valve (extremely dangerous)
3. Opening when hot (burn risk, pressure injury)
4. Too much heat (damages seals, excessive pressure)
5. Not purging steam wand (milk buildup, hygiene issues)
6. Wrong milk temperature (too hot = scalded, too cool = understeamed)
7. Not cleaning regularly (performance degrades, safety risk)

### Design Analysis

**Pressure Boiler System**:
- Functions like pressure cooker (sealed vessel builds steam pressure)
- Water heated to boiling, steam pressure builds (3-4 bar)
- Steam diverted to steam wand (for milk steaming)
- Remaining pressure can drive espresso brewing (1-2 bar at brew head)
- Safety valve: Releases excess pressure (prevents dangerous overpressure)

**Steam Wand Mechanism**:
- Valve control: User adjusts steam flow (critical for milk steaming technique)
- Single-hole tip: Generates focused steam jet (creates microfoam)
- Articulating arm: Positions wand for different pitcher sizes
- Metal construction: Durable, conducts heat (wand gets hot)

**Why It's Challenging to Use**:
- Pressure build requires time (10-15 minutes heat-up)
- Precise water fill critical (overfill = overpressure danger, underfill = insufficient steam)
- Heat management: Too much heat = overpressure, too little = insufficient steam

### Safety Considerations

**Critical Safety Rules**:
1. **NEVER overfill water**: Fill to line only (overfill = overpressure = explosion risk)
2. **NEVER block safety valve**: Always keep clear (critical pressure release)
3. **NEVER open when hot**: Wait until completely cool (pressure and burn risk)
4. **Always use low-medium heat**: High heat = excessive pressure, seal damage
5. **Monitor pressure gauge**: Stay within safe range (3-4 bar operating)
6. **Inspect before each use**: Check gaskets, safety valve, seals
7. **Replace worn parts**: Old gaskets fail, causing leaks or pressure loss

**Potential Dangers**:
- **Explosion**: If safety valve blocked and overpressurized (very rare but catastrophic)
- **Steam burns**: Steam wand produces superheated steam (serious burn risk)
- **Scalding water**: If opened hot, pressurized water can spray (severe burn risk)
- **Pressure injuries**: Rapid pressure release can cause injury

**Accident Prevention**:
- Read manual thoroughly (understand pressure and steam safety)
- Never leave unattended while heating (monitor pressure build)
- Keep children away (hot, pressurized, dangerous)
- Use on stable surface (prevent tipping, spilling)
- Regular maintenance (worn seals can fail dangerously)
- Respect the equipment (it's a pressure vessel, not a toy)

**First-Time User Advice**:
- Watch multiple YouTube tutorials (understand operation before attempting)
- Start with water only (practice heating, pressure, steam without coffee/milk)
- Fill conservatively (better too little water than too much)
- Use low heat (patience prevents overpressure)
- Practice steam wand purging (essential skill)
- Learn milk steaming technique (similar to espresso machine, requires practice)
