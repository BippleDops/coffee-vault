### Maintenance & Care

**Routine Maintenance**:
- After each use: Rinse with water, remove filter
- Weekly: Wash with soap and water
- Dishwasher: Top rack safe (though hand washing recommended for longevity)
- Valve cleaning: Occasional rinse of valve mechanism
- Storage: Store with coaster on top (keeps dust out)

**Cleaning Procedure**:
1. Remove used filter and dispose
2. Rinse Clever with warm water (removes most coffee residue)
3. Wash with dish soap and soft sponge (avoid abrasive scrubbers)
4. Clean valve area: Rinse bottom thoroughly, ensure valve moves freely
5. Rinse thoroughly (ensure no soap residue)
6. Dry or air dry (Tritan dries quickly)
7. Place coaster on top for storage

**Deep Cleaning** (Monthly or as needed):
- Soak in hot water with baking soda (removes coffee oils)
- Or soak in diluted vinegar solution (descaling if hard water)
- Scrub gently with soft brush (especially around valve)
- Rinse thoroughly
- Check valve operation (ensure smooth opening/closing)

**Valve Maintenance**:
- Check periodically: Ensure valve opens/closes smoothly
- Clean if sticky: Rinse valve area thoroughly
- Lubrication: Generally not needed (avoid oils that could affect taste)
- Replacement: Rarely needed (valve is durable)

**Common Issues & Solutions**:
- **Slow drainage**: Clean valve (coffee oils may be sticky), ensure using proper surface to activate valve
- **Leaking when lifted**: Valve seal worn (rare), check for damage
- **Coffee grounds in cup**: Filter not seated properly, or wrong filter size
- **Cloudy plastic**: Hard water deposits (vinegar soak resolves)
- **Staining**: Use baking soda paste or specialized coffee equipment cleaner

**Longevity Tips**:
- Hand wash when possible (extends valve life)
- Don't use abrasive scrubbers (scratches Tritan)
- Dry thoroughly before storage (prevents any buildup)
- Check valve regularly (ensure free movement)
- Use proper filters (#4 Melitta) for best fit
- Don't leave coffee sitting in dripper (staining prevention)

### Technique Resources

**Standard Clever Dripper Recipe**:

**Ratio**: 1:16 coffee to water (e.g., 25g coffee to 400g water)

**Grind**: Medium (like drip coffee, between pour-over and French press)

**Method**:
1. **Rinse filter**: Place #4 filter in Clever, rinse with hot water (preheat and remove paper taste)
2. **Add coffee**: Add ground coffee (25g for 400ml water)
3. **Start timer**: Begin timing
4. **Add water**: Pour all water at once (400g), aiming for saturation
5. **Stir**: Gentle stir to ensure full saturation (optional but recommended)
6. **Steep**: Let sit for 2:30-3:00 minutes (adjust to taste)
7. **Place on mug**: At 3:00, place Clever on mug/server (valve opens, coffee drains)
8. **Drain**: Let drain completely (30-60 seconds)
9. **Enjoy**: Total time ~3:30-4:00 minutes

**Variables to Adjust**:
- Steep time: 2:00-4:00 minutes (longer = stronger, shorter = lighter)
- Grind size: Finer = stronger, coarser = lighter
- Coffee dose: More coffee = stronger, less = lighter
- Water temp: 90-96¬∞C (195-205¬∞F), hotter for light roasts, cooler for dark

**James Hoffmann-Style Recipe**:
- 30g coffee, 500g water
- Medium grind
- Rinse filter
- Add coffee, add all water
- Stir well at 0:00
- Steep until 2:00
- Place on server, drain
- Clean, balanced results

**Short Steep Recipe** (Faster brewing):
- 25g coffee, 400g water
- Medium-fine grind (slightly finer than standard)
- Rinse filter
- Add coffee, add water
- Steep 1:30-2:00
- Drain
- Faster brewing, similar results

**Bloom Method** (More complex):
- 25g coffee, 400g water
- Medium grind
- Rinse filter
- Add coffee
- Add 50g water, bloom 30 seconds
- Add remaining 350g water
- Steep 2:00 from full water addition
- Drain
- Slightly more developed flavors

**Cold Brew Clever** (Experimental):
- 25g coffee, 400g cold water
- Coarse grind
- Add coffee and cold water
- Steep 12-24 hours (room temp or fridge)
- Drain
- Cold brew concentrate

**Troubleshooting Recipes**:
- **Too sour/weak**: Grind finer, steep longer, use hotter water
- **Too bitter/strong**: Grind coarser, steep shorter, use cooler water
- **Perfect but want stronger**: Add more coffee (increase dose)
- **Perfect but want lighter**: Less coffee or more water

### Design Analysis

**Valve Mechanism Innovation**:
- Simple spring-loaded design (few failure points)
- Gravity-activated (no buttons, levers, or complexity)
- Perfect seal when lifted (holds liquid indefinitely)
- Quick release when placed on surface
- Ingenious simplicity (elegant engineering)

**Why It's So Forgiving**:
- **Immersion brewing**: Full saturation ensures even extraction (no channeling like pour-over)
- **Flat bottom**: Even water distribution (like Kalita Wave)
- **Paper filter**: Removes fines and oils (clean cup, forgiving of over-extraction)
- **Fixed steep time**: Once you find your time, consistent results
- **Grind flexibility**: Works with wide range of grind sizes (not as critical as pour-over)

**Hybrid Concept Benefits**:
- **Immersion phase**: Ensures full saturation, even extraction, consistency
- **Drip phase**: Paper filter clarity, removes fines, clean finish
- **Combines strengths**: Consistency of immersion, clarity of pour-over
- **Avoids weaknesses**: Not muddy like French Press, not technique-dependent like V60

**Material Choice (Tritan)**:
- Clarity: Crystal-clear like glass (beautiful, functional)
- Durability: Shatterproof (survives drops)
- Heat resistance: Handles boiling water safely
- Food safety: BPA-free, no flavor absorption
- Cost-effective: Cheaper than glass/ceramic while being more durable

**Why Clever Is Ideal for Beginners**:
1. **Impossible to mess up**: Add coffee, add water, wait, drain. That's it.
2. **Consistent results**: Immersion provides consistency regardless of pour technique
3. **Forgiving grind**: Works with wide grind range (not as critical as pour-over)
4. **No special equipment**: Any kettle works (no gooseneck required)

### Best Practices Summary

**For Best Coffee**:
1. Use fresh coffee (roasted within 2-4 weeks)
2. Medium grind (like drip coffee)
3. Rinse filter (removes paper taste, preheats)
4. 1:16 ratio (e.g., 25g coffee to 400g water)
5. Hot water 90-96¬∞C (195-205¬∞F)
6. Stir gently after adding water (ensures full saturation)
7. Steep 2:30-3:00 minutes (adjust to taste)
8. Drain completely (place on mug, let finish)

**For Consistency**:
1. Use same dose every time (e.g., always 25g)
2. Use scale for water (e.g., always 400g)
3. Same grind setting (consistency)
4. Same steep time (e.g., always 3:00)
5. Result: Nearly identical coffee every time

**For Longevity**:
1. Rinse after each use
2. Wash with soap weekly
3. Clean valve area regularly (ensure smooth operation)
4. Don't use abrasive scrubbers (scratches Tritan)
5. Store with coaster on top (dust protection)
6. Hand wash when possible (extends valve life)
