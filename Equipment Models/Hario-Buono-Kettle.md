### Gooseneck Spout Design

**Why Gooseneck?**:
- **Precision**: Narrow opening controls flow rate
- **Aim**: Long spout allows accurate targeting
- **Consistency**: Same pour angle = same flow rate
- **Laminar flow**: Curve design reduces turbulence

**Buono Spout Geometry**:
- **Curve**: Gentle S-curve (two bends)
- **Diameter**: 7mm tip (narrow, precise)
- **Length**: 110mm projection (adequate reach)
- **Angle**: ~30¬∞ downward from body (ergonomic)
- **Taper**: Gradual taper to tip (smooth flow acceleration)

**Flow Dynamics**:
- **Laminar flow**: Smooth column (no splashing)
- **Bernoulli effect**: Narrowing accelerates flow (controlled speed)
- **Gravity**: Downward angle assists flow (natural pouring motion)
- **Surface tension**: Narrow tip creates consistent drip (predictable slow pour)

**Comparison to Other Spouts**:
- **vs. Takahiro**: Buono less precise (Takahiro narrower, more controlled), but 1/3 the price
- **vs. Fellow Stagg**: Buono slightly less precise (Stagg optimized curve), but 1/3 the price
- **vs. Standard kettle**: Buono vastly superior (standard kettle = uncontrolled gush)

### Material Science

**Stainless Steel 18-8 (SUS304)**:
- **Composition**: 18% chromium, 8% nickel, balance iron
- **Corrosion resistance**: Excellent (chromium oxide layer)
- **Food safety**: FDA approved, no leaching
- **Durability**: High (resists denting better than aluminum)
- **Thermal conductivity**: Moderate (slower than copper, faster than cast iron)

**Thin Gauge Advantages**:
- **Weight**: Lightweight (easy to pour)
- **Heating**: Fast heat-up (thin steel conducts heat quickly)
- **Cost**: Cheaper manufacturing (less material)

**Thin Gauge Disadvantages**:
- **Denting**: Dents easily (cosmetic, rarely functional issue)
- **Warping**: Can warp if overheated empty (user error, preventable)
- **Heat retention**: Poor (no insulation, cools quickly)

**Phenolic Resin Handle**:
- **Material**: Thermosetting plastic (heat-resistant)
- **Heat resistance**: Up to 120¬∞C (248¬∞F)
- **Durability**: Good (resists cracking, breaking under normal use)

### Maintenance & Care

**Daily Cleaning**:
- **Rinse**: Rinse with hot water after each use
- **Wipe exterior**: Dry with soft cloth (prevent water spots)
- **Dry interior**: Leave lid open to air-dry (prevent moisture buildup)

**Weekly Cleaning**:
- **Wash interior**: Soapy water, soft sponge (remove mineral deposits)
- **Descale**: If hard water, white vinegar rinse (dissolve mineral buildup)
- **Polish exterior**: Stainless steel cleaner (optional, cosmetic)

**Monthly Maintenance**:
- **Deep descaling**: Boil vinegar solution (1:1 vinegar:water), let sit, rinse
- **Handle inspection**: Check rivets, tighten if loose (rare issue)
- **Lid hinge**: Check spring tension (replace if weakened)

**Descaling Process** (hard water areas):
1. Fill kettle with 1:1 white vinegar:water solution
2. Boil solution (turn off when boiling starts)
3. Let sit 15-30 minutes (dissolve mineral deposits)
4. Dump solution, rinse thoroughly (5-6 rinses to remove vinegar smell)
5. Boil clean water, discard (final rinse)

**Common Issues & Solutions**:

**Water spots (cosmetic)**:
- **Cause**: Mineral deposits from water, air drying
- **Solution**: Wipe with vinegar, rinse, dry with cloth (prevents spots)

**Discoloration (rainbow effect)**:
- **Cause**: Heat exposure (normal stainless steel reaction)
- **Solution**: Cosmetic only (doesn't affect function), or polish with stainless cleaner

**Dents (cosmetic)**:
- **Cause**: Dropping, impact (thin steel dents easily)
- **Solution**: Cosmetic only (rarely affects function, live with it or replace)

**Warped base**:
- **Cause**: Overheating empty kettle (user error)
- **Solution**: Prevention only (don't overheat), warped base may wobble (replace if severe)

**Lid hinge weakening**:
- **Cause**: Repeated use, spring fatigue (wear item)
- **Solution**: Replace kettle (lid not sold separately, or DIY repair with new spring)

## üéì Usage Tips & Techniques

### Getting Started

**First Use**:
1. Wash kettle (warm soapy water, rinse thoroughly)
2. Boil water, discard (remove manufacturing residue)
3. Boil second batch, use for coffee (ready to use)

**Basic Technique** (V60 example):
1. Fill kettle with ~600ml water (500ml brew + 100ml rinse)
2. Boil on stove (4-6 minutes)
3. While boiling: Grind coffee, prep V60
4. Water boils: Remove from heat, wait 2-3 minutes (to ~93¬∞C)
5. Optional: Check temp with thermometer
6. Pour bloom (40g water, slow drip, center pour)
7. Wait 30-45 seconds
8. Pour main pour (spiral, slow and steady, total 500g)

**Pouring Posture**:
- **Hold kettle**: Firm but relaxed grip (not too tight)
- **Wrist control**: Wrist motion controls pour (not arm)
- **Elbow down**: Keep elbow at side (stable, consistent)
- **Height**: 2-4 inches above coffee bed (consistent height = consistent flow)
- **Angle**: 45-60¬∞ kettle tilt (find comfortable angle, keep consistent)

**Flow Rate Control**:
- **Slow pour**: Minimal tilt (kettle more upright)
- **Fast pour**: More tilt (kettle more horizontal)
- **Consistent flow**: Same angle = same flow rate (muscle memory)

### Advanced Techniques

**Temperature Surfing** (no thermometer):
- **Boil**: 100¬∞C (rolling boil)
- **1 min off heat**: 98-99¬∞C (very light roasts, turbo extractions)
- **2-3 min off heat**: 93-95¬∞C (light-medium roasts, V60 sweet spot)
- **4-5 min off heat**: 90-92¬∞C (medium roasts, balanced extractions)
- **6-8 min off heat**: 88-90¬∞C (dark roasts, reduced bitterness)

**Precision Temperature** (with thermometer):
- **Clip-on thermometer**: Attach to spout or body (monitor temp)
- **Infrared thermometer**: Point at water surface (quick check)
- **Target temp**: Adjust wait time to hit target (repeat for consistency)

**Preheating Kettle**:
- **Why**: Preheated kettle loses less heat (maintains brew temp longer)
- **How**: Rinse kettle with hot water before boiling brew water
- **Effect**: Slower temperature drop (5-minute loss reduced to 3-4¬∞C)

**Multi-Brew Efficiency**:
- **Boil full kettle**: 1.2L water
- **Pour first brew**: 500ml (leaves 700ml)
- **Remaining water**: Still hot (~90-95¬∞C)
- **Second brew**: Use immediately (or reheat if needed)
- **Time savings**: Faster than full boil for second brew

**Camping/Travel Tips**:
- **Pack carefully**: Nest inside pot or wrap in cloth (protect from dents)
- **Campfire use**: Works on campfire grate (wipe soot off after)
- **Wind protection**: Use windscreen on camp stove (faster boiling)
- **Water source**: Filter water before boiling (protect kettle from sediment)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Overfilling**
- **Problem**: Spills when boiling, difficult to pour
- **Solution**: Fill to 1L max (practical capacity, not 1.2L max)

**Mistake 2: Boil-dry**
- **Problem**: Overheating empty kettle (warps base, damages kettle)
- **Solution**: Never leave empty kettle on hot stove

**Mistake 3: Pouring too fast**
- **Problem**: Turbulence, uneven extraction, channeling
- **Solution**: Slow, steady pour (practice control, patience)

**Mistake 4: Inconsistent pour height**
- **Problem**: Variable flow rate, uneven extraction
- **Solution**: Maintain 2-4 inches above coffee bed (consistent height)

**Mistake 5: Ignoring temperature**
- **Problem**: Too hot (bitter, over-extracted) or too cool (sour, under-extracted)
- **Solution**: Use thermometer, or develop feel for timing (2-3 min off heat ‚âà 93¬∞C)

**Mistake 6: Skipping descaling**
- **Problem**: Mineral buildup (slower heating, reduced capacity, metallic taste)
- **Solution**: Descale monthly (hard water areas) or quarterly (soft water)
