## üìã Overview

Organic certification verifies coffee grown without synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or fertilizers - requiring 3-year transition, annual inspections, and documented practices ensuring environmental sustainability and consumer health safety.

**What is Organic**:
- **No Synthetics**: No synthetic chemicals (pesticides, fertilizers, herbicides)
- **Natural Inputs**: Compost, organic fertilizers, biological controls only
- **Transition**: 3-year transition period (land must be chemical-free)
- **Certification**: USDA, EU, JAS (Japan), others
- **Inspection**: Annual third-party verification

**Why Organic Matters**:
- **Environmental**: Soil health, biodiversity, water quality
- **Health**: No chemical residues on coffee
- **Premium**: Organic commands $0.30-1.00/lb premiums
- **Market**: Growing demand, consumer preference
- **Farmer**: Often organic by default (can't afford chemicals), certification formalizes

---

## ‚úÖ USDA Organic Standards

**Requirements**:
- **3-Year Transition**: Land chemical-free 36 months before harvest
- **Buffer Zones**: Separation from conventional farms
- **Pest Management**: Organic-approved methods only
- **Fertilizers**: Compost, organic-approved only
- **Record-Keeping**: Detailed documentation
- **Annual Inspection**: Third-party certifier visit

**Allowed**:
- Compost, manure (composted properly)
- Organic-approved pesticides (copper, sulfur, botanicals)
- Biological controls (beneficial insects)
- Cover crops, green manures

**Prohibited**:
- Synthetic pesticides
- Synthetic fertilizers
- GMOs
- Sewage sludge
- Irradiation

---

## üí∞ Economics

**Premium**: $0.30-1.00/lb above conventional  
**Certification Cost**: $500-2,000/year (barrier for small farmers)  
**Group Certification**: Cooperatives certify together (lower cost per farmer)

---

## üìù Summary

Organic certification requires 3-year chemical-free transition, natural inputs only, and annual third-party inspection - guaranteeing coffee grown without synthetic chemicals while providing environmental benefits and premium prices ($0.30-1.00/lb above conventional) that support sustainable farming.
