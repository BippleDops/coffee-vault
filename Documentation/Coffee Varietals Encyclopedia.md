## üåø Coffee Species Overview

### The Two Main Species

**Coffea Arabica** (Arabica):
- **Global Production**: 60-70%
- **Quality**: Higher, more complex
- **Caffeine**: 1.0-1.5%
- **Chromosomes**: 44 (tetraploid)
- **Growing**: Higher altitude, cooler temps, more delicate
- **Flavor**: Complex, sweet, acidic, aromatic
- **Price**: Higher
- **Use**: Specialty coffee, high-grade commercial

**Coffea Canephora** (Robusta):
- **Global Production**: 30-40%
- **Quality**: Lower, simpler
- **Caffeine**: 2.0-2.7% (almost double!)
- **Chromosomes**: 22 (diploid)
- **Growing**: Lower altitude, hotter temps, disease-resistant
- **Flavor**: Bitter, earthy, grain-like, less complex
- **Price**: Lower
- **Use**: Instant coffee, cheap blends, espresso (crema boost)

**Other Species** (rare):
- **Liberica**: 1-2% production, Malaysia/Philippines, woody, smoky
- **Excelsa**: Classified under Liberica, 7% in Southeast Asia
- **Eugenioides**: Wild species, parent of Arabica, very rare

## üå≥ Arabica Varietal Family Tree

### Heirloom / Wild Varieties

**Ethiopian Heirloom**:
- Thousands of wild varieties in Ethiopia
- Genetic diversity unmatched
- Not catalogued individually
- Source of all Arabica
- "Heirloom" is umbrella term
- Examples: Kurume, Wolisho, Dega

**Typica**:
- **Origin**: Base variety, spread from Yemen/Ethiopia
- **Characteristics**: Tall plant, low yield, exceptional quality
- **Flavor**: Clean, sweet, balanced, complex
- **Found**: Small farms worldwide, specialty focus
- **Status**: Declining due to low productivity

**Bourbon**:
- **Origin**: Natural mutation of Typica on R√©union Island (formerly Bourbon)
- **Characteristics**: 20-30% higher yield than Typica, excellent quality
- **Flavor**: Sweet, buttery, complex, balanced
- **Variations**: Red Bourbon, Yellow Bourbon, Pink Bourbon
- **Found**: Latin America, East Africa, specialty farms

## üß¨ Natural Mutations

### From Bourbon

**Caturra**:
- **Origin**: Brazil, 1930s (Bourbon mutation)
- **Characteristics**: Compact (dwarf), higher yield, good quality
- **Flavor**: Bright, clean, sweet, slightly less complex than Bourbon
- **Found**: Colombia, Central America, widespread

**Pacas**:
- **Origin**: El Salvador (Bourbon mutation)
- **Characteristics**: Similar to Caturra, compact
- **Flavor**: Good quality, balanced

**Villa Sarchi**:
- **Origin**: Costa Rica (Bourbon mutation)
- **Characteristics**: Compact, wind-resistant
- **Flavor**: Good acidity, clean

### From Typica

**Maragogype** ("Elephant Bean"):
- **Origin**: Brazil (Typica mutation)
- **Characteristics**: Extremely large beans (screen 19+), low yield
- **Flavor**: Mild, low acidity, unique
- **Status**: Novelty, rare

**Kent**:
- **Origin**: India (selected for rust resistance)
- **Characteristics**: Good quality, some rust tolerance

## üî¨ Hybrids & Crosses

### Caturra Family

**Catua√≠** (Caturra x Mundo Novo):
- **Developed**: Brazil, IAC
- **Characteristics**: Compact, productive, wind-resistant, good quality
- **Varieties**: Red Catua√≠, Yellow Catua√≠
- **Found**: Brazil, Central America, common

**Catimor** (Caturra x Timor Hybrid):
- **Developed**: 1950s-60s
- **Purpose**: Coffee Leaf Rust resistance
- **Characteristics**: Resistant, high-yield, variable quality
- **Flavor**: Can be good but often flat, woody
- **Controversy**: Quality inferior to pure Arabica
- **Found**: Asia, some Latin America

**Sarchimor** (Villa Sarchi x Timor Hybrid):
- **Similar to Catimor**: Rust-resistant but quality trade-off
- **Found**: India, some Latin America

## üåü Prestige Varieties

### Gesha / Geisha

**Origin**: Gesha village, Ethiopia (wild coffee)  
**Fame**: Hacienda La Esmeralda, Panama (2004 Best of Panama auction)  
**Record**: $2,568/lb (2024 auction)

**Characteristics**:
- Jasmine, bergamot, tropical fruit, tea-like
- Exceptional cup quality
- Low yield
- Distinctive leaf shape (elongated)
- Delicate plant

**Flavor**:
- Floral: Jasmine, honeysuckle, bergamot
- Fruit: Tropical, stone fruit, citrus
- Tea-like body
- Complex, layered
- Wine-like acidity

**Where Grown**:
- Panama (most famous)
- Colombia (excellent results)
- Costa Rica, Guatemala, Ethiopia
- Prices: $40-300/lb

**Brewing**: Light roast only, delicate methods (pour-over, Chemex)

## üõ°Ô∏è Disease-Resistant Varieties

### Developed for Coffee Leaf Rust (La Roya)

**Timor Hybrid** (Arabusta):
- **Origin**: Natural Arabica x Robusta hybrid, Timor Island
- **Purpose**: Spontaneous rust-resistant hybrid
- **Quality**: Lower than pure Arabica
- **Use**: Breeding parent for resistant varieties

**Catimor** (Caturra x Timor):
- High-yield, rust-resistant
- Quality variable (improving)

**Sarchimor** (Villa Sarchi x Timor):
- Similar to Catimor

**IHCAFE 90, Lempira** (Honduras):
- Local Catimor selections
- Adapted to Honduras

## üèÜ Experimental & Rare

**Pacamara** (Pacas x Maragogype):
- **Origin**: El Salvador (1950s)
- **Characteristics**: Large beans, exceptional quality potential
- **Flavor**: Complex, fruit-forward, wine-like, full body
- **Status**: Specialty darling, competition coffee

**SL-28 & SL-34** (Kenya):
- **Developed**: Scott Laboratories, 1930s
- **Purpose**: Quality + drought tolerance
- **Flavor**: Blackcurrant, wine, complex, bright
- **Status**: Defines Kenyan coffee

**Yellow Bourbon** (Brazil):
- Natural mutation (yellow cherries)
- Very sweet, complex
- Premium pricing
- Distinctive

**Pink Bourbon**:
- Rare mutation
- Exceptional quality
- Very limited availability

**Laurina** (Bourbon Pointu):
- **Origin**: R√©union Island
- **Characteristics**: Low caffeine (0.6%), pointed beans
- **Flavor**: Delicate, complex
- **Status**: Very rare, expensive

**Java** (Historic Typica):
- Original Indonesian Typica
- Historic importance
- Now rare in pure form

## üîç Reading Coffee Bags

### Variety Labeling

**Specific Variety Listed**:
- "Bourbon", "Gesha", "SL-28" = higher quality indicator
- Roaster cares about variety
- Traceability likely good

**"Heirloom"**:
- Usually Ethiopian
- Could mean many varieties
- Generally good quality

**No Variety Listed**:
- Commercial coffee
- Lower traceability
- Variety unknown or mixed

**"Blend"**:
- Multiple varieties mixed
- Can be intentional (balance) or just available inventory
