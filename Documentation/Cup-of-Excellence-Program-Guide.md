## üìã Overview

Cup of Excellence is specialty coffee's premier quality competition and auction - blind cupping by international judges identifying exceptional coffees (87+ points) that sell at premium auctions, directly benefiting winning farmers while showcasing origin quality.

**What is Cup of Excellence**:
- **Annual Competition**: Held in 10-15 coffee-producing countries
- **Blind Cupping**: International judges, rigorous protocols
- **Score Threshold**: 87+ points to win (exceptional quality)
- **Auction**: Winning lots auctioned online (premium prices)
- **Direct Benefit**: Auction proceeds go directly to farmers

**Why CoE Matters**:
- **Quality Recognition**: International validation of excellence
- **Farmer Premium**: Auction prices 3-10x normal ($10-100+/lb)
- **Origin Promotion**: Showcases country quality globally
- **Transparency**: Public scores, farmer stories, traceability
- **Industry Standard**: CoE winners set quality benchmarks

---

## üèÖ Competition Process

**Stage 1: National Cupping**:
- **Submission**: Farmers submit samples
- **Pre-Selection**: National judges cup all submissions
- **Top Lots**: ~100-150 advance

**Stage 2: International Jury**:
- **Blind Cupping**: International judges (Q Graders, experts)
- **Rigorous**: Multiple rounds, elimination
- **Top 30-40**: Advance to final round

**Stage 3: Final Round**:
- **Presidential**: Highest-level judging
- **87+ Points**: Required for CoE winner status
- **Rankings**: 1st through 30th place (typically)

**Stage 4: Auction**:
- **Online Auction**: Winning lots sold to highest bidders globally
- **Premium Prices**: Often $10-100+/lb green
- **Direct Payment**: Auction proceeds ‚Üí farmers (minus CoE fee)

Cup of Excellence competition identifies exceptional coffees (87+ points) through international blind judging, then auctions winning lots at premium prices ($10-100+/lb) with proceeds going directly to farmers - providing quality recognition, farmer premiums, and origin promotion while setting industry quality benchmarks.
