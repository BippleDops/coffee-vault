## üìã Overview

Coffee's journey from Ethiopian forests to global commodity spans 1,000+ years - from legendary discovery by Kaldi's goats to Ottoman coffeehouses, colonial plantations, and modern specialty movement. Understanding coffee history explains current industry, variety distribution, and cultural significance.

**Historical Timeline**:
- **800s-1200s**: Discovery in Ethiopia (Kaldi legend ~850 AD)
- **1400s**: Cultivation in Yemen, Mocha port trade
- **1500s**: Ottoman Empire adoption, Istanbul coffeehouses
- **1600s**: European introduction, colonial expansion begins
- **1700s**: Caribbean, Central/South American plantations established
- **1800s**: Coffee becomes global commodity
- **1900s**: Instant coffee, second wave, specialty emergence
- **2000s**: Third wave, fourth wave movements

---

## üåç Ethiopian Discovery (Pre-1000 AD)

**Kaldi Legend** (~850 AD):
- **Story**: Goat herder Kaldi notices goats energized after eating red berries
- **Location**: Kaffa region, Ethiopia
- **Discovery**: Coffee's stimulating effects recognized
- **Monks**: Local monks use coffee to stay awake during prayers
- **Historicity**: Likely mythological, but Ethiopia's coffee origin is fact

**Wild Coffee Forests**:
- Ethiopia has wild Coffea arabica forests (only place naturally)
- Indigenous consumption: Chewed, made into balls with fat, brewed
- **"Buna" Ceremony**: Ancient Ethiopian coffee ritual continues today

---

## üïå Yemeni Cultivation (1400s-1500s)

**First Cultivation**:
- **1400s**: Coffee cultivated in Yemen (from Ethiopian beans)
- **Sufi Monasteries**: Used for staying awake during night prayers
- **Mocha Port**: Yemen's Mocha port becomes coffee trade center
- **Monopoly**: Yemen controlled coffee trade (1400s-1600s)
- **"Mocha"**: Name from port (Mocha coffee, mocha-java blend)

**Ottoman Spread**:
- **1500s**: Coffee reaches Ottoman Empire (Turkey)
- **Istanbul**: First coffeehouses (1550s) - social revolution
- **Qahveh Khaneh**: Persian/Turkish coffeehouses become intellectual centers

---

## ‚òï European Introduction (1600s)

**Venice** (1600s):
- Coffee reaches Europe through Venetian traders
- Initially controversial (some called it "Satan's drink")
- **Pope Clement VIII**: Tasted, approved, "baptized" coffee (legend)

**Coffeehouses Spread**:
- **London** (1650s): English coffeehouses, centers of enlightenment
- **Paris** (1670s): Caf√© culture, intellectual salons
- **Vienna** (1683): After Ottoman siege, captured coffee beans

---

## üå± Colonial Expansion (1700s)

**Dutch Colonial**:
- **1696**: Coffee to Java (Indonesia) - first outside Arabia/Ethiopia
- **Dutch East India Company**: Established plantations

**French Colonial**:
- **1720s**: Coffee to Caribbean (Martinique, Haiti)
- **Gabriel de Clieu**: Legendary journey bringing coffee seedling

**Americas**:
- **Brazil** (1727): Portuguese bring coffee, becomes dominant
- **Caribbean**: Jamaica, Haiti, Cuba establish production
- **Central America** (1700s-1800s): Guatemala, Costa Rica, others

---

## üìà 19th Century Expansion

**Global Commodity**:
- Coffee becomes second-most traded commodity (after oil)
- **Brazil Dominance**: Becomes #1 producer (maintains today)
- **Leaf Rust** (1876): Ceylon (Sri Lanka) Arabica destroyed, switches to tea
- **East Africa**: Coffee introduced to Kenya, Tanzania (late 1800s-early 1900s)

---

## ‚òï 20th Century Evolution

**Instant Coffee** (1901):
- Satori Kato invents instant coffee (Japanese chemist)
- WWII: Instant coffee for troops, mass adoption

**Second Wave** (1960s-1980s):
- **Peet's** (1966): Alfred Peet, dark roasts, quality focus
- **Starbucks** (1971): Pike Place, eventually global
- **Specialty Emergence**: Quality coffee vs. commodity

**Third Wave** (1990s-2000s):
- **Intelligentsia, Counter Culture, Stumptown**: Direct Trade pioneers
- **Light Roasts**: Origin-forward, terroir focus
- **Transparency**: Supply chain openness
- **Competition**: World Barista Championship, Cup of Excellence

**Fourth Wave** (2010s-Present):
- **Science**: Precision, measurement, optimization
- **Technology**: Advanced equipment, data-driven
- **Sustainability**: Environmental and social focus intensifies

---

## üìù Summary

Coffee evolved from Ethiopian forests (~850 AD) through Yemeni cultivation (1400s), Ottoman coffeehouse culture (1500s), European adoption (1600s), colonial plantation expansion (1700s-1800s), to global commodity and specialty movements (1900s-present), creating today's complex industry balancing commodity production, specialty quality, and sustainability imperatives.
