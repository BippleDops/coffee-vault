## üìã Overview

Fair Trade certification ensures minimum prices, democratic cooperatives, and social premiums for coffee farmers - providing economic safety net and community development funding while guaranteeing ethical sourcing for consumers.

**What is Fair Trade**:
- **Minimum Price**: Floor price regardless of C-Market ($1.40/lb Arabica + premiums)
- **Social Premium**: $0.20/lb for community projects
- **Cooperative**: Democratic farmer organizations required
- **Standards**: Labor, environmental, economic standards
- **Certification**: Third-party verification (FLO - Fairtrade Labelling Organizations)

**Why Fair Trade Matters**:
- **Price Protection**: Farmers guaranteed minimum (safety net when C-Market crashes)
- **Community Development**: Social premium funds schools, health, infrastructure
- **Empowerment**: Democratic cooperatives, farmer voice
- **Market Access**: Fair Trade buyers, stable relationships
- **Consumer**: Ethical sourcing verification

---

## üí∞ Fair Trade Pricing

**Minimum Prices**:
- **Arabica Washed**: $1.40/lb minimum
- **Arabica Natural**: $1.35/lb minimum
- **Organic Premium**: +$0.30/lb
- **Social Premium**: +$0.20/lb (community projects)

**When C-Market Above Minimum**:
- Farmers receive C-Market + $0.20/lb social premium
- **Price follows market** when above floor

**When C-Market Below Minimum**:
- Farmers receive $1.40/lb + $0.20 premium
- **Protection** from market crashes

---

## ü§ù Cooperative Requirements

**Democratic Structure**:
- **One Member, One Vote**: Democratic governance
- **Transparency**: Financial transparency required
- **Participation**: Member participation in decisions
- **Benefits**: Profits distributed to members

**Social Premium Use**:
- **Community Decision**: Members vote on premium use
- **Education**: Schools, scholarships
- **Health**: Clinics, health programs
- **Infrastructure**: Roads, processing equipment, water systems
