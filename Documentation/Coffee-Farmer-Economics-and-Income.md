## ğŸ“‹ Overview

Coffee farmer economics reveal harsh reality - with commodity prices often below production cost ($1-2/lb) leaving smallholder farmers in poverty, while specialty ($4-20/lb) and Direct Trade provide sustainable livelihoods, explaining why quality focus and fair pricing are ethical imperatives.

**Farmer Reality**:
- **25+ million farmers** globally depend on coffee
- **80% smallholders**: <5 hectares (12 acres)
- **Poverty**: Commodity prices often = poverty ($2-4/day income)
- **Specialty Hope**: Quality premiums enable sustainable income
- **Fair Trade/Direct Trade**: Alternative models improving farmer share

---

## ğŸ’° Income by Market

**Commodity** (C-Market, $1-2/lb):
- **Farmer Income**: $1,000-3,000/year (below poverty)
- **% of Retail**: 10-20% (middlemen capture majority)
- **Sustainability**: Not economically viable long-term
- **Reality**: Many farmers leave coffee (migration, poverty)

**Specialty** (Quality Premium, $4-10/lb):
- **Farmer Income**: $4,000-12,000/year (sustainable)
- **% of Retail**: 30-40% of retail price
- **Quality Investment**: Enables processing improvements
- **Sustainable**: Economically viable, families stay in coffee

**Direct Trade/Fair Trade** ($6-20/lb):
- **Farmer Income**: $8,000-25,000/year (good living)
- **% of Retail**: 50-70% of retail price
- **Best**: Maximum farmer benefit
- **Transforms**: From poverty to sustainable livelihood

## ğŸ“Š Production Costs

**Typical Costs** (Smallholder):
- **Labor**: $0.50-1.00/lb (harvest, processing)
- **Inputs**: $0.20-0.50/lb (fertilizer, pest control if conventional)
- **Processing**: $0.10-0.30/lb (cooperative fees)
- **Transport**: $0.05-0.15/lb
- **Total**: $0.85-1.95/lb production cost

**When C-Market = $1.20/lb**:
- **Farmer Loss**: Coffee sold below cost
- **Crisis**: Farmer poverty, abandonment

**When Specialty = $6/lb**:
- **Farmer Profit**: $4-5/lb margin
- **Sustainable**: Investment, livelihood, family support

## ğŸ“ Summary

Coffee farmer economics vary dramatically by market position - commodity ($1-2/lb) often below production cost creating poverty, while specialty ($4-10/lb) and Direct Trade ($6-20/lb) provide sustainable incomes enabling farmers to invest, improve quality, and support families. Specialty/Fair Trade/Direct Trade are ethical necessities, not luxuries.
