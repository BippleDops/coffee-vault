## üìã Overview

The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) is global specialty coffee's governing body - setting standards for quality (cupping protocols, grading), providing education (certifications, training), and fostering community through events (World of Coffee, competitions) that unite industry professionals worldwide.

**What is SCA**:
- **Global Organization**: Represents specialty coffee industry worldwide
- **Standards**: Cupping protocols, water standards, grading systems
- **Education**: SCA Coffee Skills Program (barista, brewing, roasting, etc.)
- **Events**: World of Coffee, competitions, symposiums
- **Research**: Coffee science research support
- **Community**: 10,000+ members globally
- **Formed**: 2017 merger of SCAA (American) + SCAE (European)

**Why SCA Matters**:
- **Standards**: Industry follows SCA protocols (cupping, grading, water)
- **Education**: SCA certifications are industry standard
- **Quality**: Defines specialty (80+ cupping score)
- **Competitions**: Hosts World Championships (barista, brewing, roasting)
- **Unity**: Global industry connection, best practices sharing

## üìä SCA Standards

**Cupping Protocol**:
- **8.25g coffee : 150ml water** (1:18.18 ratio)
- **Sample roast**: Agtron 58-63
- **Temperature**: 200¬∞F pour
- **Timing**: 4-minute steep before evaluation
- **Global Standard**: Everyone uses same protocol

**Water Standards**:
- **TDS**: 75-250 ppm
- **Total Hardness**: 50-175 ppm
- **Alkalinity**: 40-75 ppm
- **pH**: 6.5-7.5

**Grading**:
- **Specialty**: 80+ cupping score, <5 defects per 300g
- **Premium**: 85+ points
- **Outstanding**: 90+ points (rare)
