Coffee economics spans commodity futures trading to ultra-premium specialty auctions - with C-Market commodity prices ($1-2/lb) at bottom and Panama Geisha auctions ($1,000/lb) at top, creating complex value chain where farmers receive 10-50% of retail price depending on market position.

- **Specialty**: $4-12/lb (quality premium)
- **Ultra-Premium**: $12-100+/lb (Geisha, competition, rare)

## ğŸ“Š C-Market (Commodity)

**What is C-Market**:
- **Futures Market**: ICE (Intercontinental Exchange) coffee futures
- **Commodity Pricing**: Base price for commercial Arabica
- **Volatile**: Fluctuates daily based on supply/demand
- **Floor**: Often below production cost (farmer crisis)

**Problems**:
- Farmers receive commodity price regardless of quality
- Volatile, unpredictable
- Often below cost of production
- No quality differentiation

## ğŸ’ Specialty Premiums

**Quality Premiums**:
- **Good**: +$1-2/lb above C-Market
- **Excellent**: +$3-6/lb
- **Exceptional**: +$6-15/lb
- **Competition**: +$10-100+/lb

**Why Premiums**:
- Quality (cupping scores)
- Processing (honey, natural, experimental)
- Variety (Geisha, Pacamara, heirloom)
- Origin (reputation, terroir)
- Certifications (organic, fair trade)
- Rarity (limited production)
