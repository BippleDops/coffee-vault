Systematic tasting journaling develops palate and builds coffee knowledge - recording aroma, flavor, body, acidity, and impressions creates reference library enabling pattern recognition, preference understanding, and sensory improvement over time.

**Why Journal Tasting**:
- **Palate Development**: Practice improves sensory acuity
- **Pattern Recognition**: Learn varietals, processing, origins

## ğŸ“ What to Record

**Essential**:
- **Coffee**: Bean name, roaster, origin
- **Roast Date**: Freshness tracking
- **Brew Method**: V60, espresso, French Press, etc.
- **Recipe**: Dose, water, ratio, time, temp
- **Date**: When you brewed/tasted

**Sensory**:
- **Aroma**: Dry grounds, wet grounds, brewed coffee
- **Flavor**: Dominant notes, secondary notes
- **Acidity**: Level (low/medium/high), type (citric, malic, etc.)
- **Body**: Mouthfeel (light/medium/full)
- **Sweetness**: Perceived sweetness
- **Finish**: Aftertaste length and quality
- **Balance**: How components integrate
- **Overall**: Holistic impression, rating (1-5 or 1-10)

**Notes**:
- **Impressions**: Free-form observations
- **Comparisons**: Similar to other coffees
- **Evolution**: How coffee changed as it cooled
- **Next Time**: Adjustments to try

## ğŸ“Š Rating System

**Simple** (1-5):
- 5: Exceptional, rebuy immediately
- 4: Excellent, would rebuy
- 3: Good, acceptable
- 2: Below expectations
- 1: Poor, avoid

**SCA-Style** (0-10 per attribute):
- More detailed, professional
- Aroma, Flavor, Acidity, Body, Balance, Overall (each 0-10)
- Total out of 60+ points
