## ğŸ“‹ Overview

Third wave coffee (1990s-2000s) transformed specialty through Direct Trade, light roasts, origin transparency, and coffee-as-craft philosophy - elevating coffee from commodity to artisanal product while improving farmer livelihoods and consumer appreciation.

**Three Waves**:
1. **First Wave** (1800s-1970s): Coffee as commodity, instant coffee, mass market (Folgers)
2. **Second Wave** (1970s-1990s): Dark roasts, espresso drinks, Starbucks, Peet's
3. **Third Wave** (1990s-present): Light roasts, Direct Trade, origin focus, craft coffee

---

## ğŸŒŸ Third Wave Characteristics

**Philosophy**:
- **Coffee as Craft**: Artisanal approach, precision
- **Origin Matters**: Terroir, single-origin focus, transparency
- **Light Roasts**: Preserve origin character (vs. dark roast homogenization)
- **Direct Trade**: Farmer relationships, fair pricing
- **Quality**: Uncompromising standards, competition focus
- **Education**: Consumer education, transparency

**Pioneers** (1994-2005):
- **Intelligentsia** (1995, Chicago): Direct Trade, transparency
- **Counter Culture** (1995, Durham): Transparency, sustainability
- **Stumptown** (1999, Portland): Cool factor, quality, culture
- **Blue Bottle** (2002, Oakland): Japanese precision, freshness obsession
- **Heart, Ritual, others** (2000s): Portland, SF third-wave emergence
