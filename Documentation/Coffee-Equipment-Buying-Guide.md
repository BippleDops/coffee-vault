## üìã Overview

Quality coffee equipment enables great coffee at home - with grinder being most important investment ($100-500 for quality burr grinder), followed by brewing method ($20-200), scale ($15-50), and kettle ($30-150) creating complete setup for excellent coffee.

**Investment Priority**:
1. **Grinder** (MOST IMPORTANT): $100-500 - Quality burr grinder essential
2. **Scale**: $15-50 - Precision necessary
3. **Kettle**: $30-150 - Temperature control ideal
4. **Brewer**: $20-200 - Method-dependent
5. **Accessories**: $20-100 - Nice-to-have

**Total**: $185-1,000 for complete quality setup

---

## ‚öôÔ∏è Grinder Recommendations (PRIORITY #1)

**Entry** ($100-150):
- **Baratza Encore**: $140, conical burr, reliable, upgrade path
- **Timemore C2**: $70-90, hand grinder, excellent for price
- **Best**: Encore for electric, Timemore for manual/budget

**Mid-Range** ($200-350):
- **Baratza Virtuoso+**: $250, better burrs than Encore
- **Fellow Ode**: $300, flat burrs, pour-over specialist
- **Comandante**: $200-250, hand grinder gold standard
- **Best**: Fellow Ode for filter, Comandante for quality hand grinding

**Espresso** ($300-600):
- **Baratza Sette 270**: $380, espresso specialist, stepless
- **Eureka Mignon**: $400-500, excellent espresso, quiet
- **Niche Zero**: $600, single-dose perfection
- **Best**: Sette for value, Niche for ultimate

**Why Grinder Matters Most**: Bad grinder = impossible to make great coffee (distribution, consistency critical)

## ‚òï Brewer Recommendations

**Pour-Over** ($8-50):
- **V60**: $8-25, versatile, technique-rewarding
- **Kalita Wave**: $35-60, forgiving, consistent
- **Chemex**: $40-50, beautiful, clean
- **Clever Dripper**: $25, easiest quality method
- **Best**: Clever for beginners, V60 for enthusiasts

**Immersion** ($20-40):
- **French Press**: $20-50, full-bodied, simple
- **AeroPress**: $30-40, versatile, portable
- **Best**: AeroPress versatility, French Press simplicity

**Espresso** ($300-3,000+):
- **Entry**: Gaggia Classic Pro ($450), mod platform
- **Mid**: Rancilio Silvia ($750), solid performer
- **Prosumer**: Lelit Bianca ($1,500), flow control
- **Best**: Depends on budget, commitment level

## üìè Scale Recommendations ($15-200)

**Budget** ($15-30):
- **Amazon Basics**: $15-20, adequate 0.1g precision
- **Hario Drip Scale**: $50, built-in timer
- **Best**: Any 0.1g precision with timer under $30

**Premium** ($100-200):
- **Acaia Pearl**: $130-140, beautiful, precise, app connectivity
- **Acaia Lunar**: $220, espresso-specific, mini size
- **Best**: Acaia Pearl for enthusiasts, budget for most
