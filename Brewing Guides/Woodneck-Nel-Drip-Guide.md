## üìã Overview

Woodneck Nel Drip is traditional Japanese cloth-filter pour-over brewing using flannel filter that produces exceptionally smooth, full-bodied, clean coffee with characteristics between paper filter and French Press - removing sediment while retaining beneficial oils for rich, creamy mouthfeel.

**What Makes Nel Drip Special**:
- **Cloth Filter**: Flannel (Nel = flannel in Japanese) filters fines but allows oils
- **Full Body**: Richer than paper filter, cleaner than French Press
- **Smooth**: Exceptionally smooth, gentle, round
- **Traditional**: Japanese kissaten (coffee house) classic method
- **Romantic**: Nostalgic, traditional, aesthetic appeal
- **Reusable**: Flannel filter lasts months with care
- **Environmental**: No disposable filters
- **Unique Flavor**: Distinct from paper pour-over

**Challenges**:
- **Maintenance**: Cloth filter requires care, storage in water
- **Cleaning**: Must rinse after each use, boil periodically
- **Storage**: Keep in water in refrigerator (prevents drying/cracking)
- **Lifespan**: 3-6 months typical, then replace
- **Slow**: Slower flow than paper filters
- **Learning Curve**: Technique matters more than standard methods

**History**:
- **Traditional Japan**: Used in kissaten since early-mid 1900s
- **Hario "Woodneck"**: Famous brand, wooden neck/handle
- **Revival**: Specialty coffee rediscovering traditional methods
- **Present**: Niche but passionate following

## üìù Standard Recipe

**Parameters**:
- **Coffee**: 20-25g
- **Water**: 300-350g (1:14 to 1:15 ratio)
- **Grind**: Medium (slightly finer than French Press, coarser than paper pour-over)
- **Temp**: 195-205¬∞F (91-96¬∞C)
- **Total Time**: 3:30-4:30

**Process**:
1. **Prepare Filter**: Rinse flannel with hot water
2. **Add Coffee**: 22g grounds to filter
3. **Bloom**: 40-50g water, 30-45 seconds
4. **Pour**: Gentle spiral pours to 350g
5. **Draw-Down**: 3:30-4:30 total (slower than paper)
6. **Serve**: Immediately

**After Brewing** (Critical):
1. **Rinse Filter**: Thoroughly with hot water
2. **Store**: Submerge in water, refrigerate
3. **Weekly**: Boil filter 10 minutes (remove coffee oils)

## ‚òï Flavor Impact

**Cloth Filter Characteristics**:
- **Oils Retained**: Coffee oils pass through (vs. paper filters)
- **Body**: Full, rich, creamy, smooth
- **Sediment-Free**: Removes fines and sediment (vs. French Press)
- **Smooth**: Exceptionally smooth, round, mellow
- **Less Bright**: Acidity muted compared to paper filters
- **Fuller**: Heavier mouthfeel, coating
- **Unique**: Distinct from paper or metal filters
