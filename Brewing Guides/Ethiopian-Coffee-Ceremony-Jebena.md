## üìã Overview

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is coffee's most ancient and culturally rich preparation ritual - roasting green beans over charcoal, hand-grinding with mortar and pestle, brewing in clay jebena pot, and serving three ceremonial rounds while burning incense. It's coffee as spiritual practice, social bonding, and cultural heritage spanning centuries.

**Why Ethiopian Ceremony Matters**:
- **Oldest Tradition**: Coffee's birthplace, most ancient ritual
- **Cultural Significance**: Central to Ethiopian social life, hospitality
- **Three Rounds**: Abol (first/strongest), Tona (second), Baraka (third/blessing)
- **Social**: Community gathering, conversation, bonding
- **Spiritual**: Incense (frankincense), blessing rituals
- **UNESCO**: Recognized cultural heritage
- **"Buna Dabo Naw"**: Ethiopian saying ("Coffee is our bread")

**Full Ceremony Duration**: 30-60 minutes (can't be rushed)

## üìù Traditional Ceremony Process

**Phase 1: Roasting** (5-10 minutes):
1. **Green Beans**: Start with green Ethiopian coffee
2. **Roasting Pan**: Over charcoal brazier
3. **Roast by Hand**: Shake pan, roast evenly to medium-dark
4. **Aroma Sharing**: Pass roasted beans for guests to smell
5. **Community**: First sensory sharing moment

**Phase 2: Grinding** (5 minutes):
1. **Mortar and Pestle**: Hand-pound roasted beans
2. **Rhythm**: Rhythmic pounding (meditative)
3. **Fine Grind**: Grind to medium-fine powder

**Phase 3: Brewing** (5-10 minutes):
1. **Jebena**: Add ground coffee to clay pot
2. **Water**: Cold water added
3. **Boil**: Place on charcoal, bring to boil
4. **Foam**: Watch for foam rise
5. **Remove**: Just before boiling over

**Phase 4: Serving - Three Rounds**:

**Abol** (·ä†·â¶·àç - First Round):
- **Strongest**: First brewing, most concentrated
- **Honored**: Guests served first
- **Blessing**: Often with blessing/prayer

**Tona** (·â∂·äì - Second Round):
- **Second Brewing**: Same grounds, new water
- **Milder**: Less concentrated than first
- **Conversation**: Social bonding continues

**Baraka** (·â†·à®·ä´ - Third Round/Blessing):
- **Third Brewing**: Final round, weakest
- **Blessing**: "Baraka" means blessing
- **Completion**: Ceremony concludes

**Throughout**:
- **Incense**: Frankincense burning
- **Popcorn**: Sometimes popcorn served
- **Social**: Conversation, community, bonding
