## üìã Overview

Cowboy coffee is camping's classic brew method - coarse grounds boiled in pot over campfire, settled with cold water, producing strong, rustic coffee with sediment that's perfect for outdoor adventures. Simple, minimal equipment, traditional American frontier coffee.

**Characteristics**:
- **Minimal Equipment**: Just pot, fire, coffee, water
- **Camping Perfect**: Ideal for outdoor brewing
- **Boiling**: Water boiled with coffee (unique)
- **Sediment**: Grounds settle to bottom, pour carefully
- **Strong**: Concentrated, robust, bold
- **Traditional**: American frontier, cowboy culture
- **Simple**: No filters, no precision, no fuss

---

## üìù Traditional Method

**Process**:
1. **Boil Water**: Heat water in pot over fire
2. **Add Coffee**: Remove from heat, add coarse grounds (2 tbsp per cup)
3. **Steep**: Return to edge of fire, 3-5 minutes (don't boil aggressively)
4. **Settle**: Remove from heat, add splash cold water (grounds sink)
5. **Pour**: Carefully pour, leaving grounds at bottom
6. **Enjoy**: Strong campfire coffee

---

## üí° Tips

**For Better Cowboy Coffee**:
- Coarse grind (prevents over-extraction, easier to settle)
- Don't boil aggressively (bitter)
- Cold water settling trick (grounds sink faster)
- Pour carefully (leave sediment)
