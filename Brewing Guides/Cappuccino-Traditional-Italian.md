## ğŸ“‹ Overview

Cappuccino is Italy's iconic espresso-milk drink - equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and foam creating layered texture and balanced flavor that's consumed before 11am in Italian tradition. Named for Capuchin monks' brown robes, cappuccino represents Italian coffee culture's morning ritual.

**Traditional Cappuccino**:
- **Ratio**: 1:2:2 (espresso:milk:foam)
- **Size**: 5-6 oz total (smaller than American versions)
- **Foam**: Distinct foam layer (~1 inch thick)
- **Morning Only**: Italians drink cappuccino before 11am only (milk = breakfast)
- **Layers**: Espresso base, steamed milk, foam top
- **Traditional**: No flavors, no extra shots (Italian purity)

## ğŸ“ Traditional Recipe

**Italian Cappuccino**:
- **Espresso**: 1-2 oz single/double shot
- **Steamed Milk**: 2 oz
- **Foam**: 2 oz (thick, distinct layer)
- **Total**: 5-6 oz
- **Cup**: Ceramic cappuccino cup (6 oz)

**Process**:
1. Pull espresso shot
2. Steam milk (create foam, not just microfoam)
3. Pour steamed milk into espresso
4. Spoon foam on top
5. Optional: Cocoa powder dusting
