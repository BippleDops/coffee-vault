## üìã Overview

Kyoto-style cold drip (also called Dutch coffee or slow drip) is coffee's most elegant and theatrical cold brewing method - using gravity and time to drip ice-cold water through coffee over 3-12 hours, creating exceptionally smooth, sweet, complex cold coffee that showcases origin character impossible in traditional cold brew immersion.

**What Makes Kyoto Cold Drip Special**:
- **Visual Theater**: Gorgeous glass towers, mesmerizing drip process, caf√© centerpiece
- **Slow Extraction**: 3-12 hours, one drop at a time (~1 drip per second)
- **Percolation**: Water passes through coffee (like pour-over) not immersion
- **Complexity**: Preserves origin character better than immersion cold brew
- **Smooth**: Exceptionally smooth, zero acidity, zero bitterness
- **Sweet**: Natural sweetness amplified, no need for additions
- **Clean**: Filtered extraction, clarity unlike immersion cold brew
- **Expensive**: Equipment $150-800+ for quality towers

**Kyoto vs. Immersion Cold Brew**:
- **Kyoto**: Percolation, slow drip, complex, clean, expensive equipment
- **Immersion**: Full immersion, 12-24 hours steep, chocolate-forward, cheap (mason jar works)
- **Choose Kyoto if**: Want complexity, visual appeal, caf√© presentation, willing to invest
- **Choose Immersion if**: Want simplicity, chocolate-smooth, budget-conscious

## üìù Standard Recipe

**Parameters**:
- **Coffee**: 60-80g (coarse grind)
- **Ice Water**: 600-800ml (ice + water in top chamber)
- **Ratio**: 1:10 to 1:12 (concentrated)
- **Grind**: Coarse (French Press grind)
- **Drip Rate**: ~40-60 drops per minute (1 drop per 1-1.5 seconds)
- **Total Time**: 3-12 hours (adjust drip rate)
- **Dilution**: Serve straight or dilute 1:1

**Setup**:
1. **Coffee in Middle Chamber**: 70g coarse grounds
2. **Filter Paper** (if included): On top of coffee
3. **Ice + Water in Top**: Fill top chamber with ice and cold water
4. **Adjust Drip Rate**: Valve controls drip speed
5. **Start**: Open valve, one drop every 1-1.5 seconds
6. **Wait**: 6-8 hours typical
7. **Store**: Refrigerate concentrated result, dilute when serving

## ‚è±Ô∏è Drip Rate Experiments

**Fast** (1 drop per second, 3-4 hours):
- **Result**: Still smooth but less complex
- **Use**: When time-limited

**Standard** (1 drop per 1.5 seconds, 6-8 hours):
- **Result**: Balanced, complex, smooth
- **Recommended**: Standard Kyoto drip

**Slow** (1 drop per 2-3 seconds, 10-12+ hours):
- **Result**: Maximum complexity, exceptionally smooth
- **Use**: When showcasing expensive coffee

**Overnight**: Popular - start evening, ready morning
