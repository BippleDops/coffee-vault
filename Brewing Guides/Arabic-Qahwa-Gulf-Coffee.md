## üìã Overview

Arabic Qahwa is Gulf States' traditional coffee - lightly roasted beans boiled with cardamom (and sometimes saffron, cloves) in dallah pot, served in small handle-less cups as hospitality ritual central to Arab culture. Very light roast, highly spiced, and culturally significant as UNESCO Intangible Heritage.

**Characteristics**:
- **Very Light Roast**: Cinnamon roast, lightest coffee roasting
- **Cardamom**: Essential spice, dominant flavor
- **Saffron**: Optional luxury addition
- **Dallah**: Traditional brass/copper pot (distinctive spout)
- **Small Servings**: Tiny cups, multiple refills
- **Hospitality**: Offering coffee is sacred duty
- **UNESCO**: Recognized cultural heritage

**Cultural Significance**:
- **Hospitality**: Refusing coffee is insult
- **Status**: Quality of coffee/spices shows host's status
- **Gathering**: Social bonding, business, family
- **Tradition**: Centuries-old practice, unchanged

---

## üìù Traditional Preparation

**Ingredients**:
- **Coffee**: Very light roast, coarse ground
- **Cardamom**: Essential, ground fresh (8-10 pods per pot)
- **Saffron**: Optional, luxury ($$$)
- **Cloves, Rosewater**: Regional variations

**Process**:
1. **Boil Water**: In dallah pot
2. **Add Coffee**: Light roast, coarse
3. **Add Cardamom**: Ground cardamom (generous)
4. **Boil**: Gentle simmer 5-8 minutes
5. **Optional Saffron**: Add if using
6. **Settle**: Let grounds settle
7. **Serve**: Small cups, pour from height (tradition)
8. **Multiple Servings**: Refill cups 2-3 times minimum
