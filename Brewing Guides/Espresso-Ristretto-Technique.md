Ristretto is espresso's concentrated extraction - pulling shorter ratios (1:1.5-1:2) for maximum sweetness, body, and intensity while minimizing bitterness. Traditional in Southern Italy, ristretto delivers espresso's sweetest expression through restricted water flow.

**What is Ristretto**:
- **Italian**: "Ristretto" = restricted/limited
- **Shorter Ratio**: 1:1.5 to 1:2 (less water)
- **Sweeter**: Extracts sweetness, minimizes bitters
- **Concentrated**: More intense, syrupy, thick
- **Southern Italy**: Traditional in Naples, Southern cafÃ©s
- **Sweet Spot**: Maximum sweetness, minimum bitterness

**Why Ristretto is Sweet**:
- **Extraction Order**: Sweetness extracts first, bitters last
- **Short Extraction**: Stops before bitter compounds extract
- **Concentrated**: Sweet compounds concentrated in small volume
