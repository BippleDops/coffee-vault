# ‚ö° Turbo Shot Espresso Guide

**High-flow, short-contact-time espresso for bright, sweet, complex shots**

---

## What is a Turbo Shot?

Turbo shots are a contemporary espresso technique that uses:
- **Coarser grind** than traditional espresso
- **Higher flow rate** (faster extraction)
- **Shorter contact time** (15-20 seconds total)
- **Similar yield** to traditional shots (1:2-1:2.5 ratio)

**Result**: Bright, sweet, clean espresso with reduced bitterness and enhanced clarity.

**Developed by**: Scott Rao and refined by modern specialty cafes

## Why Turbo Shots Work

### Traditional Espresso Challenges
- Fine grind ‚Üí channeling risk
- Long contact ‚Üí over-extraction at edges
- High pressure ‚Üí uneven flow

### Turbo Shot Advantages
1. **Coarser grind** ‚Üí more even extraction
2. **Faster flow** ‚Üí less channel formation
3. **Shorter time** ‚Üí sweeter, less bitter
4. **Higher clarity** ‚Üí more origin character

**Scientific Basis**: Reduces extraction time variance across the puck, leading to more uniform extraction.

## Step-by-Step Recipe

### Equipment Needed
- Espresso machine (temp control recommended)
- Grinder with fine adjustments
- Scale (0.1g precision)
- Timer
- Distribution tool (optional but recommended)

### Basic Turbo Shot Recipe

**Target**: 18g in ‚Üí 40g out in 17-19 seconds

#### Step 1: Grind Adjustment
1. Start with your normal espresso grind
2. **Coarsen** by 3-4 clicks/notches
3. Aim for consistency between table salt and fine sand

#### Step 2: Dose & Distribution
1. Dose: **18g** (¬±0.5g)
2. Distribute evenly (WDT tool recommended)
3. Level carefully

#### Step 3: Tamp
1. Firm, even tamp
2. Polish and ensure level
3. Wipe portafilter rim

#### Step 4: Extraction
1. Lock portafilter immediately
2. Start extraction
3. **Target flow**:
   - First drops at 3-5 seconds
   - Steady stream by 7 seconds
   - Full yield at 17-19 seconds

#### Step 5: Evaluate
1. **Too fast** (< 15s): Grind finer by 1 click
2. **Too slow** (> 21s): Grind coarser by 1 click
3. **Taste**: Adjust based on flavor

## Flavor Profile Expectations

### Well-Extracted Turbo Shot
- ‚úÖ **Bright** acidity (not sour)
- ‚úÖ **Clean** finish
- ‚úÖ **Sweet** throughout
- ‚úÖ **Complex** fruit/floral notes
- ‚úÖ **Light** body

### Troubleshooting Flavors

**Too Sour/Sharp**:
- Grind 1-2 clicks finer
- Increase dose to 19g
- Raise temperature 1¬∞C

**Thin/Weak**:
- Decrease yield to 36g (1:2 ratio)
- Increase dose to 19-20g
- Ensure proper distribution

**Still Bitter**:
- Go even coarser (surprising but works!)
- Reduce temperature 1¬∞C
- Check puck preparation

## Advanced Techniques

### Flow Profiling
For machines with flow control:
1. **Preinfusion**: 2-3 seconds at 2 bar
2. **Ramp**: Quick rise to 6-8 bar
3. **Hold**: Maintain pressure through extraction
4. **Total**: 17-20 seconds

### Basket Selection
- **18g VST/IMS**: Ideal for turbo shots
- **20g ridgeless**: Also works well
- **Avoid**: Stock baskets (uneven flow)

### Puck Preparation Importance
Turbo shots are **more forgiving** but still benefit from:
- WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique)
- Level tamping
- Clean basket edges
- Dry puck surface

---

## Bean Selection

### Best Beans for Turbo Shots
1. **Light roasts** - Showcases acidity and origin
2. **Washed processed** - Clean, bright flavors
3. **African coffees** - Ethiopian, Kenyan
4. **High-altitude** - Complex, fruity

### Beans to Avoid (Initially)
- Very dark roasts (may taste sour)
- Robusta blends (can be harsh)
- Extremely fresh (< 7 days) or old (> 35 days)

## Milk Drinks with Turbo Shots

Turbo shots work great in milk drinks:

**Cappuccino/Flat White**:
- Bright, fruity notes complement milk
- Clean finish cuts through sweetness
- Lower bitterness = balanced drinks

**Latte**:
- Origin character shines through milk
- Sweeter profile = less syrup needed

**Recommendation**: 1:2.5 ratio (18g ‚Üí 45g) for milk drinks

## Common Mistakes

### ‚ùå Not Going Coarse Enough
- **Problem**: Still extracting like traditional
- **Solution**: Go 5-7 clicks coarser, trust the process

### ‚ùå Stopping Too Early
- **Problem**: Under-extracted, sour
- **Solution**: Aim for full 36-40g yield

### ‚ùå Wrong Expectations
- **Problem**: Comparing to traditional body
- **Solution**: Appreciate clarity and complexity

### ‚ùå Poor Puck Prep
- **Problem**: Channeling still occurs
- **Solution**: Invest in distribution tool (WDT)
