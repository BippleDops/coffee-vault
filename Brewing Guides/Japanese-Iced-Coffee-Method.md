## üìã Overview

Japanese Iced Coffee (also called flash-chilled or hot-bloom iced coffee) revolutionizes iced coffee by brewing hot directly onto ice, rapidly cooling coffee to lock in aromatics and preserve bright, complex flavors that cold brew cannot achieve. This method delivers caf√©-quality iced coffee in 3 minutes instead of 12-24 hours, with superior clarity, brightness, and origin expression.

**What Makes Japanese Iced Coffee Revolutionary**:
- **Fast**: 3 minutes vs. 12-24 hours (cold brew)
- **Bright**: Preserves acidity and aromatics (cold brew mutes both)
- **Complex**: Hot extraction captures full flavor spectrum
- **Origin Character**: Showcases terroir, variety, roast level clearly
- **Aromatics**: Locks in volatile compounds via rapid cooling
- **Versatile**: Works with any coffee, any roast level
- **Fresh**: Brew on demand, no overnight planning
- **Clean**: Pour-over filter clarity (vs. immersion muddiness)

**Japanese Iced vs. Cold Brew**:
- **Japanese**: Hot brew, fast, bright, acidic, complex, origin-specific
- **Cold Brew**: Cold brew, slow, smooth, low-acid, chocolatey, mellow
- **Choose Japanese if**: Want brightness, complexity, acidity, aromatics
- **Choose Cold Brew if**: Want smoothness, low-acid, chocolate, easy-drinking

**Both Are Excellent** - different purposes, different profiles

## üìù Standard Recipe

**The Key**: Replace half the water with ice

**Parameters**:
- **Coffee**: 20g
- **Total Water Equivalent**: 300g
  - **Hot Water**: 150g (brewed)
  - **Ice**: 150g (in carafe)
- **Ratio**: 1:15 (effective ratio after ice melts)
- **Grind**: Fine-Medium (finer than hot pour-over)
- **Water Temp**: 200-205¬∞F (hot for extraction)

**Process**:
1. **Weigh Ice**: 150g ice into carafe/server
2. **Place Dripper**: On carafe with ice
3. **Rinse Filter**: Hot water rinse
4. **Add Coffee**: 20g ground coffee
5. **Brew**:
   - **Bloom**: 30-40g hot water, 30-45 seconds
   - **Main Pours**: Complete 150g hot water total by 2:00-2:30
   - **Draw-Down**: Finish by 3:00
6. **Result**: Hot coffee melts ice = perfectly chilled coffee

## üí° Why It Works

**Flash-Chilling Chemistry**:
- **Hot Extraction**: Captures full flavor spectrum (acids, aromatics, complexity)
- **Rapid Cooling**: Locks in volatile aromatic compounds before they escape
- **Prevents Oxidation**: Quick cooling prevents flavor degradation
- **Bright Acidity**: Acids extracted fully, preserved by cooling
- **Aromatics**: Volatile compounds trapped via sudden temperature drop

**Science**: Hot water extracts compounds cold water cannot, rapid cooling preserves them

## üéØ Adjustments

**For Different Coffees**:

**Light Roasts** (African, bright):
- Hot water: 160g, Ice: 140g (more hot extraction)
- Finer grind
- Result: Explosive fruit, bright, complex

**Medium Roasts**:
- Standard 150g hot, 150g ice
- Result: Balanced, sweet, refreshing

**Dark Roasts**:
- Hot: 140g, Ice: 160g (less extraction)
- Coarser grind
- Result: Smooth chocolate, low-acid iced coffee
