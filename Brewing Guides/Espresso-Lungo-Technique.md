## üìã Overview

Lungo is espresso's extended extraction - pulling longer shots (1:3-1:4 ratios) for larger volume and milder intensity while maintaining espresso character. Traditional in Northern Italy, lungo offers espresso experience with more approachability and volume than ristretto/normale.

**What is Lungo**:
- **Italian**: "Lungo" = long/extended
- **Longer Ratio**: 1:3 to 1:4 (vs. normale 1:2)
- **More Water**: Extended extraction, more volume
- **Milder**: Less concentrated, gentler than normale
- **Northern Italy**: Traditional in Milan, Northern Italian caf√©s
- **Americano Alternative**: More body than americano (no bypass water)

**Lungo vs. Normale vs. Ristretto**:
- **Ristretto**: 1:1.5 (18g ‚Üí 27g) - Concentrated, sweet, intense
- **Normale**: 1:2 (18g ‚Üí 36g) - Balanced, standard espresso
- **Lungo**: 1:3-1:4 (18g ‚Üí 54-72g) - Extended, milder, larger volume

## ‚òï Flavor Characteristics

**Lungo Profile**:
- **Milder**: Less intense than normale/ristretto
- **More Volume**: Larger shot, more dilute
- **Bitter Risk**: Extended extraction can extract bitters (adjust grind)
- **Body**: Medium-Full (less than normale)
- **Balance**: When done right, balanced and approachable

## üéØ Tips

**Avoid Over-Extraction**:
- Grind coarser than normale
- Good coffee essential (poor coffee shows defects in lungo)
- Stop before blonde/pale (over-extracted)
