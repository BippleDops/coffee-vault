## üìã Overview

Flat White is Australia/New Zealand's microfoam masterpiece - double ristretto with velvety microfoam creating silky texture where milk integrates seamlessly with espresso. Smaller than latte, milkier than cortado, defined by perfect microfoam execution.

**What is Flat White**:
- **Microfoam**: Tiny bubbles, velvety texture (not frothy cappuccino foam)
- **Double Ristretto**: 2 oz concentrated espresso base
- **Small**: 5-6 oz total (smaller than latte)
- **Integration**: Milk and espresso blend seamlessly
- **No Foam Layer**: "Flat" surface (microfoam throughout, not separate layer)
- **Aussie/Kiwi**: Cultural icon, national drink debate

---

## üìù Recipe

**Standard Flat White**:
- **Espresso**: Double ristretto (18g ‚Üí 30-32g, concentrated)
- **Milk**: 3-4 oz steamed with perfect microfoam
- **Total**: 5-6 oz drink
- **Ratio**: ~1:3 espresso to milk

**Process**:
1. Pull double ristretto (sweet, concentrated)
2. Steam milk to perfect microfoam (velvety, no large bubbles)
3. Pour milk into espresso (microfoam integrates)
4. Serve in ceramic cup (6 oz)

## üéØ Microfoam Technique

**Perfect Microfoam**:
- **Tiny Bubbles**: Microscopic, velvety, paint-like texture
- **Integration**: No separation, homogeneous throughout
- **Temperature**: 140-150¬∞F (60-65¬∞C) - not too hot
- **Glossy**: Shiny, reflective surface
- **Pourable**: Flows smoothly, integrates with espresso

**Steaming**:
- Position steam wand just below surface
- Create vortex (whirlpool motion)
- Stretch milk initially (incorporate air)
- Roll/polish (tiny bubbles, integration)
- No large bubbles (that's cappuccino foam)
