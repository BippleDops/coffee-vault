## üìã Overview

Ethiopia Wolisho is one of the identified Ethiopian landrace varietals - part of Ethiopia's vast genetic diversity producing distinctively floral-berry coffees with clean, complex profiles. While most Ethiopian coffee is sold as generic "heirloom," Wolisho-specific lots showcase one of Ethiopia's important indigenous varieties.

**What Makes Wolisho Notable**:
- **Ethiopian Landrace**: Indigenous variety, genetic diversity preservation
- **Floral-Berry Profile**: Distinctive flavor signature
- **Increasingly Identified**: More lots labeled as Wolisho (improving traceability)
- **Quality**: Excellent when grown at altitude
- **Research**: Part of Ethiopian variety identification research
- **Genetic Resource**: Important for global coffee biodiversity

**Ethiopian Variety Context**:
- Ethiopia has **thousands** of wild indigenous varieties
- Most sold as "Ethiopian Heirloom" (mixed)
- **Wolisho, Kurume, Dega**: Identified, characterized varieties
- **74110, 74112**: Jimma Research Center selections
- **Future**: More variety-specific lots as research progresses

**Why Variety Identification Matters**:
- **Traceability**: Better understanding of genetics
- **Selection**: Farmers can plant best varieties
- **Premiums**: Specific varieties command premiums
- **Preservation**: Genetic diversity protection
- **Quality**: Understanding variety-flavor relationships
