## üìã Overview

Peru Bourbon combines traditional Bourbon variety with Peruvian organic farming and Andean terroir - producing balanced, chocolate-caramel coffees with exceptional ethical credentials and value pricing. As Peru's quality variety alongside Typica, Bourbon delivers sweetness and complexity that elevates Peru beyond commodity perception.

**Why Peru Bourbon Matters**:
- **Organic**: Peru's organic certification leader extends to Bourbon
- **Fair Trade**: Strong cooperative structure supports Bourbon farmers
- **Traditional Variety**: Bourbon quality elevates Peruvian coffee
- **Andean Terroir**: High-altitude expression through quality genetics
- **Value**: Organic + Fair Trade + Bourbon quality at accessible prices
- **Smallholder**: Family farms, traditional cultivation
- **Sweet Profile**: Bourbon sweetness + Peruvian terroir = exceptional balance

**Peru's Bourbon Heritage**:
- **Introduction**: 1700s-1800s, brought by Spanish colonists
- **Traditional**: Became established in Peruvian highlands
- **Organic by Default**: Smallholders couldn't afford chemicals (Bourbon maintained)
- **Present**: ~15-25% of Peruvian coffee (Bourbon + Typica combined)
- **Quality Focus**: Maintained for specialty market positioning

## üå± Bourbon in Peru

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Traditional**: Standard Bourbon morphology
- **Low Yield**: Challenge for farmers (organic premiums compensate)
- **Heritage**: Maintained for quality, not productivity
- **Organic**: Grown without synthetic inputs (default practice)

**Why Still Grown Despite Low Yield**:
- **Organic Premiums**: 30-50% premium over conventional
- **Fair Trade**: Additional premiums support farmers
- **Specialty**: Cup quality enables specialty market access
- **Cooperative**: Washing station infrastructure supports quality processing
- **Value Chain**: Farmers capture higher value percentage through certifications
