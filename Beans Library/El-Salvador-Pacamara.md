## üìã Overview

El Salvador Pacamara is one of coffee's most distinctive and celebrated varieties - a deliberately bred hybrid combining Pacas (compact, productive) with Maragogipe (giant beans, unique flavor). The result is enormous beans producing intensely fruity, floral, complex coffees that win competitions globally and command ultra-premium prices.

**What Makes Pacamara Extraordinary**:
- **Giant Beans**: Largest coffee beans in commercial production (screen 18-22+)
- **Unique Genetics**: Deliberate hybrid, not natural selection
- **Extreme Complexity**: Tropical fruit, floral, multi-dimensional
- **El Salvador Origin**: Developed in El Salvador, performs best there
- **Competition Favorite**: Cup of Excellence regular winner (87-92+ points)
- **Ultra-Premium**: $15-50+/lb green, $25-60/12oz roasted
- **Visual Impact**: Beans 50-100% larger than standard coffee
- **Polarizing Flavors**: Intense, unusual, love-it-or-hate-it profiles
- **Low Yield**: Very low productivity limits production

**Historical Development**:
- **1958**: Developed at ISIC (Salvadoran Coffee Research Institute)
- **Parents**: 
  - **Pacas**: Salvadoran Bourbon mutation, compact
  - **Maragogipe**: Brazilian Typica mutation, elephant beans
- **Goal**: Combine Pacas productivity with Maragogipe uniqueness
- **Result**: Got unique flavors but NOT high productivity (low yield)
- **1960s-1980s**: Limited commercial adoption (low yield problematic)
- **1990s-2000s**: Specialty coffee discovers Pacamara
- **2000s-Present**: Competition darling, premium specialty variety
- **Global Spread**: Now grown in Nicaragua, Honduras, Guatemala (El Salvador best)

## üå± The Pacamara Variety

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Height**: Tall, 3-4 meters
- **Leaves**: Large, broad (from Maragogipe parent)
- **Cherries**: Very large, elongated
- **Beans**: **Huge** - 50-100% larger than standard Arabica
  - Screen 18-22+ (vs. normal 14-17)
  - Visually impressive, dramatic
  - Sometimes called "Elephant Beans"
- **Roots**: Strong, deep

**Agricultural Reality**:
- **Yield**: Very Low (30-50% of Caturra/Catuai)
- **Spacing**: Requires wide spacing (large plants)
- **Disease**: Susceptible to rust (like most traditional varieties)
- **Management**: Requires altitude and care
- **Economic**: Only viable with specialty premiums ($15-50+/lb green)
- **Harvest**: Large beans easier to pick but low density means fewer per tree

**Why Farmers Grow Despite Challenges**:
- **Premium**: 100-300% premium over standard coffee
- **Recognition**: "Pacamara" name carries cachet globally
- **Competition**: Wins awards, brings farm recognition
- **Specialty Demand**: Roasters actively seek Pacamara
- **Uniqueness**: Differentiation from commodity coffee
