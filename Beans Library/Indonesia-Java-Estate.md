## üìã Overview

Java coffee represents Indonesian coffee's colonial heritage - from large estates established by Dutch in 1600s-1700s producing the original "Java" that became synonymous with coffee itself ("Cup of Joe"), to modern specialty estates reviving washed processing and quality focus. Java combines Indonesian earthy character with estate coffee refinement, producing full-bodied, low-acid, chocolate-spice coffees with rustic charm.

**Historical Significance**:
- **First Outside Arabia/Ethiopia**: Coffee cultivation outside origin regions began in Java (1696)
- **Dutch East India Company**: Established coffee plantations in Java (1700s)
- **"Java" = Coffee**: The word "Java" became slang for coffee (English language)
- **"Cup of Joe"**: Possibly derived from "Cup of Java"
- **Mocha-Java Blend**: Original coffee blend (Yemen Mocha + Java), 1700s-1800s, still famous
- **Colonial Legacy**: Large estates from Dutch era still operate (Jampit, Blawan, Kayumas)
- **Leaf Rust Epidemic** (1876-1900): Destroyed Java's Arabica, replanted with Robusta
- **Arabica Return** (1920s-present): Estates slowly reintroduced Arabica
- **Today**: Mix of heritage estates and smallholder production

**Java vs. Sumatra**:
- **Java**: More estate-focused, some washed processing, slightly higher altitude
- **Sumatra**: Smallholder-dominated, almost all wet-hulled, lower altitude
- **Both**: Indonesian character - earthy, full-bodied, low-acid
- **Java Advantage**: Estate quality control, occasional fully washed lots
- **Sumatra Volume**: Much larger production

## üå± Varieties

**Typica** (Heritage Estates):
- Original variety (1600s-1800s), mostly destroyed by rust
- Reintroduced 1920s-1950s
- Tall, low-yield, excellent quality
- Estate focus

**S-795** (Indian Hybrid):
- Introduced post-rust as replacement
- Kent √ó Liberica hybrid, rust-resistant
- Good cup quality, productive
- Common in estates

**Catimor** (Smallholder):
- Rust-resistant, high-yield
- Lower cup quality
- Commodity production

## ‚òï Flavor Profile

**Wet-Hulled Java** (Most Common):

**Flavors**:
- **Earthy**: Forest floor, soil, mushroom (Indonesian signature)
- **Herbal**: Sage, thyme, dried herbs, eucalyptus
- **Chocolate**: Dark chocolate, cocoa powder
- **Spice**: Black pepper, clove, cardamom, cinnamon
- **Tobacco**: Pipe tobacco, aged leather
- **Cedar**: Woody, cedar, sawdust (not negative)

**Sensory**:
- **Acidity**: Very Low - Smooth, mellow, gentle
- **Body**: Very Full - Syrupy, heavy, coating, substantial
- **Sweetness**: Moderate - Earthy, molasses sweetness
- **Complexity**: Moderate - Layered earthiness, savory notes
- **Finish**: Long - Earthy persistence, coating

**Washed Java** (Estate Specialty, Rare):

**Flavors**:
- **Chocolate**: Milk chocolate, cocoa, sweet chocolate
- **Nuts**: Roasted nuts, walnut, almond
- **Spice**: Sweet spice, cinnamon, clove
- **Fruit**: Subtle fruit (apple, mild citrus)
- **Clean**: Much cleaner than wet-hulled

**Sensory**:
- **Acidity**: Medium-Low - Balanced, gentle, not bright
- **Body**: Medium-Full - Smooth, balanced, creamy
- **Sweetness**: High - Caramel, chocolate, clean sweetness
- **Complexity**: Moderate-High - Cleaner, more nuanced than wet-hulled
- **Finish**: Clean, sweet - chocolate lingers

**Verdict**: Washed Java is revelation - shows Indonesian terroir without extreme earthiness. Rare but worth seeking.
