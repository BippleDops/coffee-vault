## üìã Overview

El Salvador Bourbon represents Central American coffee's Bourbon renaissance - a small nation that maintained traditional Bourbon varieties while neighbors switched to hybrids, creating distinctive terroir expression through heritage genetics and volcanic soil. El Salvador's commitment to Bourbon, combined with innovative honey processing, has created unique specialty identity.

**What Makes El Salvador Bourbon Special**:
- **Bourbon Preservation**: Maintained Bourbon when others switched (1990s-2000s)
- **Volcanic Terroir**: Multiple volcanoes create diverse microclimates
- **Honey Processing Innovation**: El Salvador pioneered honey process levels
- **Quality Focus**: Small country, specialty-focused (quality over volume)
- **Competition Success**: Cup of Excellence regular winners
- **Sweet Profile**: Exceptional sweetness, chocolate-caramel dominant
- **Complex**: Layered, interesting, rewarding

**El Salvador's Coffee Renaissance**:
- **Civil War** (1980-1992): Coffee industry devastated
- **Post-War Recovery** (1990s-2000s): Quality focus strategy
- **Bourbon Renaissance**: Chose quality (Bourbon) over productivity (hybrids)
- **Honey Processing**: Became honey process innovation leader
- **Specialty Recognition** (2000s-present): International specialty embraces Salvadoran coffee
- **Small but Mighty**: Tiny production, outsized specialty reputation

**Why Bourbon Survived in El Salvador**:
- Post-war, couldn't afford replanting (blessing in disguise)
- Specialty market values Bourbon quality
- Premiums justified maintaining traditional varieties
- National coffee identity built on Bourbon heritage
- Cultural and economic decision to preserve rather than replace
