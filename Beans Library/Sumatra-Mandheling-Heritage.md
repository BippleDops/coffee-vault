## üìã Overview

Sumatra Mandheling is Indonesian coffee's classic icon - named for Mandailing people of North Sumatra, producing quintessential earthy, herbal, full-bodied Sumatran coffee through wet-hulling that defines Indonesian character globally. Mandheling represents what most people imagine when thinking "Indonesian coffee."

**What "Mandheling" Means**:
- **Mandailing**: Ethnic group in North Sumatra (Tapanuli region)
- **Name**: Coffee named for people/region (like "Colombian")
- **Not Variety**: Common misconception - Mandheling is region/processing, not variety
- **Classic**: Most recognizable Indonesian coffee name
- **Heritage**: Traditional Sumatran coffee, decades of export history

**Characteristics**:
- **Classic Indonesian**: Quintessential Sumatran profile
- **Wet-Hulled**: Giling Basah creates signature earthiness
- **Very Full Body**: Among heaviest-bodied coffees globally
- **Very Low Acid**: Smooth, gentle, stomach-friendly
- **Earthy-Herbal**: Forest floor, herbs, cedar (love it or hate it)
- **Dark Roast Friendly**: Excels at darker roast levels
- **Reliable**: Consistent Sumatran quality
- **Common**: Widely available, recognizable name
