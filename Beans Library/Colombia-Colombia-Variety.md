## üìã Overview

Colombia variety is Colombian coffee research's first rust-resistant success - developed 1980s as response to coffee rust, providing disease protection with acceptable cup quality. While superseded by improved Castillo variety (2005), Colombia variety remains common (10-15% production) and represents Colombian coffee's rust resistance journey.

**Characteristics**:
- **First Success**: First Colombian rust-resistant variety achieving commercial success
- **Acceptable Quality**: Good cup quality (better than early Catimors)
- **Productive**: Higher yield than Bourbon/Caturra
- **Rust-Resistant**: Strong disease protection
- **Superseded**: Castillo improved upon Colombia variety (better quality)
- **Still Common**: 10-15% production (farmers slow to replace)
