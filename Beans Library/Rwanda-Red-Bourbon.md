## üå± Red Bourbon Variety in Rwanda

**Why Red Bourbon Dominates Rwanda** (95%+ of production):

1. **Historical**: Introduced during colonial era (1930s), became established
2. **Quality**: Exceptional cup quality ideal for specialty focus
3. **Specialty Match**: Rwanda chose quality strategy, Bourbon delivers
4. **Altitude**: Performs excellently at 1,700-2,200 MASL (Rwanda's range)
5. **Recognition**: "Rwanda Bourbon" has market identity
6. **No Replacement**: Unlike other origins, Rwanda didn't replace Bourbon with hybrids
7. **Rust Management**: Accepts rust challenges for quality benefits

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Tall**: 3-4 meters, typical Bourbon height
- **Bronze Tips**: Young leaves bronze-tipped
- **Red Cherries**: Classic red cherry ripening
- **Yield**: Low (challenge for farmers)
- **Disease**: Rust-susceptible (ongoing challenge)

**Agricultural Challenges**:
- Low yield (30-40% less than modern hybrids)
- Rust susceptibility (requires management)
- Tall plants (harvesting challenges on steep slopes)

**Why Continue Growing**:
- Specialty premiums justify low yields
- Rwanda's quality reputation built on Bourbon
- Buyer expectation for Rwandan Bourbon
- National coffee identity

## üîÑ Processing: Kenyan-Inspired Washing

**Rwanda's Processing Revolution**:
- Post-genocide, Rwanda adopted Kenyan processing methods
- Built modern washing stations (200+ stations now)
- Cooperative ownership model
- Quality-focused processing protocols

**Washed Processing** (95%+ of Rwanda):

**Similar to Kenya** (but single fermentation more common):

1. **Selective Harvest**: Ripe red cherries only
2. **Depulping**: Same day at washing station
3. **Fermentation**: 12-36 hours (altitude-dependent, cool highlands = longer)
   - Some stations: Double fermentation (like Kenya)
   - Most stations: Single fermentation + thorough washing
4. **Washing**: Multiple rinses in clean channels
5. **Soaking**: 12-24 hours (some stations, like Kenya)
6. **Drying**: Raised African beds, 10-21 days
   - Shaded structures protect from intense sun
   - Sorted daily for defects
   - Even, slow drying for quality
7. **Dry Milling**: Parchment removed, sorted by size and density

**Result**: Exceptionally clean, bright, complex - East African signature

## ‚òï Flavor Profile

**Rwanda Red Bourbon (Washed, Light-Medium Roast)**:

**Primary Flavors**:
- **Berry**: Blackberry, raspberry, cranberry, mixed berries
- **Citrus**: Orange, lemon, grapefruit, tangerine (bright, clean)
- **Floral**: Jasmine, orange blossom, delicate florals
- **Chocolate**: Milk chocolate, cocoa (emerges in medium roasts)
- **Caramel**: Brown sugar, toffee, butterscotch

**Sensory**:
- **Acidity**: High - Vibrant, citric, malic, bright (similar to Kenya/Tanzania)
- **Body**: Medium-Full - Silky, smooth, substantial (fuller than Kenya)
- **Sweetness**: Very High - Berry sweetness, brown sugar, honey
- **Complexity**: Very High - Layered, multi-dimensional, evolving
- **Balance**: Excellent - Acidity-body-sweetness harmonize
- **Finish**: Long - Berry and citrus persist, clean aftertaste

**Comparison**:
- **Similar to Kenya**: Berry, brightness, complexity
- **Sweeter**: More pronounced sweetness than Kenya
- **Fuller Body**: Heavier mouthfeel, more coating
- **Less Intense Acidity**: Bright but not as electric as Kenya SL-28
- **More Balanced**: Less extreme, more approachable
- **Floral Notes**: More delicate florals, less blackcurrant-specific
- **Unique**: Distinct Rwanda character, not Kenya clone

**"Potato Defect" Reality**:
- **Rare Defect**: Some Rwandan coffee affected by potato taste defect (PTD)
- **Cause**: Antestia bug damage during growing
- **Impact**: 1-5% of beans potentially affected
- **Detection**: Difficult pre-roasting, appears in cup
- **Improving**: Better bug management reducing incidence
- **Not Widespread**: Most Rwandan coffee unaffected
- **Quality Control**: Better sorting reduces risk
