## üìã Overview

Geisha 1931 represents the original 1931 Ethiopian collection that became Panama's record-breaking variety - the specific genetic line that traveled from Ethiopia (1931) ‚Üí Kenya germplasm ‚Üí CATIE Costa Rica ‚Üí Panama (1963) ‚Üí global fame (2004+). This is THE Geisha that changed coffee forever, creating the template for ultra-premium specialty.

**What Makes Geisha 1931 Special**:
- **Original Line**: Specific genetic selection from 1931 Ethiopian collection
- **Panama Fame**: This is the Geisha that won Best of Panama 2004, set records
- **Genetic Bottleneck**: Limited original seed collection = narrow genetics
- **Selected**: 90+ years of cultivation selected specific traits
- **Uniform**: More consistent than Ethiopian wild-type Gesha (genetic uniformity)
- **Famous Flavor**: Jasmine-bergamot-tropical profile = Geisha 1931 signature
- **Global**: Planted worldwide post-2004 (everyone wants Panama Geisha genetics)
- **Record Prices**: $100-1,029/lb green auctions

**The 1931 Journey**:
- **1931**: Seeds collected near Gesha village, Ethiopia (British consul Richard Whalley)
- **1936**: Sent to Kitale, Kenya germplasm collection
- **1953**: Kenya sends seeds to CATIE (Costa Rica agricultural research)
- **1963**: Hacienda La Esmeralda (Panama) receives seeds from CATIE, plants on farm edges
- **1963-2003**: Ignored 40 years, considered unremarkable (low yield)
- **2004**: La Esmeralda separates Geisha lot, enters Best of Panama competition
  - **Wins**: Unprecedented scores, revolutionary flavors
  - **Auction**: $21/lb (record for 2004)
  - **Industry Shock**: Coffee world stunned
- **2005-Present**: Prices escalate ($50, $170, $350, $601, **$1,029/lb** record 2019)
- **Global Planting**: Everyone wants Geisha 1931 genetics
- **Present**: Most famous, expensive coffee variety globally

## üåç Geisha 1931 vs. Ethiopian Wild Gesha

**Geisha 1931** (Single Genetic Line):
- **Genetic Bottleneck**: From limited 1931 seed collection
- **Uniform**: 90+ years of selection in Panama/Central America
- **Consistent**: Predictable jasmine-bergamot profile
- **Famous**: "Panama Geisha" flavor = Geisha 1931
- **Refined**: Selected for specific characteristics over decades
- **Price**: Most expensive (Panama Geisha name premium)

**Ethiopian Wild Gesha** (Population Diversity):
- **Genetic Breadth**: Multiple wild trees, continuous evolution
- **Variable**: More flavor profile variation
- **Authentic**: Original terroir expression
- **Different**: Not identical to Panama (terroir + genetics)
- **Value**: Often cheaper than Panama (less prestige)

**Both Are Geisha**: Same original source, different expressions through terroir and selection
