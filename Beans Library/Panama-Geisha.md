## üìã Overview

Panama Geisha is the coffee that changed everything - a variety dismissed for 40 years that became the world's most expensive and celebrated coffee when Hacienda La Esmeralda separated and entered it into competition in 2004. With unprecedented floral (jasmine, bergamot), tea-like, and tropical fruit flavors, Panama Geisha commands $100-1,000+/lb and has redefined what specialty coffee can be.

**Historical Journey**:
- **1931**: Seeds collected in Gesha village, Ethiopia by Richard Whalley
- **1953**: Seeds sent from Kenya germplasm to CATIE (Costa Rica research station)
- **1963**: **Hacienda La Esmeralda** (Panama) receives seeds, plants on farm edges
- **1963-2003**: Ignored, considered low-yielding, picked mixed with other varieties
- **2004**: Esmeralda separates Geisha lot, enters Best of Panama competition
  - **Wins**: Panama competition, scoring unprecedented
  - **Auction**: Sells for record $21/lb green
  - **Industry Shocked**: Flavor profile unlike anything tasted before
- **2005-Present**: Prices escalate
  - 2010: $170/lb
  - 2013: $350/lb
  - 2017: $601/lb (Elida Estate)
  - **2019**: $1,029/lb (Elida Natural Geisha) - **World Record**
- **Impact**: Transformed specialty coffee, proved ultra-premium viable
- **Legacy**: Sparked Geisha plantings worldwide, elevated specialty pricing ceiling

**Why Panama Geisha is Expensive**:
1. **Rarity**: Very limited production (low yield variety)
2. **Quality**: Unmatched floral complexity
3. **Demand**: Global demand from ultra-premium buyers
4. **Competition**: Auction prices drive market
5. **Prestige**: Hacienda La Esmeralda fame
6. **Terroir**: Panama Boquete microclimate ideal
7. **Processing**: Meticulous, labor-intensive
8. **Brand**: "Panama Geisha" is luxury status symbol
9. **Records**: Repeated world record prices create hype
10. **Scarcity**: Can't easily scale production

**Is Panama Geisha Different from Ethiopian Gesha?**:
- **Same Variety**: Both from 1931 Ethiopian collection
- **Different Terroir**: Panama vs. Ethiopia expression
- **Selection**: 90+ years of Panama cultivation selected specific traits
- **Flavor Differences**:
  - **Panama**: Jasmine, bergamot, tropical fruit, refined
  - **Ethiopia**: More variable, wild-type diversity, authentic terroir
- **Pricing**: Panama more expensive (prestige, scarcity, competition)
- **Recognition**: Panama Geisha is global icon, Ethiopian Gesha authentic origin
