## üìã Overview

Brazil Catuai represents Brazilian coffee's practical excellence - a productive, reliable, high-quality variety developed specifically for Brazilian conditions. Combining Caturra's quality with Mundo Novo's productivity and resilience, Catuai has become one of Brazil's most important varieties, delivering the classic sweet, chocolatey, low-acid Brazilian profile at scale while maintaining specialty-grade potential.

**What Makes Catuai Important**:
- **Most Widely Planted**: One of Brazil's dominant varieties (30-40% of production)
- **Developed in Brazil**: Brazilian agricultural research success story
- **High Quality + High Yield**: Rare combination (quality usually sacrifices productivity)
- **Wind Resistant**: Short, compact plants resist wind damage
- **Mechanization-Friendly**: Uniform ripening enables mechanical harvesting
- **Specialty-Grade**: Can achieve 84-88+ cup scores when grown well
- **Reliable**: Consistent quality across different terroirs and climates
- **Red and Yellow**: Comes in both red-cherry and yellow-cherry variants
- **Global Adoption**: Planted in many origins beyond Brazil

**Historical Development**:
- **1950s-1960s**: Developed by IAC (Instituto Agron√¥mico de Campinas)
- **Parent**: Caturra (compact, quality) √ó Mundo Novo (productive, resilient)
- **Goal**: Combine quality with productivity for Brazilian conditions
- **1970s-1980s**: Mass adoption across Brazil
- **Success**: Became dominant variety alongside Mundo Novo
- **Legacy**: Proved quality and productivity compatible
- **Influence**: Used in further breeding programs globally

## üå± The Catuai Variety

### Genetic Background

**Hybrid Development**:
- **Parents**: 
  - **Caturra**: Bourbon mutation, compact, good quality, low yield
  - **Mundo Novo**: Typica √ó Bourbon natural hybrid, high yield, robust
- **Cross**: Caturra √ó Mundo Novo = Catuai
- **Goal**: Combine Caturra's quality + compactness with Mundo Novo's productivity
- **Success**: Achieved both goals, widely adopted
- **Release**: 1970s by IAC (Instituto Agron√¥mico de Campinas), Brazil
- **Variants**: Red Catuai and Yellow Catuai (cherry color variants)

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Height**: Compact, short (2-2.5 meters vs. 3-4m for traditional Bourbon)
- **Internodes**: Short spacing between branches
- **Branching**: Dense, lateral branches strong and numerous
- **Cherries**: Red or yellow (two color variants)
- **Leaves**: Dark green, medium size
- **Roots**: Strong, deep (drought tolerance moderate)

**Agricultural Advantages**:
- **Wind Resistance**: Short, strong branches resist wind damage (critical for open plateaus)
- **Density Planting**: Compact size allows more plants per hectare
- **Mechanization**: Uniform ripening and size enable mechanical harvesting
- **Yield**: High yield comparable to modern hybrids
- **Quality**: Maintains good cup quality (rare for high-yield varieties)
- **Adaptability**: Grows well 800-1,400 MASL (wide range)

**Challenges**:
- **Disease**: Susceptible to coffee leaf rust (like most Arabicas)
- **Requires Fertilization**: High productivity demands nutrients
- **Pests**: Susceptible to coffee berry borer
- **Not Ultra-Premium**: Good quality but not competition-winning complexity

**Why Widely Adopted**:
- Balances quality, yield, resilience
- Economically viable for farmers
- Works in mechanized and manual systems
- Reliable, predictable production
- International market acceptance

## üîÑ Processing Methods

### Natural (Dry) Processing ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **Brazilian Specialty**

**Brazil's Strength**:
Brazil perfected large-scale natural processing, and Catuai excels in this method.

**Process**:
1. **Harvest**: 
   - **Mechanical** (common for Catuai in Cerrado): Strip-picking entire branches
   - **Selective** (smaller farms): Hand-picked ripe cherries only
2. **Sorting**: Floatation tanks remove under-ripe and defects
3. **Drying**:
   - **Patios** (traditional): Concrete or brick, raked daily, 15-25 days
   - **Raised African Beds** (specialty): Better airflow, 20-30 days
   - **Mechanical Pre-Drying** (some farms): Guardiolas for initial moisture removal
4. **Storage**: Dried cherries stored in warehouse
5. **Dry Milling**: Fruit removed when ready to export/roast

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Sweetness**: Extreme - fruit sugars penetrate bean
- **Chocolate**: Dark chocolate, chocolate brownie, cocoa powder
- **Dried Fruit**: Raisin, date, fig, dried berry
- **Body**: Full to very full, syrupy, creamy
- **Acidity**: Very low, smooth, gentle
- **Complexity**: Deep chocolate-fruit complexity
- **Clean**: Brazilian natural processing is cleanest in world (dry climate advantage)

### Pulped Natural (Semi-Washed / Honey) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Brazil's Innovation**:
Brazil invented pulped natural processing, and Catuai performs excellently.

**Process**:
1. **Harvest**: Mechanical or selective
2. **Depulping**: Skin removed mechanically, mucilage left on
3. **Drying**: Sticky beans dried with mucilage intact
   - Patios or raised beds
   - 12-20 days
   - Raked frequently to prevent mold
4. **Dry Milling**: Dried mucilage removed

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Balance**: Between washed and natural
- **Sweetness**: High (higher than washed, lower than natural)
- **Chocolate**: Milk chocolate, caramel
- **Fruit**: Subtle, clean fruit notes (not extreme)
- **Body**: Medium-full, smooth
- **Acidity**: Low-medium (more than natural, less than washed)
- **Clarity**: Better than natural, more body than washed
- **Versatility**: Excellent for blending

### Washed Processing (Less Common) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Rare for Catuai in Brazil**:
- Brazilian climate favors natural/pulped natural
- Washed processing requires significant water (scarce in some regions)
- Mostly used for specific export markets or specialty requests

**When Used**:
- Specialty micro-lots
- Boutique farms with water access
- European market preference

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Clarity**: High, clean, bright
- **Acidity**: Higher than natural (but still low for coffee generally)
- **Chocolate-Nuts**: Primary flavors
- **Body**: Medium, lighter than natural
- **Different**: Loses classic Brazilian character somewhat
