## üìã Overview

Kenya Batian is modern Kenyan coffee breeding triumph - disease-resistant variety that actually maintains Kenyan signature brightness and complexity, proving quality and resistance can coexist. Named after Mount Kenya's third-highest peak, Batian represents Kenya's sustainable specialty coffee future.

**What Makes Batian Important**:
- **Quality + Resistance**: Rare combination (maintains Kenyan character with disease protection)
- **SL Genetics**: Incorporates SL-28 and SL-34 genetics
- **Berry Character**: Preserves Kenyan blackcurrant signature (somewhat)
- **Productive**: Higher yield than SL varieties
- **Compact**: Easier to manage than tall SL trees
- **Future**: Likely to dominate as farmers replant
- **Success**: Shows modern breeding can preserve terroir character

**Development**:
- **Parents**: Complex - Ruiru 11, SL-28, SL-34, Rume Sudan genetics
- **Goal**: Combine resistance (Ruiru 11) with quality (SL-28/34)
- **Success**: Achieved both better than previous attempts
- **Released**: 2010 by Coffee Research Institute
- **Adoption**: Rapidly increasing (10-15% of Kenyan coffee, growing)
- **Recognition**: Specialty buyers accepting Batian (quality adequate)

- **Much Better**: Significantly superior cup quality
- **Complexity**: Higher complexity, better acidity
- **Brightness**: More vibrant, cleaner
- **Success**: Batian succeeded where Ruiru 11 fell short
