## üìã Overview

Ethiopia Sidamo (properly spelled Sidama, the indigenous zone name) is one of Ethiopia's most famous and beloved coffee regions, producing both washed and natural processed coffees with exceptional berry character, wine-like complexity, and balanced sweetness that bridge the gap between Yirgacheffe's delicate florals and Harrar's bold fruit intensity.

**What Makes Sidamo Special**:
- **Balance**: More balanced than Yirgacheffe, less extreme than Harrar
- **Versatility**: Both washed and natural processing produce excellence
- **Berry Character**: Blueberry, strawberry, wine-like notes signature
- **Large Region**: Diverse microclimates, varied flavor profiles
- **Accessible**: More approachable than extreme Ethiopian profiles
- **History**: One of Ethiopia's original commercial coffee regions
- **Volume**: Significant production supports availability
- **Guji Connection**: Guji was part of Sidamo until separated (2010)

**The Sidamo/Sidama Name**:
- **Sidama**: Ethnic group and zone name (correct)
- **Sidamo**: Coffee trade name (historical)
- **Both Used**: Interchangeably in coffee industry
- **2020 Change**: Officially Sidama Zone (political reorganization)
- **Coffee Labels**: Still see both "Sidamo" and "Sidama"
