## üìã Overview

Ecuador Sidra is one of specialty coffee's most exciting emerging varieties - an Ethiopian-origin variety (possibly related to Geisha family) that performs exceptionally in Ecuadorian highlands, producing intensely floral, tropical-fruit-forward coffees winning international competitions and commanding ultra-premium prices while putting Ecuador on the specialty coffee map.

**What Makes Sidra Extraordinary**:
- **Geisha-Like**: Similar genetics to Geisha, comparable complexity
- **Ecuadorian Success**: Performs exceptionally in Ecuador specifically
- **Competition Winner**: Cup of Excellence podium regular (88-92+ points)
- **Tropical Fruit**: Mango, passion fruit, pineapple dominant
- **Jasmine Forward**: Intense floral character
- **Lower Price**: More affordable than Panama Geisha ($20-40/lb vs. $100-1000/lb)
- **Innovation Vessel**: Perfect for experimental processing (anaerobic, etc.)
- **Ecuador's Signature**: Defining Ecuadorian specialty coffee globally

**The Sidra Mystery**:
- **Ethiopian Origin**: Believed from Ethiopia (Sidamo connection debated)
- **Genetics**: Possibly Geisha family or related landrace
- **Name**: "Sidra" pronunciation suggests Ethiopian Sidamo link (unconfirmed)
- **Introduction**: How/when arrived in Ecuador unclear
- **Discovery**: Ecuadorian producers recognized exceptional quality ~2010s
- **Research**: Genetic analysis ongoing
