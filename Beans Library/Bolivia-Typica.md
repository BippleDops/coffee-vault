## üìã Overview

Bolivia Typica is South America's hidden specialty gem - high-altitude Andean coffee from remote Yungas region producing complex, balanced, exceptionally sweet coffees that rival neighboring Peru and Colombia but remain largely unknown in specialty market. Bolivia's extreme altitude (up to 2,300 MASL) and organic farming create distinctive profiles worth discovering.

**Why Bolivia Typica is Special**:
- **Extreme Altitude**: Up to 2,300 MASL (among highest coffee in South America)
- **Typica Quality**: Traditional variety, excellent complexity
- **Organic**: 95%+ organic by default (smallholders can't afford chemicals)
- **Yungas**: Unique microclimate, Andean cloud forest
- **Underrated**: Exceptional quality, minimal recognition
- **Value**: High quality at accessible pricing
- **Geisha Trials**: Some farms experimenting with Geisha (high-altitude suitable)

**Challenges**:
- **Infrastructure**: Very limited export infrastructure
- **Remote**: Difficult terrain, transport challenges
- **Political**: Political instability affects market access
- **Small Production**: Tiny compared to neighbors
- **Unknown**: Minimal specialty market presence

**Opportunity**:
- **Undiscovered**: Potential for specialty breakthrough
- **Quality**: Competition-level quality when reaching market
- **Direct Trade**: Growing relationships with specialty importers

## üåç Geography - Yungas Region

**Location**: Yungas region, La Paz Department, Eastern Andes slopes

**Characteristics**:
- **Altitude**: 1,200-2,300 MASL (extreme)
- **Climate**: Cool, cloud forest, Andean
- **Terrain**: Steep mountainsides, challenging cultivation
- **Soil**: Rich, forested slopes
- **Microclimate**: Andean cloud forest, unique

**Why Altitude Matters**:
- 2,000-2,300 MASL rare globally
- Extreme altitude = slow maturation, complexity, density
- Cool temperatures, diurnal variation
- Stress conditions ideal for flavor development
