## üìã Overview

Guatemala Bourbon represents Central American coffee at its finest - a traditional heirloom variety producing sweet, complex, balanced coffees with chocolate, caramel, and bright citrus notes that define the classic Guatemalan profile. Though challenged by coffee rust and low yields, Bourbon persists on quality-focused farms where its exceptional cup quality justifies the agricultural challenges.

**What Makes Guatemala Bourbon Special**:
- **Heirloom Variety**: Original coffee brought to Americas (R√©union Island ‚Üí Guatemala 1700s-1800s)
- **Exceptional Quality**: Sweet, complex, balanced - sets standard for Guatemalan coffee
- **Traditional**: Heritage variety, preserves historical flavor
- **Premium**: Commands 20-40% premiums over modern hybrids
- **Competition**: Bourbon regularly wins Cup of Excellence
- **Terroir Expression**: Showcases Guatemala's volcanic terroir clearly
- **Declining**: Being replaced by rust-resistant varieties (economic reality)
- **Specialty Focus**: Maintained by quality-obsessed producers

**Historical Legacy**:
- **1700s-1800s**: Bourbon brought from R√©union Island (via Martinique) to Guatemala
- **1800s-1900s**: Became dominant variety across Guatemala
- **Peak**: 1950s-1980s, 60-80% of Guatemalan coffee was Bourbon
- **1970s-Present**: Gradual replacement by higher-yielding, rust-resistant varieties
- **Current**: ~15-25% of production, declining but valued
- **Future**: Likely to remain in specialty/heritage farms only

## üå± The Bourbon Variety

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Height**: Tall, 3-4 meters (10-13 feet)
- **Leaves**: Bronze-tipped when young (diagnostic feature)
- **Cherries**: Red when ripe, medium size
- **Beans**: Elongated, uniform, dense
- **Branching**: Lateral branches at 45-60¬∞ angle
- **Yield**: Low (30-40% less than Caturra or Catuai)

**Agricultural Challenges**:
- **Coffee Leaf Rust**: Highly susceptible to Hemileia vastatrix
- **Low Yield**: Economically challenging without specialty premiums
- **Disease Pressure**: Requires management or accepts losses
- **Tall Plants**: Difficult to harvest and manage
- **Slow to Mature**: Longer to first production than modern varieties

**Why Farmers Still Grow Bourbon**:
- **Cup Quality**: Exceptional, competition-level
- **Premium Pricing**: 20-40% premiums justify lower yields
- **Specialty Demand**: Roasters specifically seek Bourbon
- **Heritage**: Traditional, cultural significance
- **Reputation**: "Bourbon" name carries quality recognition
