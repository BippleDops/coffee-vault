## üìã Overview

Costa Rica Villa Sarchi is a natural Bourbon mutation discovered in Costa Rica's Central Valley in the 1950s, combining Bourbon's excellent cup quality with compact plant size and improved productivity. This distinctly Costa Rican variety produces clean, bright, honey-sweet coffees that express Costa Rica's pristine washed processing and volcanic terroir with precision and elegance.

**What Makes Villa Sarchi Special**:
- **Costa Rican Origin**: Discovered and developed in Costa Rica
- **Bourbon Mutation**: Natural mutation, not hybrid - maintains Bourbon quality
- **Compact Size**: Smaller than Bourbon (easier harvest, higher density planting)
- **Better Yield**: 30-40% higher yield than standard Bourbon
- **Excellent Quality**: Maintains Bourbon cup quality despite better productivity
- **Clean Cup**: Costa Rica's legendary processing creates exceptional clarity
- **Honey Sweet**: Signature honey-sweetness in flavor profile
- **Balanced**: Approachable, versatile, broadly appealing
- **National Pride**: Uniquely Costa Rican, represents quality-focused breeding

**Historical Development**:
- **1950s**: Natural mutation discovered in Villa Sarchi village, Central Valley
- **Observation**: Farmers noticed compact Bourbon plants with good yield
- **Research**: ICAFE (Costa Rican Coffee Institute) studied and propagated
- **1960s-1970s**: Distributed to farms, adopted gradually
- **Peak**: 1970s-1990s, 15-25% of Costa Rican coffee
- **Decline**: Replaced by Caturra, Catuai (even more productive)
- **Present**: ~10-15% of production, valued for quality
- **Resurgence**: Specialty market appreciates distinctiveness

## üå± Villa Sarchi Variety

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Height**: Compact, 2-3 meters (vs. Bourbon 3-4m)
- **Internodes**: Short spacing (mutation characteristic)
- **Leaves**: Bronze-tipped like Bourbon
- **Cherries**: Red when ripe, medium size
- **Beans**: Similar to Bourbon, good size
- **Branching**: Dense, lateral branches

**Agricultural Advantages**:
- **Compact**: Easier harvesting, higher plant density possible
- **Better Yield**: 30-40% more productive than standard Bourbon
- **Quality**: Maintains Bourbon cup characteristics
- **Adaptable**: Works 1,200-1,800 MASL range

**Challenges**:
- **Rust**: Still susceptible (like Bourbon)
- **Lower Yield**: Than Caturra, Catuai, hybrids
- **Economic**: Only viable with specialty premiums
