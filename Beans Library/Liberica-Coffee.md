## üìã Overview

Liberica is coffee's third commercial species (after Arabica and Robusta) - producing dramatically larger beans, asymmetrical shapes, and polarizing woody-smoky flavors beloved in Philippines and Malaysia but rare elsewhere. Liberica represents coffee biodiversity and regional preference diversity.

**What Makes Liberica Unique**:
- **Different Species**: Coffea liberica, not Arabica (genetically distinct)
- **Giant Beans**: 30-50% larger than Arabica (irregular, asymmetrical)
- **Unique Flavor**: Woody, smoky, bold, unlike Arabica/Robusta
- **Regional**: Philippines national coffee, Malaysian specialty (kap√© barako)
- **Polarizing**: Love-it-or-hate-it reactions
- **Rare**: 1-2% global production
- **Low Altitude**: Grows 0-900 MASL (lowland species)

**Historical Context**:
- **Native**: West Africa (Liberia especially)
- **1890s**: Brought to Philippines, Malaysia as rust-resistant alternative
- **Saved Philippines**: Replaced Arabica after rust epidemic
- **National Coffee**: "Barako" in Philippines (cultural icon)
- **Malaysia**: "Kopi Liberica" specialty
- **Decline**: Largely replaced by Robusta globally
- **Niche**: Survives as regional specialty
