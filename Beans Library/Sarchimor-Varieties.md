## üìã Overview

Sarchimor varieties represent improved rust-resistant coffee - combining Villa Sarchi (Costa Rican compact Bourbon) with Timor Hybrid to create better cup quality than Catimor while maintaining disease protection. Sarchimor is foundation for successful modern varieties like Lempira, Parainema, and Marsellesa.

**Why Sarchimor Matters**:
- **Better Than Catimor**: Noticeably superior cup quality
- **Rust-Resistant**: Disease protection maintained
- **Productive**: Good yield (better than traditional, less than Catimor)
- **Breeding Parent**: Used to create successful varieties (Lempira, Parainema)
- **Specialty-Acceptable**: Quality adequate for specialty market (borderline)
- **Practical**: Enables sustainable farming with acceptable quality

**Development**:
- **Villa Sarchi Parent**: Better quality base than Caturra (Catimor parent)
- **Timor Hybrid**: Resistance genetics
- **1970s-1980s**: Developed and distributed
- **Adoption**: Central America primarily
- **Success**: Led to improved selections (Lempira, Parainema)

**Derivatives** (Improved Sarchimors):
- **Lempira** (Honduras): Sarchimor selection, specialty-grade
- **Parainema** (Honduras): Sarchimor derivative, excellent quality
- **Marsellesa**: World Coffee Research Sarchimor, quality focus
