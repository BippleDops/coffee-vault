## ðŸ“‹ Overview

Guatemala Typica represents coffee's original genetics expressed through Guatemala's ideal volcanic terroir - the tall, low-yielding, exceptionally complex variety that defined coffee quality for centuries, now maintained only on heritage farms where its outstanding cup quality justifies agricultural challenges.

**Why Typica Matters**:
- **Original Variety**: Genetic foundation of global coffee (brought from Yemen/Ethiopia to Java to Americas 1700s)
- **Exceptional Quality**: Unmatched complexity, clarity, balance
- **Heritage**: Represents historical coffee, genetic preservation
- **Guatemala's Best**: When found, delivers Guatemala's finest expression
- **Rare**: <5% of production (low yield makes it economically challenging)
- **Competition**: Typica lots win competitions when entered
- **Premium**: Commands significant premiums
- **Genetic Resource**: Important for coffee breeding programs globally

**Historical Journey**:
- **1700s**: Typica brought from Java to Americas
- **Guatemala**: Arrived early-mid 1700s, became established
- **1800s-1900s**: Dominant variety in Guatemala
- **Peak**: Pre-1950, 70-80% of Guatemalan coffee was Typica
- **Decline**: Replaced by more productive varieties (Bourbon, Caturra, Catuai)
- **Present**: ~3-5% of production, heritage farms only
- **Status**: Endangered commercially, preserved for quality

## ðŸŒ± The Typica Variety

**Plant Characteristics**:
- **Height**: Very tall, 3.5-5 meters (tallest Arabica variety)
- **Conical Shape**: Christmas tree silhouette, tapering top
- **Leaves**: Bronze-tipped when young (diagnostic)
- **Internodes**: Wide spacing between branches
- **Cherries**: Elongated, pointed shape
- **Beans**: Long, narrow, elegant shape
- **Branching**: Low lateral branch density

**Agricultural Challenges** (Why Rare):
- **Very Low Yield**: 30-50% LESS than Bourbon, 50-70% less than Caturra/Catuai
- **Tall**: Extremely difficult to harvest (ladders required)
- **Disease**: Highly susceptible to coffee rust, pests
- **Delicate**: Less vigorous than other varieties
- **Economics**: Only viable with ultra-specialty premiums ($15-30+/lb green)
- **Labor**: Harvesting tall trees labor-intensive and expensive

**Why Heritage Farms Maintain Typica**:
- **Cup Quality**: Unmatched complexity and elegance
- **Premium Pricing**: 50-100% premiums over standard varieties
- **Competition**: Competition-winning potential
- **Heritage**: Cultural and historical significance
- **Genetic Preservation**: Maintaining coffee biodiversity
- **Specialty Buyers**: Direct trade partners specifically seek Typica
- **Farm Pride**: Prestige of growing original heirloom
