Timor Hybrid is coffee's genetic workhorse - a spontaneous natural hybrid of Arabica and Robusta discovered in Timor that provides rust resistance genetics for countless modern varieties (Catimor, Sarchimor, Castillo, Lempira, Ruiru 11, and many more). While Timor Hybrid itself has poor cup quality, its genetic contribution to coffee sustainability is immeasurable.

**Why Timor Hybrid is Critical**:

- **Genetic Resource**: Invaluable for coffee breeding programs
- **Not for Drinking**: Poor cup quality (Robusta traits)

**Hybrid Composition**:

**Why Rarely Grown for Consumption**:

- **Bitter**: More bitter than pure Arabica
- **Used for Breeding**: Not drinking coffee

**Backcrossing Strategy**:
1. **F1**: Timor Hybrid (50% Robusta) - poor quality, rust-resistant
2. **Backcross to Arabica**: Timor √ó Arabica ‚Üí 75% Arabica
3. **Backcross Again**: Result √ó Arabica ‚Üí 87.5% Arabica
4. **Multiple Generations**: Eventually 95%+ Arabica genetics
5. **Selection**: Choose plants with resistance + improved cup quality
6. **Result**: Varieties like Castillo, Lempira (good quality + resistance)
