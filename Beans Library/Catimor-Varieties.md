## üìã Overview

Catimor is coffee's commodity workhorse - rust-resistant hybrid combining Caturra with Timor Hybrid to create productive, disease-resistant plants that sacrifice cup quality for agricultural practicality. While specialty coffee generally avoids Catimor, it enables economically viable coffee farming in challenging conditions and forms foundation of commodity market.

**Characteristics**:
- **Rust-Resistant**: Strong disease protection (Timor Hybrid genetics)
- **Very Productive**: 50-80% higher yield than traditional varieties
- **Lower Quality**: Cup quality below Bourbon, Caturra, traditional varieties
- **Commodity**: Primary use in commercial/commodity production
- **Global**: Grown worldwide (especially Asia, Central America)
- **Economic**: Enables profitable farming without specialty premiums

**Development**:
- **1950s-1960s**: Created by crossing Caturra √ó Timor Hybrid
- **Goal**: Rust resistance + productivity
- **Trade-Off**: Accepted lower quality for resistance/yield
- **Adoption**: Rapid global adoption (1970s-1980s)
- **Reality**: Saved coffee farming but compromised quality
- **Specialty Reaction**: Specialty movement rejects Catimor (seeks quality)
