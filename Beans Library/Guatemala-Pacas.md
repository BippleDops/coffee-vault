## üìã Overview

Guatemala Pacas is a compact Bourbon mutation (originally from El Salvador) that thrives in Guatemalan volcanic highlands, producing balanced, sweet, chocolate-citrus coffees with excellent cup quality and better productivity than standard Bourbon. As parent to the famous Pacamara variety, Pacas represents the bridge between traditional quality and modern productivity.

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Bourbon Quality**: Maintains Bourbon sweetness and complexity
- **Compact**: Smaller than Bourbon (easier harvesting, higher density planting)
- **Better Yield**: 30-40% more productive than Bourbon
- **Guatemala Expression**: Volcanic terroir creates chocolate-citrus-spice profile
- **Pacamara Parent**: One half of Pacamara genetics (Pacas √ó Maragogipe)
- **Reliable**: Consistent quality, forgiving to brew
- **Value**: Excellent quality-to-price ratio for Guatemalan coffee

**Discovery & History**:
- **1949**: Discovered in El Salvador (Pacas family farm, hence name)
- **Bourbon Mutation**: Natural dwarf mutation of Bourbon variety
- **1950s-1960s**: Spread to Guatemala, Honduras, Nicaragua
- **Adoption**: Widely planted for improved productivity
- **Pacamara Creation** (1958): Crossed with Maragogipe ‚Üí Pacamara
- **Present**: 10-15% of Guatemalan coffee, valued for quality-productivity balance

## üå± Pacas Variety Characteristics

**Plant Morphology**:
- **Height**: Compact, 2-2.5 meters (vs. Bourbon 3-4m)
- **Internodes**: Short spacing between branches (mutation characteristic)
- **Leaves**: Bronze-tipped when young (Bourbon trait)
- **Cherries**: Red when ripe, medium size
- **Beans**: Similar to Bourbon, good size and density
- **Branching**: Dense lateral branches

**Agricultural Advantages**:
- **Compact Size**: Easier to harvest, prune, manage
- **Higher Density**: More plants per hectare possible
- **Better Yield**: 30-40% more productive than Bourbon
- **Quality Maintained**: Bourbon cup characteristics preserved
- **Wind Resistant**: Short, sturdy structure (important for exposed highlands)

**Challenges**:
- **Coffee Rust**: Susceptible like all Bourbon types
- **Lower Yield**: Still less productive than modern hybrids (Catuai, Catimor)
- **Economic Pressure**: Can only compete with specialty premiums
- **Declining**: Gradually being replaced by even more productive varieties

**Why Still Grown**:
- Cup quality superior to hybrids
- Specialty market preference
- Pacas name recognition (especially as Pacamara parent)
- Balance of quality and productivity acceptable for many farms
