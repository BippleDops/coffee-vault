## üìã Overview

Colombia Pink Bourbon is one of coffee's most intriguing and misunderstood varieties - a rare, naturally occurring mutation that produces pink cherries and delivers explosive fruit flavors that challenge everything you thought Colombian coffee could be. Once dismissed as "red Bourbon planted too high," Pink Bourbon has emerged as Colombia's specialty darling, commanding premium prices and winning competitions with its unprecedented sweetness and complexity.

**What Makes Pink Bourbon Extraordinary**:
- **Pink cherries**: Unique pink/orange color when ripe (not red, not yellow)
- **Extreme fruitiness**: Berry, tropical fruit, wine-like - unlike typical Colombian profiles
- **Rare**: Less than 1% of Colombian coffee production
- **Mysterious genetics**: Origin and genetics still debated
- **Ultra-premium**: Commands 50-200% premiums over standard Colombian coffee
- **Competition winner**: Regular Cup of Excellence podium finisher
- **Processing versatility**: Shines in washed, honey, natural, and experimental processes
- **High-altitude specialist**: Best at 1,700+ MASL
- **Recent fame**: Exploded in specialty scene 2015-2020
- **Instagram coffee**: Visually stunning, highly shareable

**The Pink Bourbon Mystery**:
- **Name**: "Pink Bourbon" because cherries are pink and assumed to be Bourbon variant
- **Genetics**: Still debated - possibly Ethiopian landrace, possibly unique mutation
- **Not Red Bourbon**: DNA testing shows NOT simply red Bourbon
- **Classification uncertainty**: May not even be Bourbon lineage
- **Discovery**: Farmers in Huila noticed distinct pink-cherry trees ~2010
- **Recognition**: Specialty buyers discovered exceptional cup quality ~2015
- **Status**: Research ongoing, genetics being studied

## üåç Origin & Growing Regions

### Primary Growing Regions

**Huila (Southern Colombia)**:
- **Altitude**: 1,600-2,100 MASL
- **Epicenter**: Where Pink Bourbon first gained recognition
- **Soil**: Rich volcanic, mineral-dense
- **Climate**: Cool, high altitude, ideal for Pink Bourbon
- **Character**: Fruit-forward, berry, complex acidity
- **Recognition**: Most famous Pink Bourbon comes from Huila
- **Notable Farms**: Multiple award-winning micro-lots

**Cauca (Southwestern Colombia)**:
- **Altitude**: 1,700-2,000 MASL
- **Terrain**: Mountainous, volcanic
- **Climate**: Cool, misty, high-altitude ideal
- **Character**: Tropical fruit, floral, wine-like
- **Recognition**: Growing Pink Bourbon production
- **Quality**: Exceptional, competition-level

**Nari√±o (Far Southwest)**:
- **Altitude**: 1,800-2,100 MASL (some of Colombia's highest)
- **Climate**: Very cool, extreme altitude
- **Character**: Extremely bright, floral, complex
- **Status**: Emerging Pink Bourbon region
- **Potential**: Exceptional but limited production

**Why High Altitude is Essential**:
- Pink Bourbon requires 1,700+ MASL for best expression
- Lower altitudes: loses complexity, less fruit, generic
- Higher altitudes: extreme fruit, florals, sweetness develop fully
- Cool temperatures slow maturation = flavor development
- Pink Bourbon more altitude-sensitive than standard varieties

## üå± The Pink Bourbon Variety

### Botanical Characteristics

**Plant Appearance**:
- **Cherries**: Pink/orange color when ripe (distinctive)
  - Not red (like Bourbon)
  - Not yellow (like Yellow Bourbon)
  - Pink/orange/salmon colored
  - Color varies slightly tree to tree
- **Leaves**: Bronze-tipped when young (like Bourbon)
- **Height**: Tall, similar to Bourbon
- **Branching**: Open, similar to traditional varieties
- **Beans**: Medium size, elongated
- **Visual ID**: Cherry color is primary identifier

**Agricultural Traits**:
- **Yield**: Low to moderate (similar to Bourbon)
- **Disease Susceptibility**: Susceptible to coffee leaf rust (like Bourbon)
- **Drought Tolerance**: Moderate
- **Altitude Requirement**: HIGH - needs 1,700+ MASL for quality
- **Management**: Requires careful attention, not beginner variety
- **Economic Viability**: Only profitable with specialty premiums

**Challenges for Farmers**:
1. **Low Yield**: 30-50% less than Caturra or Castillo
2. **Rust Susceptible**: Requires disease management or organic methods
3. **Altitude**: Only viable in highest elevations (limits planting areas)
4. **Cherry Identification**: Pink cherries harder to identify ripeness (vs. red)
5. **Market**: Requires access to specialty buyers paying premium

**Why Farmers Grow It Despite Challenges**:
- **Premium Pricing**: $15-40/lb green (vs. $4-8 for standard Colombian)
- **Recognition**: Pink Bourbon name carries cachet
- **Competition**: Wins awards, brings media attention
- **Specialty Demand**: Roasters specifically seek Pink Bourbon
- **Farm Differentiation**: Unique offering, not commodity

### The Genetics Mystery

**What We Know**:
- Pink cherry color is recessive gene expression
- DNA analysis shows NOT standard Bourbon
- Possibly Ethiopian heirloom landrace
- OR unique spontaneous mutation in Colombia
- Genetic research ongoing

**What's Debated**:
- **Is it Bourbon?**: Likely not, despite name
- **Ethiopian origin?**: Some evidence suggests Ethiopian genetics
- **Spontaneous mutation?**: Could be unique Colombian development
- **Unique variety?**: May be distinct variety, not Bourbon variant
- **Naming**: "Pink Bourbon" may be misnomer, but name stuck

**Genetic Studies**:
- World Coffee Research investigating
- DNA testing shows distinct from Red/Yellow Bourbon
- May be reclassified in future
- Regardless of genetics, cup quality undeniable

## üîÑ Processing Methods

### Washed (Traditional Colombian) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Process**:
1. **Selective Harvest**: Only ripe pink cherries (requires trained pickers)
2. **Depulping**: Same day as harvest
3. **Fermentation**: 12-36 hours (altitude-dependent)
4. **Washing**: Clean water channels
5. **Drying**: Raised African beds, 12-20 days (slow drying preserves complexity)

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Clarity**: Extreme clarity of fruit notes
- **Fruit**: Berry (strawberry, raspberry, blueberry), stone fruit
- **Floral**: Jasmine, orange blossom, perfumed
- **Acidity**: Very high, vibrant, citric and malic
- **Sweetness**: Very high, fruit-driven
- **Body**: Medium to medium-full
- **Complexity**: Layered, evolving, complex
- **Clean Cup**: Exceptional cleanliness

**Best For**: Showcasing Pink Bourbon's inherent fruit character

### Honey Process (Miel) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Increasingly Popular**:
- Pink Bourbon excels in honey processing
- Multiple levels used (yellow, red, black honey)
- Amplifies sweetness and fruit

**Process**:
- Depulp, leave mucilage on bean
- Dry with mucilage intact (10-18 days)
- Constant turning, monitoring

**Honey Levels**:
- **Yellow Honey**: 30-50% mucilage, balanced
- **Red Honey**: 50-75% mucilage, very fruity
- **Black Honey**: 75-100% mucilage, extreme fruit bomb

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Sweetness**: Extreme, candy-like, fruit sugars intense
- **Fruit**: Berry explosion, tropical fruit, jammy
- **Body**: Fuller than washed, syrupy
- **Complexity**: Very high, layered fruit
- **Acidity**: Still high but fruit-balanced
- **Unique**: Distinctive, memorable profiles

**Competition Favorite**: Honey Pink Bourbon wins awards regularly

### Natural (Dry) Process ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Rare but Exceptional**:
- Less common due to Colombia's humidity
- When successful, produces extraordinary results
- Requires ideal dry season conditions

**Process**:
- Whole pink cherries dried on raised beds
- 20-30 days drying time
- Meticulous sorting, turning, monitoring
- High risk (mold) but high reward (flavor)

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Fruit Bomb**: Extreme fruit intensity
- **Berry**: Blueberry jam, strawberry, blackberry compote
- **Tropical**: Pineapple, mango, passion fruit, guava
- **Wine**: Red wine, fermented fruit, boozy
- **Body**: Full, syrupy, viscous, coating
- **Sweetness**: Off-the-charts, dessert-like
- **Acidity**: Moderate (fruit-driven rather than bright)
- **Complexity**: Overwhelming complexity

**Polarizing**: Some love extreme fruit, some find it too much

**Price**: Natural Pink Bourbon can fetch $30-80+/lb green

### Anaerobic Fermentation ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Cutting-Edge Processing**:
- Pink Bourbon perfect candidate for experimental processing
- Anaerobic fermentation creates unique profiles
- Sealed tanks, controlled environment, CO‚ÇÇ-rich

**Methods**:
- **Anaerobic Natural**: Whole cherries in sealed tank, 48-96 hours
- **Anaerobic Honey**: Depulped with mucilage, sealed tank
- **Anaerobic Washed**: Fermentation in sealed tank before washing

**Flavor Impact**:
- **Exotic Fruit**: Lychee, passion fruit, guava, papaya
- **Funky**: Wine-like, fermented, complex funk
- **Floral**: Intense perfume, jasmine, rose
- **Sweetness**: Extreme
- **Unique**: Unlike any standard coffee
- **Complexity**: Multi-dimensional, challenging

**Market**: Ultra-premium specialty, $40-100+/lb green, competition-level

**Roaster Favorite**: Creates unique, memorable, shareable coffees

## üî• Roasting Pink Bourbon

### Optimal Roast Levels

**Light Roast (City)** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **IDEAL**
- **Best expression**: Showcases fruit, floral, complexity
- Preserves delicate berry and tropical notes
- Bright acidity fully expressed

**Light-Medium (City+)** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **VERSATILE**
- **Excellent balance**: Fruit + developing sweetness
- Berry still dominant
- Acidity mellows slightly
- Body increases
- Caramel emerges
- Versatile for multiple brew methods
- Broader appeal than pure light

**Medium (Full City)** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
- **Trade-off**: Sweetness vs. fruit intensity
- Caramel and chocolate develop
- Berry diminishes (still present)
- Acidity balanced
- Fuller body
- Good for those finding light too bright
- **Risk**: Loses some Pink Bourbon magic

**Medium-Dark (Vienna)** ‚≠ê‚≠ê
- **Not recommended**: Origin character fades
- Fruit notes mostly gone
- Chocolate and roast flavors dominate
- **Waste of Pink Bourbon**: Better varieties for this roast

**Dark Roast (French)** ‚≠ê **Never**
- Absolute waste
- All Pink Bourbon character destroyed
- Might as well use commodity coffee
- Don't do this

**Verdict**: Light to light-medium roasts essential. Medium acceptable. Anything darker wastes Pink Bourbon's unique character.

### Roasting Technique

**Pink Bourbon Roasting Characteristics**:
- **Density**: High (high-altitude growth)
- **Delicate Compounds**: Fruit and floral compounds volatile
- **Narrow Window**: Easy to over-develop and lose magic
- **Challenge**: Balance development with preserving fruit

**Recommended Light Roast Profile**:
1. **Charge**: 385-400¬∞F (196-204¬∞C)
2. **Drying**: 5-6 minutes to yellow
3. **Browning**: 3-4 minutes to first crack
4. **First Crack**: 9-11 minutes total
5. **Development**: **45-75 seconds post-crack** (short!)
   - Longer = more caramel, less fruit
   - Shorter = more fruit, brighter
6. **Drop Temp**: 402-410¬∞F (206-210¬∞C)
   - City to City+ roast level
   - Light to light-medium brown

**Critical Tips**:
- **Don't over-develop**: Pink Bourbon's magic is delicate
- **Short development**: 45-75 seconds post-crack maximum
- **Cool rapidly**: Preserve fruit compounds
- **Rest 3-7 days**: Needs rest but degasses faster than darker roasts
- **Sample first**: Learn coffee before committing
- **Err light**: Under-development better than over for Pink Bourbon

## üçµ Brewing Pink Bourbon

### Best Brewing Methods

**Pour Over (V60, Kalita Wave, Chemex)** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **PERFECT**
- **Ideal**: Showcases clarity, fruit, complexity
- **Ratio**: 1:16 to 1:17 (e.g., 15g : 255g water)
- **Grind**: Medium-fine
- **Water**: 200-205¬∞F (93-96¬∞C) - hot for bright extraction
- **Time**: 2:30-3:30 total
- **Water Quality**: Critical - use excellent water for Pink Bourbon
- **Why Perfect**:
  - Clean extraction, no mudding
  - Fruit and floral fully expressed
  - Clarity showcases complexity
- **Result**: Berry, tropical fruit, floral, clean, vibrant

**Aeropress** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
- **Versatile**: Can highlight different aspects
- **Ratio**: 1:15 to 1:16
- **Grind**: Medium-fine
- **Water**: 200¬∞F
- **Time**: 1:30-2:00 (shorter for brighter, longer for fuller)
- **Method**: Inverted or standard
- **Result**: Clean, bright, fruit-forward, slightly fuller body than pour-over

**Espresso** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **Challenging but Rewarding**
- **Controversial**: Some say waste of Pink Bourbon, others love it
- **Light espresso roast required** (don't use medium+ roast)
- **Dose**: 18g
- **Yield**: 45-50g (1:2.5 ratio - longer for fruit)
- **Time**: 25-30 seconds
- **Temp**: 200-202¬∞F
- **Result**: Fruit bomb espresso, berry syrup, complex, intense
- **Milk Drinks**: Incredible in cappuccino (berry + milk = unique)
- **Challenge**: High acidity can be intense in espresso
- **Cost**: $4-8 per shot (expensive experiment)
- **Verdict**: Try once if you can afford it, pour-over better value

**French Press** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
- **Works** but not ideal
- Oils reduce clarity
- Body excellent, but Pink Bourbon's strength is clarity
- **Ratio**: 1:15
- **Grind**: Medium-coarse
- **Steep**: 4:00
- **Result**: Full-bodied, berry-forward, less clarity

**Cold Brew** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
- **Good** but loses bright acidity (Pink Bourbon strength)
- Berry flavors persist
- Very sweet, smooth
- **Ratio**: 1:8 concentrate
- **Time**: 16-20 hours
- **Result**: Berry juice, tropical fruit, smooth, sweet
- **Note**: Expensive for cold brew, consider cheaper beans

**Cupping** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
- **Professional tasting**: Ideal for evaluating Pink Bourbon
- SCA protocol
- Showcases full range of flavors
- Competition standard

**Verdict**: Pour-over is king for Pink Bourbon. Espresso for adventurous. Avoid methods that muddy clarity.
