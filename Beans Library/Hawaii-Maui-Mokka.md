## üìã Overview

Hawaii Maui Mokka is coffee's tiniest beans producing surprising complexity - ancient Yemeni variety with beans half the size of normal coffee, grown on Maui slopes creating delicate, floral, sweet profiles at luxury pricing ($60-100+/lb). Ultra-rare, expensive, and fascinating for coffee extremes exploration.

**What Makes Mokka Extraordinary**:
- **Smallest Beans**: Half normal coffee size (peaberry-sized but not peaberry)
- **Ancient**: Yemeni heirloom variety (pre-Typica/Bourbon)
- **Rare**: Minimal global production (Hawaii primary source)
- **Delicate**: Floral, sweet, complex, refined
- **Expensive**: $60-100+/lb roasted (rarity + Hawaii labor)
- **Unique**: Completely different from standard coffee

**The Mokka Variety**:
- **Origin**: Yemen (pre-colonial coffee)
- **Small Beans**: Genetic characteristic
- **Low Yield**: Extremely low productivity
- **Delicate**: Fragile plants, challenging cultivation
- **Hawaii**: One of few places still growing Mokka commercially
