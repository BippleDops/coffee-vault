## üìã Overview

Ethiopia Bebeka is southwestern Ethiopia's rustic specialty - producing wine-like, full-bodied, chocolate-spice coffees with earthy undertones that make it Ethiopia's boldest and least delicate option. Bebeka represents Ethiopian coffee's full-spectrum diversity: from Yirgacheffe's tea-like delicacy to Bebeka's bold earthiness.

**Characteristics**:
- **Southwest Ethiopia**: Different terroir from famous eastern regions
- **Lower Altitude**: 1,000-1,700 MASL (lower than Yirgacheffe/Sidamo)
- **Forest Coffee**: Some wild/semi-wild forest coffee
- **Bold**: Full-bodied, rustic, earthy, wine-like
- **Natural Processing**: Predominantly natural (dry) processed
- **Commercial**: Larger production, less boutique than micro-regions
- **Underrated**: Less famous, good value
- **Different**: Not typical Ethiopian profile (no floral-tea delicacy)

## ‚òï Flavor Profile

**Natural Bebeka (Medium Roast)**:
- **Wine**: Red wine, fermented fruit, vinous, boozy, grape-like
- **Chocolate**: Dark chocolate, cocoa powder, bittersweet chocolate
- **Earthy**: Forest floor, soil, mushroom (milder than Harrar)
- **Spice**: Cinnamon, clove, cardamom, black pepper, sweet spice
- **Berry**: Blueberry, blackberry (natural processing)
- **Tobacco**: Aged tobacco, leather (rustic notes)
- **Body**: Full to Very Full - Syrupy, heavy, coating, substantial
- **Acidity**: Medium - Moderate, wine-like (not bright citric)
- **Sweetness**: Moderate - Wine-chocolate sweetness, earthy
- **Complexity**: Moderate - Layered but rustic (not refined)
- **Rustic**: Bold, earthy, not elegant
- **Finish**: Long - Wine-earth notes persist

**Character**: Bold rustic Ethiopian - wine-earth-chocolate, full-bodied
