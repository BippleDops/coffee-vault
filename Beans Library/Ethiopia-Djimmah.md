## üìã Overview

Ethiopia Djimmah (also Jimma) is western Ethiopia's bold, earthy coffee - naturally processed heirloom producing wine-like, full-bodied profiles with earthy undertones that make it Ethiopia's most polarizing and least "typical Ethiopian" coffee. While overshadowed by Yirgacheffe and Sidamo, Djimmah offers unique Ethiopian expression for those seeking body and earthiness over floral delicacy.

**Characteristics**:
- **Natural Processing**: Predominantly dry-processed (traditional method)
- **Earthy**: More earthy than other Ethiopian (Harrar-like but different)
- **Wine-Like**: Fermented fruit, vinous complexity
- **Full Body**: Heaviest-bodied Ethiopian coffee
- **Commercial**: Larger production, commercial volumes
- **Lower Grade**: Often Grade 4-5 (vs. Grade 1-2 in Yirgacheffe)
- **Underrated**: Quality lots exist but overshadowed
- **Bold**: Aggressive, not delicate

**Jimma Zone Context**:
- **Home of Coffee Research**: Jimma Agricultural Research Center (JARC)
- **Varieties**: 74110, 74112 improved varieties from Jimma research
- **Traditional**: Long coffee history
- **Forest Coffee**: Some wild/semi-wild forest coffee

## ‚òï Flavor Profile

**Natural Djimmah (Medium Roast)**:
- **Earthy**: Earth, forest floor, mushroom, soil (present but not extreme)
- **Wine**: Red wine, fermented fruit, grape, vinous, boozy
- **Chocolate**: Dark chocolate, cocoa powder, chocolate
- **Spice**: Cinnamon, clove, cardamom, sweet spice, black pepper
- **Berry**: Blueberry, blackberry (natural processing)
- **Leather**: Aged leather, tobacco (background)
- **Body**: Full to Very Full - Heavy, syrupy, coating, substantial
- **Acidity**: Medium - Moderate, wine-like (not bright citric)
- **Sweetness**: Moderate - Earthy-wine sweetness, chocolate
- **Complexity**: Moderate-High - Layered earthiness, spice, wine
- **Rustic**: Bold, rustic, not refined
- **Finish**: Long - Earthy-wine notes persist

**Character**: Bold Ethiopian - earthy, wine-like, full-bodied, polarizing

**Washed Djimmah** (Less Common):
- **Cleaner**: Less earthy, more citrus-floral
- **Balanced**: More typical Ethiopian character
- **Better**: When washed, shows Djimmah can be clean
- **Rare**: Most Djimmah is natural processed
