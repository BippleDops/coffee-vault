## üìã Overview

India Kent is coffee's first successful rust-resistant variety - developed in India 1920s, later introduced to Tanzania where it became significant. Kent represents early coffee breeding success: combining resistance with acceptable quality decades before modern genetics.

**Characteristics**:
- **Historical**: First rust-resistant variety (1920s)
- **Indian Origin**: Bred in India, exported to East Africa
- **Tanzania**: Became important in Tanzania
- **Balanced**: Chocolate-spice, clean profiles
- **Moderate Quality**: Better than Catimor, not Bourbon-level
- **Heritage**: Important coffee breeding history

**Development**:
- **1920s**: Developed in India (Kent Coffee Research Station)
- **Rust Epidemic**: Response to coffee rust (pre-Timor Hybrid era)
- **Success**: Achieved resistance + acceptable quality
- **Tanzania**: Introduced 1920s, became significant
- **Present**: Historical variety, some production continues
