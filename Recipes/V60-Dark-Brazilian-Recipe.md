## ğŸ“ Step-by-Step Instructions

### Detailed Steps

1. **Preparation** (0:00)
   - Boil water then cool to 88Â°C (critical - avoid high temps)
   - Weigh 18g coffee beans
   - Grind to medium-coarse (28 clicks Comandante)
   - Rinse V60 filter with hot water
   - Preheat V60 and server (but not too hot)
   - Tare scale with V60 and coffee

2. **Bloom** (0:00 - 0:40)
   - Start timer
   - Add 50g water (2.8x dose) in circular motion
   - Ensure all grounds saturated (darker roasts bloom less)
   - Gentle swirl to ensure even saturation
   - Wait until 0:40 (shorter bloom for dark roasts)

3. **First Pour** (0:40 - 1:10)
   - Pour to 150g total (100g water added)
   - Slow, controlled spiral from center outward
   - Maintain steady pour height (5-10cm)
   - Complete pour by 1:10

4. **Second Pour** (1:10 - 1:40)
   - Pour to 250g total (100g water added)
   - Same spiral technique
   - Complete pour by 1:40

5. **Final Pour** (1:40 - 2:10)
   - Pour to 288g total (38g water added)
   - Center pour to maintain flat bed
   - Complete pour by 2:10

6. **Drawdown** (2:10 - 2:30)
   - Allow complete drawdown
   - Target completion: 2:30
   - Bed should be flat
   - Dark roasts drain faster due to lower density

7. **Finish** (2:30)
   - Remove V60
   - Gentle swirl of brewed coffee
   - Serve immediately

### Technique Notes

**Pour Technique**: Gentle, controlled spiral maintaining even flow
**Agitation Method**: Minimal swirl after bloom, very gentle during pours
**Pour Velocity**: Moderate and steady (aim for ~8-10g/second)
**Target Bed Appearance**: Flat, even bed at finish

## ğŸ’¡ Tips & Troubleshooting

**Tips**:

**Adjustment Guide**:
- **Too Bitter**: Lower temperature, coarser grind, shorter brew
- **Too Weak**: Increase dose, grind slightly finer, increase temperature to 90Â°C

- **Astringent**: Lower temperature significantly (86Â°C)

- **Too Thin**: Increase dose or grind finer
- **Oily Residue**: Normal for dark roasts, use fresh filters

This V60 recipe is specifically designed for dark roast Brazilian coffees, which require lower temperatures and coarser grinds to avoid over-extraction and bitterness. The key is brewing at 88Â°C or below.
