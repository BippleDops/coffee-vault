## ğŸ“ Step-by-Step Instructions

### Detailed Steps (4:6 Method Adaptation)

1. **Preparation** (0:00)
   - Boil water to 97Â°C (will drop to 95Â°C)
   - Weigh 20g coffee beans
   - Grind to medium-fine (21 clicks Comandante)
   - Rinse V60 filter thoroughly with hot water
   - Preheat V60 and server
   - Tare scale with V60 and coffee

2. **Bloom - First Pour** (0:00 - 0:45)
   - Start timer
   - Add 50g water (2.5x dose) in circular motion
   - Ensure all grounds saturated
   - Gentle swirl for even saturation
   - Natural process creates more gas - expect vigorous bloom
   - Wait until 0:45

3. **Second Pour** (0:45 - 1:30)
   - Pour to 120g total (70g water added)
   - Slow spiral pour from center outward
   - Control sweetness/acidity balance
   - Wait until 1:30

4. **Third Pour** (1:30 - 2:10)
   - Pour to 200g total (80g water added)
   - Same spiral technique
   - Complete pour by 2:10
   - Wait for drawdown

5. **Fourth Pour** (2:10 - 2:50)
   - Pour to 260g total (60g water added)
   - Maintain spiral pattern
   - Complete pour by 2:50

6. **Final Pour** (2:50 - 3:20)
   - Pour to 300g total (40g water added)
   - Center pour to maintain flat bed
   - Complete pour by 3:20

7. **Drawdown** (3:20 - 3:30)
   - Allow complete drawdown
   - Target completion: 3:30
   - Bed should be flat
   - Natural process may take slightly longer

8. **Finish** (3:30)
   - Remove V60
   - Gentle swirl of brewed coffee
   - Serve immediately

### Technique Notes

**Pour Technique**: Slow, deliberate spiral maintaining even flow
**Agitation Method**: Gentle swirl after bloom, minimal during pours
**Pour Velocity**: Slow and steady (aim for ~7-9g/second)
**Target Bed Appearance**: Flat, even bed with some fines visible (normal for naturals)

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Muddy Cup**: Ensure thorough filter rinse, check grind consistency
- **Fermented Taste**: May be bean quality issue, try lower temp
- **Thin Body**: Grind finer, increase temperature
- **Muted Flavors**: Increase temperature significantly (96Â°C)

## ğŸ“ Notes

This V60 recipe is specifically designed for natural process Ethiopian coffees, which are known for explosive fruit flavors, berry notes, and wine-like complexity. Natural process requires higher temperatures and specific techniques to fully extract the fruit character.

Natural processed coffees are dried with the cherry still on the bean, creating intense fruit fermentation flavors. This recipe uses the 4:6 method adaptation to control sweetness while maximizing fruit extraction.

Works exceptionally well with:
- Ethiopian natural Guji (best match - blueberry bomb)
- Ethiopian natural Sidamo (excellent - strawberry, tropical fruit)
- Ethiopian natural Yirgacheffe (excellent - floral, berry)
- Ethiopian natural Harrar (very good - wine-like, complex)

**Key Insight**: Natural Ethiopians are polarizing. Some people love the intense fruit bomb character, others find it too much. This recipe balances intensity with clarity.

**Iced Coffee Note**: Natural Ethiopian iced coffee is phenomenal - use 1:13 ratio and brew directly over ice for incredible summer drinks.
