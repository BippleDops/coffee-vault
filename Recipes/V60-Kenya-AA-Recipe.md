6. **Drawdown** (2:15 - 2:45)
   - Allow complete drawdown
   - Target completion: 2:45

## ğŸ“ Notes

This V60 recipe is specifically designed for Kenyan AA washed coffees, which are known for their bright acidity, black currant notes, and unique savory-sweet tomato-like character. Kenya's double fermentation washing process creates exceptional clarity and complexity.

Kenyan coffees are among the world's most distinctive and complex. The SL28 and SL34 varieties combined with double fermentation washing create a flavor profile unlike any other origin.

Works exceptionally well with:
- Kenya AA Nyeri (best match - black currant, tomato)
- Kenya AA Kirinyaga (excellent - citrus, wine-like)
- Kenya AA Kiambu (excellent - complex, bright)
- Kenya AB (very good - similar profile, slightly smaller beans)

**Key Insights**:
- High temperature (95-96Â°C) is critical for full extraction
- Minimal agitation preserves characteristic acidity
- Allow coffee to cool - complexity develops significantly
- Black currant and tomato notes are positive characteristics
- Freshness is critical - Kenya loses character after 3-4 weeks

**Water Quality**: Kenyan coffees are particularly sensitive to water quality. Use filtered water with moderate mineral content (50-150 TDS). Too soft water will create flat, lifeless cups. Too hard water will increase astringency.
