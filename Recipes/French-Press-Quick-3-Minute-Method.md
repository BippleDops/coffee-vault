## üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

### Quick Steps (3-Minute Method)

1. **Preparation** (0:00)
   - Boil water (let cool 30 sec if no temp control)
   - Weigh 30g coffee OR 4 tablespoons
   - Grind to coarse
   - Optional: Preheat press quickly with hot water
   - Add ground coffee to press
   - Start timer (or note time)

2. **Add Water** (0:00)
   - Add all water at once
   - Pour gently but don't overthink it
   - Fill to top of French Press
   - Don't stir yet

3. **Steep** (0:00 - 3:00)
   - Place lid on press (plunger up)
   - Set timer for 3 minutes
   - Walk away - do other morning tasks
   - Crust forms on top (normal)

4. **Stir and Press** (3:00)
   - At 3:00, remove lid
   - Stir gently 5-10 times
   - Crust breaks and sinks
   - Replace lid immediately

5. **Press Gently** (3:00)
   - Press plunger gently to below surface
   - Don't press all the way down
   - Takes 10 seconds
   - Some grounds will remain suspended (okay)

6. **Pour and Serve** (3:00)
   - Pour immediately into mug
   - Don't let sit on grounds (continues extracting)
   - Some sediment is normal
   - Enjoy your quick, full-bodied coffee

### Technique Notes

**Speed vs Quality**: This method sacrifices ~10-15% quality for 50%+ time savings
**Key Simplifications**: No foam removal, shorter steep, quick press
**Still Good**: Produces rich, full-bodied coffee suitable for daily drinking
**Sediment**: Expect more sediment than Hoffmann method (acceptable tradeoff)

## üí° Tips & Troubleshooting

**Tips**:
- **Speed is the point** - don't overthink technique
- Coarse grind is critical - prevents sediment
- Can skip preheat if rushed (minor quality loss)
- "Just off boil" water works fine if no thermometer
- Pour immediately - don't let sit on grounds
- Some sediment is normal and acceptable
- Perfect for weekday mornings
- Consistent measurements = consistent results
- Make it part of your routine (shower while steeping)
- 85-90% as good as 8-minute method in 1/3 the time

**Common Mistakes**:
- Grinding too fine - creates muddy, bitter coffee
- Letting sit on grounds - continues extracting, gets bitter
- Pressing hard to bottom - increases sediment
- Overthinking it - this is meant to be simple

**Adjustment Guide**:
- **Too Bitter**: Reduce steep to 2:30, lower temp, or coarser grind
- **Too Weak**: Increase dose to 35g or steep to 3:30
- **Too Much Sediment**: Grind coarser, don't press to bottom
- **Inconsistent**: Measure coffee more carefully

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Bitter Every Time**: Water too hot or steep too long - reduce both
- **Weak Every Time**: Not enough coffee - increase to 35g
- **Gritty/Muddy**: Grind too fine - use coarser setting
- **Can't Repeat Results**: Measure coffee by weight, not volume
