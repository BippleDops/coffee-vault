## üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

### Detailed Steps

1. **Preparation** (0:00)
   - Boil water to 95¬∞C (will drop to 93¬∞C)
   - Weigh 20g coffee beans
   - Grind to medium (24 clicks Comandante)
   - Rinse V60 filter with hot water
   - Preheat V60 and server
   - Tare scale with V60 and coffee

2. **Bloom** (0:00 - 0:45)
   - Start timer
   - Add 60g water (3x dose) in circular motion
   - Ensure all grounds saturated
   - Gentle swirl to ensure even saturation
   - Wait until 0:45

3. **First Pour** (0:45 - 1:20)
   - Pour to 160g total (100g water added)
   - Slow, controlled spiral from center outward
   - Maintain steady pour height (5-10cm)
   - Complete pour by 1:20

4. **Second Pour** (1:20 - 1:55)
   - Pour to 260g total (100g water added)
   - Same spiral technique
   - Complete pour by 1:55

5. **Final Pour** (1:55 - 2:30)
   - Pour to 320g total (60g water added)
   - Center pour to maintain flat bed
   - Complete pour by 2:30

6. **Drawdown** (2:30 - 3:00)
   - Allow complete drawdown
   - Target completion: 3:00
   - Bed should be flat with minimal residue on walls
   - If draining too fast/slow, adjust grind

7. **Finish** (3:00)
   - Remove V60
   - Gentle swirl of brewed coffee
   - Serve immediately

### Technique Notes

**Pour Technique**: Steady, controlled spiral maintaining even flow
**Agitation Method**: Gentle swirl after bloom, minimal during pours
**Pour Velocity**: Moderate flow (aim for ~9-11g/second)
**Target Bed Appearance**: Flat, even bed at finish

## üí° Tips & Troubleshooting

**Tips**:
- Preheat everything thoroughly for temperature stability
- Use filtered water with 50-150 TDS for best results
- Grind immediately before brewing for maximum freshness
- Pour slowly and steadily for even extraction
- Generous bloom (3x dose) enhances sweetness
- Aim for flat bed at finish - indicates even extraction
- Wait 2-3 minutes before drinking for optimal flavor

**Common Mistakes**:
- Pouring too aggressively - disturbs coffee bed
- Skipping preheat - temperature drops during brew
- Inconsistent pour rate - creates uneven extraction
- Under-blooming - reduces sweetness
- Using water too hot (>95¬∞C) - extracts bitterness
- Grinding too coarse - leads to underextraction

**Adjustment Guide**:
- **Too Weak/Thin**: Grind finer, increase temperature, extend brew time
- **Too Bitter**: Grind coarser, decrease temperature, shorten brew time
- **Lacks Sweetness**: Increase bloom water, reduce agitation
- **Lacks Body**: Decrease ratio, increase dose
- **Uneven Extraction**: Improve pour technique, check grind distribution

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Channeling**: Improve grind distribution, gentle bloom agitation
- **Bitterness**: Lower temperature, coarser grind
- **Sour/Thin**: Increase temperature, finer grind
- **Muddy Cup**: Grind coarser, reduce agitation
