## üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

### Detailed Steps (Inverted Method)

1. **Preparation** (0:00)
   - Boil water, cool to 93¬∞C
   - Weigh 18g coffee
   - Grind to medium-fine
   - **Invert AeroPress**: Place plunger in chamber to mark 4, flip upside down
   - Ensure stable surface
   - Add ground coffee to inverted chamber
   - Tare scale

2. **Add Water - Phase 1** (0:00 - 0:10)
   - Start timer
   - Pour 200g water
   - Pour steadily to saturate all grounds
   - Fill chamber nearly to top
   - Complete by 0:10

3. **Stir** (0:10 - 0:20)
   - Stir gently 15 times in circular motion
   - Ensure complete saturation
   - Break up any clumps
   - Spoon off excess foam if desired
   - Complete by 0:20

4. **Steep** (0:20 - 2:00)
   - Let coffee steep undisturbed
   - Full immersion extraction
   - No drip-through (that's the point of inverted)
   - This is where magic happens
   - Can be extended to 2:30 for very light roasts

5. **Prepare to Flip** (2:00)
   - Rinse filter cap with hot water
   - Screw filter cap on firmly
   - Place mug on top of AeroPress (upside down)
   - Double-check everything is secure

6. **Flip** (2:00)
   - **Carefully** flip entire assembly
   - Hold AeroPress and mug together firmly
   - Flip quickly but controlled
   - Place on stable surface
   - Don't panic - it's easier than it looks

7. **Press** (2:00 - 2:30)
   - Begin pressing gently and steadily
   - Take ~30 seconds to complete press
   - Stop when you hear hissing
   - Don't press final air through

8. **Finish** (2:30)
   - Remove AeroPress carefully (can drip)
   - Discard puck
   - Swirl coffee in mug
   - Serve immediately

### Technique Notes

**Inversion Technique**: Chamber inverted, sealed by plunger
**Pour Technique**: Steady pour to fill chamber
**Agitation Method**: 15 gentle stirs, more than standard method
**Flipping Technique**: Controlled but confident flip
**Press Technique**: Steady, even pressure
**Safety**: Ensure stable surface, careful when flipping

## üí° Tips & Troubleshooting

**Tips**:
- **Flipping confidence**: Practice with just water first
- Don't overfill chamber - leave 1-2cm space at top
- Ensure filter cap screwed on tightly before flip
- Flip over stable surface in case of spills
- Hold mug and AeroPress together firmly when flipping
- Flip quickly but controlled - don't hesitate
- More agitation (15 stirs) compensates for chamber shape
- Longer steep time = fuller extraction and body
- Perfect for showcasing complex, light roasts
- Creates noticeably fuller body than standard method

**Common Mistakes**:
- Overfilling chamber - causes spills when flipping
- Filter cap not tight - disaster when flipping
- Hesitating during flip - awkward and risky
- Grinding same as standard - should be slightly finer
- Not stirring enough - grounds settle, uneven extraction
- Pressing too hard at end - bitter, messy
- Flipping over unstable surface - asking for trouble

**Adjustment Guide**:
- **Too Bitter**: Reduce steep time to 1:30, lower temp, or coarser grind
- **Too Weak**: Increase dose, finer grind, or longer steep
- **Over-extracted**: Reduce steep time significantly
- **Under-extracted**: Steep longer, hotter water, finer grind
- **Hard to Press**: Grind coarser, reduce dose slightly
- **Spills When Flipping**: Less water, tighter cap, more confidence

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Spills During Flip**: Overfilled or cap not secure - reduce water level
- **Over-extracted**: Too long steep or too hot - reduce time/temp
- **Weak Body**: More coffee, finer grind, or longer steep
- **Bitter**: Reduce steep time or lower temperature
- **Messy Flip**: Practice makes perfect - don't hesitate

## üìù Notes

The inverted method is the enthusiast's choice for AeroPress brewing. It provides complete control over steep time and creates fuller-bodied, more intensely extracted coffee than the standard method. The tradeoff is slightly more complexity and the (small) risk of spills during the flip.

**Inverted vs Standard**:
- **Inverted Advantages**: Full immersion, more control, fuller body, more extraction
- **Standard Advantages**: Safer, simpler, easier cleanup, consistent
- **When to Use Inverted**: Complex light roasts, maximum extraction desired, you're comfortable with technique
- **When to Use Standard**: Everyday brewing, travel, dark roasts, simplicity preferred

**The Flip**:
The flip is intimidating at first but becomes second nature with practice. Key points:
1. Ensure cap is tight
2. Don't overfill
3. Hold mug and AeroPress together
4. Flip quickly but controlled
5. Practice with water first if nervous

**Best Use Cases**:
- Showcasing complex, light roast coffees
- Ethiopian, Kenyan coffees (captures full complexity)
- When you want fuller body than standard method
- Weekend brewing when you have time
- When you want maximum control over extraction

**Enthusiast Perspective**:
Many AeroPress enthusiasts prefer inverted method exclusively. The fuller body and complete control over steep time make it worth the slight extra complexity. Once you master the flip, it's not significantly harder than standard method.

**Competition Note**: AeroPress World Championship winners use both standard and inverted methods. There's no "better" method - just different approaches. Choose based on your priorities and comfort level.
