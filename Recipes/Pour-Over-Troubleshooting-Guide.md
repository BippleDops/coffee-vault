## ‚òï TASTE PROBLEMS

### Problem: Coffee Tastes Bitter

**Symptoms**:
- Lingering harsh, unpleasant taste
- Dry, astringent mouthfeel
- Burnt or charred flavors
- Unpleasant aftertaste

**Root Causes**:
1. Over-extraction (extracted too much from beans)
2. Water temperature too high
3. Grind too fine
4. Brew time too long
5. Too much agitation

**Solutions** (in order of impact):

1. **Reduce Water Temperature** (-2-5¬∞C)
   - Try 91¬∞C instead of 94¬∞C
   - Biggest impact for bitterness

2. **Grind Coarser** (2-3 clicks)
   - Reduces extraction rate
   - Shortens brew time naturally

3. **Reduce Brew Time**
   - Pour faster
   - Less pours (reduce from 5 to 3)

4. **Less Agitation**
   - Gentler stirring
   - No swirl after bloom

5. **Check Coffee Age**
   - Very stale coffee can taste bitter
   - Fresh beans (1-4 weeks off roast) better

**Quick Fix**: Lower temperature to 90¬∞C, grind 2 clicks coarser. Test.

---

### Problem: Coffee Tastes Sour/Acidic

**Symptoms**:
- Sharp, unpleasant acidity
- Makes you pucker
- Tastes incomplete/unfinished
- Watery body

**Root Causes**:
1. Under-extraction (didn't extract enough)
2. Water temperature too low
3. Grind too coarse
4. Brew time too short
5. Insufficient contact time

**Solutions** (in order of impact):

1. **Increase Water Temperature** (+2-5¬∞C)
   - Try 95-96¬∞C instead of 93¬∞C
   - Biggest impact for sourness

2. **Grind Finer** (2-3 clicks)
   - Increases extraction rate
   - Naturally extends brew time

3. **Extend Brew Time**
   - Pour slower
   - More pours for controlled extraction

4. **More Agitation**
   - Stir after bloom
   - Gentle swirl mid-brew

5. **Check Freshness**
   - Too-fresh coffee (1-3 days off roast) can be sour
   - Let rest 5-7 days

**Quick Fix**: Raise temperature to 95¬∞C, grind 2 clicks finer. Test.

**Note**: Light roasts naturally have more acidity. Distinguish between pleasant brightness and unpleasant sourness.

---

### Problem: Coffee Tastes Weak/Watery

**Symptoms**:
- Thin body
- Lacks intensity
- Can see through coffee
- Tastes diluted

**Root Causes**:
1. Not enough coffee (too low dose)
2. Ratio too high (too much water)
3. Under-extraction
4. Grind too coarse

**Solutions**:

1. **Increase Coffee Dose** (+2-3g)
   - From 20g to 22-23g
   - Most direct solution

2. **Reduce Water Amount**
   - Change ratio from 1:17 to 1:15
   - Reduces dilution

3. **Grind Finer**
   - Increases extraction
   - More strength

4. **Check Brew Method**
   - Ensure full immersion during bloom
   - All grounds saturated

**Quick Fix**: Add 2-3g more coffee, grind 1 click finer. Test.

---

### Problem: Coffee Tastes Flat/Muddy/Dull

**Symptoms**:
- Lacks clarity
- Tastes muted
- No distinct flavors
- Heavy, unclear body

**Root Causes**:
1. Coffee is stale (>6 weeks off roast)
2. Excessive fines in grind
3. Over-agitation
4. Water quality issues
5. Dirty equipment

**Solutions**:

1. **Check Coffee Freshness**
   - Use beans 1-4 weeks off roast
   - Stale coffee can't be fixed with technique

2. **Reduce Agitation**
   - Less stirring
   - Gentler pours
   - No excessive swirling

3. **Improve Grinder**
   - Blade grinders produce excessive fines
   - Invest in burr grinder

4. **Water Quality**
   - Use filtered water
   - Target 50-150 TDS
   - Too soft water tastes flat

5. **Clean Equipment**
   - Rinse brewer and filters
   - Clean grinder regularly
   - Old coffee oils cause flat taste

**Quick Fix**: Use fresh beans, reduce agitation, use filtered water.

---

### Problem: Coffee Has Off-Flavors (papery, metallic, stale)

**Symptoms**:
- Tastes like paper/cardboard
- Metallic taste
- Musty or stale flavors
- Unpleasant chemical taste

**Root Causes**:
1. Filter not rinsed (papery)
2. Poor water quality (metallic)
3. Stale coffee (musty)
4. Dirty equipment (old coffee oils)
5. Coffee packaging/storage issue

**Solutions**:

1. **Rinse Filter Thoroughly**
   - Use 200-300ml hot water
   - Rinse until no paper taste in rinse water
   - This eliminates papery taste

2. **Use Filtered Water**
   - Removes chlorine and metals
   - Dramatically improves taste

3. **Check Coffee Freshness**
   - Roast date should be 1-6 weeks ago
   - Store in airtight container
   - Don't freeze (contrary to popular belief)

4. **Clean Equipment**
   - Wash brewer, carafe, grinder
   - Remove old coffee oils
   - Monthly deep clean

5. **Check Storage**
   - Airtight container
   - Cool, dark place
   - Don't refrigerate (moisture issues)

**Quick Fix**: Rinse filter thoroughly, use filtered water, check coffee roast date.

## ‚è±Ô∏è PHYSICAL/TIMING PROBLEMS

### Problem: Coffee Drains Too Fast (<2:00 for V60)

**Symptoms**:
- Brew finishes before target time
- Water flows through quickly
- Often correlates with weak, sour coffee

**Root Causes**:
1. Grind too coarse
2. Not enough coffee (thinner bed)
3. Channeling (water finding paths)
4. Pouring too aggressively

**Solutions**:

1. **Grind Finer** (2-4 clicks)
   - Most direct solution
   - Increases resistance, slows flow

2. **Increase Dose**
   - Thicker bed = longer contact time
   - Try +2-3g

3. **Pour More Gently**
   - Slower pour rate
   - Avoid disturbing bed

4. **Check for Channeling**
   - See channeling section below
   - Ensure even distribution

**Quick Fix**: Grind 3 clicks finer. Test.

---

### Problem: Coffee Drains Too Slow (>3:30 for V60)

**Symptoms**:
- Brew takes forever to finish
- Stalls during drawdown
- Often correlates with bitter, over-extracted coffee

**Root Causes**:
1. Grind too fine
2. Too much coffee (very thick bed)
3. Filter clogged with fines
4. Excessive agitation creating compacted bed

**Solutions**:

1. **Grind Coarser** (2-4 clicks)
   - Most direct solution
   - Reduces resistance

2. **Reduce Dose (if excessive)**
   - Standard dose: 15-22g for V60
   - More than 25g can slow significantly

3. **Less Agitation**
   - Stirring compacts bed
   - Reduce or eliminate stirring

4. **Check Grinder**
   - Excessive fines clog filter
   - Upgrade to quality burr grinder if needed

**Quick Fix**: Grind 3 clicks coarser. Test.

---

### Problem: Channeling (water finding paths, uneven extraction)

**Symptoms**:
- Can see water running down sides
- Some areas of bed look dry
- Inconsistent extraction
- Uneven coffee bed at finish
- Holes or gaps in coffee bed

**Root Causes**:
1. Uneven coffee distribution before brewing
2. Pouring on filter walls (not coffee)
3. Aggressive pouring disturbs bed
4. Coffee not fully saturated during bloom

**Solutions**:

1. **Level Coffee Bed**
   - Tap/shake brewer before adding water
   - Ensure even distribution
   - Critical step often skipped

2. **Don't Pour on Walls**
   - Keep spiral within coffee bed
   - Aim for center and work outward
   - Stop before reaching filter walls

3. **Gentle, Controlled Pours**
   - Slow pour rate
   - Steady flow
   - Don't pour from high height

4. **Ensure Full Bloom Saturation**
   - Use enough bloom water (2.5-3x dose)
   - Gentle stir or swirl
   - Wait full bloom time

5. **WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique)**
   - Use thin tool to stir grounds before brewing
   - Breaks up clumps
   - Ensures even distribution

**Quick Fix**: Level coffee bed before brewing, ensure full bloom saturation, avoid pouring on walls.

## üîÑ CONSISTENCY PROBLEMS

### Problem: Every Brew Tastes Different

**Symptoms**:
- Good some days, bad others
- Can't predict results
- Frustrating inconsistency

**Root Causes**:
1. Inconsistent measurements (coffee, water, time)
2. Temperature variations
3. Grind inconsistency
4. Technique variations
5. Variable coffee freshness

**Solutions**:

1. **Measure Everything**
   - Weigh coffee to 0.1g precision
   - Weigh water
   - Time brew consistently
   - Measurements eliminate variables

2. **Control Temperature**
   - Use kettle with temp control
   - Consistent temperature = consistent extraction
   - ¬±2¬∞C affects taste significantly

3. **Consistent Grind**
   - Same setting every time
   - Quality burr grinder
   - Clean grinder regularly

4. **Document Recipe**
   - Write down: dose, water, ratio, grind setting, temp, time
   - Use same recipe every time
   - Only change ONE variable at a time

5. **Fresh Coffee**
   - Buy in smaller quantities
   - Use within 2-4 weeks of roast
   - Freshness affects taste significantly

**Quick Fix**: Start measuring everything with scale and timer. Document exact recipe.

## üõ†Ô∏è EQUIPMENT PROBLEMS

### Problem: Filter Clogs/Stalls

**Symptoms**:
- Brew suddenly slows or stops
- Water doesn't drain
- Coffee backs up

**Root Causes**:
1. Grind too fine (excessive fines)
2. Old/bad grinder producing fines
3. Wrong filter size/type
4. Grounds compacted from agitation

**Solutions**:

1. **Grind Coarser**
   - Immediate solution
   - Reduces fines

2. **Improve Grinder**
   - Blade grinders produce excessive fines
   - Burr grinder worth investment

3. **Check Filter**
   - Right size for brewer?
   - Genuine filters vs cheap alternatives
   - Quality matters

4. **Less Agitation**
   - Stirring can compact bed
   - Reduce stirring

**Quick Fix**: Grind coarser, check filter type.

---

### Problem: Water Bypassing Coffee (flows around sides)

**Symptoms**:
- Water visible running down filter walls
- Coffee in center looks dry
- Weak, underextracted coffee

**Root Causes**:
1. Pouring on filter walls (not coffee)
2. Filter not properly seated
3. Coffee not distributed evenly

**Solutions**:

1. **Pour Only on Coffee**
   - Spiral pour within coffee bed boundaries
   - Don't aim for filter walls
   - Start center, work outward, stop before walls

2. **Check Filter Seating**
   - Filter should sit flush against brewer
   - Some filters need shaping
   - Rinse helps filter stick

3. **Distribute Coffee Evenly**
   - Shake/tap brewer before bloom
   - Level bed
   - No piles or valleys

**Quick Fix**: Pour only on coffee (not filter walls), ensure filter is properly seated.

## üí° PREVENTION TIPS

**Prevent Problems Before They Start**:

1. **Invest in Quality Tools**
   - Burr grinder (single biggest impact)
   - Scale (0.1g precision)
   - Temperature-controlled kettle
   - Good beans from reputable roaster

2. **Develop Consistent Routine**
   - Same recipe every time
   - Same technique
   - Document what works
   - Change ONE variable at a time when adjusting

3. **Use Fresh, Quality Beans**
   - Buy from roasters with roast dates
   - Use within 2-4 weeks
   - Store properly (airtight, cool, dark)

4. **Maintain Equipment**
   - Clean grinder monthly
   - Rinse brewers after use
   - Replace filters when needed
   - Descale kettle periodically

5. **Understand Extraction**
   - Temperature: Higher = more extraction
   - Grind: Finer = more extraction
   - Time: Longer = more extraction
   - Agitation: More = more extraction
   - Adjust these variables to fix problems

## üéì EXTRACTION FUNDAMENTALS

**Understanding Why Problems Happen**:

**Extraction** = pulling solubles from coffee into water

**Under-Extraction** (<18% extraction):
- Tastes sour, weak, incomplete
- Didn't extract enough from beans
- Fix: Increase extraction (hotter, finer, longer, more agitation)

**Ideal Extraction** (19-22% extraction):
- Balanced, sweet, complex
- Got the good stuff from beans
- Goal: Stay in this range

**Over-Extraction** (>23% extraction):
- Tastes bitter, harsh, astringent
- Extracted too much (including bad stuff)
- Fix: Decrease extraction (cooler, coarser, shorter, less agitation)

**The Variables**:
1. **Temperature**: Higher = more extraction
2. **Grind**: Finer = more extraction
3. **Time**: Longer = more extraction
4. **Agitation**: More = more extraction
5. **Dose**: More coffee = potentially more extraction

**Fixing Taste Issues**:
- Bitter = over-extracted ‚Üí reduce extraction
- Sour = under-extracted ‚Üí increase extraction
- Adjust variables accordingly

## üìù Notes

Pour-over troubleshooting is systematic problem-solving. Most issues trace to extraction level (under or over). Understanding extraction principles lets you diagnose and fix problems logically rather than randomly adjusting variables.

**The Iterative Process**:
1. Identify problem (taste/timing/physical)
2. Diagnose root cause (under/over-extraction, technique, equipment)
3. Implement solution (change ONE variable)
4. Test results (brew again)
5. Iterate (adjust based on results)

**Most Common Issues** (in order):
1. **Inconsistent measurements** - Buy a scale!
2. **Wrong grind size** - Most common taste issue
3. **Wrong temperature** - Second most common taste issue
4. **Stale coffee** - Fresh beans matter more than technique
5. **Poor water quality** - Filtered water is essential

**The 80/20 Rule**:
80% of pour-over problems are solved by:
1. Weighing coffee and water
2. Controlling temperature
3. Adjusting grind size appropriately
4. Using fresh beans (1-4 weeks off roast)
5. Using filtered water

Master these five things before worrying about advanced technique.

**Remember**: Even experts don't nail every brew. Pour-over has variables. Goal is consistency, not perfection. If 8/10 brews are good, you're doing great.
