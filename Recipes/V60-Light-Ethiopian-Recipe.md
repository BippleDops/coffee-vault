## üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

### Detailed Steps

1. **Preparation** (0:00)
   - Boil water to 96¬∞C (will drop to 94¬∞C)
   - Weigh 18g coffee beans
   - Grind to medium-fine (22 clicks Comandante)
   - Rinse V60 filter with hot water
   - Preheat V60 and server
   - Tare scale with V60 and coffee

2. **Bloom** (0:00 - 0:45)
   - Start timer
   - Add 50g water (2.5x dose) in circular motion
   - Ensure all grounds saturated
   - Gentle swirl to ensure even saturation
   - Wait until 0:45

3. **First Pour** (0:45 - 1:15)
   - Pour to 150g total (100g water added)
   - Slow, controlled spiral from center outward
   - Maintain steady pour height (5-10cm)
   - Complete pour by 1:15

4. **Second Pour** (1:15 - 1:45)
   - Pour to 250g total (100g water added)
   - Same spiral technique
   - Complete pour by 1:45

5. **Final Pour** (1:45 - 2:15)
   - Pour to 300g total (50g water added)
   - Center pour to maintain flat bed
   - Complete pour by 2:15

6. **Drawdown** (2:15 - 2:45)
   - Allow complete drawdown
   - Target completion: 2:45
   - Bed should be flat with minimal residue on walls
   - If draining too fast/slow, adjust grind

7. **Finish** (2:45)
   - Remove V60
   - Gentle swirl of brewed coffee
   - Serve immediately

### Technique Notes

**Pour Technique**: Slow, controlled spiral pour maintaining steady flow  
**Agitation Method**: Gentle swirl after bloom, minimal agitation during pours  
**Pour Velocity**: Moderate and steady (aim for ~8-10g/second)  
**Target Bed Appearance**: Flat, even bed at finish with minimal high-and-dry

## üí° Tips & Troubleshooting

**Tips**:
- Preheat everything thoroughly - temperature stability is critical
- Use filtered water with 50-150 TDS for best results
- Grind immediately before brewing for maximum freshness
- Pour slowly and steadily - patience yields clarity
- Gentle bloom swirl ensures even saturation
- Aim for flat bed at finish - indicates even extraction
- Wait 3-5 minutes before drinking for flavors to develop

**Common Mistakes**:
- Pouring too aggressively - disturbs coffee bed
- Skipping preheat - temperature drops during brew
- Inconsistent pour rate - creates uneven extraction
- Over-agitating - can lead to muddy cup
- Using water too hot (>96¬∞C) - can extract astringency
- Grinding too fine - creates slow drawdown and over-extraction

**Adjustment Guide**:
- **Too Sour/Weak**: Grind finer, increase temperature, extend brew time
- **Too Bitter/Harsh**: Grind coarser, decrease temperature, shorten brew time
- **Lacks Clarity**: Reduce agitation, increase ratio slightly
- **Lacks Body**: Decrease ratio, increase dose
- **Uneven Extraction**: Improve pour technique, check grind distribution

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Channeling**: Improve grind distribution, gentle bloom agitation
- **Astringency**: Lower temperature, coarser grind
- **Flat Flavors**: Increase temperature, ensure fresh beans
- **Muddy Cup**: Grind coarser, reduce agitation
