## üìã Recipe Overview

**Description**: The quintessential Chemex recipe designed for medium roast Central American coffees. This method produces an exceptionally clean, balanced cup with clarity and sweetness, showcasing why Chemex has been the gold standard for filter coffee since 1941. Perfect for daily brewing and sharing with guests.

**Recipe Philosophy**: Chemex's thick bonded filters and hourglass design naturally create ultra-clean cups. This recipe leverages those characteristics while ensuring proper extraction of medium roast coffees through optimal ratio (1:17), appropriate temperature, and controlled pour timing.

**Recipe Source**: Based on traditional Chemex brewing method with modern precision
**Derived From**: Chemex company recommendations, third-wave refinements
**Based On**: Peter Schlumbohm's original design principles (1941)

**Best For**:
- Daily morning coffee ritual
- Serving 2-3 people simultaneously
- Showcasing clean, balanced Central American coffees
- Those who value clarity and clean finish
- Beginners learning pour over technique
- Offices and shared brewing environments

**Not Ideal For**:
- Single-serving brewing (better to use V60 or AeroPress)
- Dark roasts (too clean, misses body)
- Ultra-light floral coffees (may over-filter delicate notes)
- Those preferring heavy-bodied coffee

## üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

### Preparation (3-4 minutes before brewing)

**Step 1: Water Heating**
- Fill gooseneck kettle with 800-900g filtered water (more than needed)
- Set temperature to 95¬∞C (will drop to 93¬∞C when ready)
- Turn on and wait for target temperature
- Kettle should hold temperature

**Step 2: Coffee Weighing**
- Place container on scale, tare
- Weigh 42g whole coffee beans
- Verify accuracy (¬±0.5g)
- Use fresh beans (7-28 days off roast)

**Step 3: Grinding**
- Clean grinder of any old coffee
- Set grind to medium-coarse (26-28 clicks Comandante)
- Grind beans immediately before brewing
- Check consistency: uniform particle size
- Tap grinder to release all grounds

**Step 4: Filter Preparation**
- Open Chemex bonded filter (square style)
- Fold filter into cone: fold in half, then quarters
- Open into cone with three layers on one side
- Place in Chemex with three-layer side facing spout
- Ensure filter sits flat against walls, fully covering

**Step 5: Rinse and Preheat**
- Pour 200-300g hot water through filter
- This rinses paper taste and preheats Chemex
- Swirl water to preheat entire glass vessel
- Lift filter slightly, discard rinse water through spout
- Ensure Chemex is warm to touch

**Step 6: Final Setup**
- Place preheated Chemex on scale
- Ensure filter sits properly (three layers on spout side)
- Tare scale to zero
- Add 42g ground coffee to filter
- Gently shake Chemex to level coffee bed
- Create small well in center with finger (helps blooming)
- Prepare timer
- Verify kettle at 93¬∞C (¬±1¬∞C)

### Brewing Process (4:00-4:30 total)

**Phase 1: Bloom (0:00 - 0:45)**

**0:00 - Start**
- Start timer as first water touches coffee
- Begin pouring in center of coffee bed
- Slow, controlled circular pour
- Spiral outward from center to edges

**0:00 - 0:20 - Bloom Pour**
- Pour to 110g total (2.6x coffee dose)
- Ensure all grounds saturated (no dry spots)
- Stay ~1cm away from filter edges
- Complete pour by 0:20

**0:20 - 0:25 - Gentle Agitation**
- Pick up Chemex, gentle swirl (circular motion)
- OR use spoon to gently fold edges to center
- Ensure complete, even saturation
- Coffee will bloom, bubble, degas

**0:25 - 0:45 - Bloom Rest**
- Set down Chemex, do not disturb
- CO2 releases, creating foam/bubbles
- Fresh coffee (7-14 days) blooms vigorously
- Older coffee (21-28 days) blooms less
- Entire bed should be evenly saturated

**Bloom Chemistry**: Medium roasts have less CO2 than light roasts but still benefit from bloom. CO2 creates hydrophobic barrier; bloom removes it, allowing even extraction.

**Phase 2: First Main Pour (0:45 - 1:30)**

**0:45 - Start First Pour**
- Water should be 93¬∞C
- Begin slow, controlled pour in center
- Steady spiral from center outward
- Pour rate: ~4-5g per second

**Pour Technique**:
- Gooseneck 6-10cm above coffee surface
- Slow, steady, continuous pour
- Concentric circles from center to outer edge
- Stay 1cm from filter wall
- Maintain pencil-width stream
- Avoid disturbing coffee bed aggressively

**0:45 - 1:20 - Active Pouring**
- Pour to 350g total (+240g from bloom)
- Steady, controlled spiral motion
- Coffee bed rises evenly
- Chemex chamber fills partially
- Even turbulence across surface

**1:20 - 1:30 - Complete Pour**
- Finish at 350g by 1:30
- Stop pouring
- Allow water to draw down

**1:30 - 1:45 - Drawdown Pause**
- Water drains through coffee bed
- Level drops toward coffee surface
- This pause allows even extraction
- Bed should remain flat

**Phase 3: Second Main Pour (1:45 - 2:30)**

**1:45 - Start Second Pour**
- Begin second pour at 1:45
- Same technique: center spiral outward
- Maintain ~4-5g per second

**1:45 - 2:20 - Active Pouring**
- Pour to 550g total (+200g from previous)
- Steady spiral motion
- Coffee bed rises again
- Chamber fills more

**2:20 - 2:30 - Complete Pour**
- Finish at 550g by 2:30
- Stop pouring
- Allow brief drawdown

**2:30 - 3:00 - Drawdown Pause**
- Water drains through bed
- Longer pause than first
- Allows controlled extraction
- Bed settling flat

**Phase 4: Final Pour (3:00 - 3:45)**

**3:00 - Start Final Pour**
- Begin third pour at 3:00
- Slightly slower, gentler than previous
- Focus on center, maintain flat bed

**3:00 - 3:35 - Active Pouring**
- Pour to 700g total (+150g from previous)
- Slower, more delicate pour
- Avoid disturbing edges
- Final water addition

**3:35 - 3:45 - Complete Pour**
- Finish at 700g by 3:45
- All water added
- Coffee bed should be flat and even
- No high-and-dry coffee on filter walls

**Phase 5: Final Drawdown (3:45 - 4:30)**

**3:45 - 4:30 - Complete Drainage**
- No more water added
- Water drains through thick bonded filter
- Chemex's thick filter = slower drawdown than V60
- Should be steady, not stalling
- Target completion: 4:15 (¬±15 seconds acceptable)

**Visual Cues**:
- Water level gradually decreasing
- Coffee bed settling flat
- Minimal residue on filter
- Even color across bed
- Clear, clean brewed coffee visible in chamber

**4:15-4:30 - Final Moments**
- Last water dripping through
- Bed should be completely flat
- Minimal coffee on filter walls
- All water drained

**4:30 - Completion**
- Remove filter and spent grounds
- Inspect coffee bed:
  - Flat = excellent extraction
  - Domed/uneven = channeling occurred
  - High-and-dry = poured too hard on walls
- Discard filter and grounds

**Phase 6: Serving**

**Immediately After Brewing**:
- Gentle swirl of Chemex to integrate coffee
- Smell the aroma from spout (chocolate, nuts, fruit)
- Pour into preheated cups
- Serve immediately or place Chemex lid

**Serving Options**:
- Serve directly from Chemex (beautiful presentation)
- Decant into insulated carafe (maintains temperature longer)
- Pour into individual cups (personal service)

**Ideal Drinking Temperature**:
- **0-3 min**: Too hot, aromatics strong, flavors muted
- **3-12 min**: Optimal - full flavor development
- **12-25 min**: Cooling, new flavors emerge
- **25+ min**: Room temp, different but interesting profile

**Tasting Sequence**:
- First sip: Aroma, initial impression, acidity
- Second sip: Sweetness, body, balance
- Third sip: Complexity, finish, aftertaste
- As cools: Flavor evolution, new notes appearing

## üéØ Chemex Technique Mastery

### Understanding Chemex's Unique Characteristics

**Why Chemex is Different**:
- **Thick Bonded Filter**: 20-30% thicker than standard, removes oils and fines
- **Hourglass Shape**: Creates optimal flow rate, controls extraction
- **Glass Construction**: Non-reactive, doesn't affect flavor
- **Air Channel**: Spout-side design allows air escape, prevents vacuum
- **Large Capacity**: Designed for batch brewing (2-4 cups optimal)

**Chemex Advantages**:
- Ultra-clean cup (cleaner than V60, French Press, AeroPress)
- Beautiful presentation (brew and serve in same vessel)
- Consistent results (less technique-dependent than V60)
- Excellent for sharing (batch brewing strength)
- Highlights clarity and brightness

**Chemex Trade-offs**:
- Less body than immersion methods (filters out oils)
- Can over-filter delicate flavors (very light roasts)
- Slower than other methods (4-5 minutes)
- Not ideal for single servings (minimum 2 cups)
- Requires specific bonded filters (not universal)

### Pouring Technique for Chemex

**Chemex Pour Differences from V60**:
- **Slower Pour**: Thicker filter needs slower pour rate (4-5g/sec vs 3-4g/sec for V60)
- **Higher Pour Point**: Can pour from 8-12cm (thicker filter handles it)
- **Less Agitation**: Filter provides plenty of contact time, less agitation needed
- **Longer Pauses**: Allows drawdown between pours
- **Coarser Grind**: Compensates for longer contact time

**The Perfect Chemex Pour**:
- Steady, controlled spiral motion
- Start center, spiral outward
- Return to center, repeat
- Maintain consistent pour rate
- Stay 1cm from filter edge
- Pencil-width water stream
- Height: 6-10cm above coffee surface

**Common Pouring Mistakes**:
- **Pouring Too Fast**: Creates channeling, uneven extraction
- **Pouring on Filter Edge**: Water bypasses coffee
- **Inconsistent Pour Rate**: Some areas over-extract, others under
- **Too Much Agitation**: Can create muddy cup
- **Skipping Bloom**: Poor saturation, uneven extraction

### Filter Folding Mastery

**Square Filter Folding**:
1. Filter is circular when opened
2. Fold exactly in half (semicircle)
3. Fold in half again (quarter circle / cone shape)
4. Open cone: three layers one side, one layer other side
5. Three-layer side faces spout (allows air channel)
6. Smooth filter against Chemex walls

**Half-Moon Pre-Folded Filters**:
- Already shaped, just open into cone
- Three-layer side marked or obvious
- Place three-layer side toward spout
- Easier for beginners

**Filter Rinsing Importance**:
- Removes paper taste (especially natural filters)
- Preheats Chemex glass
- Ensures filter seals against walls
- Use 200-300g hot water
- Discard all rinse water before brewing

### Target Outcomes

- **Sweetness**: Clear, pronounced (caramel, brown sugar, honey)
- **Finish**: Clean, sweet, no bitterness
- **Complexity**: 3-5 distinct flavor notes
- **Balance**: Harmonious, nothing overpowering

**Measurable Metrics**:
- **Total Time**: 4:00-4:45 (target 4:15)
- **TDS**: 1.25-1.40%
- **Extraction**: 19-21%
- **Yield**: ~680g from 700g water (42g recipe)

### Problem-Solving Guide

**Problem: Brew Too Fast (<3:45)**

**Symptoms**:
- Finishes before 3:45
- Weak, watery flavor
- Sour notes
- Thin body
- Underextracted

**Solutions**:
- Grind finer (2-3 clicks)
- Pour more slowly
- Increase coffee dose
- Ensure filter placed correctly
- Check for filter defects/tears

**Problem: Brew Too Slow (>5:00)**

**Symptoms**:
- Takes over 5 minutes
- Bitter, harsh
- Astringent
- Over-extracted
- Muddy

**Solutions**:
- Grind coarser (3-5 clicks)
- Pour faster
- Reduce coffee dose slightly
- Less agitation during bloom
- Check grinder for excessive fines

**Problem: Channeling**

**Symptoms**:
- Fast channels visible
- Uneven coffee bed
- Simultaneously weak and bitter
- Craters or domes in bed

**Solutions**:
- Level coffee bed before brewing (shake Chemex)
- Improve pour technique (stay off walls)
- Reduce bloom agitation
- Ensure filter seated properly
- Check grind distribution

**Problem: Bitter, Harsh, Astringent**

**Symptoms**:
- Bitter aftertaste
- Dry, puckering sensation
- Unpleasant harshness
- Over-extracted

**Solutions**:
- Lower temperature (91-92¬∞C)
- Grind coarser
- Faster pours (less contact time)
- Confirm roast level (may be too dark)
- Fresh coffee essential

**Problem: Sour, Weak, Underdeveloped**

**Symptoms**:
- Sour acidity (not bright)
- Weak strength
- Grassy notes
- Thin body
- Under-extracted

**Solutions**:
- Raise temperature (94-95¬∞C)
- Grind finer
- Slower pours
- Increase dose
- More agitation during bloom

**Problem: Lacks Clarity (Muddy)**

**Symptoms**:
- Cloudy appearance
- Muddy flavors
- Can't distinguish notes
- Sediment in cup

**Solutions**:
- Grind coarser (reduce fines)
- Less agitation
- Gentler pours
- Ensure using Chemex bonded filters (not regular)
- Check grinder quality

**Problem: Flat, Boring**

**Symptoms**:
- One-dimensional
- No complexity
- Dull aroma
- Boring cup

**Solutions**:
- Use fresher coffee (7-14 days off roast)
- Higher quality beans
- Check water quality (50-150 TDS)
- Verify temperature accuracy
- Try different coffee entirely

## üìä Extraction Science

### Medium Roast Characteristics

**What Makes Medium Roast Special**:
- Balanced development (acidity + sweetness + body)
- Caramelization present but not dominant
- Origin character still present
- Less acidic than light roasts
- Less bitter than dark roasts
- Most approachable for general audiences

**Extraction Needs**:
- **Target**: 19-21% extraction
- **Temperature**: 92-94¬∞C (moderate)
- **Grind**: Medium-coarse (balanced)
- **Time**: 4-5 minutes (adequate contact)
- **Ratio**: 1:16-17 (balanced strength)

### Chemex Filter Science

**Why Bonded Filters are Different**:
- 20-30% thicker than standard paper filters
- Three-layer construction (two layers one side, one layer other)
- Removes coffee oils (diterpenes like cafestol, kahweol)
- Removes fine particles (sediment)
- Creates ultra-clean cup

**What Gets Filtered Out**:
- Coffee oils (reduces body, increases clarity)
- Fine particles (prevents muddiness)
- Some aromatic compounds (very volatile)
- Some dissolved solids (lower TDS than immersion)

**What Passes Through**:
- Dissolved solids (flavor compounds)
- Acids (brightness)
- Sugars (sweetness)
- Water-soluble compounds
- Caffeine

**Health Consideration**: Paper filtered coffee removes cafestol and kahweol, which can raise cholesterol. Chemex particularly effective at this.

### Water Chemistry for Chemex

**Optimal Water Profile**:
- **TDS**: 75-150 ppm
- **Hardness**: 60-120 ppm
- **Alkalinity**: 40-80 ppm
- **pH**: 6.5-7.5
- **Calcium**: 30-70 ppm
- **Magnesium**: 10-30 ppm

**Why Water Matters**:
- Water is 98%+ of final cup
- Minerals extract flavor compounds
- Alkalinity buffers acidity
- Too soft: flat, underextracted
- Too hard: harsh, overextracted

## üí° Expert Tips

### Pro Tips from Specialty Coffee Baristas

**Tip 1: The Three-Layer Rule**
- Always place three-layer side toward spout
- Creates air channel, prevents vacuum
- Allows proper flow rate
- Critical for consistent brewing

**Tip 2: Preheat Thoroughly**
- Use 200-300g rinse water (not just 50-100g)
- Chemex is large, needs more heat
- Swirl rinse water to heat walls
- Warm Chemex prevents temperature drop

**Tip 3: Batch Size Matters**
- Chemex performs best at 2-4 cups (30-60g coffee)
- Single servings better with V60 or AeroPress
- Too little coffee = fast, underextracted
- Sweet spot: 40-50g coffee

**Tip 4: The Pause is Intentional**
- Pauses between pours allow controlled extraction
- Different from continuous pour methods
- Gives thick filter time to drain
- Creates cleaner cup

**Tip 5: Grind Coarser Than V60**
- Chemex needs coarser grind due to:
  - Longer contact time (4-5 min vs 3 min)
  - Thicker filter (more resistance)
  - Larger batch size (more coffee bed depth)
- Typically 3-5 clicks coarser than V60

**Tip 6: Embrace the Clarity**
- Chemex designed for clean, clear cups
- Don't fight the method - use appropriate coffees
- Best for balanced, clean origins
- Not ideal for heavy-bodied or funky coffees

### Advanced Techniques

**Temperature Stepping** (Experimental):
- Bloom at 90¬∞C (gentle extraction)
- Main pours at 94¬∞C (full extraction)
- Theory: Gentle bloom, aggressive main extraction
- Results: Mixed, some prefer it

**Single Large Pour** (Quick Method):
- Bloom: 110g, 0:45
- Single continuous pour: 110g ‚Üí 700g over 2:00-2:30
- Total time: 3:30-4:00
- Trade-off: Faster but less control

**Bypass Method**:
- Brew concentrate (42g:600g = 1:14.3)
- Add 100g bypass water after brewing
- Final ratio: 1:16.7
- Benefit: Adjustable strength, safety net
