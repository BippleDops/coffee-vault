## üìù Step-by-Step Instructions

### Detailed Steps

1. **Preparation**
   - Weigh 200g coffee beans
   - Grind to coarse (French press or coarser)
   - Weigh 1000g room temperature water
   - Prepare cold brew maker or large container
   - Optional: use filtered water for best results

2. **Combine** (Day 1, Evening)
   - Add ground coffee to container
   - Add all water
   - Stir gently to ensure all grounds saturated
   - Ensure no dry pockets
   - Cover container
   - Note start time

3. **Steep** (12-24 hours)
   - Let steep at room temperature OR
   - Steep in refrigerator (some prefer this)
   - Do not disturb during steeping
   - **Room temp**: 12-16 hours
   - **Refrigerator**: 18-24 hours (slower extraction)
   - Longer steep = stronger, more bitter
   - Shorter steep = lighter, brighter

4. **Strain** (Day 2, Next Day)
   - Prepare filter system
   - Strain through paper filter, cloth, or nut milk bag
   - This takes time - be patient (15-30 minutes)
   - Don't press grounds (increases bitterness)
   - Let gravity do the work
   - Can double-strain for ultra-clean result

5. **Store**
   - Transfer to clean container
   - Cover and refrigerate
   - Label with date
   - Lasts 1-2 weeks refrigerated
   - Concentrate ready to use

6. **Serve**
   - Dilute concentrate 1:1 with water or milk
   - Pour over ice
   - Add sweetener if desired
   - Enjoy smooth, cold coffee

### Technique Notes

**Water Temp**: Room temp extracts faster, refrigerator slower
**Steep Time**: Longer increases strength and slight bitterness
**Straining**: Don't press - let gravity work
**Dilution**: 1:1 is standard, adjust to taste
**Storage**: Refrigerate concentrate, lasts 1-2 weeks

## üí° Tips & Troubleshooting

**Tips**:
- **Make batches** - cold brew stores 1-2 weeks, make large batches
- Use filtered water for best taste
- Coarse grind is critical - too fine creates muddy brew
- Don't press grounds when straining - increases bitterness
- Room temp steeping is faster than refrigerator
- Label with date - track freshness
- Perfect make-ahead option for busy weeks
- Dilute to taste - some prefer 1:2 ratio
- Excellent base for milk drinks, iced lattes
- Very cost-effective vs buying cold brew

**Common Mistakes**:
- Grinding too fine - creates muddy, bitter brew
- Steeping too long (>24 hours) - excessive bitterness
- Pressing grounds when straining - releases bitterness
- Not saturating all grounds - weak, uneven extraction
- Using light roast coffees - cold brew mutes their best qualities
- Not refrigerating concentrate - spoils quickly

**Adjustment Guide**:
- **Too Weak**: More coffee, longer steep time
- **Too Bitter**: Shorter steep, coarser grind, don't press when straining
- **Too Muddy**: Grind coarser, better filtering, don't press
- **Sour/Underextracted**: Steep longer (unlikely with 16+ hours)
- **Too Strong**: Use 1:6 ratio or dilute more when serving

**Troubleshooting**:
- **Muddy/Sediment**: Grind coarser, better filter, don't press
- **Bitter**: Steep less time, coarser grind
- **Weak**: More coffee, longer steep
- **Sour**: Steep significantly longer
- **Off Flavors**: Check bean freshness, clean equipment

## üìù Notes

Cold brew is the make-ahead champion of coffee brewing. Make a batch on Sunday, enjoy smooth iced coffee all week. The long, cold extraction creates a fundamentally different flavor profile than hot coffee - lower acidity, more sweetness, smooth even at high concentrations.

**Cold Brew Philosophy**:
Cold brew extracts differently than hot brewing. Cold water extracts fewer acids and bitter compounds while extracting sugars and chocolate notes. The result is smooth, sweet, low-acid coffee that's perfect for iced drinks.

**Cold Brew vs Iced Coffee**:
- **Cold Brew**: Brewed cold over 12-24 hours, smooth, low-acid, concentrated
- **Iced Coffee** (Flash Brew): Hot coffee over ice, bright, acidic, aromatic
- **When to Use Cold Brew**: Make-ahead, milk drinks, prefer smooth/low-acid
- **When to Use Flash Brew**: Want bright acidity, aromatic, single servings

**Best Use Cases**:
- Make-ahead for busy weeks (perfect)
- Summer iced coffee (excellent)
- Milk-based iced drinks (excellent - concentrate mixes well)
- Low-acid coffee needs (very good)
- Batch brewing for multiple days (ideal)
- Office/work - bring concentrate, dilute daily (great)

**Perfect Coffees for Cold Brew**:
- Brazilian natural/pulped natural (chocolate, caramel, nuts)
- Colombian medium roast (balanced, sweet)
- Indonesian (earthy, full-bodied)
- Medium to medium-dark roasts (sweet, smooth)

**Cost Analysis**:
Making cold brew at home costs ~$1.50-2.00 per serving vs $4-6 at caf√©s. At 2 servings/day, saves $700-1000+ per year.

**Commercial Cold Brew**:
Most commercial cold brew is made using this method at scale. Some use Toddy systems, others use large-batch immersion vessels. The principle is identical - coarse grind, cold water, long steeping, no pressure.

**Concentrate Benefits**:
Making concentrate (vs ready-to-drink) offers flexibility:
- Dilute to desired strength
- Use for milk drinks at full strength
- Takes less storage space
- Lasts longer (higher concentration more stable)
